No Flanders/Belgium presence. The "Flanders origin" family tradition is the generic Transylvanian Saxon origin myth (repeated in Wikipedia's colonist-origin formula). It is **not surname-specific** and has no documentary corroboration for Kascher specifically. The Brazilian cluster is worth a separate investigation thread — possibly via late-1800s Saxon emigration waves from Transylvania to Brazil.

Etymology: inconclusive. Silesian/East-German forms Kasch / Kasche derive from Slavic pet-forms of Lukas, but are likely unrelated to the Transylvanian Kascher. No obvious connection to the occupational "Käser" (cheesemaker).
