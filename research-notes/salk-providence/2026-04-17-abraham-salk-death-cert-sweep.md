# Abraham Salk 1925 RI Death Certificate Sweep — 2026-04-17

## Headline outcomes

1. **The Feb 22, 1925 death certificate is NOT currently online.** The RI State Archives Preservica digitization covers Feb 16–19, 1925 Providence deaths (49 pages, reg. # through 543); the Feb 20–28 batch that contains Abraham Salk's certificate is a **filing gap** on the public platform. User must submit a formal request via the RI State Archives online form.
2. **NEW discovery — Abraham Salk had at least THREE daughters**, not just Elizabeth. Previously-unknown sisters of Elizabeth (Salk) Salmanson are surfaced via Rhode Island Jewish Historical Association (RIJHA) obituary files.
3. **NEW evidence supporting a direct Hyman Salk / Salk's Hardware connection** via Providence's endogamous Salk cluster. Hyman's wife was *also* named Elizabeth Salk (née Salk) — daughter of **Louis and Dena (Hassel) Salk**, the first named grandparents-generation figures we have in this branch.

## 2. Hyman Salk / Salk's Hardware connection (Tier 2)

Source: Warwick Beacon / independentri.com article + RIJHA S obits.

- **Hyman Salk** founded Salk's Hardware 1900 (bought a Providence tailor's shop).
- Son Sam Salk extended to Oakland Beach 1917.
- Hyman's wife: **Elizabeth (Salk) Salk** — same surname before marriage. Daughter of **Louis Salk** and **Dena (Hassel) Salk**. Born 18 April 1876, Russia. Member Congregation Sons of Abraham. Buried Lincoln Park Cemetery.
- Children of Hyman × Elizabeth: Mrs. Max (Florence) Brodsky, Louis Salk, Samuel Salk.

**Analysis.** Two Salk families in Providence who intermarried: if Hyman's wife Elizabeth was Abraham's cousin (or niece), this firmly establishes the endogamous Salk cluster in the 1890s–1920s Providence Russian-Lithuanian Jewish community. The appearance of **Louis Salk** as Elizabeth-the-wife-of-Hyman's father is *potentially* the same "Louis (Elizar/Lazar) Salk" named in vault Open Question #18 (the Troskunai Zalk connection candidate). If Louis is Abraham's father OR uncle, this is a two-generation breakthrough.

**Caution.** "Elizabeth Salk" is the name of *both* Abraham's daughter (our ancestor, wife of Barnett Salmanson) and Hyman's wife (different person). These are two Elizabeth Salks in the same city in the same decade. Do not conflate. The wife of Hyman was b. 1876; our Elizabeth was b. ~1887 (GEDCOM) or similar.

## 4. Actionable paths for the death certificate itself

### Path A — Rhode Island State Archives (recommended, Tier 1 when delivered)

- **Form URL**: https://forms.sos.ri.gov/VitalRecordsOrderForm
- **Subject**: Abraham Salk, died Feb 22, 1925, Providence, RI
- **Contact**: RI State Archives, 401-222-2353
- **Access rules**: RI death records 50+ years old are public
- **Fee and turnaround**: not listed on intro page; user must check the form
- **Status**: gate this as the single most decisive record; the digitized Feb 16-28 Preservica folder is mis-labeled (only Feb 17-19 included) so the cert is not self-serve online

### Path B — FamilySearch (parallel, free)

- **Collection**: "Rhode Island, vital extracts ... deaths 1901-1953" (Collection ID 1935767)
- **Film**: #6082179 (158-reel RI death certificate microfilm, digitized from RI State Archives)
- **URL**: https://www.familysearch.org/search/collection/1935767
- **Access**: FamilySearch login required; images may be viewable only at a FamilySearch Center depending on the contract
- **Search**: given name Abraham, surname Salk, death year 1925, place Rhode Island

### Path C — RI Death Index microfilm 1921–1930

- FamilySearch catalog 713149 (microfiche 1-79): https://www.familysearch.org/en/search/catalog/713149
- Free with FamilySearch login at a FamilySearch Center

### Path D — Cemetery office inquiries

- **Lincoln Park Cemetery** (1469 Post Rd, Warwick RI; 401-737-5333) — most likely burial location given the family pattern. Ask for Abraham Salk burial record Feb 1925 and whether they hold a copy of the burial permit / death certificate.
- **Sons of Israel and David Cemetery** (Reservoir Ave, Providence) — burial records 1859-1950 held by **American Jewish Archives Cincinnati** (513-221-1875). Less likely but worth ruling out given Harvey M Salk was buried there.
