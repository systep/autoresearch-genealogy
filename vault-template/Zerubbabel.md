## Biography

Zerubbabel was a post-exilic leader of the Jewish community who returned from Babylonian captivity. According to the books of Ezra and Haggai, he led the first group of Jews back to Jerusalem and oversaw the rebuilding of the Second Temple. He is described as the grandson of King Jeconiah (Jehoiachin), the penultimate king of Judah who was deported to Babylon in 597 BCE.

The GEDCOM designates him as "3rd Exilarch" and records his father as Shealtiel (Ben Jehoiachin, second Exilarch) at @I502096@. His grandfather Jeconiah appears at @I502097@ as "nineteenth and penultimate king of Judah." His son Hananiah (5th Exilarch, b. 485 BCE, d. 440 BCE) appears at @I502094@.

The genealogical chain from Zerubbabel through the Exilarchs to Bustanai and eventually to the Luria family represents many centuries of claimed descent that is not independently verifiable for most links.
