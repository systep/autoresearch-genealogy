## Biography

David Bright was born David Brecht on August 9, 1740 in Heidelberg, Lancaster County, Pennsylvania (GEDCOM, Geni). He was the son of Johann Michael Brecht II, a German Reformed Church member who had emigrated from Schriesheim in the Palatinate of Germany in 1726, and Margareta Simone, a French Huguenot from Pfeffelbach.

David was a miller by trade and worked for several years near Schaefferstown, Pennsylvania. He later moved the mill seat to the Swatara Valley above the Blue Mountains in Berks County, about 20 miles north of Schaefferstown, where he married Mary Grant (also recorded as Mary Gale), a neighbor's daughter (RootsWeb "Descendants of Johann Brecht"; Montgomery, "Historical and Biographical Annals").

During the Revolutionary War, David supplied large quantities of flour for General George Washington's troops, operating about 20 miles from Valley Forge during the winter of 1777-78 (RootsWeb, Historical and Biographical Annals). His name was written "Brecht" until 1783, after which the anglicized "Bright" became the standard form (RootsWeb).

The couple had six known children: Mary "Polly" (1766-1844), Michael (1771-1833), Jesse W. (1773-1856), David Grant (1775-1851), George (c. 1782-1873), and Margaret (who married John Moore) (GEDCOM, Geni, RootsWeb). About 1784, David removed to Greenbrier County, Virginia, where he engaged in farming until his death in 1808 (RootsWeb).

David's will, written in Greenbrier County, gave his wife Mary a horse, saddle and bridle, two beds, two cows, and kitchen furniture. He gave his son Michael six acres of land with house and stable, and the balance of the plantation to his son Jesse. After bequests to all grandchildren, the residue of the estate went to sons David and George, and his son in law John Moore (RootsWeb, Greenbrier County will records).

David represents the critical link between the German Brecht emigrants and the Virginia/South Carolina Bright family. His son George (c. 1782-1873) married Mary "Polly" McNeil and had numerous children including [[Samuel_Graham_Bright]] (1825-1909). Samuel married Mary Jane Bernard and fathered [[George_Michael_Bright]] (1852-1939), who married Willis Henrietta Youngblood. Their son [[Albert_Graham_Bright]] (1893-1961) married Rebecca Moseley Long, connecting the Bright line to the Cox/Long/Huff families and the present family (Family_Tree.md).

David's siblings included Jacob Brecht Sr. (b. Apr 13, 1729), George (b. Feb 9, 1731), Johann Michael Bright (b. Mar 24, 1732), Katharina Sheetz (b. Apr 6, 1734), John Bright (b. Feb 20, 1736), Peter Brecht (b. May 13, 1738), Maria Bright (b. Aug 1, 1742), Sarah Jones (b. Jan 19, 1745), and Christina Pechin (b. Aug 12, 1747) (Geni, RootsWeb). NOTE: Some tertiary sources list "George Adam Bright" (1730-1804) among Johann Michael's children, but this attribution is disputed; George Adam may have been the son of Johann Stephan Brecht (1692-1747), Michael's older brother. See [[Johann_Michael_Brecht]] Data Discrepancies for details.

David's burial is recorded at Charles Evans Cemetery, Reading, Berks County, Pennsylvania (Find a Grave #47249821). The SAR Patriot Burial records also list this location. This is notable because he died in Greenbrier County, Virginia; his remains or headstone may have been relocated to Charles Evans Cemetery at a later date (Find a Grave).

David died in 1808 in Greenbrier, Virginia (GEDCOM).
