## Biography

David Bright was born David Brecht on August 9, 1740 in Heidelberg, Lancaster County, Pennsylvania (GEDCOM, Geni). He was the son of Johann Michael Brecht II, a German Reformed Church member who had emigrated from Schriesheim in the Palatinate of Germany in 1726, and Margareta Simone, a French Huguenot from Pfeffelbach.

David married Mary Gail Grant in 1769 (Geni). The couple had five known children. David's alias name "Brecht" confirms he was part of the Brecht/Bright surname transition that occurred as the family anglicized their name in colonial Pennsylvania.

David represents the critical link between the German Brecht emigrants and the South Carolina Bright family. His son George Michael Bright Sr. (b. 1782, Pine Grove Township, Berks County, PA; d. 1873, Floyd, Virginia) married Mary "Polly" McNeil and had numerous children including Samuel Graham Bright (1825-1909). Samuel married Mary Jane Bernard and fathered George Michael Bright (1852-1939), who married Willis Henrietta Youngblood. Their son Albert Graham Bright (1893-1961) married Rebecca Moseley Long, connecting the Bright line to the Cox/Long/Huff families and the present family (Family_Tree.md).

David's siblings included Jacob Brecht Sr., George Adam Bright, Johann Michael Bright (born Brecht), Katharina Sheetz (born Bright), Peter Brecht, Maria Bright, Sarah Jones (born Bright), Christiana Pechin (born Bright), and John Bright (Geni). George Adam Bright moved to Frederick County, Virginia in 1759, and is buried in Dutch Hollow Cemetery west of Greenville, SC, establishing the Bright family's arrival in the Greenville area (Family_Tree.md).

David died in 1808 in Greenbrier, Virginia (GEDCOM).
