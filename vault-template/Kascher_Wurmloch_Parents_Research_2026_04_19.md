### Hypothesis for Georg's Parents

**No named hypothesis can be advanced from secondary sources.** The surname "Kascher" does not appear in the canonical Transylvanian Saxon surname compilations, suggesting either:

1. **Local dialect variant**: Possibly a Wurmloch-specific spelling of a more common Saxon name (candidates: Kaeser/Käser "cheesemaker", Kascher-from-Katscher toponymic Silesian, or a corrupted form of Gaschler/Kaschler). No evidence yet supports any of these over the others.
2. **Recent arrival before 1792**: Georg's parents may have arrived in Wurmloch from elsewhere shortly before his birth (consistent with the 18th-century wave of "Landler" Protestant exiles from Austria/Carinthia, 1734-1776, and smaller post-war resettlements). A recently arrived family would leave a thin church record footprint in Wurmloch specifically.
3. **Record loss**: Pre-1800 Wurmloch Kirchenbuch entries may be fragmentary; confirmed coverage begins later. The decisive record (the 1792 baptism entry itself) is not known to be digitized.

**Most probable father name (speculative only)**: Given Saxon naming conventions where firstborn sons often receive the paternal grandfather's name, and Georg's own eldest documented son was Mathias (not Michael), we can weakly infer **Georg's father may have been named Mathias**. This is **Speculative** (one-inference-only, no source), logged here only as a hypothesis to test against the Kirchenbuch, not as a claim.
