## Canonical Sandys Pedigree (Tier 2 Corroboration)

### Cumbrian Origin

The Sandys family originated in the Furness / Cumberland region of the Lake District. A heraldic visitation in 1563 recorded the Rottington Hall branch as "of St Bees in the County of Cumberland" (Southwell Minster Fact Sheet 173, Tier 2). Archbishop Edwin Sandys (1519 to 1588) was born at Esthwaite Hall, about one mile south of Hawkshead, Cumbria. His parents were William Sandys, esq., of Esthwaite Hall and Graythwaite Hall, and Margaret Dixon, daughter of Sir John Dixon (Southwell Minster, Wikipedia, Tier 2). Edwin Sandys founded Hawkshead Grammar School in 1585.

This directly confirms the vault GEDCOM note that Sir Edwin Sandys (the son, 1561 to 1629) has associations with "Furnesse Fells, Lancashire" (historically Furness was in Lancashire until 1974). The birthplace Worcestershire is for the son; the ancestral seat is Cumbria / Furness.

### Worcestershire Seat

The Archbishop's eldest surviving son, Sir Samuel Sandys (1560 to 1623), acquired the lease of the royal manor of Ombersley, Worcestershire, in 1582 and was ancestor of the Lords Sandys of Ombersley (History of Parliament Online; Worcestershire Archive and Archaeology Service, Tier 2). The family also held Wickhamford Manor near Evesham (Badsey Society, Tier 2). Sir Samuel Sandys of Ombersley is vault file [[Samuel_Sandys_MP]] and is strongly corroborated.

### Virginia Company Connection

Sir Edwin Sandys (the son, 1561 to 1629), vault file [[Edwin_Sandys]], was the Archbishop's second surviving son. He served as Treasurer of the Virginia Company of London from 1619 and is credited with establishing the House of Burgesses in Virginia in 1619. Also in this generation were George Sandys (poet, traveller, Virginia Company colonial treasurer in Jamestown 1621 to 1625) and Myles Sandys (Wikipedia, Tier 2). The Virginia Company connection is Tier 2 corroborated and can be cited with confidence.

## Areas of Concern

### 1. Richard Sandys, spouse of Hester Aucher

WikiTree Sandys-65 and secondary genealogy sources identify Richard Sandys (baptised 11 August 1616, Ombersley, Worcestershire; died 1669) as son of Sir Edwin Sandys (1591 to 1623), MP for Droitwich, not as son of Sir Samuel Sandys (1560 to 1623). He purchased Downe Hall (alias Downe Court) in Kent, served as a Colonel in the Parliamentary army, and married Hester Aucher.

The vault file [[Samuel_Sandys_MP]] states the child list includes "Richard Sands/Sandys (b. 1616, Lancashire)" but the better Tier 2 correlation is that Richard (baptised 1616 Ombersley) was a grandson of Sir Samuel (via Sir Edwin of Droitwich), not a son. This discrepancy is noted here but NOT patched in person files pending a Tier 1 baptism record.

### 2. Richard Sandys (Kent) to Stephen Sands (Bensalem) bridge, DISPUTED

The most critical weakness in the vault chain is the jump from Col. Richard Sandys of Downe Court, Kent (Anglican, Royalist-adjacent establishment family) to Stephen Sands of Bensalem, Bucks County, Pennsylvania (Quaker, married Jane Cowgill 25 October 1685). Rootsweb's Quaker Corner and related genealogical forums flag this descent as scholarly questionable: the Sandys were Anglican establishment (Archbishop's descendants, prebendaries, knights), and a direct Quaker emigrant son is unlikely to have been received by Lancaster or Bucks County Meetings without controversy that would have left records.

Stephen Sands (before 1658 to 1732, WikiTree Sands-139, Find a Grave 158288364) and Elinor Sands (1692 to 1744, WikiTree Sands-23, m. John Hough 1 June 1714 at Middletown MM) are well-documented in Bucks County Quaker records. Their parentage upstream of Stephen is the weak link. No primary source currently in the vault shows Stephen as son of Col. Richard Sandys.

### 3. Eleanor Sands husband discrepancy

Vault file [[Eleanor_Sands]] notes user instructions label her husband "John Stangle" while GEDCOM and WikiTree/Geni confirm "John Hough III." Tier 2 WikiTree Sands-23 confirms marriage to John Hough, 1 June 1714, Middletown MM, Bucks County. The Stangle reference appears to be a user data error.

## Hypothesis Statement

H1 (Supported, Tier 2): Sir Edwin Sandys (1561 to 1629) and Sir Samuel Sandys (1560 to 1623) are both sons of Archbishop Edwin Sandys (1519 to 1588) of Esthwaite Hall, Cumbria, and are correctly identified in the vault. The Virginia Company connection via Sir Edwin is secure.

H2 (Moderate, needs Tier 1): Richard Sandys (bapt. 1616 Ombersley) who married Hester Aucher is a grandson, not son, of Sir Samuel Sandys. His father is more likely Sir Edwin Sandys of Droitwich (1591 to 1623). Vault parentage in [[Samuel_Sandys_MP]] may require a one-generation correction.

H3 (Speculative / Disputed): The descent from Col. Richard Sandys of Downe Court to Stephen Sands of Bensalem PA is not substantiated by any Tier 1 or Tier 2 source located in this session. Scholarly doubt exists about whether a Kent Anglican Sandys could credibly be the father of a Bucks County Quaker Sands. This bridge should be treated as speculative pending a primary record (Friends Meeting minutes naming Stephen's parents, a Kent or Lancashire parish register, or a Downe Court probate).
