# Hypothesis: William Baldwin of Hook Norton (fl. 1650s-1670s), father of John/Thomas/Francis Baldwin Chester-PA immigrants, had parents Francis Baldwin and Johan (Unknown)

## Statement

William Baldwin (b. ~1626-1631, d. ~1670, Hook Norton, Oxfordshire), husband of Mary Daves and father of the three Baldwin brothers who emigrated to Chester County Pennsylvania as Joshua Hastings's servants circa 1682-1683, was himself the son of a Francis Baldwin and a mother recorded only as Johan (surname unknown).

## Evidence For

1. **WikiTree Baldwin-2344** (Tier 3, user-contributed tree): Names Francis Baldwin as father and "Johan Unknown" as mother of William Baldwin (1626-1670) of Hook Norton, who m. Mary Daves ~1654.
2. **Downstream agreement on William + Mary Daves**: Three independent secondary/tertiary sources converge on William Baldwin (1626-1670) and Mary Daves as the parental pair of Francis Baldwin b. 20 Apr 1667 Hook Norton: WikiTree Baldwin-2016, FamilySearch GS11-74M, Chatham Historical Journal 1994. This makes the William-of-Hook-Norton identification (one generation up from John Baldwin Sr.) moderate-signal, though does NOT itself speak to William's own parents.
3. **Geni profile** for William John Baldwin (1632-1670) Geni 6000000006232230560 echoes the Hook Norton identification, though Geni merges are historically unreliable.

## Evidence Against

1. **Parish register lacuna**: Per the Baldwin-2344 profile's own caveat, a close read of the Hook Norton parish registers shows (a) no baptism entry for a William Baldwin in the expected 1620-1635 window, and (b) the Baulden children recorded in the register name their father as John, not Francis. This directly undercuts the Francis Baldwin parentage claim.
2. **Geographic implausibility**: The Francis Baldwin who is named as father is said to have been born in Hertfordshire. A Hertfordshire Francis having children in Oxfordshire in the 1620s-30s is possible but not the default pattern, and no record of Francis moving to Oxfordshire has been cited.
3. **No primary source**: Despite multiple tertiary trees repeating the claim, no Tier 1 document (parish register, probate, Quaker meeting minute, manorial record) has been located that explicitly names William Baldwin's father.

## Analysis

**Confidence on the William+Mary-Daves identification** (one generation above John Baldwin Sr.): **moderate**. Three independent secondary/tertiary sources agree; no contradicting evidence has surfaced; it is internally consistent with the confirmed Hook Norton baptism of brother Francis.

**Confidence on the Francis-Baldwin-and-Johan identification** (two generations above John Baldwin Sr., i.e. William's own parents): **speculative**. The sole named source is a single WikiTree profile which itself flags its own data as questionable. The parish register evidence (John, not Francis, as the Baulden/Baldwin father in Hook Norton) actively points away from this identification. The Hertfordshire origin attributed to Francis further weakens the claim.

**Working judgment**: Do NOT promote Francis Baldwin and Johan into the vault person hierarchy. Create a William Baldwin person file documenting the moderate-signal identification of William+Mary Daves, and leave William's parents as "unknown, tentatively Francis Baldwin and Johan (WikiTree Baldwin-2344, Tier 3, contested)."

## Decisive Records (Next Steps)

1. **Hook Norton parish register primary read** (not via user trees): Obtain digitized images from Oxfordshire History Centre or Oxfordshire FHS for the years ~1620-1680, looking for (a) baptism of any William Baldwin/Baulden, (b) marriage of William Baldwin to Mary Daves ~1653, (c) burial of William Baldwin ~1670, (d) all Baldwin/Baulden baptisms with father's name, to determine whether the dominant Hook Norton Baldwin patriarch in that generation is really named John.
2. **Banbury Monthly Meeting records** (Oxfordshire Quaker meeting for Hook Norton area): Check for birth register, certificates of removal, and sufferings lists naming Baldwin family members.
3. **Oxfordshire probate**: Search Oxford University Archives / TNA PROB 11 for wills/administrations of any William Baldwin d. ~1670 in north Oxfordshire.
4. **Chatham Historical Journal Vol 7 No 1 (March 1994) original article**: Obtain full text; the present vault citation is second-hand via a WordPress reprint. Determine whether the author cited a primary source for the William+Mary parentage or merely repeated tree data.
5. **Hertfordshire Francis Baldwin check**: If Baldwin-2344's Hertfordshire origin for Francis is serious, check Hertfordshire parish registers for a Francis Baldwin contemporaneous with these dates and for any documented migration to Oxfordshire.
