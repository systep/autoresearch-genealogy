# Hypothesis: Annie Fitterman (b. 1893) of Voroshilovka is the maternal anchor of the Kuniansky Atlanta matriline

## Statement

Anna "Annie" Fitterman, born 9 September 1893 in Voroshilovka (Podolia guberniya, Russian Empire; now Voroshylivka, Tyvriv raion, Vinnytsia oblast, Ukraine), emigrated to Atlanta, Georgia, married Louis "Louie" Kuniansky, and was mother of Max Lewis Kuniansky (b. 1917, Atlanta) plus siblings Raymond Louis, Leon Lewis, Harry P., and Isadore "Sunny" Louis Kuniansky. Her parents are not yet identified by a Tier 1 or Tier 2 source.

## Evidence For

1. Breman Museum oral history (Tier 2, secondary): Max Kuniansky interview 1990-07-28, ArchivesSpace id 28491 and thebreman.org Cuba Family Archives Oral Histories ID 871. Max names Voroshilovka as his mother's birthplace.
2. Geni profile (Tier 3): Anna Kuniansky (Fitterman), 1893-1975, Atlanta. Lists spouse Louie Kuniansky and children including Max Louis Kuniansky. Parents are marked Private; not usable.
3. Geni adjacent profiles (Tier 3): Raymond Louis Kuniansky (1923-2004), Harry P. Kuniansky, and Find a Grave memorial 235262076 for Raymond.
4. Oral/tree consensus (Tier 3): Annie's brothers Leon Fitterman (Atlanta) and Edward Fitterman (Nashville). Leon Fitterman 1875-1957 exists on FamilySearch (GW86-7BH).
5. Place context (Tier 2): jewua.org entry for Voroshilovka confirms shtetl was in Tyvriv volost, Vinnitsky yezd, Podolia guberniya, with Jewish population of 1,592 in 1897 (50 percent of town); consistent with a Fitterman family presence ca 1893.

## Evidence Against

1. No primary source (vital record, ship manifest, naturalization, census) has yet been located linking Annie Fitterman to specific parents in Voroshilovka.
2. Annie's parents on Geni are marked Private, indicating no public tree citation exists at Tier 3.
3. US Census records for Annie/Louis Kuniansky in Atlanta 1910/1920/1930/1940 have not yet been verified from a free source; Ancestry 1940 Census entry for "Louis L Kuniansky" in Georgia surfaced in results but is login-gated.

## Analysis

Annie Fitterman's Voroshilovka origin is Strong Signal because the Breman oral history (Tier 2) and the Geni tree (Tier 3) independently converge on the same shtetl. Her Atlanta marriage to Louis Kuniansky and motherhood of the Kuniansky sibling set (Max, Raymond, Harry, Isadore, Leon Lewis) is also Strong Signal via Geni plus Find a Grave plus the Breman oral history.

Her parents' identities are currently Speculative. The next practical lead is Leon Fitterman (1875-1957), plausibly her elder brother, whose US naturalization petition (likely filed in Atlanta, N.D. Ga., between 1900 and 1920) should state his father's name and birthplace. Edward Fitterman of Nashville is a parallel lead via M.D. Tenn. naturalization records.

For the Ukraine side, the Alex Krakovsky wiki (commons.wikimedia.org hosted scans) has been systematically publishing Podolia Jewish metrical books. The relevant DAVO fonds for Tyvriv/Vinnytsia uyezd Jewish metrical books should contain Fitterman / Fiterman / Фітерман / Фитерман entries for Voroshilovka. This has not yet been directly checked and is the key outstanding Tier 1 source.

## Decisive Record

Any ONE of the following would resolve parentage:

1. Alex Krakovsky / DAVO (Vinnytsia oblast archive) scanned Jewish birth metrical book for Voroshilovka 1893 (Fitterman/Фитерман entry for Chana/Hanna).
2. Fulton County, Georgia marriage license for Louis Kuniansky and Annie Fitterman (likely 1912-1916, before Max's 1917 birth), which typically names bride's father.
3. Leon Fitterman (1875-1957) US naturalization petition (N.D. Ga., Atlanta Division), which names parents and birthplace.
4. Ship manifest for Annie Fitterman's arrival at a US port (Baltimore, Philadelphia, New York) ca 1905-1913, which typically names the nearest relative in the old country and the town of origin.
