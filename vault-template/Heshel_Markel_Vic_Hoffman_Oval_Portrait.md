# Heshel Markel — Vic Hoffman Oval Portrait

## Subject Identification (RESOLVED 2026-04-25)

The subject of this oval-framed portrait is **[[Heschel_Herman_Markel]]** (Hersch Markel; b. 1862 Waniowice; d. 1916 WWI), per the family-supplied filename `Assets/Herschl Portrait from Vic Hoffman-detail.jpeg`. The "Vic Hoffman" suffix indicates this is the print held by descendant **Vic Hoffman**, distinct from the head-and-shoulders portrait of Heshel held in the main family archive (filename `Assets/Herschl Markel 1862-1916.jpeg`, captioned "grandfather Heshel Markel 1862-1916").

This is therefore a **second photograph of Heshel Markel** preserved in a different branch of the family. The two prints depict the same individual but at different sittings: the Vic Hoffman copy shows Heshel **seated, full-body, no head-covering, hand-tinted, in an ornate oval brass frame**; the main archive copy shows Heshel **head-and-shoulders, in a wide-brim black hat, sepia, no frame**. The combination suggests the family had at least two formal portrait sittings of Heshel during his life — both consistent with the documented Tier 1 dates 1862-1916.

## Description

- **Format**: Oval-framed photograph in an ornate brass / bronze frame topped with a sculpted bow / ribbon ornament. The frame is the deep oval style typical of late Victorian / Edwardian / Belle Époque parlour-display photographs (c. 1885-1915).
- **Image**: Hand-tinted (or hand-colored) studio photograph. Heshel is depicted **seated in a chair, full body or three-quarter length**, against a **greenish-teal painted backdrop** (typical of hand-tinted photographs where the studio added color to a black-and-white print).
- **Subject**: Heshel Markel, dark hair, dark beard, wearing a **long dark coat** (possibly a rabbinical or formal long coat). Hands rest visible at his sides or on the chair arms. Face appears young to middle-aged (~30-50). No visible head-covering in this sitting.
- **Posture**: Formal, seated, hands relaxed.
- **Backdrop**: Plain green-blue tone, no pattern.
- **No visible caption, signature, studio mark, or date on the image as pushed to `Assets/`.**

## Stylistic Dating

- The brass / bronze oval frame with sculpted bow ornament: c. **1885-1915** European parlor display.
- Hand-tinted photographs in this format: 1890-1920.
- The subject's beard style (full but groomed): consistent with observant Eastern European Jewish men of the 1890s-1910s.

These cues collectively suggest the portrait was **taken c. 1890-1915 in Galicia** (most likely the Sambor / Lwów region, given Heshel's documented residence in Waniowice). It then traveled with the Hoffman line of descendants — Vic Hoffman is presumably a great-grandchild or great-great-grandchild of Heshel Markel.
