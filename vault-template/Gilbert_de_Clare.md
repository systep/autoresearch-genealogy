## Biography

Gilbert de Clare, known as "the Red" (likely for his red hair), was born on September 2, 1243, at Christchurch, Hampshire. He was the eldest son of Richard de Clare, 5th Earl of Gloucester, and Maud de Lacy. He became 6th Earl of Gloucester and 5th Earl of Hertford in 1262 upon his father's death (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Gilbert was one of the most powerful magnates in England. He played a major role in the Second Barons' War (1264 to 1267), initially supporting Simon de Montfort but later switching sides to support the future [[Edward_I]] at the Battle of Evesham (1265). His first marriage to Alice de Lusignan was annulled in 1271 (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Gilbert married [[Joan_Plantagenet]], daughter of [[Edward_I]], in 1290. Despite the significant age gap (Gilbert was 47, Joan was 18), the marriage was politically important. They had four children, including [[Margaret_de_Clare]] (b. 1292), through whom the De Clare line connects to the De Audley and Neville families in the pedigree charts (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Gilbert died on December 7, 1295, at Monmouth Castle and was buried at Tewkesbury Abbey (Wikipedia, Tier 2).
