# Bustanai ben Haninai, Exilarch

> **Confidence: Moderate.** Bustanai is a Wikipedia confirmed historical figure, the first Exilarch under Arab rule. However, his role as ancestor in this tree is part of the claimed Davidic descent through the Maharal of Prague. This chain is contested by modern scholarship (Putik, Muneles). The connection from the Maharal back to King David relies on Meir Perels' Megillat Yuhasin (1745), which contains documented errors.

## Biography

Bustanai ben Haninai was the first Jewish Exilarch under Arab rule following the Muslim conquest of Persia. According to tradition, he was the last representative of the Exilarchic line during the Sassanid period and the first to hold the position under the Rashidun Caliphate. He is said to have received the captured daughter of the Persian king as a wife from Caliph Omar.

The GEDCOM records him as "Hananya 'Bustenai' ben Haninai, Exilarch & Gaon of Pumbeditha." The GEDCOM dates (b. ABT 589, d. ABT 638) appear to be significantly earlier than the Wikipedia-attested dates (~618-670), representing a discrepancy of approximately 30 years.

He is a pivotal figure in the claimed Davidic descent lineage: the chain passes from the biblical kings through the Exilarchs to Bustanai, then through the Geonim and eventually to the Luria family.
