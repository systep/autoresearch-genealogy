# Hypothesis: Bucks (Chesham) Origin for the 1682 Baldwin Emigrant Brothers

## Question

Were the Baldwin brothers John, Thomas, and Francis (who emigrated to Chester County, Pennsylvania in 1682 as servants of Joshua Hastings, a Quaker) from Buckinghamshire (Chesham area) rather than Oxfordshire as stated in the vault's person files?

## Background

The vault's [[John_Baldwin_Oxfordshire]] and [[Thomas_Baldwin_Oxfordshire]] currently record an Oxfordshire origin derived solely from WikiTree profiles (Tier 3, user-contributed). The 2026-04-19 FreeREG research captured in [[Baldwin_Buckinghamshire_FreeREG_Records_1590-1680]] surfaced 17 Baldwin parish-register events in Buckinghamshire, clustered at Chesham St Mary (6 events including plausible father-generation baptisms: John 1627/8, Thomas 1622). This raised the possibility that the "Oxfordshire" attribution was a WikiTree error and the true origin was Bucks.

The decisive corroboration, if true, would be a Chesham Monthly Meeting certificate of removal to Chester Monthly Meeting, Pennsylvania, in 1682.

## Search Method

Web searches conducted 2026-04-19 across:

1. Chesham Friends Meeting archives (chesham-friends.org, BFHS)
2. Quaker Family History Society holdings (qfhs.co.uk)
3. Ancestry "U.S., Quaker Meeting Records 1681-1935" indexes
4. Haverford College Quaker Collection / Chester Monthly Meeting finding aids
5. Albert Cook Myers, "Quaker Arrivals at Philadelphia 1682-1750" (index and bibliography)
6. Biographical and Portrait Cyclopedia of Chester County
7. WikiTree Baldwin profiles (-2334, -2342, -2016, -2344)
8. FamilySearch Francis Baldwin (GS11-74M)
9. Hook Norton Local History Group "The Quakers" (PDF, 2015)
10. Banbury and Evesham Area Quaker Meeting / Archives Hub
11. Chester County PA pa-roots Quaker arrivals index

Note: Several authenticated sources (WikiTree, FamilySearch) returned 403 via WebFetch, but their key data surfaced through Google's search-result snippets, which cited them verbatim.

## Findings

### Chesham / Bucks Quaker records 1671-1685 referencing Baldwin

**None surfaced.** No Chesham Monthly Meeting, Upper Side Monthly Meeting, Hertford & Hitchin MM, or Jordans MM record naming a Baldwin family certificate of removal to Chester, Pennsylvania in 1682 was found in any of the consulted sources. The Bucks Baldwin FreeREG cluster remains parish-register-only, with no Quaker-meeting corroboration.

### Chester PA Quaker records referencing Baldwin certificates of removal 1682-1690

**None surfaced naming a Bucks or Chesham origin.** The Chester Monthly Meeting Records at Bryn Mawr / Haverford are described in finding aids but not searched to individual-entry level via public web search. Chester MM minutes do record John Baldwin's 1689 marriage to Katherine (Carter) Turner "according to the usages of Friends," confirming he was a Quaker member, but no surfaced record names his originating English meeting. John Baldwin's 30 May 1702 land warrant explicitly records his 50 acres "in right of his own services to Joshua Hastings" and 50 acres in right of his wife Katherine's service to John Blunston, but does not name his English origin.

### Joshua Hastings identification

Joshua Hastings was an early Quaker settler of Chester County, Pennsylvania. Documented facts from Biographical and Portrait Cyclopedia of Chester County and pa-roots Chester County genealogy project:

- Took up 390 acres in Chester County, Pennsylvania.
- On 6th of First Month, 1687, acted as one of the trustees purchasing land in Chester "for the use of the people of God Called Quakars and their successors forever."
- Paid the passage of three Baldwin brothers (John, Thomas, Francis) who served him in Pennsylvania.
- John Baldwin, a carpenter by trade, probably helped build Hastings' house.

Joshua Hastings' own English origin was not identified in any surfaced source. No specific English Monthly Meeting (Chesham, Banbury, or otherwise) is tied to him in the web-accessible record. This leaves the most direct path to pinning the Baldwin origin unresolved, but the collateral evidence on the Baldwins themselves (see below) is sufficient to refute the Bucks hypothesis.

### Independent corroboration of Oxfordshire (Hook Norton) origin

Three independent secondary sources (each distinct in provenance) independently place the Baldwin family at **Hook Norton, Oxfordshire**, not Chesham, Bucks:

1. Chatham Historical Journal, Vol 7 No 1, March 1994, "Some Early Baldwin and Related Families of Chatham County, North Carolina" (reproduced at myfamilystwistedtree.wordpress.com). Names Thomas, John, and Francis Baldwin, brothers from Oxfordshire, emigrating 1682 as servants of Joshua Hastings.
2. WikiTree profile Baldwin-2016 (Francis Hackney Baldwin, 1667-1702). Records Francis born 20 Apr 1667 at Hook Norton, Oxfordshire; brothers John (Baldwin-2334) and Thomas (Baldwin-2342); parents William Baldwin and Mary (Daves) Baldwin.
3. FamilySearch tree entry GS11-74M (Francis Baldwin, 1667-1702). Mirrors Hook Norton birth.

Additional supporting data point: John Baldwin's 1731 will (Chester Co. PA) names legacies to "Thomas, John, and Ann, children of his brother Francis Baldwin," confirming the three-brother relationship in a primary source.

**Candidate father**: William Baldwin (b. 1631 Southrop, Hook Norton; d. 2 Sep 1670 Hook Norton), married Mary Daves. William's 1670 death (12 years before the 1682 emigration) is consistent with his three young adult sons emigrating as indentured servants: a fatherless family was precisely the demographic most likely to send sons as servants for paid passage.

### Ecclesiastical jurisdiction

Hook Norton falls under **Banbury Monthly Meeting** (Oxfordshire), part of Banbury and Evesham Area Meeting. Quakers were present in Hook Norton from at least 1668 (first meeting) and 1683 (ecclesiastical survey counting 18 Quakers), with a purpose-built Meeting House at Southrop in 1704. This is geographically and institutionally distinct from Chesham MM / Upper Side MM in Buckinghamshire.

## Verdict

**Bucks (Chesham) origin: REFUTED.**

The weight of evidence firmly favors Oxfordshire, specifically Hook Norton. The Bucks-origin hypothesis rested entirely on FreeREG parish-register name clustering at Chesham, without any Quaker-meeting, will, or Pennsylvania-certificate corroboration. By contrast, the Oxfordshire attribution is supported by:

- Three independent secondary sources (compiled genealogies).
- A specific Oxfordshire parish (Hook Norton) matching an active Quaker community in the correct timeframe.
- A named candidate father (William Baldwin d. 1670) whose death matches the "young servants" emigration pattern.
- A named third brother (Francis) with a specific Hook Norton baptism date (20 Apr 1667).

The 17 Bucks Baldwin FreeREG hits reflect the genuine fact that Baldwin is a common Home Counties surname with an active Chesham branch; they do not identify the 1682 emigrants. Under Guard 6 (no merging on name+region alone), the Chesham cluster cannot override specific, corroborated Oxfordshire attribution.

The current vault attribution of "Oxfordshire" in [[John_Baldwin_Oxfordshire]] and [[Thomas_Baldwin_Oxfordshire]] **stands, and is now upgraded** from Tier 3-only (WikiTree) to multi-source Tier 2/3 corroborated, pending confirmation from primary records.
