## Biography

Anne Wakehurst was the daughter of Richard Wakehurst of Wakehurst in Ardingly, Sussex, and Elizabeth Echyngham (Geni, Tier 3; Syngeneia.org, Tier 3). Her father was a prominent Sussex landowner who served as MP in 1413 and Justice of the Peace for Sussex in 1415 and 1430 (History of Parliament, Tier 1/2). Elizabeth was his second wife.

Anne married **John Gainsford, Esquire**, of Crowhurst, Surrey, c. 1438 (FamilyCentral.net, Tier 3). John was the eldest son of John Gainsford senior (d. 1450) and the elder brother of [[Nicholas_Gaynesford]] of Carshalton.

Through Anne, the Gainsford and Wakehurst families were connected. Her daughter [[Agnes_Gainsford]] first married Anne's nephew, Richard Wakehurst the younger (son of Anne's brother Richard), and then Sir John Culpeper of Bedgebury. This complex web of intermarriage between the Gainsford, Wakehurst, and Culpeper families created the circumstances for the later abduction of the Wakehurst heiresses.

## The Wakehurst Family

The Wakehurst family held land in Ardingly, Sussex, from the early 13th century. William de Wakehurst is documented purchasing 40 acres in Ardingly in 1205 (Victoria County History, Tier 1/2). The estate passed through the family until 1454, when it came to the Culpepers by marriage.

Anne's father Richard was probably the son of John Wakehurst of Wakehurst (History of Parliament, Tier 1/2). The Wakehurst pedigree thus extends at least eight generations from William de Wakehurst (1205) through to the heiresses Margaret and Elizabeth (c. 1450s).

## Connection to This Family Tree

Anne Wakehurst is the mother of [[Agnes_Gainsford]], who married Sir John Culpeper of Bedgebury. Agnes's son [[Walter_Colepeper_Calais]] continues the line to [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]], [[Martin_Culpepper]], and the Sandys/Sands descendants.

Anne is also the link connecting the Culpeper family to the ancient Wakehurst family of Sussex.
