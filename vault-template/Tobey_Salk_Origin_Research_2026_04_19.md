# Hypothesis: Tobey (Tauba) Salk's Rhode Island Death Certificate Will Name the Salk Origin Shtetl in Kovno Gubernia

## Statement

Tobey (Tauba) Salk, widow of Abraham Zolly Salk of 60 Chalkstone Avenue, Providence, died in Rhode Island sometime after February 22, 1925 (her husband's death) and before c. 1955 (she would have been approximately 95 by that point assuming she was near Abraham's age). Her Rhode Island death certificate, held by the RI State Archives, will name her parents and a specific town of origin in Kovno Gubernia (or an adjacent Lithuanian gubernia), definitively resolving the open question of the Salk family's pre-emigration shtetl.

## Evidence For

1. **Abraham's 1925 death certificate confirms Tobey survived him** (Tier 1). Marital status recorded as Married, not Widowed. Any RI death record for Tobey post-dates February 22, 1925.

2. **RI vital records 1853 to 1975 are public** and held by the RI State Archives (Simpson Hall, Cranston) and RI Department of Health. FamilySearch collection covers RI death records 1802 to 1950. Post-1925 death certificates in RI carry the following fields relevant to this research: decedent's birthplace (often town-level for immigrants), father's name and birthplace, mother's maiden name and birthplace, informant name and relationship, burial location.

3. **Geographic evidence (from prior Salk-manifests hypothesis)**: Abraham at 60 Chalkstone Avenue sat in the Kovno-Gubernia Lithuanian-Jewish cluster, near Beth David-Anshei Kovno Congregation. Endogamous Lithuanian-Jewish marriage strongly predicts Tobey originated in the same region. Vault's Hyman Salk 1873 file gives Vilkomir (Ukmerge) as his birthplace, consistent with Tobey likely originating in the same uyezd.

4. **Burial location likely Lincoln Park Cemetery, Warwick** (where her daughter Elizabeth, son-in-law Barnett Salmanson, grandson Leonard Irving Salmanson, and granddaughter-in-law Thelma Salmanson Rodbell are buried, per Find a Grave Tier 2). A headstone at Lincoln Park, if located, would provide Hebrew patronymic evidence (parallel to Elizabeth's headstone reading "Hilda bat Moshe" per her file).

## Evidence Against

1. **Ten targeted WebSearches on 2026-04-21 returned no hit** for a Tobey or Tauba Salk death record, obituary, or Find a Grave memorial. Search variants covered: Tobey/Tauba/Toba/Taube, death certificate, obituary, Chalkstone Avenue, Providence, Lincoln Park, widow-of-Abraham, Kovno, and Rhode Island Jewish Historical Association archives. This suggests her record is behind a paywall (Ancestry, FamilySearch logged-in), in unindexed microfilm, or indexed under a variant name not yet tried (Theresa Salk, Fannie Salk, Pheabe Salk, Tobe Zalk).

2. **No Find a Grave memorial** was located for Tobey Salk at Lincoln Park Cemetery during this session or prior sessions. Either the memorial was never created, was created under a variant name, or she is buried elsewhere (Sons of David and Israel Cemetery is an alternate Providence Jewish cemetery per Abraham's file).

3. **1930 Census birthplace ambiguity** (Elizabeth recorded her parents' birthplace as Poland, not Russia, per Abraham's file). If the informant on Tobey's death cert was a child raised in the United States, she may likewise have recorded a generic country-level birthplace rather than a specific shtetl.

## Analysis

The hypothesis rests on a strong Tier 1 scaffolding (RI death certificates are public, Tobey provably died post-1925 in RI, the record almost certainly exists). The open question is **indexing, not existence**. Free web search has failed to surface the record, strongly suggesting it is held behind login walls (Ancestry, FamilySearch) or in the RI State Archives microfilm. This is precisely the partnership boundary flagged in CLAUDE.md: AI can identify the target record, but a human must access the paywalled or archive-restricted index.

Confidence that Tobey's RI death certificate exists: **Strong Signal**.
Confidence that the certificate will name a specific Kovno shtetl: **Moderate Signal** (depends on the informant's knowledge; adult US-born children often recorded country-level birthplace only).
Confidence that Tobey originated in Kovno Gubernia: **Moderate Signal** (geographic and endogamy evidence converge, but no direct documentation yet).

## Decisive Record

Tobey (Tauba) Salk's Rhode Island death certificate. The record is almost certainly held by one or more of:

1. **RI State Archives** (Simpson Hall, 6 Harrington Rd, Cranston, RI 02920). Death records 1853 to 1975, public. Order via 401-222-2353.
2. **FamilySearch Rhode Island Death Records 1802 to 1950** (collection searchable with free login).
3. **Ancestry RI Deaths 1630 to 1930** (paid).
4. **RI Department of Health Vital Records** (401-222-2811).
