# Fain of Providence: Nathan Fain Origin and Sibling Reconciliation

**Date**: 2026-04-19
**Scope**: Identify Nathan Fain's pre-emigration origin and parents; resolve which Fain children belong to the Nathan/Lena line vs adjacent Providence Fain families; reconcile the chronologically implausible "Irving b. 1899 twin of Alfred b. ~1912" problem.

## Hypothesis

**H1 (Nathan Fain origin)**: Nathan Fain emigrated from the Russian Empire (likely the Pale of Settlement, Lithuania or Poland region) in the 1880s or 1890s and settled in Providence, Rhode Island, where he was a founding-era member of the Jewish immigrant community (Chalkstone Avenue / North End / East Side). His parents have not yet been named in any Tier 1 or Tier 2 source located by the current search.

**H2 (Sibling reconciliation)**: Nathan and Lena (Salk) Fain had at least the following children (confidence varies):
- **Strong Signal** (Tier 2, Alfred Fain 2005 obituary): Alfred Fain (d. 2005, b. ~1912); Alfred's unnamed twin brother (co-owner of Fain's Department Store for 53 years, predeceased Alfred); Jeanette (Fain) Rosofsky; Leonard Fain; Dora (Fain) Paster.
- **Moderate Signal** (Tier 3 MyHeritage, cross-referenced with Alfred obit): Leila (Fain) Rosen; Bertha (Fain) Feingold; Rebecca (Fain) Sandler.
- **Disputed** (requires verification): Irving I. Fain (b. Dec 18, 1899) may be Nathan and Lena's eldest son, OR may be a nephew/cousin (son of Nathan's brother in the same extended Fain family). The MyHeritage index listing Irving's siblings overlaps heavily with Alfred's 2005 obituary (Leila, Bertha), which supports Irving being Nathan/Lena's son, but direct documentary confirmation is absent.

**H3 (Twin-Irving disambiguation)**: The "Irving Fain b. 1899 + twin b. ~1912 of Alfred" problem is resolved by recognizing that Irving I. Fain (b. 1899, Nathan/Lena's son, founder/co-founder of Fain's Floorcovering) is NOT Alfred's twin. Alfred's twin brother is a DIFFERENT sibling whose given name remains unidentified in sources located to date. The "uncle" co-founder of Fain's Floorcovering mentioned in retailer histories may refer to one of Nathan Fain's brothers, not to anyone in the Alfred A. Sr. / Teknor Apex branch (which is a separate Providence Fain family).

**H4 (Two-family distinction, reaffirmed)**: Providence had at least two distinct Jewish Fain families in the early 20th century: (a) **Nathan & Lena (Salk) Fain** - Fain's Department Store (North End, ~53 years from ~1929 to 1982) and very likely Fain's Floorcovering (North Main Street, 1927); (b) **Alfred A. Fain Sr. & Elizabeth (Stoneman) Fain** - whose son Irving J. Fain (b. 1906, d. 1970) founded Teknor Apex (tire recapping 1924, plastics). Norman M. Fain (Teknor) was Irving J.'s brother. These two families may share a more distant common origin in the Russian Empire but are treated as independent lineages until evidence of a direct link emerges.

## Findings from 2026-04-19 search session (10 WebSearches)

### On Nathan Fain's origin

- **No Tier 1 or Tier 2 source** naming Nathan Fain's parents or specific pre-emigration town was located in web searches of RIJHA, Find a Grave, Legacy, JewishGen indexes, and MyHeritage summaries.
- **Context**: Chalkstone Avenue in the North End of Providence was known as "Jew Street" in 1903 (push-carts; Eastern European Jewish immigrant corridor). Lithuanian Jews and Russian-Polish Jews were the dominant groups arriving 1890s-1910s.
- **Ahavath Sholom Congregation**: founded 1905-1906, first synagogue on Providence's East Side. The Fain family was among its founders (per Rhode Island Jewish Historical Notes and Jewish Rhode Island coverage). Alfred A. Fain (Sr.) participated in Ahavath Sholom fundraising in 1916. Whether Nathan Fain was also a member is not confirmed by web sources but is likely given the North End geography and the timing.
- **Related context**: Alfred A. Fain Sr. (of the Teknor line, NOT Nathan's branch) started a tire recapping company in Providence in 1924 that became Teknor Apex. His son Irving J. Fain (b. 1906) is the Harvard debate captain, fair-housing advocate, and RI Heritage Hall of Fame inductee.

### On the Fain's Floorcovering founder

- Retailer and secondary sources (Fains Fine Carpet listings, moneyinc.com) state "Irving Fain and his uncle" opened Fain's Floorcovering at the North Main Street Art Deco storefront in 1927.
- These sources appear to **conflate Irving I. Fain (b. 1899, Nathan's line) with Irving J. Fain (b. 1906, Alfred A. Sr.'s line)**. The 1927 date aligns with Irving J.'s Harvard graduation, but operational continuity through Barnet "Bunny" Fain (Irving I.'s son, who joined in 1956) strongly suggests the actual founder was **Irving I. Fain (b. 1899)**, working with an uncle (a brother of Nathan Fain, name unknown).
- **This is the most likely correct attribution**: Fain's Floorcovering belongs to the Nathan/Lena line through Irving I. Fain.

### On Alfred Fain's obituary (2005)

- Confirmed: Alfred Fain, 93, of 1 Butler Avenue, Providence, died Friday (Feb 11, 2005) at Eastgate Nursing Home. Born in Providence, son of the late Nathan & Lena (Salk) Fain, lifelong Providence resident. Husband of Sylvia (Kniznik) Fain.
- Co-owner of Fain's Department Store with his twin brother for 53 years, retired 1982. He and his late brother were the first honorees of Wanskuck Boys Club of Providence.
- Brother of the late Jeanette Rosofsky, Leonard Fain, and Dora Paster.

### On Irving I. Fain (b. 1899) siblings via MyHeritage

- MyHeritage's Irving Israel Fain (b. Dec 18, 1899, Rhode Island) profile states he had 11 siblings including **Leila (Fain) Rosen** and **Bertha (Fain) Feingold**.
- The overlap with Alfred's 2005 obituary (Jeanette Rosofsky, Leonard, Dora) is not reproduced in the MyHeritage summary, but absence on the summary page is not evidence of absence in the underlying tree. The overlap of surname patterns (Rosen, Feingold, Rosofsky, Paster, Sandler) is consistent with one extended sibling set.
- MyHeritage provides no parent names on the free summary; accessing the full profile requires an account.
