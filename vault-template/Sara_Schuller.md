# Stephania Benning (GEDCOM: "Sara Schuller")

**CRITICAL NAME DISCREPANCY:** The VATER church certificate (Assets/05.pdf, Tier 1, Pastor A. Bogeschdorfer, Waldhuetten, 10 Feb 1938) identifies this person as **"verwitwete Stephania Benning"** (widowed Stephania Benning). The GEDCOM, FamilySearch, MyHeritage, and all pedigree charts call her **"Sara Schuller."** These are clearly the same person: same birth date (27 Jan 1829), same cause of death (uterine cancer/Gebaermutterkrebs), same husband (Michael Kascher b. 1834, Wurmloch). The Tier 1 church register supersedes the Tier 3 GEDCOM. See cross_reference_audit.md, Discrepancy #19.

## Biography

Per the **1938 Waldhütten church certificate "VATER"** (Tier 1 primary source, signed by Pastor A. Bogeschdorfer, extracted from the Geburts-Tauf-Trauungs-und Toten Matrikeln of the Evangelical Church A.B. in Waldhütten), this woman's name is recorded as **"verwitwete Stephania Benning"**, meaning she was a widow named Stephania whose first married surname was Benning (her first husband being a Stephann Benning, now deceased).

She was born 27 January 1829 in Waldhütten (now Valchid), in the Kis-Kukullo Megye region of Hungary (present day Sibiu district, Romania). She was the daughter of **[[Johann_Schuller]]** (b. September 1797, Waldhütten) and **[[Sara_Benning]]** (b. December 1800, Waldhütten). Her maiden surname was therefore Schuller (father's surname) or possibly Benning (mother's surname). The fact that her mother was also a Benning raises the possibility that both her mother and her first husband came from the same Benning family in Waldhütten.

She first married a man named **Stephann Benning**, who died before 1860 (date unknown), leaving her a widow. She then married **Michael Kascher** (b. 20 September 1834, Wurmloch) on **4 January 1860** in Waldhütten.

Stephania and Michael Kascher had at least two children: **[[Regina_Kascher]]** (b. 3 August 1861) and **[[Michael_Kascher_Sr_1864]]** (b. 30 March 1864), who later emigrated to Youngstown, Ohio. Her siblings (per GEDCOM) included Johannes Schuller, Michael Schuller, and Catharina Schuller.

Stephania died **22 May 1876** of **uterine cancer** (Gebaermutterkrebs) in Waldhütten. Her husband Michael Kascher died 28 June 1880 of pneumonia (Lungenentzuendung) in Waldhütten.

### Analysis of the Name Discrepancy

The GEDCOM and all derivative trees (FamilySearch, MyHeritage, Ancestry, Geni) call this person "Sara Schuller." The Tier 1 church certificate calls her "verwitwete Stephania Benning." Possible explanations:

1. **Mother's name confusion**: Her mother was "Sara Benning." Someone may have confused the mother's given name (Sara) with the daughter's, and the mother's maiden surname (Benning) with the father's surname (Schuller), producing "Sara Schuller" by combining the wrong given name with the wrong parent's surname.
2. **Double given name**: Transylvanian Saxons sometimes used multiple given names. She may have been "Stephania Sara" or "Sara Stephania," with different documents using different names.
3. **Schuller as maiden name**: Her father was Johann Schuller, so her maiden surname was Schuller. The GEDCOM may record her under her maiden name rather than her first married name (Benning). But this does not explain the given name difference (Sara vs. Stephania).
4. **Transcription cascade**: One early GEDCOM contributor may have recorded "Sara Schuller" (perhaps from an incomplete reading of the Waldhütten registers), and this error was then copied into dozens of derivative trees, creating a false consensus.

The church certificate (Tier 1), extracted directly from the parish registers by the serving pastor, is the authoritative source. The name **Stephania Benning** is adopted as the correct identification.
