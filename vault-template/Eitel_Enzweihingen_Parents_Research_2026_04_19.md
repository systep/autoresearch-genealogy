# Hypothesis: Parents of Susanna Barbara Eitel of Enzweihingen

Target subject: [[Susanna_Barbara_Eitel]] (mother of [[Johann_Schwartz]] / Johann Christoph Schwarz bap. 19 May 1822 Enzweihingen; wife of [[Johann_Conrad_Schwarz]]).

## Status

**Open.** No Tier 2+ source identified by WebSearch on 2026-04-19 directly names Susanna Barbara Eitel's parents. Confirmed, however, that an Ortsfamilienbuch (OFB) for Enzweihingen exists online and a Seelenregister begins 1813, both of which are expected to resolve this question but require a browser visit (not a WebSearch excerpt).

## Findings (2026-04-19, 8 WebSearches)

1. **Ortsfamilienbuch Enzweihingen exists and is online** at online-ofb.de (hosted through genealogy.net). Confirmed indices for:
   - Surname list, letter S: https://online-ofb.de/famlist.php?ofb=enzweihingen&b=S
   - Life/birth/marriage list: https://online-ofb.de/ortslist.php?ofb=enzweihingen
   The OFB is a reconstructed family book. If a Susanna Barbara Eitel (b. ca. 1785-1800) married to a Johann Conrad Schwarz appears there, the OFB entry should list her parents. This is a Tier 2 source (compiled genealogy from parish registers).
2. **Enzweihingen Seelenregister** (soul register / family chronicle) begins 1813 per a surfaced search result. A Seelenregister entry for the Schwarz-Eitel household would name both spouses' parents and confirm birthplaces. Tier 1-adjacent (parish administrative record).
3. **Ancestry collection "Württemberg, Germany, Lutheran Baptisms, Marriages, and Burials, 1500-1985"** (collection 61023) covers 1000+ parishes including Enzweihingen and is the definitive digitized Tier 1 source for the Eitel baptism ~1785-1800 and the Schwarz-Eitel marriage ~1815-1822.
4. **Ancestry collection "Württemberg, Germany, Family Tables, 1550-1985"** (collection 61389) holds Familienregister / Familientabellen, which often predate the Seelenregister and would directly list Susanna Barbara Eitel's household with her parents.
5. **FamilySearch Germany, Births and Baptisms 1558-1898** (the same index that captured her 1822 son's baptism) may also contain her own baptism; no direct hit was visible in WebSearch snippets, indicating the entry is not surfaced to open web searchers, but it remains a candidate on the logged-in FamilySearch interface.
6. **Eitel surname** is confirmed South German / Swabian; multiple Eitel individuals from nearby towns (e.g., Esslingen OFB: Johanna Margareta Eitel b. 1757) show the surname's regional density. No single Enzweihingen Eitel family surfaced in WebSearch snippets.
7. **Negative result:** No WebSearch-visible WikiTree, Geni, Ancestry public tree, or FamilySearch Family Tree profile for Susanna Barbara Eitel (Enzweihingen, ca. 1785-1800) was returned. Tertiary trees, if they exist, are not indexed for these terms.
8. **Negative result:** No WebSearch-visible direct marriage record for Johann Conrad Schwarz and Susanna Barbara Eitel (1815-1822 Enzweihingen).

## Best Current Hypothesis

**H1 (most likely):** Susanna Barbara Eitel was baptized in Enzweihingen or an immediately neighboring Württemberg parish (Vaihingen an der Enz, Horrheim, Riet, Gündelbach, Ensingen, Aurich) between approximately 1785 and 1800, to an Eitel household resident in Enzweihingen. Her father was an Enzweihingen burgher (likely farmer, vintner, or tradesman given the village's character); her mother's maiden surname is unknown. The Enzweihingen OFB should resolve both names.

**H2 (alternative):** Susanna Barbara was born in a nearby Vaihingen-Oberamt village and moved to Enzweihingen at marriage. The Enzweihingen marriage register for 1815-1822 would name her Heimatort (home parish) and her father.

**Neither H1 nor H2 can yet be assigned names.** Confidence: Speculative (no sources reviewed).
