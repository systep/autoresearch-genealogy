# Daniel Hough

**IMPORTANT: This person is NOT the father of James Huff (1768-1842).** The pedigree chart and GEDCOM incorrectly connect Daniel Hough of Bucks County, PA to James Huff of Brunswick County, VA. WikiTree research (Hough-149, Huff-1770, Huff-1775) demonstrates that James Huff's father was a different Daniel Huff (c. 1710/1720-1777) of Brunswick County, VA, who married Mary Holloway. See [[Daniel_Huff_Brunswick]] for the correct ancestor.

## Biography

Daniel Hough was born June 14, 1693 in Falls Township, Bucks County, Pennsylvania. He was the son of **John Hough** (c. 1653-c. 1732), known as "ye Elder of Middletown," who emigrated from the hamlet of Hough in Wilmslow Parish, Cheshire, England on "the Friendship" in 1683 as a Quaker. His mother was **Hannah Worthington** (c. 1660-1736).

Daniel married **Elizabeth Dudley**. According to WikiTree (Hough-149), after a dispute over his father's estate (his brother refused to give him any part, as his father had already advanced him seventy pounds exceeding the share of other siblings), Daniel may have removed to New Jersey.

**The Hough/Huff surname tradition:** A popular but unproven tradition holds that Daniel "went off in a Huff" to New Jersey and changed his name from Hough to Huff. WikiTree notes this is "without a shred of proof." It has been proposed that Daniel Hough was the father of Daniel Huff II (b. c. 1720), who married Mary Worley (or Wehrle) on March 4, 1741 in Burlington, New Jersey, and eventually moved to Stokes County, North Carolina, where he died c. 1793.

Daniel Hough's siblings included John Hough II, Mary (Hough) Fisher, Stephen Hough, Hannah (Hough) Ashton, Sarah (Hough) Richardson, Joseph Hough Sr., and others.

**Connection to Huff family of Brunswick County, VA:** The pedigree chart and GEDCOM incorrectly identify this Daniel Hough (1693, Bucks County PA) as the father of James Huff (1768, Brunswick County VA). WikiTree research demonstrates that James Huff's actual father was a different man: Daniel Huff (c. 1710/1720-1777), a resident of Brunswick County, VA, who received a King's grant of 360 acres on Pigeon Roost Creek in 1753, married Mary Holloway, and left a will in 1773 (probated 1777) naming sons Daniel, Philemon, Reuben, Lewis, and James. The Stokes County NC Daniel (who married Mary Worley) and the Brunswick County VA Daniel (who married Mary Holloway) appear to be different individuals, possibly cousins. The Hough/Huff Name Study on WikiTree documents this complexity. The scholarly work of Professor Granville W. Hough and the Huff/Hough Newsletter (published by Max K. Huff, Vol. 1 Nos. 1-6, 1992-1993, cataloged at FamilySearch #638426) provide the most detailed analysis.

Daniel Hough died c. 1755.
