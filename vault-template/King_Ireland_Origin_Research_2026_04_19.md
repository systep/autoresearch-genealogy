# King Ireland Origin Research (2026-04-19)

## Question

What is the specific Ulster county, parish, and Scottish lowland origin of [[Robert_King_II]] (b. ~1750/1756 Ireland, d. 1826 Anderson Co, SC), founder of the vault's South Carolina King line? What was his exact 1770 immigration route?

## Current Best Hypothesis

**Primary hypothesis (Moderate Signal):** Robert King II originated in **central/northern County Antrim, Ulster**, in the Ballymena/Ballymoney/Kellswater corridor, and was a **Scots-Irish (Ulster-Scots) Presbyterian or Covenanter** whose paternal line descended from Lowland Scots planters (likely Ayrshire, Galloway, or Wigtownshire) who settled Ulster during or after the 17th century Plantation.

**Supporting reasoning:**

1. The Find a Grave monument (erected 1929 by grandson John Macky King) states explicitly: born Ireland 1756, emigrated 1770, settled 1772.
2. Multiple Tier 3 sources (WikiTree King-12061, Dolby-84; Geni) converge on the **ship Jupiter from Larne, 1770**. Larne (Co. Antrim) was one of the three dominant Ulster emigration ports (with Belfast and Londonderry) during 1770-1775.
3. The **Marquis of Donegal lease crisis of 1771** in central and southern County Antrim caused large-scale tenant eviction and a surge of Antrim emigration to the Carolinas, 1770-1772. The King family's 1770 departure fits this economic pressure window exactly.
4. The **1772 Rev. William Martin Covenanter migration** moved ~1,200 Ulster Presbyterians (mainly from Ballymoney, Kilraughts, Derrykeighan, Ballyrashane, The Vow, and Kellswater in County Antrim and northern Londonderry) through Larne, Belfast, and Newry to Charleston, then on to the western South Carolina backcountry (Abbeville, Pendleton District). Pendleton District is the precursor to Anderson County, where Robert King settled in 1772. The geography, timing (just 1-2 years earlier), and destination match this network almost perfectly.
5. "Settled near this spot in 1772" on the 1929 monument suggests the family may have lingered in a coastal or Pennsylvania staging area briefly before moving to the Pendleton backcountry, consistent with the Charleston-to-backcountry Scots-Irish route of that decade.

**Secondary hypothesis (Speculative):** The single WikiTree tradition citing **Wexford** as birthplace is likely conflation with another Robert King line. Wexford is in southeastern Ireland and was predominantly Anglo-Irish Church of Ireland / Catholic, not a Larne embarkation catchment. If Wexford is correct, the Larne ship story is wrong; if the Larne ship story is correct, Wexford is wrong. The Larne story has more independent attestation.

## Gap Analysis

- **No primary passenger list** for a ship Jupiter from Larne in 1770 appears in published emigrant ship lists (checked Proctor, RootsWeb, Olive Tree, Causeway Coastal, Ulster Ancestry via search). The ship Jupiter is attested as a 1774-1775 Scotland-to-North-Carolina vessel but not the 1770 Larne voyage in open sources.
- **No primary Irish church record** has been connected to Robert King I or II.
- The **Ulster Historical Foundation (ancestryireland.com)** holds the most likely primary records: Antrim parish registers (Kellswater Reformed Presbyterian, Ballymena, Connor, Ahoghill) and the pre-1800 emigration collections. These are member-only and not AI-searchable.

## Candidate Scottish Origin Counties (for deeper ancestry)

Most Antrim Presbyterian planters descended from southwest Scotland: **Ayrshire** (most likely, given Antrim plantation patterns), **Wigtownshire**, **Kirkcudbrightshire (Galloway)**, or **Renfrewshire**. King is a common surname in all four; Ayrshire has the highest 17th century King density in Scottish parish registers.
