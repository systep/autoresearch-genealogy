## Biography

James Henderson Sr. is the earliest documented Henderson in the line leading to [[Argaleus_Hercules_Henderson]] of Chatham Co NC and [[Ezekiel_Henderson]] of Greenville Co SC. Estimated birth c. 1632 (probable Perthshire, Scotland; unverified). He was physically on the Virginia Eastern Shore by May 1661.

### Primary records (Tier 1)

1. **5 May 1661**, Hungars Parish, Northampton Co Virginia: James and wife Mary baptize their eldest son John. This is the earliest firm record. (Hungars Parish register; corroborated by WikiTree Henderson-1383, WikiTree Henderson-1463, Geni.)

2. **17 November 1665**, Accomack Co Virginia: James Henderson patents **400 acres on the south side of the Pocomoke River**, bounded on the east by land surveyed for Thomas Davis, in return for **transporting 8 persons** into the county (headrights). The tract was reassigned to Somerset Co Maryland after the 1668 VA/MD boundary resolution placed the south side of the Pocomoke within Maryland. Source: Nugent, *Cavaliers and Pioneers: Abstracts of Virginia Land Patents and Grants*, Vol. I. This patent situates James among the earliest English/Scots colonial land holders on the lower Pocomoke, about 15 years before the main Makemie-led Ulster-Scot Laggan emigration to Somerset (1683).

3. **16 August 1667**, Accomack Co Virginia: Second patent to James Henderson, 150 acres, headrights Michael Towes, Joseph Miller, and Davy Glascock. Source: Nugent, *Cavaliers and Pioneers* Vol. II.

4. **28 November 1689**, Somerset Co Maryland: James Henderson signs the **Address of Loyalty to King William and Queen Mary**. The document, signed by ~235 Somerset inhabitants, is dominated by Presbyterian Ulster-Scot signers (Knox, Alexander, etc.) with a minority of ~15-20 Church of England men. Source: Archives of Maryland Volume 106 (Somerset County Judicial Records 1689-1690); transcription at MDGenWeb somerset/histrec/1689.htm. Note: the document is properly titled "Address of Loyalty", not "Grand Jury Advice of Loyalty" as sometimes mis-cited.

5. **before 9 August 1692**, Somerset Co Maryland: Dies, age approximately 60.

### Related

- **Gilbert Henderson** (possible brother or close kin): appears as a headright of Thomas Johnson Jr. in Northampton Co VA, May 1652 (Nugent, *Cavaliers and Pioneers* Vol. I). If related, this pushes the Henderson arrival on the Eastern Shore back to c. 1650.

### Context and origin hypothesis

James is part of the mid-17th century English/Scots migration to the Virginia Eastern Shore. His presence at Hungars Parish in 1661 predates the organized Makemie-Trail Laggan migration to Somerset (1683) by about 22 years. On signing the 1689 Address, James was embedded in the Presbyterian Ulster-Scot community that had by then taken root around him.

Speculative Scottish origin (Moderate Signal): a cluster of Hendersons in Perth and Fife commissariot and parish records in the 16th-17th centuries has been identified by WikiTree researchers as a candidate source for James and Gilbert. Clan Henderson tradition places the principal Lowland seat at Fordell, Fife, with secondary concentration in Shetland and a documented movement through Liddesdale to Ulster (first Donegal, then Tyrone and Antrim) in the Plantation era.

No primary Scottish or Ulster record naming this James Henderson has been located. See [[Henderson_Scots_Irish_Origin_Research_2026_04_19]] for the full hypothesis and research plan.
