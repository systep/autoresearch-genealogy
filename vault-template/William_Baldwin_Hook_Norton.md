## Biography

William Baldwin lived in Hook Norton, Oxfordshire, in the mid-17th century. He married Mary Daves around 1653-1654. Three of his sons, John, Thomas, and Francis, later emigrated to the Chester County, Pennsylvania area as servants of the Quaker Joshua Hastings circa 1682-1683, settling in the Quaker Colony of southeastern Pennsylvania and Delaware. William himself appears not to have emigrated (the "General William Baldwin of Marston Moor who died at sea" legend that circulates in some online trees is internally inconsistent with a Hook Norton 1670 burial and is treated here as folklore, not evidence).

William's identification as the father of the three Chester immigrants rests on the Chatham Historical Journal 1994 article (Tier 2) and multiple corroborating tertiary sources. Confidence on William himself is **moderate**. Confidence on his own parents (Francis Baldwin and Johan) is **speculative**, rests on one contested Tier 3 source, and is NOT adopted into this vault person file.
