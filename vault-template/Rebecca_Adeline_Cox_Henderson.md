## Biography

Rebecca Adeline Cox was born September 13, 1813, the eldest of 12 children of **Thomas B. Cox** (1792-1857) and **Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson** (1795-1876). Her father was the son of William Cox Jr. (d. 1798) and Connie Baker, placing Rebecca in the direct colonial Cox line from William Cox Sr. (c. 1730-1814) who migrated from Orange County, North Carolina to Greenville County, South Carolina in the 1780s.

She married **Rev. Henry Langford Henderson** (1812-1886). This was a **first cousin marriage**: Rebecca's mother Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson and Henry's father Brashier Henderson (1790-1847) were **siblings**, both children of Ezekiel Henderson (1763-1846) and Elizabeth Brasher (married October 10, 1782). The Henderson family was established in Greenville County after Ezekiel's Revolutionary War service (pension S6994).

Rebecca and Henry had **10 children**:
1. Jane Henderson
2. Mary Ann E. Henderson
3. Oliver Henderson
4. Frances Emeline "Emma" Henderson
5. Rebecca Caroline Henderson
6. Winfield Jasper Henderson
7. Annette Arline "Nettie" Henderson
8. William H. B. Henderson
9. **Thomas Langford Henderson** (b. Aug 11, 1855; d. Oct 12, 1930; married Mary Frances "Fannie" Cox, b. 1858, on Dec 25, 1877)
10. Julia H. L. Henderson

The family was deeply rooted in the Standing Springs Baptist Church community, which was organized in 1818 in the home of James Cox (Rebecca's great uncle, brother of her grandfather William Cox Jr.). Rev. Henry Henderson served as a minister in this community.

Rebecca's brother **Thomas Manning Cox** (1828-1897) married Sarah Elizabeth Clark (1836-1879); their daughter Roxanne Cox (1865-1912) married William Goldsmith Long (1862-1937), connecting this line to the Bright and Kascher families.

Henry died November 18, 1886. Rebecca survived him by eight years, dying September 26, 1894 at age 81. Both are buried at **Standing Springs Cemetery**, Simpsonville, South Carolina.
