# Hai Gaon (Hai ben Sherira)

> **Confidence: Moderate.** Hai Gaon is a Wikipedia confirmed, well documented historical figure. He was the last major Gaon of the Pumbeditha Academy. However, his role as ancestor in this tree is part of the claimed Davidic descent through the Maharal of Prague. This chain is contested by modern scholarship (Putik, Muneles). The connection from the Maharal back to King David relies on Meir Perels' Megillat Yuhasin (1745), which contains documented errors.

## Biography

Hai Gaon (939-1038) was the last major Gaon (head) of the Pumbeditha Academy in Babylon, serving in that capacity from 998 until his death. His father Sherira Gaon preceded him in the role. Hai Gaon was one of the most prolific authors among the Geonim, producing extensive responsa (legal rulings), commentaries on the Talmud, and other works. He corresponded with Jewish communities across the known world and his legal opinions carried immense authority.

The GEDCOM does not contain a separate individual record for Hai Gaon. He is referenced only in the composite name of @I501262@ ("Abu Nasr Yehiel Azariah R' Yechiel Ben Yosef Gaon Ibn Nagrela / Al-nagrila II [gd.son Of Hai Gaon] / Luria II [maharal Ancestor]"), who is described as a grandson of Hai Gaon, born c. 1060, died c. 1110.

In the claimed Davidic descent lineage, Hai Gaon's granddaughter (or descendant) is said to have married into the ibn Nagrela (ha-Nagid) family, connecting the Geonim to the Luria line.
