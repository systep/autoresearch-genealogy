## Biography

Boruch Portugaler, known as "Batlan," was a Jewish resident of Poznan (Posen), Poland. The surname "Portugaler" indicates Portuguese/Sephardic Jewish origin, suggesting the family descended from Jews who left Portugal (possibly during the expulsions of 1496-1497) and eventually settled in Poland.

"Batlan" was a term used in Jewish communities for a person who devoted himself to communal service and Torah study, often at the community's expense, rather than engaging in commerce. It was sometimes used as an epithet or secondary surname.

Boruch is primarily significant as the father of Rachel Portugaler, who married Shneur Zalman (grandfather of the Alter Rebbe, Shneur Zalman of Liadi, founder of Chabad Hasidism). According to the Hebrew text in the GEDCOM (from Chabadpedia), "Rebbetzin Rachel was born in the year 5453 (1693) in the city of Pozna, to her father Rabbi Boruch Batlan." The text further notes that Rachel's mother's family was descended from the Maharshal (Rabbi Shlomo Luria), who was known as a kabbalist.

Rachel was described as an exceptionally learned and pious woman. Her father reportedly took care that her scholarship not become widely known (Chabadpedia via GEDCOM).
