## Biography

Robert Brasseur was a French Huguenot who fled the 1620s Catholic persecution in France to the Isle of Thanet in Kent, England, where five of his children's baptisms are registered between 1622 and 1632 (Katherine 1622-1632; John 1624; Thomas 1626). His French parish of origin is not yet primary-sourced; Normandy (Dieppe or Le Havre) or Aunis (La Rochelle) are the two candidate regions most consistent with a Channel-coast Thanet landfall. See [[Brasseur_Nansemond_1636_Primary_Source_Research_2026_04_19]].

He arrived in Virginia by June 1636, when the first known record of Robert Brasseur in the colony, a renewal of a promissory note in Warrisquicke Co., is captured in Nugent, Cavaliers and Pioneers, Vol. 1, p. 41. He subsequently accumulated land in Nansemond County:

- **1638**: joint purchase of 600 acres in Upper Co. of New Norfolk (predecessor of Nansemond), on the S. side of Warrisquicke Creek at the head, butting upon Nanzemund (Matrevers) River, from Peter Johnson, in partnership with Peeter Rey.
- **6 October 1640**: 100-acre patent in Upper Norfolk Co., on the western branch of Nansemond River, opposite a 400-acre tract of Lawrence Peters, for the transport of Peeter Bersairdier and Reene Bersairdier.
- **12 April 1653**: 1,200-acre grant in Nansemond Co., at the head of the southern branch of the Nanzemond River, for transporting himself, wife Florence, and children Mary, Perside, Katherine and Bennet Brasseur, plus other settlers.

Robert died before 21 January 1659/60. His daughter Martha had by then married [[Captain_William_Moseley_1650_Rappahannock]]; a Rappahannock County deed of that date recites "...whereas Robert Brusner [sic] gent new deced did by his last will and testament give unto his daughter Martha now wife me William Mosely..." specifying land in Nansemond (Rappahannock Records 1656-64, p. 99). This is the decisive Tier 1 tie between Robert, his Nansemond estate, and the Moseley family of Old Rappahannock.

## Distinguishing from Robert Brasseur of Calvert County, MD

A different individual, "Robert Brasseur of Calvert County," appears in Maryland records around 1660-1663. That man is **not** this Robert; they have been conflated in many tertiary trees. The Nansemond Robert died before 21 Jan 1659/60 per the Rappahannock deed above; the Calvert Co. Robert died c. 1663 in Maryland. The Calvert Co. man is likely a son or nephew of the Nansemond Robert (possibly Benois / Bennet). See [[Brasher_Brashier_Origin_Research_2026_04_19]] for the migration path implications.
