## Biography

Thomas Stackhouse was baptized on May 1, 1601, at the Church of St. Alkelda, Giggleswick, York, England (WikiTree Stackhouse-58, Tier 3). He was the son of John (Johannis) Stackhouse and Ellen Clark of Giggleswick. His siblings included James Stackhouse (b. c. 1603), Henry Stackhouse, and Robert Stackhouse (Geni, Tier 3).

Thomas married Anna Mayos (born c. 1608, Giggleswick), daughter of Edward Mayos. Anna died in July 1690 in Giggleswick and was buried there (FamilySearch, Tier 3).

Thomas and Anna's children included: John Stackhouse, Ellen Elin (who married Ralph Cowgill and emigrated to Pennsylvania), Thomas Stackhouse Sr. (who also emigrated), Ellen Stackhouse, and Jennet Stackhouse (Geni, FamilySearch, Tier 3).

Thomas Stackhouse died in October 1686 in Giggleswick, Yorkshire, and was buried there. His family were members of the Society of Friends (Quakers). His descendants, particularly his daughter Ellen and grandson Thomas Stackhouse Sr., were among the Quaker families who emigrated from Yorkshire to Pennsylvania in the 1680s (WikiTree, Tier 3).

Thomas Stackhouse Sr., son of this Thomas, came to Pennsylvania with William Penn on the ship "Lamb" on October 22, 1682 (WikiTree Stackhouse-95 and Stackhouse-63, Tier 3).
