## Biography

Nicholas Gaynesford (c. 1427 to 1498) of Carshalton, Surrey, was a Justice of the Peace, several times Member of Parliament, and High Sheriff of Surrey and Sussex. He served in the royal households from c. 1461 until his death in 1498 (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Nicholas was the youngest of three sons of John Gainsford senior of Crowhurst (will dated 12 November 1448, proved 9 November 1450). His older brothers were John (who married Anne Wakehurst and was father of [[Agnes_Gainsford]]) and William (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

**Career highlights:**
- Appointed Controller of petty customs in the Port of London, October 1449 (Wikipedia, Tier 2)
- Admitted to Lincoln's Inn with brother John, 1452 to 1453 (Wikipedia, Tier 2)
- MP for Bletchingley, Surrey, 1453; Knight of the Shire for Surrey, 1455 to 1456 (Wikipedia, Tier 2)
- Appointed King's Servitor and Usher of the King's Chamber, 1461 (Wikipedia, Tier 2)
- Constable and Keeper of Odiham Castle and Park, Hampshire (life grant) (Wikipedia, Tier 2)
- High Sheriff of Surrey and Sussex (Wikipedia, Tier 2)
- Usher to the Chamber of Edward IV, and by 1476 to Queen Elizabeth Woodville (Wikipedia, Tier 2)
- Leader in the Kentish rising of 1483 against Richard III; attainted 1483 but pardoned (Wikipedia, Tier 2)
- Esquire to Henry VII and Elizabeth of York after the Battle of Bosworth Field, 1485 (Wikipedia, Tier 2)

Nicholas married **Margaret Sidney**, one of the gentlewomen to Queens Elizabeth Woodville and Elizabeth of York. They had four sons and four daughters (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

He acquired the manor of Burghersh alias Stone Court in Carshalton and settled there (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

## Connection to This Family Tree

Nicholas Gaynesford is the brother of John Gainsford (the elder brother who married Anne Wakehurst). John's daughter [[Agnes_Gainsford]] married Sir John Culpeper of Bedgebury. The line continues: Agnes's son [[Walter_Colepeper_Calais]] to [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]] to [[Martin_Culpepper]] to the Sandys/Sands line. Nicholas is therefore the great-uncle of Walter Colepeper.
