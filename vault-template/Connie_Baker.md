## Biography

Connie Baker was born in 1760. She married **William Cox Jr.**, son of [[William_Cox_Sr]] (c. 1730-1814) and Alice ("Alce"/"Alic," surname unknown). William Cox Sr. had migrated from Orange County, North Carolina to Greenville County, South Carolina around 1787, along with intermarried families including the Longs, Blacks, Payne/Paines, Lees, and Brashiers/Brazures. William Jr. was part of this migration.

Connie and William Jr. had at least one documented son: **Thomas B. Cox** (b. May 15, 1792; d. June 12, 1857). Additional children may exist but are not documented in current sources.

William Cox Jr. died in 1798, when Thomas B. Cox was approximately six years old. After William Jr.'s death, Connie remarried to **William Richardson**. The Richardson surname appears in the broader Greenville County network; Martha Brashier married into the Richardson family (per GEDCOM data showing "Martha Brashier (born Richardson)" and siblings including Elias Richardson and Hannah Richardson).

Thomas B. Cox went on to marry Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson (1795-1876), daughter of [[Ezekiel_Henderson]] (Revolutionary War veteran). Through Thomas's son Thomas Manning Cox (1828-1897) and granddaughter Roxanne Cox (1865-1912), this line connects to the Long, Bright, Kascher, and Salmanson families.
