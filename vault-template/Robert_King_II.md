## Biography

Robert King II (c. 1750, Ireland; d. December 13, 1826, Anderson County, SC) was an Irish immigrant and American Revolutionary War veteran who settled in what is now Anderson County, South Carolina. He emigrated from Ireland in 1770 aboard the **ship Jupiter from Larne, County Antrim, Northern Ireland** (WikiTree Dolby-84, Geni; Tier 3, user-contributed), with his father Robert King I (1725-before 1797), his wife Tabitha Dolby, and two uncles. Larne was a major embarkation port for Scotch-Irish emigration to the American colonies; other ships from Larne to South Carolina in this period include the James & Mary (1772) and Lord Dunluce (1772). No original passenger list for the Jupiter has been located. The family settled near present-day Belton, Anderson County, circa 1772.

Robert II fought in the Revolutionary War, reportedly at the Battle of Kings Mountain (October 7, 1780), serving in the Washington District Regiment, North Carolina Militia. He is a DAR (Daughters of the American Revolution) recognized patriot.

He and Tabitha had 23 children, 21 of whom survived to adulthood. This extraordinarily large family became one of the most prominent in Anderson County. Known children include:
