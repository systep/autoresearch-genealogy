## Biography

Johann Michael Brecht was born on May 28, 1706 in Schriesheim, near Mannheim in the Palatinate of Germany, and christened on May 30, 1706 (GEDCOM). His father was Johannes Michael Brecht (a wine grower in Schriesheim), and his mother was Anna Katharina Hoffman. Johann Michael worked as a vinegrower and drinking establishment proprietor in Schriesheim before emigrating (GEDCOM).

On May 6, 1726, Anna Catherina Brecht (widow of Johannes Michael Brecht I, who had died in 1719) petitioned with her two sons Stefan and Johann Michael to be allowed to emigrate to America (Geni project "Conrad Kuntz Brecht 1563-1612 family"; RootsWeb "Descendants of Johann Brecht"). The family sold their vineyards and tavern to pay for passage. They landed in Philadelphia in October 1726, settling first in the Germantown section (Geni, RootsWeb). Note: the formal Pennsylvania oath lists (Strassburger and Hinke, "Pennsylvania German Pioneers") begin in 1727, when Governor Patrick Gordon required Continental Europeans to swear allegiance, so **no official ship passenger list exists for this 1726 arrival**. The ship name is not recorded in any consulted source and is likely unrecoverable. The Historical Society of Pennsylvania has "The Bright-Brecht Family of Pennsylvania" (227 pages, discover.hsp.org/Record/marc-67954) and Albert G. Green published "Historical Sketch of the Bright Family of Berks County, PA" (1900, FamilySearch catalog #1036369), either of which may contain additional immigration details. (Immigration search 2026-04-18)

Johann Michael married Margareta Simone (also known as Marguerite Julienne Simone) in April 1728 in Heidelberg, Lancaster County, Pennsylvania (Geni). Margareta was a French Huguenot from Pfeffelbach (then part of France, now Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany), who had emigrated via Rotterdam on October 2, 1727 (GEDCOM). By 1729 the couple had moved to Berks County, settling on 78 acres (RootsWeb).

Together they had 10 known children (GEDCOM, Geni). The family settled in the Heidelberg Township / Schaefferstown area of what became Lebanon County, Pennsylvania. Johann Michael was a member of the German Reformed Church (GEDCOM).

Johann Michael's siblings included Anna Margaretha Durst, Appolonia Bender/Eichleberger, Anna Elizabeth Brecht, Balthasar Brecht, Johannes Stephan Brecht, Susanna Margaretha Roescher, Johann Brecht, Anna Katharina Dehuff, and Janet Steele (Geni). His father Johannes Michael Brecht was the son of [[Balthasar_Hans_Brecht]] (1636-1703) of Schriesheim.

Johann Michael's children included Jacob Brecht Sr. (b. Apr 13, 1729), George (b. Feb 9, 1731), Michael (b. Mar 24, 1732), Katherine (b. Apr 6, 1734), John (b. Feb 20, 1736), Peter (b. May 13, 1738), [[David_Bright]] (b. Aug 9, 1740), Maria (b. Aug 1, 1742), Sarah (b. Jan 19, 1745), and Christina (b. Aug 12, 1747) (GEDCOM, Geni, RootsWeb "Descendants of Johann Brecht").

NOTE: Some user contributed trees (GEDCOM, Geni) list "George Adam Bright" (1730-1804) as a son of Johann Michael Brecht II. However, the RootsWeb "Descendants of Johann Brecht" page and multiple WikiTree/WeRelate sources attribute George Adam to Johann Michael's brother Johann Stephan Brecht (1692-1747). Johann Michael's son "George" (b. Feb 9, 1731, d. ~1783) appears to be a different individual from George Adam Bright (b. Sep 2, 1730, d. Jun 14, 1804, buried Dutch Hollow Hanger Cemetery, Middlebrook, Augusta County, Virginia). The parentage of George Adam Bright is disputed and should be treated as (unverified). See Data Discrepancies.

The first documented use of the anglicized surname "Bright" in the family appears in the signature of Michael (the third son, b. 1732) on the administration account for the estate of Conrad Bower in 1768, whose widow he married (Geni project, RootsWeb). George Adam Brecht began calling himself "George Adam Bright" when he moved to Frederick County, Virginia in the summer of 1759, and the rest of the family gradually adopted the anglicized form, so that by the time of the Revolution they were known as the Bright family (Geni, RootsWeb).

[[David_Bright]] is the ancestor of the direct Bright line connecting to the present family.

Johann Michael died on September 13, 1794 in Reading, Berks County, Pennsylvania (GEDCOM, Geni). WikiTree profile Brecht-58 documents this individual.
