## Biography

William Baldwin (circa 1441, circa 1479) of Aylesbury, Buckinghamshire, England, was the father of Sir John Baldwin, who became Chief Justice of the Common Pleas (1470, 1545). William inherited estates from his uncle John Baldwin, who was a bencher of Gray's Inn, connecting the family to the English legal establishment.

William's wife is disputed between sources. WikiTree identifies her as Jane Aylesbury, while Baker's History of Northamptonshire names her as Agnes Dormer. This discrepancy has not been resolved (see Data Discrepancies below).

Through his son Sir John Baldwin, this line connects to the Baldwin family in the vault, ultimately leading to [[John_Baldwin_Oldest]] and the American Baldwin line.
