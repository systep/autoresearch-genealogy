## Biography

Leonard Bland Rodbell was the second husband of [[Thelma_Markel]] (Thelma Markel Salmanson Rodbell). Thelma married Leonard in 1978 after the death of her first husband [[Leonard_Irving_Salmanson]] in 1975 (Find a Grave #231959788). The couple lived in Atlanta, Georgia.

Leonard was born May 17, 1916 in Atlanta, Georgia, according to the GEDCOM (unverified). He died November 7, 1988 (unverified). His parents were **Joseph Henry Rodbell** and **Fannie Turetsky Rodbell** (confirmed by brother Clyde's 2015 obituary, Dignity Memorial). Joseph Henry Rodbell founded Capitol Hide & Metal Co. in 1911, which Leonard evolved into **Apex Plumbing Specialties Co.** in 1947 (Supply House Times; Breman Museum archives). The company grew into **Apex Supply Company**, a major plumbing distribution business eventually sold to Home Depot in 2000.

Leonard's brother **Clyde Armand Rodbell** (Aug 16, 1927 - Jan 10, 2015) spent 54 years at Apex Supply alongside Leonard, becoming President then Chairman. Clyde's obituary (Dignity Memorial) confirms Leonard predeceased him, referring to "his brother, the late Leonard Rodbell." Clyde is buried at **Arlington Memorial Park, Sandy Springs, Fulton County, Georgia** (Find a Grave #141232078).

Leonard's sons (Sidney, Paul, Arthur Rodbell) are listed as stepchildren of Thelma on her Find a Grave memorial (#231959788). Sidney Rodbell joined Apex Supply in 1968 and became president in 1997.

After Leonard's death, Thelma established the Thelma S. Rodbell Charitable Foundation (Cranston, RI; tax exempt since 1999), which has distributed $2.3 million in 386 grants since 2014 (ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer).
