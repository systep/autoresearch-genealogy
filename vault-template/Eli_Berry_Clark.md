## Biography

Eli Berry Clark was born on 31 July 1825 in Greenville County, South Carolina, the eldest known child of Jeddiah W. Clark and Gilley Elizabeth Baldwin. He was named "Berry" possibly after Nathan Berry, the first pastor of Standing Springs Baptist Church (organized 1818), reflecting the family's deep roots in the Standing Springs community.

Eli Berry married Eliza Jane Cox, a member of the Cox family that had been settled in the Simpsonville/Standing Springs area since 1787. This Clark-Cox marriage echoed his sister Sarah Elizabeth Clark's marriage to Thomas Manning Cox, demonstrating the tightly intermarried nature of the Clark, Cox, Henderson, Long, and Baldwin families in this community.

Eli Berry and Eliza Jane had at least one daughter:
1. Martha Ann "Mattie" Clark, who married Winfield Jasper Henderson (b. 9 Jul 1847; d. 10 Jan 1918, Greenville County, SC) on 15 December 1868. In 1880, Winfield and Mattie lived in Gantt Township, Greenville County. They had ten children: Hetty M., Marcus W., John H. B., Andrew J., Jenny T., Mary Ellen "Mame," Frank J., Rebecca Elizabeth, Little Kate, and Charles Manly Henderson.

Eli Berry Clark died on 26 November 1895, aged 70, in Greenville County, South Carolina. He was buried at what is now Sonpoint Baptist Fellowship Church Cemetery (formerly Bethuel Baptist Church Cemetery) in Greenville, SC.
