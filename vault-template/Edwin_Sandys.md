## Biography

Sir Edwin Sandys (1561 to 1629) was an English politician, statesman, and one of the founders of the Virginia Company of London. He was the second surviving son of Edwin Sandys, Archbishop of York, and his wife Cecily Wilford (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Sandys was educated at Corpus Christi College, Oxford, and studied law at the Middle Temple, London (GEDCOM, Tier 3). He entered Parliament in 1589 and served in the House of Commons at various times until 1626 (Wikipedia, Tier 2). He was MP for Droitwich, Worcestershire, in 1614 (GEDCOM).

He was a founding member of the proprietary Virginia Company of London, which established the first permanent English settlement at Jamestown, Virginia, in 1607. Sandys became Treasurer of the Virginia Company in 1619, succeeding Sir Thomas Smythe. He is credited with establishing representative government in Virginia, overseeing the creation of the House of Burgesses in 1619 (Wikipedia, Encyclopedia Virginia, Tier 2).

Sandys resided at Northbourne Court, Kent, and at the Middle Temple, London (GEDCOM). He was knighted and held the title of Knight. He never traveled to Virginia himself but was instrumental in the colony's governance from England.

He died on October 31, 1629, at Northbourne, Kent, and was buried the following day at Northbourne (GEDCOM, Tier 3).

His brother, [[Samuel_Sandys_MP]], was also a knight and MP. His son Richard Sandys married Hester Aucher and is an ancestor in this family tree through the Sands/Sandys line connecting to the Hough family via Eleanor Sands (GEDCOM).
