## Biography

Sir John de Hardreshull was born on August 24, 1291, at Pachesham, Surrey. He was the son of William de Hardreshull and Juliana de Hache. His father died before November 10, 1303, when John was about twelve years old (WikiTree Hardreshull-2, Tier 3; University of Michigan genealogy, Tier 3).

**Ancestry and the Barony of Hache:**

Through his mother Juliana, Sir John inherited the **Barony of Hache**. Juliana was the daughter and heiress of **Eustace de Hache, 1st Baron Hache** (d. 1306), an English noble who fought at the **Battle of Falkirk** (1298), was present at the **Siege of Caerlaverock** (1300), and signed the **Barons' Letter to Pope Boniface VIII** (1301) (Wikipedia: Eustace de Hache, Tier 2). Eustace married Amice, daughter of John de Lew.

John's father William de Hardreshull (1269 to c. 1303) was the son of John de Hardreshull (c. 1247 to before 1276) and **Joan de Neville**, daughter of **Sir Philip de Neville of Scotton, Lincolnshire** (d. 1273). The Neville of Scotton family was a branch of the great Neville dynasty. Joan's brother Robert de Neville succeeded their father (WikiTree Neville-185, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3).

**Military Career:**

Sir John served as a **Knight Banneret** under Edward III. He was in the **first division** at the **Battle of Crecy** (August 26, 1346), serving under the nominal command of Edward of Woodstock, Prince of Wales (the Black Prince), who was then sixteen years old (WikiTree, Tier 3). The Knight Banneret rank was one of the highest military distinctions, carrying the right to lead troops under one's own banner.

**Marriages:**

Sir John married first **Margaret de Stafford** (c. 1294 to after 1374), daughter of James de Stafford of Sandon, Staffordshire. James was the son of William de Stafford and Isabel Stafford, of a cadet branch of the **Stafford Barony** (WikiTree Stafford-477, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3). The Staffords of Sandon were fourth cousins of the great Earl Stafford.

He married second **Maud Mussenden**, an heiress. The order of these marriages is disputed among sources (WikiTree, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3).

His daughter **Elizabeth** was a co-heiress who married Sir John Colepeper (c. 1305 to 1376), bringing the Hardreshull estates into the Culpeper family.
