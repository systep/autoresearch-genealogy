## Biography

Masha Markel Tachner was the second of eight surviving children (out of 14 born) of **[[Heschel_Herman_Markel]]** (Heshel Markel; b. 1862; d. 1916) and **[[Leah_Frei]]** (b. 1864; d. 1940) (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). She married a man surnamed **Tachner** (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). Note: her brother-in-law (her husband's brother) **Max Tachner** later married her sister **[[Sarah_Markel_Tachner]]** in New York, connecting the two families twice.

The Yichus narrative also records that **Vigdor Markel** and **Etti Markel** were among those murdered (Tier 2). It is not specified whether these were Masha's children, cousins, or other relatives.

On Rosh Hashanah 1939, Masha and her family were among those "forced to leave the premises and marched to the Rodzinka Forest where they were murdered" (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). The perpetrators are described as "Polish Cossacks" in the narrative, likely referring to local collaborators or irregular forces during the German invasion of Poland in September 1939. The information about the Rodzinka Forest murders was provided by **Renya Tzoar** (nee Weidenfeld), a surviving relative who was hidden in a convent during the war (Yichus narrative, Tier 2).

Masha was "murdered with family," indicating her husband and children perished alongside her (Yichus narrative, Tier 2).

## Holocaust Record Search (2026-04-17)

Research conducted 2026-04-17 into Holocaust records for the Sambor district. See [[Avraham_Chaim_Markel]] for the full analysis.

**Key finding**: The "Rosh Hashanah 1939" date in the Yichus narrative is almost certainly incorrect. The historical record shows no mass killing of Jews in a forest near Sambor in September 1939. The family was most likely murdered during the 1941-1943 Holocaust events in the Sambor district (ghetto establishment March 1942; deportations to Belzec August-December 1942; forest executions at Radlowicze January and June 1943).

The Sambor Yizkor Book necrology lists "Markel, Yosef and Family (5 People)" but does not list Masha by name. The necrology is acknowledged as incomplete. Masha's husband's surname (Tachner) was searched in Yad Vashem and USHMM databases but returned no results via web search (database access returned 403; HUMAN ACTION REQUIRED to search directly).

The name "Vigdor Markel" mentioned in the Yichus as among those murdered may be Masha's husband or a child; this is unconfirmed.
