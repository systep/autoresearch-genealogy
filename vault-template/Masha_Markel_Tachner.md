## Biography

Masha Markel Tachner was the third of eight surviving children (out of 14 born) of **[[Heschel_Herman_Markel]]** (Heshel Markel; b. 1862; d. 1916) and **[[Leah_Frei]]** (b. 1864; d. 1940) (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). She married a man surnamed **Tachner** (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). Note: her brother-in-law (her husband's brother) **Max Tachner** later married her sister **[[Sarah_Markel_Tachner]]** in New York, connecting the two families twice.

The Yichus narrative also records that **Vigdor Markel** and **Etti Markel** were among those murdered (Tier 2). It is not specified whether these were Masha's children, cousins, or other relatives.

On Rosh Hashanah 1939, Masha and her family were among those "forced to leave the premises and marched to the Rodzinka Forest where they were murdered" (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). The perpetrators are described as "Polish Cossacks" in the narrative, likely referring to local collaborators or irregular forces during the German invasion of Poland in September 1939. The information about the Rodzinka Forest murders was provided by **Renya Tzoar** (nee Weidenfeld), a surviving relative who was hidden in a convent during the war (Yichus narrative, Tier 2).

Masha was "murdered with family," indicating her husband and children perished alongside her (Yichus narrative, Tier 2).
