## Biography

George Washington King (b. ~1815, South Carolina) was a farmer who lived in the Anderson County and Greenville County areas of upstate South Carolina. He was the son of [[James_King_Sr]] (b. ~1788, farmer with $3,000 real estate) and Mary King (b. ~1795), and brother of Robert King (b. ~1813, Baptist clergyman).

He married **Lucinda Elizabeth Chamblee** (born SC). She went by her middle name "Elizabeth" in census records. Two independent death certificates reference her:
- Judson King's death certificate (1921): mother's maiden name recorded as what appears to be "Shambler" but is almost certainly a misread of **"Chamblee"** ("Ch" misread as "Sh," "amblee" misread as "ambler").
- George W. King Jr.'s death certificate (1936, File No. 7632): mother's maiden name clearly recorded as **"Lucinda Chamblee."**
- Geni profile for George Washington King confirms wife as **"Loucinda Elizabeth Chamblee."**

The Chamblee surname is documented in the Greenville/Anderson County area of South Carolina.

**1850**: Family lived in the Eastern Sub Division of Anderson County, SC (Census Family 401). George W. was a farmer, age 35, with wife Elizabeth (25) and five children: Judson (9), Mary E (8), Tabbitha (6), Elizabeth (3), James M (2). His father James King (age 62, Family 398) and brother Robert King (age 37, clergyman, Family 397) lived nearby on the same census page.

**1870**: George W. (55) and Elizabeth appear in South Carolina (specific township unclear) with two younger children: Francis C (6-8) and James (3). Elizabeth's age is recorded as 28, but this is almost certainly a misread of 48, consistent with her being born ~1822-1825. The older children (Judson, Mary E, etc.) had all left home by this time.

**1880**: George W. (64) and Elizabeth (55) appear in **Oaklawn Township, Greenville County** (Census T9, Roll 1255210, Sheet 121-A) with five younger children still at home: George W. Jr. (22), Martha E. (20), William E. (17), Robert Lee (14), and Fannie. The family had moved from Anderson County to Greenville County at some point between 1850 and 1870/1880. Notably, this is the **same township** where son [[Judson_King]] lived with his own family.

George W.'s occupation remained farmer throughout. The naming of son "Robert Lee King" (b. ~1866) after Confederate General Robert E. Lee indicates Confederate sympathies, consistent with son Judson's service in the 16th SC Infantry.

**Note**: A separate George W. King (age 65, Cooper) appears in the 1880 Census in Flat Springs, Lexington County, SC. This is a **different individual**; our George W. King is the farmer in Oaklawn, Greenville County.

## Relationship to Judson King: Father (Confirmed)

George Washington King is [[Judson_King]]'s father. Supported by:
- Judson (age 9) is listed in George W. King's household in the 1850 census, among the other children.
- The death certificate of [[Judson_King]] (1921, Tier 1) explicitly names "Washington King" as his father.
- In 1880, both George W. and Judson live in Oaklawn Township, Greenville County (father and adult son as neighbors).
- Judson named his own children Tabitha J. and Mary E. (1880 Census), matching siblings from the 1850 household (Tabbitha, Mary E).
- **Strong Signal**: Four Tier 1 sources (1850 Census, 1880 Census co-location, death certificate, 1920 Census confirming parents born SC) all align.
