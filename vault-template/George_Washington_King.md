## Biography

George Washington King (b. ~1815, South Carolina) was a farmer in the Eastern Sub Division of Anderson County, South Carolina. In the 1850 Census (Series M432, Family 401, Image 536), he appears as head of household, age 35, with his wife Elizabeth (age 25) and five children: Judsan (Judson, age 9), Mary E (age 8), Tabbitha (age 6), Elizabeth (age 3), and James M (age 2).

He appears on the same census page as **James King** (age 62, Family 398) and **Robert King** (age 37, Baptist clergyman, Family 397), both likely his father and brother respectively. The three King households are enumerated within a few lines of each other, indicating they were neighbors.

## Relationship to Judson King: Father

George Washington King is [[Judson_King]]'s father. This is supported by:
- Judson (age 9) is listed in George W. King's household in the 1850 census, among the other children.
- The death certificate of [[Judson_King]] (1921, Tier 1) explicitly names "Washington King" as his father. "Washington King" = George Washington King.
- The age gap (26 years) is standard for parent-child.
- Judson named his own children Tabitha J. and Mary E. (1880 Census), matching the names of his siblings in George W.'s 1850 household (Tabbitha, Mary E).
- George W.'s wife Elizabeth (b. ~1825) was ~16 when Judson was born (~1841), which was common in the rural antebellum South.

The "Shambler" (or variant) recorded as mother's maiden name on Judson's death certificate likely refers to Elizabeth's maiden name. **Moderate Signal**: two Tier 1 sources (death certificate + 1850 census) independently confirm the father-son relationship.
