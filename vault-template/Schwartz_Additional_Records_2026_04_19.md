## The Catharine age problem (critical)

| Source | Year | Catharine's age | Implied Catharine birth year |
|---|---|---|---|
| Record F (Detroit 1860 census, Tier 1) | 1860 | 32 | ~1828 |
| Record C (Iowa 1870 census, Tier 1) | 1870 | 49 | ~1821 |

If Records C and F were the same Catharine, she would be 42 in 1870 (32 + 10). The 49 reported is **17 years older than consistent with a single person**. Two possibilities:

1. **Different couples**: Record F (Detroit 1860) is a Schwartz family unrelated to Record C (Iowa 1870) and to vault [[Johann_Schwartz]]. Würt­temberg was a major source of German immigration to the US; Schwartz is a very common surname; "John + Catharine" is a very common couple; multiple unrelated Schwarz + Catharine pairs from Württemberg arriving in different US cities in the 1850s is entirely plausible.
2. **Same John, two Catharines**: John Schwarz of Detroit 1860 was widowed by his first Catharine (age 32), remarried a second Catharine (of his own age ~49 in 1870) after losing his first wife, and relocated from Detroit to Iowa. The three Detroit-born sons would have been 16, 13, 10 in 1870 — expected still in the household, but they are NOT co-resident in Iowa 1870. Inconsistent.

Either way, **my earlier merge of Record C (Iowa 1870) with the vault's Johann Christoph Schwarz (Württemberg baptism 1822) is not supported at Strong Signal level**. The Record A baptism is a solid Tier 1 identification for the vault's Johann (Enzweihingen, 17 May 1822, parents Johann Conrad + Susanna Barbara Eitel). But linking that person to any US-resident John Schwartz / Catharine household remains **open**. The 1870 Iowa record is a CANDIDATE, not a confirmed identity; it has competing explanations.
