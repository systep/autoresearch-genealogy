## Biography

Sterna Segal was the wife of [[Shneur_Zalman_of_Liadi]], the Alter Rebbe and founder of the Chabad-Lubavitch Hasidic movement. She is known as the "1st Rebbetzin of CHABAD" (GEDCOM, Geni.com).

She was the daughter of Yehuda Leib Segal, who was among the prominent members of the Vitebsk Jewish community and an 8th-generation descendant of the Maharil Segal (Chabadpedia). Her mother was Beila (born Levinsohn) (FamilySearch). She came from a very wealthy family and supported the Alter Rebbe, who devoted all his time to studies (GEDCOM note).

She married Shneur Zalman in July 1760 (12 Menachem Av 5520), when he was 15 years old (FamilySearch, Chabadpedia). The marriage took place in the Vitebsk Oblast, Belarus (FamilySearch). At the time of the wedding, her family did not know of Shneur Zalman's involvement with the Hasidic movement. When this became known, her parents demanded she divorce him, but she refused (Chabadpedia).

After the Alter Rebbe's death in 1812, she moved to live with her grandson, the Tzemach Tzedek (3rd Rebbe), in Lubavichi, near her son the Mitteler Rebbe (Chabadpedia).

Her siblings included David M'Stepin Segal and Mrs. Akiva Segal (FamilySearch).

**Confidence: strong.** Wikipedia confirms her marriage to the Alter Rebbe. Multiple independent sources (Chabadpedia, FamilySearch, Geni.com) corroborate her identity. The Alter Rebbe's wife is well documented in Chabad historiography.
