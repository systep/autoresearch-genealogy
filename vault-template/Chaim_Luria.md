## Biography

Chaim Luria appears in the GEDCOM as a female member of the Luria family, born in Safed (Israel) in 1615, who married Rabbi Chaim HaCohen Katz. Despite the masculine-sounding given name "Chaim," the GEDCOM explicitly marks this individual as female (SEX F) with married name Katz. The GEDCOM source page reads "Chaim Katz (born Luria)."

This individual is significant in the claimed Davidic descent lineage as the link between the Luria family and the Katz/Loew line that leads to the Maharal of Prague's descendants. Through the marriage to Rabbi Chaim HaCohen Katz, the Luria lineage connects to the broader Loew family network.

Born in Safed, a major center of Jewish mysticism and learning in the 16th and 17th centuries, and the city associated with Rabbi Isaac Luria (the Arizal), though the exact relationship to Isaac Luria is not specified in the GEDCOM.
