## Corrections to prior vault attribution

The former vault name "Srul Mendel Zalmanson" was a MyHeritage tree mis-indexing. The Tier 1 revision list shows:
- Actual given name: **Srol'** (Yiddish for "Israel" = Hebrew Yisrael / Russian Sroel)
- Actual patronymic: **son of Zalman**
- No "Mendel" appears anywhere in the Tier 1 record — the MH trees fabricated or borrowed this name

The middle-name "Mendel" was a propagation error across 7+ MH user trees, likely originating from a Geni merge that incorrectly combined our Borisov Srol' with a different Zalmanson.

## 1906 Duma voter lists (corroboration)

The Borisov 1906 Minsk Vedomosti (Duma voter registry, 1st Congress) records three ZALMANZON property-owners in Borisov, all with father's patronymic "Berkovich" (= son of Berko):

These are almost certainly sons of **Berka ZALMANZON** (b. ~1838, the first-listed son in the 1850 household) — i.e., our Barnett Salmanson's first cousins. Berka's own naming pattern (Zalman, Israil, Srol for his sons) echoes his father's name (Zalman as grandfather) and his own brother-in-arms pattern.

## Reconciliation with RI Death Index

The 1907 RI Death Index names Louis's father as "Joseph Salmonson" and mother as "Ida". The Tier 1 Borisov 1850 record names Louis's likely father as "Srol'" (son of Zalman) and mother as "Gita". These disagree.

**Possible explanations:**
- Barnett Salmanson (informant on 1907 death cert, never knew his paternal grandparents) approximated Yiddish names into English: "Srol'" → "Joseph"?? (not a typical transliteration — Srol/Israel = "Israel" in English, not "Joseph"), "Gita" → "Ida" (plausible informal anglicization)
- Louis's father may have had a compound name "Yosef Srol' Zalmanzon" with both Hebrew names, Yosef used in US by son and Srol' recorded in Russian-era revision
- The RI index entry may contain a clerical error

The 1858 Borisov revision list would resolve this — if it names Louis as a son of Srol' and confirms Srol's family composition, the identification is locked at Tier 1.

## Biography

Srul Mendel Zalmanson was born in 1820. His father was **Yekutiel Zalman Wallis/Volis**, and his mother was **Rivka Beila (Shneuri)**, a **daughter of the Mitteler Rebbe** (Rabbi Dovber Shneuri, 1773-1827, the 2nd Lubavitcher Rebbe) and therefore a **granddaughter of Shneur Zalman of Liadi** (the Alter Rebbe, 1745-1812, founder of Chabad-Lubavitch). This connection is confirmed at **Strong Signal** by Geni.com, Chabad sources, and JewishGen. The surname "Wallis" is a transliteration of "Volis," a Ukrainian Jewish surname; Yekutiel Zalman's father was Yosef Bunim, himself a son-in-law of Rabbi Levi Yitzchak of Berditchev.

Srul Mendel was a **brother** of Rabbi Levi Yitzchok Zalmanson (1812-1872), who married Dvorah Leah Schneursohn (daughter of the 3rd Lubavitcher Rebbe). Another sibling was Sarah Freida (Wallis), who married Israel Noah Schneerson (the Rebbe of Nizhyn). This means the family was connected to the Schneerson dynasty through BOTH their mother (daughter of the Mitteler Rebbe) and their brother's marriage (to the Tzemach Tzedek's daughter).

Srul Mendel had two wives: a first wife whose name is unknown, and Gita Zalmanson. His children include Berko, Liba, and Levi Itzhak (b. 1851).

He died in 1854 at approximately age 34 in **Barysaw (Borisov)**, in the Minsk Region of Belarus. His son Levi Itzhak was only about 3 years old at the time of his death.
