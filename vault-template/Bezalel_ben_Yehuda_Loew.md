## Biography

Bezalel ben Yehuda Loew is identified in the GEDCOM and on Geni.com as the great-grandfather of the Maharal of Prague. He was reportedly a rabbi, and the GEDCOM associates him with the Geni.com profile for "Betzalel Hazaken (Descendant of King David, 92nd Generation) Loew" (unverified).

His son, Chaim ben Bezalel Loew-Beer, became Rabbi of Worms and was the Maharal's grandfather. However, the genealogical chain from Bezalel ben Yehuda through Yehuda Leib the Elder is contested by modern scholarship (see Data Discrepancies below).

The GEDCOM age at death of "About 20-21" is clearly erroneous if born 1420 and died 1460 (that would be age 40). This metadata error in the GEDCOM reduces confidence.

Confidence: **Speculative**. This individual appears only in genealogical compilations derived from Meir Perels' Megillat Yuhasin (1745), which Putik (2009) has shown contains deliberate errors. No primary source confirms his existence independently. The Geni.com profile title itself includes a question mark. The Davidic descent claim through this line is contested.
