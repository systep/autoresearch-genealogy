## Biography

Isaacke Worthington, described as "de Hough" (indicating residence in Hough, a township within Wilmslow Parish, Cheshire), was the father of Hannah Worthington, who was baptized October 14, 1660 at Wilmslow. His wife was Dulcebella Rossell (born Helme), who also appears in the vault as [[Doulsabell_Helme]]. The "Rossell" and "Helme" surnames in the GEDCOM trace to this couple.

Hannah Worthington married [[John_Hough_1660|John Hough]] circa 1680. She also appears in vault records as [[Hannah_Thompson_Rossell]], where the "Rossell" is her mother's married surname and "Worthington" is her father's surname. This naming confusion has been a source of discrepancy across user-contributed trees (see [[Daniel_Hough]] Data Discrepancies).

The designation "de Hough" places Isaacke in the same Hough township where the Hough family resided, suggesting close geographic ties between the Worthington and Hough families prior to the marriage of Hannah and John Hough.
