## Biography

Judson King was born June 20, 1841 in Greenville, South Carolina, to Washington King and a mother whose maiden name appears to be Shambler (reading uncertain). He was a farmer who lived and died in the Dunklin Township area of Greenville County. He died on May 12, 1921 at approximately 9 A.M. from chronic nephritis, at the age of 79 years and 11 months. He had been under medical care since at least August 1920 (SC Death Certificate #7013).

His birth year of 1841 means he was of military age during the Civil War (age 20 at Fort Sumter). No military records have yet been located; this is a research priority.

A user-provided photograph shows Judson King with a woman, presumably his wife, in formal late 19th or early 20th century attire. The wife's name has not yet been identified.

The death certificate informant, H.H. King of Pelzer, SC, was likely a son or close relative. Pelzer is a mill town in the southern part of Greenville County, consistent with the Dunklin Township / Fork Shoals area where Judson lived and was buried.
