## Biography

Georg Kascher was born in March 1792 in Wurmloch (Romanian: Valea Viilor), in the Sibiu district of Transylvania (present day Romania). He was the earliest known ancestor in the Kascher line. A VGSS (Verein für Genealogie der Siebenbürger Sachsen, the Transylvanian Saxon Genealogical Society) note records his year as 1800, which may indicate a church register reference date rather than a birth date.

Georg was a **Landbauer** (farmer) in Wurmloch. He married **Maria Wagner** (b. 1795, Wurmloch). Together they had six children: Katharina, Maria, Anna, Mathias, Michael, and Johann.

His son **Michael Kascher** (b. September 20, 1834, Wurmloch; d. June 28, 1880, Waldhütten; cause of death: Lungenentzuendung/pneumonia) married **Stephania Benning** (b. 27 January 1829; d. 22 May 1876, uterine cancer; GEDCOM incorrectly calls her "Sara Schuller"; see cross_reference_audit.md #19). Their children were [[Regina_Kascher]] (b. 3 Aug 1861; d. 4 Mar 1933) and [[Michael_Kascher_Sr_1864]] (1864-1935), who emigrated to Youngstown, Ohio. Through Michael Sr.'s son [[Michael_Kascher_Transylvania]] (1889-1975), the line continues to [[Henry_Michael_Kascher]] and ultimately to the Salmanson family.

Wurmloch (Valea Viilor) is a Transylvanian Saxon village in Sibiu County, Romania. The Kascher family moved from Wurmloch to the nearby village of Waldhütten (Valchid) by the next generation. The community was part of the ethnic German settlement in Transylvania dating to the 12th century.

**Pre-1792 research (2026-04-10):** Extensive searching found no records predating Georg. The original Genealogy.com poster who provided Georg's data also noted they could not find anything earlier. Family folklore claims a **Flanders origin**, which is consistent with the documented 12th century migration of Flemish and German settlers to Transylvania under King Geza II of Hungary (1141-1162). The "Kascher" surname is rare and does not appear in standard Transylvanian Saxon surname lists. Best remaining avenues: (1) VGSS genealogy database (vgss.de/genealogie-datenbank/) for digitized Wurmloch church records, (2) Zentralarchiv of the Evangelical Church A.B. in Sibiu (TeutschHaus, Str. The 1819-1820 Transylvanian Census (FamilySearch microfilm) would confirm the Kascher family's presence in Wurmloch at that date.

Georg Kascher (age 27-28 in 1819) should appear under Nagybaromlak (Hungarian name for Wurmloch). Available at FamilySearch Centers. Would confirm family presence and possibly reveal other Kascher households (potential siblings of Georg's father).

**Ancestry collections**: Collection 5412 (Romania Vital Records, 1607-1914) and Collection 62878 (Transylvania Birth/Marriage/Death, 1688-1919) cover Transylvanian Evangelical church records. Subscription required. May contain pre-1800 Wurmloch baptisms.

**Kascher surname etymology**: Extremely rare surname. Most common in Brazil (78 bearers per Forebears.io). Possible origins: (a) occupational, from "Kaeser" (cheesemaker) with dialect shift, (b) toponymic, from Katscher (Silesian town, now Kietrz, Poland), (c) Transylvanian Saxon dialect variant. No definitive etymology established. The rarity is consistent with a localized Transylvanian origin rather than a common German occupational name.
