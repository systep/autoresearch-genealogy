## Biography

William Holloway lived in Brunswick County, Virginia, during the latter half of the 18th century. His will, dated 16 October 1784 and proved 22 November 1784 (Brunswick Co VA Will Book 2, pp. 422-423), names his wife Martha and divides the estate into seven parts among six living children and the children of his deceased son Obediah ("Obed's children"). The plantation was bequeathed to Martha until her death or remarriage. Specific bequests:
- Acreage to sons Dan, Jesse, and Asa (also named as executors).
- 100 pounds to son Caleb.
- Daughters Martha Patty (Moseley) and Muhulda Anne (Pool) named.

The will was witnessed by **William Moseley Jr.** (likely father of Samuel Moseley, William's son-in-law), Harmon Read, and Littleberry Pearson. The estate sale occurred on 1 April 1789, after Martha's death; executors were Jesse and Asa Holloway. Among purchasers were **James Huff** and **William Huff** (probably son-in-law and grandson of Mary Holloway Huff via the adjacent Daniel Huff line, suggesting an extended Holloway-Huff kinship).

**Connection to the vault's Mary Holloway Huff (wife of [[Daniel_Huff_Brunswick]], d. 1777):** Mary is **NOT** named in this 1784 will. She therefore is most likely not William's daughter. Possibilities:
1. Mary was William's sister (compatible with Daniel Huff's death in 1777 placing Mary's marriage in the prior generation),
2. Mary was a niece (daughter of an earlier-deceased Holloway brother),
3. Mary descended from a different Brunswick Co VA Holloway family (less likely given the geographic and economic overlap evidenced by the 1789 estate-sale purchases by Huff family members).

The "William Huff" purchaser at the 1789 estate sale and the "James Huff" purchaser are most plausibly Mary's sons by Daniel Huff (Daniel Jr. and James, named in Daniel's 1773 will), participating in their grandmother's estate liquidation -- which would be consistent with possibility (1) or (2). See [[Mary_Holloway_Huff]] for the open kinship question. New OQ added in [[Open_Questions]].
