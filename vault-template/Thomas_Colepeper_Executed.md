## Biography

Sir Thomas Colepeper of Bayhall was a medieval English knight from the Culpeper family of Kent. He was the son of Thomas de Colepeper of Brenchley and grandson of [[Thomas_de_Colepeper_Recognitor]] (WikiTree Colepeper-7, Tier 3).

Thomas married **Margery**, a daughter associated with the Bayhall estate in Pembury, Kent (WikiTree Bayhall-1, Tier 3). Through this marriage, the Colepeper family acquired the manor of Bayhall, which became a principal family seat for subsequent generations.

Thomas took the side of Thomas, Earl of Lancaster, against King Edward II during the baronial conflicts of 1321 to 1322. He was also involved with Bartholomew de Badlesmere in the rebellion. As Governor of Winchelsea, he was captured and executed at Winchelsea in 1321 (some sources give 1322) (WikiTree Colepeper-7, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3).

Following his execution, his possessions were forfeited to the Crown. However, the lands in Buxted and Maresfield were restored in 1324 to his widow Margery (Geni, Tier 3). The family's fortunes were later fully restored under Edward III, with Thomas's son John regaining the family estates and rising to become Sheriff of Kent.
