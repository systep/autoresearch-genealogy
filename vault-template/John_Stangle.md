## Biography

John Stangle was born in 1744 in England. He married **Eleanor Sands** and immigrated to America circa 1783 (age approximately 39). The surname Stangle is confirmed as German in origin, a variant of Stengel/Stangl (from Middle High German "stangen" + "el," meaning "little stick"). His birth in England combined with a German surname is consistent with **Palatine German migration** through England, a well documented pattern in the 18th century. Georg Stengel (b. 1713, Endingen) is documented as going to "New England" before 1752, confirming German Stengel migration to Colonial America, though no direct link to John has been established.

John Stangle's family is connected to the intermarried cluster of families in the Greenville/Simpsonville, South Carolina area, alongside the Coxes, Hendersons, Longs, Brights, and others.

No corroborating sources have been found. 2. Children's birth dates originally appeared to be in the 1600s, but this is a century-digit misread; all dates are in the 1700s. See cross-reference audit Discrepancy #2.
