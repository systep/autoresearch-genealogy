## Biography

Tucker Woodson Jr. was born c. 1744 in Goochland County, Virginia, son of Tucker Woodson Sr. (b. 1720, d. 1795) and Sarah Hughes. He married Elizabeth Moore (b. 1742, daughter of John Moore and Mary Jouett) in 1765 in Albemarle County, Virginia. He reportedly signed the Albemarle Declaration of Independence and was killed during the American Revolution in 1779 in Albemarle County, Virginia.

His son Tucker Moore Woodson (1777-1831) is the father of [[Mary_Ann_Woodson_Cooley|Mary Ann Woodson Cooley]] (1803-1850), second wife of [[Hiram_Cooley]] and a direct ancestor of [[Charstee_King]]. (Tucker Moore Woodson is currently recorded in Family_Tree.md and [[Mary_Ann_Woodson_Cooley]] under the name "Tucker Woodson 1762-1831"; the 1777 vs 1762 date conflict is a separate cross-reference audit issue, NOT modified by this file per Guard Rail #3.)
