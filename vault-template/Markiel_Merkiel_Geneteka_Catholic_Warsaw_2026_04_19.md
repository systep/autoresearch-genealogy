# Geneteka Catholic Merkiel/Markiel Records, Warsaw and Lublin, 1802-1941

Results of a Geneteka (JRI-Poland's Catholic sibling database for Polish Roman Catholic parish registers) search for surname variants **Merkiel / Markiel / Markul / Markuła / Markielewska**. Total: **41 records** (16 births, 11 marriages, 13 deaths, 1 additional death).

## Critical finding: these are Catholic, not Jewish

All records cluster in **Warsaw Roman Catholic parishes** (św. Krzyż, św. Jan, św. Andrzej, św. Aleksander, św. Barbara, św. Wojciech, Nawiedzenie NMP, św. Jakub, Narodzenie NMP, Wszystkich Świętych), with one isolated Lublin record. Given names are Catholic Polish (Apolonia, Katarzyna Elżbieta, Franciszek Ksawery Hieronim Beniamin, Dionizy, Felicjanna, Zofia Anna, Stefania, Aleksandra, Władysław, Kazimierz, Julia, Marian, Stanisław, Fryderyk August, Teofil Leonard, etc.).

**The vault's Markel line is Ashkenazi Jewish** — Mojzesz Majer Markiel, Dov Markel, Heschel Herman Markel, Nissen Mendel Markel, Morris Markel, Hannah Markel, Sarah Markel Tachner, Florence Markel Mermelstein, Masha Markel Tachner, Minnie Markel Sternbach, Henie Markel. Catholic Polish Merkiels of Warsaw and Ashkenazi Jewish Markels are **almost certainly unrelated** surname-sharers, not kin. The surname Markel/Merkiel exists independently in both Polish-Catholic and German/Yiddish-Jewish naming traditions.

**Disposition**: NEGATIVE RESULT for the Markel line. This Geneteka Catholic dataset is NOT our family; no person files are created from it. Logged here as disambiguation so that future researchers don't accidentally merge the Warsaw Catholic Merkiel cluster with the vault's Jewish Markels.

## Why this is almost certainly NOT our Markel line

1. **Religious context**: All parishes are Roman Catholic. The vault's Markel ancestors (Mojzesz Majer Markiel, Dov Markel, etc.) are Jewish. Jewish vital records in Congress Poland / Russian Poland are kept in **separate civil registers** (ASC księgi stanu cywilnego for non-Christian populations), not in Catholic parish books.
2. **Given names**: Catholic Polish (Apolonia, Bogumił, Felicjanna, Kazimierz, Teofil Leonard, Marian, Stefania) vs Jewish (Mojzesz, Hershel, Nissen Mendel, Dov, Henie, Masha). No overlap.
3. **Geographic concentration**: Warsaw. The vault's Markel line has no documented Warsaw connection; it is tied (provisionally) to smaller-town Polish-Lithuanian-Russian Jewish communities.
4. **Women's surnames**: Merkiel women here marry Tomaszewski, Kuziński, Kamiński — all Polish Catholic surnames. Our Jewish Markel women marry into Zipperman, Salk, Tachner, Mermelstein, Sternbach — all Jewish surnames.

## What to search next for the actual Jewish Markel line

1. **JewishGen JRI-Poland** (jri-poland.org) — sibling database to Geneteka, specifically for Jewish Polish vital records. Search **Markiel / Markel / Markelman / Markielman** with same year range. Coverage is strongest for the Lublin, Kielce, Radom, and Łódź gubernias.
2. **JewishGen Unified Search** — covers multiple databases (Russia, Ukraine, Lithuania). Search same surnames.
3. **Alex Krakovsky's Wiki** (kdkv.name) for scanned Russian-Empire vital record books, especially if the Markel origin town can be localized.
4. **USC Shoah Foundation** and **Yad Vashem Pages of Testimony** for any Markel killed in the Holocaust — might name parents/siblings living in specific pre-war towns.
5. **Steve Morse One-Step** + Ellis Island / Castle Garden passenger arrivals for any Markel / Markl / Merkel arriving from Russian Poland 1880-1914 with a recorded origin town.
