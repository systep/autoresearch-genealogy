# Hypothesis: Plantagenet Royal Line Back to Norman, Carolingian, and Merovingian Eras

## Statement

Henry II of England (b. 1133) and Eleanor of Aquitaine (b. c. 1122-1124) descend, through three principal lines (Norman ducal, Angevin comital, and Aquitanian ducal), from Rollo of Normandy, from the Counts of Anjou (House of Ingelger), and from the Ramnulfid Dukes of Aquitaine. All three lines are claimed, by standard peerage sources, to extend backward to the Carolingian dynasty, and through traditional but contested material, to the Merovingians and Clovis I.

## Scope

This hypothesis documents the chain as a single structured list. It does NOT recommend creating individual person files for the approximately 30+ intervening generations. Where a vault person file already exists, it is wikilinked.

## Tier Legend

- **Tier 2 (Secondary, well-corroborated peerage chain)**: Consistently reported in Complete Peerage, Burke's, Wikipedia, and Geni curated projects. Defensible but not primary-sourced by modern standards.
- **Tier 3 (Tertiary, traditional or disputed)**: Early Merovingian segment, pre-911 Norse ancestry of Rollo, and the Tertullus-Petronilla parentage of Ingelger. Reported in medieval chronicles but rejected or doubted by modern medievalists.

## Analysis

The chain splits cleanly into three defensibility zones.

**Zone 1 (Tier 2, defensible)**: Henry II and Eleanor back to approximately 900 CE. The Norman ducal line from Rollo to William I to Henry II, the Angevin comital line from Ingelger to Geoffrey Plantagenet, and the Aquitanian ducal line from Ramnulf I to Eleanor are all uniformly attested in standard peerage reference works. Dates are often approximate but the sequence of persons and their relationships are not seriously disputed.

**Zone 2 (mixed)**: 900 CE back to approximately 700 CE. The direct Carolingian male line (Charlemagne, Pepin the Short, Charles Martel, Pepin of Herstal, Ansegisel, Arnulf of Metz) is reliably documented. Connections from the Norman, Angevin, and Aquitanian lines INTO the Carolingians rely on cognatic (non-patrilineal) bridges via consorts, and some of those bridges are more secure than others. Rollo's marriage to Poppa of Bayeux and later Norman marriages into Frankish nobility carry Carolingian descent. Ingelger's claimed maternal Carolingian descent via Petronilla is disputed. Fulk I the Red's marriage to Roscilla de Loches and later Fulk II's marriage to Gerberga (a Carolingian connection) are more defensible.

**Zone 3 (Tier 3, traditional)**: Pre-700 CE Merovingian segment. The traditional bridge via Arnulf of Metz to Cologne Merovingians to Clovis is not supported by modern medievalist consensus. It represents Carolingian-era political genealogy, constructed to legitimize Pepin's usurpation of 751, rather than documented descent.

The legendary Norse ancestry of Rollo (via Rognvald of More) is preserved in Icelandic saga tradition (Heimskringla) but is also Tier 3.

## Decisive Record

No single record can settle the pre-Carolingian segment. For any rigorous confidence beyond Charlemagne, primary charter or contemporary narrative evidence would be required and does not survive. The chain should be treated as documentary tradition, not proof, for generations earlier than approximately 700 CE.
