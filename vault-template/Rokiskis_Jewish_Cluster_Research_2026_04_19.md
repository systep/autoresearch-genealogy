# Rokiskis Jewish Cluster Research Plan (2026-04-19)

## Scope

Vault contains a Rokiskis-anchored Jewish cluster. Specific individuals (all Jewish, tied directly or collaterally to Rokiskis in Zarasai/Novoalexandrovsk uyezd, Kovno guberniya):

- Zalmanson/Salmanson: [[Srul_Mendel_Zalmanson]], [[Levi_Yitzchak_Zalmanson]], [[Pinkhas_Aron_Zalmanson]], [[Pinkhas_Aron_Zalmanson_1823]], [[Srul_Mendel_Zalmanson_1820]], [[Gita_Zalmanson]], [[Rivkah_Zalmanson]], [[Dvorah_Leah_Zalmanson]], [[Shifra_Zalmanson]], [[Levi_Itzhak_Zalmanson]], [[Shneer_Zalmanson]], [[Sura_Sosya_Zelmanzon]].
- Sheinberg: [[Ghersh_Hirsh_Tzvi_Sheinberg]], [[Mendel_Sheinberg]], [[Elka_Sheinberg]], [[Shneer_Zalman_Sheinberg]], [[Dvora_Liza_Sheinberg]].
- Deych: [[Zawel_Deykh]], [[Rochel_Tsine_Deych]].
- Wallis/Walles: [[Keyla_Wallis]], [[Reiza_Sura_Wallis]], [[Reiza_Sura_Wallis_1827]], [[Yekutiel_Zalman_Wallis]], [[Sarah_Freida_Wallis]], [[Moshe_Yehuda_Leib_Walles]].

Anchor event: 1877 marriage of Levi Yitzchak Zalmanson in Rokiskis (referenced in Open Questions around Zawel Deykh).

## Search Results Summary (WebSearch, 2026-04-19)

Ten targeted WebSearches across JewishGen, LitvakSIG, and Rokiskis KehilaLinks returned **no direct surname-level hits** for Zalmanson, Salmanson, Sheinberg, Deych/Deich, Wallis, or Walles in the public search-engine-indexed pages. This is expected: the All Lithuania Database (ALD) and LitvakSIG spreadsheet releases are **behind a search form (not crawlable)**, and the Rokiskis-specific indexes are delivered as PDFs or Excel spreadsheets to qualifying donors. Web search can confirm infrastructure; it cannot substitute for querying the ALD form directly.

### Confirmed Infrastructure (Tier 2, institutional)

- Rokiskis belongs administratively to **Zarasai uyezd (Novoalexandrovsk)**, Kovno Guberniya. LitvakSIG's **Zarasai District Research Group** is the correct SIG for this cluster. Confirmed.
- Rokiskis town dwellers' administration compiled a **book of abstracts from vital records 1876-1894** (critical: this window covers the 1877 Levi Yitzchak Zalmanson marriage anchor). Abstracts are less complete than originals but should name bride, groom, fathers, and place.
- Additional Rokiskis-SIG-owned datasets: **1897 All-Russian Census (Rokiskis portion), candle tax records, box tax records, 1891-1915 Postal Bank Records, 1908 Family List, index to vital records from Kaunas Archives**.
- **LitvakSIG Vital Records Translation (VRT) Project**: 650,000+ of est. 800,000 19th/early-20th-century Lithuanian Jewish vital records translated; many flow into the ALD.
- **Rokiskis Yizkor Book** (Yisker-bukh fun Rakishok un umgegnt, ed. M. Bakalczuk-Felin) is online at JewishGen with a family-name index.

### Hits by Surname

- **Zalmanson / Salmanson**: No indexed hits via WebSearch. Variant spellings to try in ALD: Zalmanson, Salmanson, Zelmanson, Zelmanzon, Zalmansohn, Salmonson.
- **Sheinberg**: No indexed hits via WebSearch. Variants: Sheinberg, Scheinberg, Shainberg, Sheynberg, Szejnberg, Schoenberg (German).
- **Deych / Deykh**: No indexed hits via WebSearch. Variants: Deych, Deich, Deytsch, Deutsch, Dejc. This is a high-value target because Zawel Deykh is the specific Open Question anchor.
- **Wallis / Walles**: No indexed hits via WebSearch. Variants: Wallis, Walles, Valis, Valles, Walis, Vales.

Web search could not retrieve structured records inside the ALD. **Absence of web hits is not evidence of absence in the ALD itself.**

## Hypothesis

**H1 (primary)**: The 1877 marriage of Levi Yitzchak Zalmanson is recorded in the Rokiskis vital record abstracts (LVIA holdings, 1876-1894 series). The bride entry should name Rochel Tsine Deych and her father Zawel Deykh, resolving the Zawel Deykh identity Open Question.

**H2**: The Sheinberg, Wallis, and additional Zalmanson individuals (b. 1820-1830s) appear in the Rokiskis 1897 All-Russian Census, the 1908 Family List, and possibly the Box Tax and Candle Tax payer lists, since the cluster is clearly resident in Rokiskis across multiple generations.

**H3**: At least some of these surnames appear in the Rokiskis Yizkor Book (Holocaust-era memorial volume), particularly if descendants remained in Rokiskis through the 20th century.

## Next Steps (Ordered)

### Step 1: Direct ALD Query (free, human-driven)

The All Lithuania Database at https://www.litvaksig.org/all-lithuania-database/ is free and searchable without membership. Human operator should:

1. Search each surname variant listed above, filtering by **District = Zarasai / Novoalexandrovsk** and **Town = Rokiskis / Rakiskis / Rokishok**.
2. Capture every hit (record ID, date, record type, relatives) into a per-surname table appended to this file.
3. Cross-reference against vault person files to match dates and patronymics.

### Step 2: Rokiskis SIG Membership and District Research Group Contribution

LitvakSIG records are released first to qualifying contributors to the **Zarasai District Research Group** (typical $100 USD contribution unlocks the complete unrestricted spreadsheets 18 months before public release). Given the density of this cluster, contribution is likely cost-effective.

- Contact: Zarasai District Research Group coordinator via litvaksig.org/research/district-research/zarasai-district-research-group.
- Expected deliverable: full Excel spreadsheets for Rokiskis vital records, revision lists, family lists, tax lists.

### Step 3: LVIA Direct Order (paid)

If ALD and SIG releases do not surface the 1877 marriage record, order from the **Lithuanian State Historical Archives (LVIA)** in Vilnius.

- Target fond: Rokiskis town dwellers' administration abstracts 1876-1894 (the exact fond number should come from the Zarasai DRG coordinator or the archyvai.lt genealogical search service at https://www.archyvai.lt/en/genealogical-search.html).
- Specify: 1877 marriage record, groom Levi Yitzchak Zalmanson, bride Rochel Tsine Deych, father of bride Zawel Deykh.

### Step 4: Rokiskis Yizkor Book Name Index Review

Manually review the JewishGen Rokiskis Yizkor Book pages (https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/rokiskis/rokiskis.html) and the Yizkor Book Master Name Index (https://www.jewishgen.org/databases/yizkor/Names/) for Zalmanson, Sheinberg, Deych, Wallis variants. Yizkor entries are Tier 2/3 but can establish 20th-century persistence in Rokiskis and name descendants killed in the Shoah.

### Step 6: Census and Tax List Cross-Reference

Once Rokiskis SIG datasets are accessible (via membership or public release):

- 1897 All-Russian Census: match household heads by surname, ages, and given names against vault person files.
- 1908 Family List: locate all four surname clusters to confirm continued Rokiskis residence and identify grandchildren not yet in the vault.

## Open Questions Raised

1. What is the exact LVIA fond and inventory number for the Rokiskis 1876-1894 vital record abstracts?
2. Does the 1877 Rokiskis marriage abstract include Zawel Deykh as father of the bride (resolving the existing OQ) or only the bride's given name?
3. Are the Zalmanson individuals born in 1820 and 1823 (Pinkhas_Aron_Zalmanson_1823, Srul_Mendel_Zalmanson_1820) covered by the 1858 Revision List for Rokiskis? Revision Lists predate the 1876-1894 abstracts and would be the best source for these earlier births.
