## Biography

Mathias Falk was born June 3, 1867, the son of **Mathias Falk** (1837-1892) and **Agnetha Probstdorfer** (1847-1888). The Falk family was part of the **Transylvanian Saxon** community, ethnic Germans settled in Transylvania since the 12th century. The family was based in **Petresti** (German: Petersdorf), Alba County, in present-day Romania. His mother Agnetha died when he was approximately 21 years old, and his father died four years later.

Mathias's siblings (from the same parents) included Marguarita (m. Pfaff), Simon, Rosina, Agneta (m. Binder), and an unnamed girl (Girl Falk).

The Falk lineage extends back several generations through Transylvanian records: his paternal grandfather Mathias Johann Falk (1811-1873) married Katherina Konnerth (1817-1876); his paternal great-grandfather was another Mathias Falk, married to Maria Brosert. The pattern of the name "Mathias" repeating across at least four generations is notable.

Mathias Falk married **Katharina Pfaff** (b. 1871). Katharina's parents were Mathias Pfaff (1836-1899) and Katherina Dressler (1844-1934) (per the Rosina Falk person file).

Their daughter **Rosina (Rasina) Falk** (1896-1999) married [[Michael_Kascher_Transylvania]] (1889-1975) on May 10, 1913 in Beaver, Pennsylvania (microfilm #001290365). The marriage record confirms "Mathias Falk" as the bride's father. Through Rosina's son [[Henry_Michael_Kascher]] and granddaughter [[Kathryn_Ann_Kascher]], this line connects to the Salmanson family.

Mathias died in 1934. The circumstances and location of his death are not documented in available sources.
