## Biography

John Baldwin Sr. was born circa 1730 in Chester, Pennsylvania. He migrated to Chatham County, North Carolina, arriving by 1756 or earlier. He married Alice Francis "Alsey" Lindley (1736 to 1781), daughter of James Lindley of Chester County, Pennsylvania. The Lindley family were Quakers who had emigrated from County Carlow, Ireland in 1713 and were founders of the New Garden Monthly Meeting. James Lindley later became a prominent figure in North Carolina (NCpedia).

John and Alice had at least seven children:
1. Rachel Baldwin (b. 1753); married Thompson; moved to Tennessee
2. Samuel Baldwin (b. 1757); owned Baldwin's Mill on Terrell's Creek, Chatham County
3. John James Baldwin (b. 1770); moved to South Carolina
4. Nancy Baldwin (b. 1773); married John Reeves; moved to South Carolina
5. [[Eli_Baldwin]] (b. c.1775); migrated to Greenville County, SC
6. John Baldwin Jr.
7. William Baldwin (b. 1777)

Alice died in 1781, when Eli was approximately 6 years old. It is unknown whether John Baldwin Sr. remarried.

In August 1801, John Baldwin Sr. deeded land to his son Eli Baldwin in Chatham County (Deed Book L, pp. 239-240). This was likely a transfer in anticipation of Eli's migration to South Carolina. John Baldwin Sr. died circa 1811 in Chatham County, North Carolina.

Note: At least three of John's children (John James, Nancy, and Eli) migrated from Chatham County to South Carolina, establishing a Baldwin family presence there that would eventually connect to the Clark, Cox, Henderson, and Long families through marriage.
