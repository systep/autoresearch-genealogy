# Schwartz Kolmsee West Prussia Research (2026-04-19)

## Scope and Distinction

This hypothesis concerns the **Schwartz branch entering the vault via Christian Schwartz (b. 24 Dec 1841, "Kolmsee, West Prussia")**. This line is **separate** from the Johann Christoph Schwarz branch (b. 1822, Enzweihingen, Wurttemberg). Up to 30+ Schwartz files in the vault may distribute across these two (possibly three) distinct Schwartz/Schwarz families. Candidate descendants believed to belong to this Kolmsee line include Augusta Anna Bertha Schwartz, Louis F Schwartz, Hermon Schlack Schwartz, Charles Christian Schwartz, Fred Christian Schwartz, Carl Schwartz, and Otto Christian Schwartz, though none are yet independently confirmed to descend from Christian of Kolmsee.

## Disambiguation of "Kolmsee"

"Kolmsee" as a place name in West Prussia is not attested in standard gazetteers (Meyers Gazetteer, GOV genealogy.net, Kartenmeister return no village named Kolmsee in Westpreussen from the searches run on 2026-04-19). GenWiki lists "Kolmsee" under Familienname (surname), supporting the view that the MyHeritage GEDCOM place string is a corruption.

Three candidate resolutions were considered:

| Candidate | Location | Kreis / District | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kulmsee / Culmsee / Chelmza** | ~14 miles north of Thorn (Torun) | Kreis Thorn, RB Marienwerder, West Prussia | **Most likely.** Phonetically and orthographically the closest; well-documented Evangelical parish; Culmsee register covers 1819-1865 which spans the 1841 birth. |
| Kolmsee (as a surname that became a place-label in error) | n/a | n/a | Plausible source of GEDCOM confusion. |
| A village in Kreis Graudenz / other Westpreussen kreis | n/a | no gazetteer match found | Not supported. |

**Working conclusion (Moderate confidence, pending primary record):** "Kolmsee" in the MyHeritage GEDCOM almost certainly refers to **Kulmsee (Culmsee), Kreis Thorn, Regierungsbezirk Marienwerder, Westpreussen** (modern Chelmza, Torun County, Kujawsko-Pomorskie, Poland).
