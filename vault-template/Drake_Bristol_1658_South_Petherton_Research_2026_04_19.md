# Drake Bristol 1658 and South Petherton Corroboration Research

## Research Question

Can the oral tradition that vault Drakes descend from Richard Drake, serge maker of South Petherton, Somerset, who emigrated via Bristol on 6 September 1658 indentured to Andrew Ball, mariner, be corroborated in primary or near-primary sources, and can Richard Drake's own parents be identified in the South Petherton parish register?

## Summary of Findings (2026-04-19)

### The 1658 Bristol Indenture (Strong Signal at Tier 2)

Five independent Tier 2 web sources (xroyvision Drake archive, WikiTree Drake-3124, rootsweb freepages Drake, ncgenweb Warren & Nash Counties Drake PDF, Geni profile) consistently report the following:

- On **6 September 1658**, at Bristol, **Richard Drake, serge maker**, indentured himself (3 year term) to **Andrew Ball, mariner**, for transport to the Virginia colony.
- The same indenture included **Thomazine (Thompson/Tomzin) Drake** (4 years, the wife), **Mary Drake** (4 years), **John Drake** (10 years), and **Thomas Drake** (12 years).
- Richard Drake was resident in **South Petherton Parish, Somerset**, during 1647-1650 before relocating to Bristol.
- Richard prospered in Virginia as a serge maker and acquired land in Isle of Wight County.

These accounts are consistent enough in detail (names, terms, date, destination) to suggest they derive from a single primary transcription: **Peter Wilson Coldham, The Bristol Registers of Servants Sent to Foreign Plantations, 1654-1686 (Genealogical Publishing Company, 1988)**, which itself transcribes the original Bristol servants register now held at Bristol Archives (reference 04220, formerly 04220/1-2).

The Virtual Jamestown Indentured Servants database (virtualjamestown.org/indentures/) hosts an online searchable version of Coldham's Bristol data under db=bristol_ind, covering over 10,000 records 1654-1686.

**Tier assessment**: The Coldham printed transcription is Tier 2 (published secondary compilation of primary records). The original Bristol servants register is Tier 1. Web summaries that reference Coldham are Tier 2 derivative.

### South Petherton Parish Registers (Key Resource)

- Parish Church of **Ss Peter and Paul, South Petherton** registers survive from 1574.
- **Baptisms available: 1598-1639 and 1650-1978** (gap 1640-1649).
- **Marriages available: 1649-1973**.
- **Burials available: 1657-1975**.
- FreeREG has transcribed portions; southpethertoninformation.org.uk hosts downloadable PDFs including "South Petherton Baptisms 1650-1670".
- The National Archives reference for the original register (1574-1674) is Somerset Heritage Centre D\P\pet.s/2/1/1.

The 1640-1649 gap likely encompasses Richard Drake's marriage to Thomazine (presumed 1640-1647) and possibly the baptisms of his older children. Richard's own baptism, if he was born c. 1610-1620 in South Petherton, should fall within the available 1598-1639 baptism register.

### Reported 1650 South Petherton Drake Baptism

Secondary sources reference a Drake child baptized **7 June 1650** at South Petherton. The exact child's name and parents as recorded in the register have not been directly confirmed from the primary source in this session. This baptism is a prime target for direct lookup in the South Petherton Baptisms 1650-1670 PDF or via FreeREG.

### Claimed Parents of Richard Drake (Unverified, Tier 3)

WikiTree Drake-3124 (a user-contributed profile) names Richard Drake's parents as:

- **Father**: Robert Drake (sometimes rendered Robert Fawcett Drake)
- **Mother**: Susanna Burnell

**No primary source is cited on WikiTree for this parentage.** The "Fawcett" middle name is almost certainly spurious (middle names were uncommon in early 17th century English yeomanry). The Burnell surname for Susanna may reflect genuine research or may be imported from another Drake line. **Do not treat this parentage as established** until a South Petherton baptism entry for Richard Drake circa 1610-1620 naming Robert (Drake) and Susanna (Burnell?) as parents can be located.

## Best Candidate Parents for Richard Drake

**Working hypothesis only. Not proven.**

If the 1598-1639 South Petherton baptism register contains a Richard Drake baptism in the 1610-1620 window, the parent names recorded there will be definitive. Candidate matches worth checking in the FreeREG South Petherton baptism extract:

1. **Robert Drake (spouse Susanna)**, South Petherton, circa 1610-1620 baptisms. This is the WikiTree claim and must be tested against the actual register.
2. Alternative Drake heads-of-household baptizing sons Richard in South Petherton 1605-1625. The serge-maker trade suggests yeoman or minor gentry status, so the father's name may recur in South Petherton land and probate records.
3. Nearby parishes (Kingsbury Episcopi, North Petherton, Martock, Crewkerne) should be checked as secondary candidates if South Petherton yields no Richard Drake baptism in the target window.

## Confidence Tier

- **Moderate Signal** for the 1658 Bristol indenture event (multiple independent Tier 2 sources, derived from a common primary transcription in Coldham 1988).
- **Moderate to Strong Signal** for Richard Drake's South Petherton residency 1647-1650 (corroborated across the same sources, backed by the 7 June 1650 baptism reference).
- **Speculative** for the specific parentage (Robert Drake and Susanna Burnell). Single Tier 3 source, no primary citation.
- **Speculative** for the connection between this Richard Drake of 1658 and the vault's Matthew Bolivar Drake (b. 1814 NC). Three to five generations of intermediate connective tissue in Isle of Wight VA, Southampton VA, Warren/Nash NC remain undocumented.

## Specific Next Steps

1. **Direct lookup of the South Petherton Baptisms 1650-1670 PDF** at https://www.southpethertoninformation.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/South-Petherton-Baptisms-1650-1670-for-website.pdf to confirm the 7 June 1650 Drake baptism, identify the child's name, and confirm Richard and Thomazine as parents.
2. **FreeREG search**: Ss Peter and Paul South Petherton baptisms 1598-1639 for all Drake entries. Record every Richard Drake baptism 1605-1625 and its parent names. This is the highest-value free search.
3. **FreeREG search**: Ss Peter and Paul South Petherton marriages 1649-1673 for any Drake marriage (e.g. later marriages of John or Thomas if they returned to England, unlikely but worth noting).
4. **Bristol Archives reference 04220**: Request digital image of the 6 September 1658 Andrew Ball indenture entry. This is the Tier 1 primary source. Bristol Archives offers paid copy services.
5. **Coldham, Bristol Registers of Servants Sent to Foreign Plantations 1654-1686 (1988)**: Locate a library copy (WorldCat, archive.org borrowable) and transcribe the exact 6 September 1658 entry verbatim, including any marginal notations.
6. **Coldham, Complete Book of Emigrants (multivolume, 1607-1660, 1661-1699)**: Cross check for the same Drake family entry; Coldham cross references between his works.
7. **Isle of Wight County, Virginia land patents** (Library of Virginia, Cavaliers and Pioneers by Nell Marion Nugent): Search 1658-1690 for Richard Drake headright claims and land grants. Headright patents are Tier 1 and would anchor Richard in Virginia independent of the indenture.
8. **South Petherton probate (Somerset Heritage Centre)**: Wills and inventories of Drakes 1600-1660 may name Richard as son or brother.
9. **Virtual Jamestown indenture database**: Search surname Drake, year range 1654-1686, to confirm the 1658 entry matches Coldham and to capture any adjacent Drake indentures that might be kin.
10. **Disambiguate from the circa 1713 Richard Drake** appearing in ncgenweb Warren/Nash County compilation. Likely a descendant (grandson or great grandson) of the 1658 immigrant, not a separate immigration. Isle of Wight VA deed and will records 1690-1720 should clarify.

## Guard Rail Compliance

The link between the 1658 immigrant and the 1814 Matthew Bolivar remains **speculative** and is flagged accordingly in Matthew Bolivar Drake's person file.

Per Guard 2 (do not trust user trees without corroboration): WikiTree's claim of Robert Drake and Susanna Burnell as Richard's parents is explicitly marked unverified pending primary source review.
