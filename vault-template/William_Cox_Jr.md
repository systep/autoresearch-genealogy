## Biography

William Cox Jr. was born in Orange County, North Carolina, the son of **William Cox Sr.** (c. 1730-1814) and **Alice** (surname unknown). His father owned 110 acres on Haw River and Cane Creek in Orange County (purchased April 22, 1772 from John Howlet for 60 pounds, Deed Book #3 p. 599).

William Cox Sr. sold his North Carolina land on October 13, 1787 to John Millison for 250 pounds and migrated to Greenville County, South Carolina with intermarried families: the Longs, Blacks, Payne/Paines, Lees, and Brashiers/Brazures. William Jr. migrated with the family.

He married **Connie Baker** (b. 1760). After William Jr.'s death in 1798, Connie remarried to **William Richardson**.

William Jr. and Connie's son **Thomas B. Cox** (May 15, 1792 - June 12, 1857) married Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson and had 12 children, including Rebecca Adeline Cox (1813-1894) and Thomas Manning Cox (1828-1897). Through Thomas Manning Cox's daughter Roxanne Cox (1865-1912), who married William Goldsmith Long, this line connects to the Bright, Kascher, and Salmanson families.

William Cox Jr. died in 1798, predeceasing his father William Cox Sr. by 16 years. William Cox Sr.'s estate was administered after his November 1814 death; a January 4, 1817 sale of 155 acres involved Abraham Cox, Mary Cox, Isaac Cox, Thomas Long, and George Croft.

**WARNING:** Many databases (Ancestry, FamilySearch, Geni.com) incorrectly list William Richardson Cox (1692-1767, Quaker from Delaware) as the father of William Cox Sr. The Cox Family History Project and WikiTree Cox-14022 confirm this is an **erroneous merger**. William Sr. was NOT a Quaker and NOT from Delaware. DNA testing (Y-37, FamilyTreeDNA) places this line in haplogroup R1b-I "Carolina Coxes" subgroup (R-M269), consistent with British Isles origin but NOT matching the Quaker/Delaware Cox line.
