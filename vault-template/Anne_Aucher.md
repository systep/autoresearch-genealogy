## Biography

Anne Aucher was the daughter and heiress of Henry Aucher, Esquire, of Lossenham in Newenden, Kent, and Elizabeth Guildford. She was described as "daughter and heir of Harry Aucher of Lossenham" (WikiTree Aucher-1, Tier 3).

Anne married **Walter Culpepper**, Under-Marshal of Calais, c. 1480 at Bedgebury (WikiTree Culpepper-600, Tier 3). They had five children: three sons and two daughters. Their youngest son [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]] (c. 1509 to 1559) became the ancestor of most living Culpeppers worldwide.

Walter died before June 24, 1515, and was buried in Calais. Anne survived him by many years. In her will, she requested to be buried beside her father in England, not beside her husband in Calais (WikiTree Aucher-1, Tier 3). She died after September 4, 1532.

## The Aucher Family of Kent

The Aucher family claimed descent from **Aucherus, the Saxon**, described as "a man of great note at Newenden, before the Conquest" (Landed Families of Britain and Ireland, Tier 2). This pre-Conquest origin is traditional and unverifiable.

The documented pedigree begins with **Thomas Fitzaunger** (also Fitz-Aucher, Alcher), who possessed the manor of Lossenham in Newenden in the reign of King John (1199 to 1216) and founded the **Carmelite Priory at Lossenham** c. 1241 to 1243, the third Carmelite house established in England (Kent Archaeological Society, Tier 2; British History Online, Tier 1/2). Thomas was buried in the choir of the Carmelite chapel when he died c. 1260.

The family held Lossenham continuously until it passed by marriage to the Culpepers in the reign of Henry VII, through Anne's marriage to Walter.

**Anne's father Henry Aucher** (c. 1445 to 1494) was the son of Thomas Aucher of Lossenham. Thomas married Isabella (or Alicia) Boleyn (WikiTree, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3). If this Boleyn connection is accurate, the Auchers were connected to the Boleyn family of Norfolk/Kent, the same family that produced Anne Boleyn, Queen of England. However, recent scholarship on soc.genealogy.medieval has called into question some of the Aucher pedigree connections, including certain marriages attributed to earlier generations (Google Groups discussion, Tier 3).

Henry married **Elizabeth Guildford**, daughter of Sir John Guildford, Knight, of Halden (WikiTree Aucher-2, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3). The Guildford family was a prominent Kentish family closely connected to the Tudor court.

**Anne's cousin Anthony Aucher** (d. 1558) ended his career as Marshal of Calais (the same fortress where Walter had served as Under-Marshal), and died from wounds received when the French captured the town in January 1558 (WikiTree Aucher-21, Tier 3).

## Connection to This Family Tree

Anne Aucher is the mother of [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]] and the wife of [[Walter_Colepeper_Calais]]. The line continues: William to [[Martin_Culpepper]] to Mary/Mercy to [[Samuel_Sandys_MP]] and the Sandys/Sands descendants.

The Aucher family also connects to this tree through [[Hester_Aucher]], who married [[Richard_Sandys]] in a later generation. Two Aucher women thus married into the direct ancestral line at different points.
