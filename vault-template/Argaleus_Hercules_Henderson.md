## Biography

Argaleus Hercules Henderson I was born circa 1720-1727 in Onslow County, North Carolina, the son of **James Isaac Henderson Jr.** and **Mary Rhodes** (~1700, possibly from Scotland per some trees). James Isaac Henderson Jr. was born after 1705 in Somerset County, Maryland (not Scotland as some trees claim), and settled in Onslow County, NC. His father was James "the Mariner" Henderson (c. 1689-1752, Somerset County, Maryland), and his grandfather was William "the Sawyer" Henderson (b. before 1668, likely Accomack or Northampton County, Virginia), who married Sarah Bishop in 1685 in Somerset County, Maryland.

**Name origin:** "Argaleus" derives from Sir Philip Sidney's romance *The Countess of Pembroke's Arcadia* (1590), popularized through Francis Quarles' verse poem *Argalus and Parthenia* (1629, which went through 30+ editions by 1730). A "Parthenia Merrill" married a Charles Henderson on the Eastern Shore, showing the name circulated in the Henderson family's social world.

Argaleus was a **Quaker**, affiliated with the Cane Creek Meeting. However, he was **disowned** by the Meeting for his participation in the **North Carolina Regulators movement** (1768-1771), a populist uprising against colonial officials in the western counties. He signed **Regulator Advertisement No. 9** in May 1768.

He married **Molly Mary Clark** (1722-1804).

**Property:** Argaleus owned land on the south side of the Haw River. In 1778, he acquired 100 acres at the mouth of Berry Branch in Chatham County (Chatham County Deed Book B, p. 128).

His will was dated **September 6, 1804**, Chatham County, North Carolina. Subsequently, on **November 9, 1804**, Argaleus executed a deed to his son **John Henderson**, conveying the house and plantation where John resided, described as being on the east side of Meadow Branch on the north side of Brookses Creek, estimated at 150 acres. The deed was proved in open court at Chatham County November Sessions 1804 by the oath of John Hackney Sr., a subscribing witness. This suggests Argaleus may have left John out of his original will and used this deed to provide for him separately.

The **estate inventory of Argaleus Henderson** was completed in 1806, with seven images of the probate contents available for review (Chatham County probate records).

His son **Ezekiel Henderson** (1763-1846) served in the Revolutionary War (private, 1781-1783, cavalry and infantry, pension S6994) and moved to Greenville County, South Carolina after the war. Ezekiel married Elizabeth Brasher (October 10, 1782) and had 15 children, including Brashier Henderson (1790-1847, father of Rev. Henry L. Henderson) and Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson (1795-1876, who married Thomas B. Cox). Through these two children and their descendants' first cousin marriage, the Henderson line connects to the Cox, Long, Bright, Kascher, and Salmanson families.

**NOTE on James Isaac Henderson Jr.'s origin:** The claim that James arrived as a convict on the ship "Friendship" in 1716 is NOT SUPPORTED. The Friendship of Belfast carried 80 Jacobite prisoners to Maryland on August 20, 1716, but no James Henderson appears on any of the 10 Jacobite transport ships' passenger lists. The Henderson family was already established in Somerset County, MD by the 1680s. The "S.S. Friendship" designation is anachronistic (no steamships in 1716). This claim likely originated from user-contributed trees, not primary sources (Strong Signal for negative result).

**UPGRADE 2026-04-19: Upstream Henderson line to Tier 1.** The progenitor [[James_Henderson_Sr_1632_VA]] (c.1632-bef.1692) is now supported by three independent Tier 1 primary records: (1) Hungars Parish Northampton Co VA baptism of son John, 5 May 1661; (2) 400-acre Pocomoke River land patent, Accomack Co VA, 17 Nov 1665, abstracted in Nugent, *Cavaliers and Pioneers* Vol. I; (3) signer of the 28 Nov 1689 Somerset County MD Address of Loyalty to William and Mary (Archives of Maryland Vol. 106). The VA/MD Eastern Shore leg of the lineage (James Sr. to William "the Sawyer" to James "the Mariner" to James Isaac Jr. to Argaleus) is now **Strong Signal** for the 1661-1752 timeframe. The pre-1661 Scottish/Ulster origin remains **Moderate Signal** pending a matching Hearth Money Roll, muster roll, or Laggan Presbytery session entry. ### Standing Springs Baptist Church (founded 1818) — Charter member context

The Standing Springs Baptist Church historical marker (hmdb.org marker #168369) and the Simpsonville Sentinel 200-year-anniversary article (2018) record that the church was organized in 1818 as an arm of Fork Shoals Baptist Church, with **10 charter members: "Arguless, Elizabeth and Rachel Henderson, William Mayhall, Rhonda Moore, Thomas, Delila and James Richardson and Christopher and Hannah Robbins"**. "Arguless" is an obvious orthographic variant of Argaleus / Argalus. This individual (b. ~1720, d. 1804-1806 Chatham Co NC) **was deceased by 1818** and therefore cannot be the charter member; the charter "Arguless Henderson" is most plausibly **Argaleus Hercules Henderson II (b. June 22, 1784)**, son of Ezekiel and grandson of this person, who had moved to Greenville County by the 1810s. "Elizabeth Henderson" is plausibly **Elizabeth Brasher Henderson** (Ezekiel's wife). "Rachel Henderson" is **identified as round-2 prompt-08 finding 2026-05-02** as **Rachel Jane Hughes Henderson (b. 1786)**, wife of Argaleus Hercules Henderson II (married 1811 Greenville Co SC), per familytreesite.org Henderson page (Tier 3) corroborated by other Tier 3 user-tree snippets. The 1818 charter trio "Arguless, Elizabeth and Rachel Henderson" thus reads as Argaleus II (charter), his mother Elizabeth Brasher Henderson (charter), and his wife Rachel Jane Hughes Henderson (charter) — a husband-mother-wife triplet. Confidence: Moderate Signal (single Tier 3 source, but internally consistent and uncontradicted; Tier 1 corroboration would be the 1811 Greenville marriage record OR the original Standing Springs minute book). This corroborates the family's continued Baptist (post-Quaker-disownment) affiliation across the NC-SC migration. Tier 2 (historical marker text + Simpsonville Sentinel 2018 secondary article); Tier 1 primary source would be the original 1818 Fork Shoals Baptist church book / Standing Springs minute book on Furman University microfilm. Source: hmdb.org/m.asp?m=168369 (agentic fetch 403; web-search snippet retrieved 2026-05-02). See also [[James_Cox]] (land donor) for cluster-cluster intermarriage context. **Research gap RESOLVED 2026-05-02 round 2**: "Rachel Henderson" = Rachel Jane Hughes Henderson, wife of Argaleus II (familytreesite.org Henderson page, Tier 3, corroborated by Tier 3 user trees).

## Scots-Irish Origin (Upstream)

See [[Henderson_Scots_Irish_Origin_Research_2026_04_19]] for the working hypothesis on the pre-colonial origin of this Henderson line. Summary (Moderate Signal): the upstream ancestor **James Henderson Sr. (c.1632-bef.1692)** of Hungars Parish, Northampton Co VA (by May 5, 1661) and later Somerset Co MD (1666 Pocomoke patent) signed the 1689 Somerset County Grand Jury "Advice of Loyalty" alongside Ulster-Scot Presbyterian neighbors from the Laggan (eastern Donegal, Lifford area) who had followed Francis Makemie and William Trail to Somerset in 1683. Published research notes a cluster of Hendersons around **Perth, Scotland** in 16th/17th century parish records as probable ancestors. Proposed migration path: Scotland (Perth/Fife) -> Ulster (probably Donegal/Laggan) -> Virginia Eastern Shore (by 1661) -> Somerset Co MD (by 1666) -> Onslow NC (c.1720) -> Chatham NC -> SC. Not yet corroborated by a primary parish or hearth-money record; see the hypothesis file for specific next steps.
