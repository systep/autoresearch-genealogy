# Hypothesis: Arolsen Archives holds Holocaust-era records for Salmanson/Zalmanson relatives

## Statement

The Arolsen Archives (formerly ITS, Bad Arolsen) likely hold records (concentration camp, ghetto, forced labor, DP registration, or ITS correspondence) for relatives of the vault's Salmanson/Zalmanson family line, given the line's documented origin in the Russian Empire (Lithuania and adjacent regions) and the scale of Holocaust persecution there. However, a constrained external (Google site-scoped) sweep on 2026-04-21 did not surface any direct, high-confidence hits, and further work requires the internal Arolsen search UI.

## Search Scope and Method

- Environment: WebSearch only (no WebFetch), 13 queries used of a 15-query budget.
- Target: collections.arolsen-archives.org and secondary Arolsen index references (USHMM ITS collection, Yad Vashem, EHRI).
- Variants tested: Salmanson, Zalmanson, Salmansohn, Zalmansohn, Solmonson, Solomonson, Zelmanzon, Zelmanson, Shlomovitz, Shlomovich, Zalmanoff, Zalmanov, Zalman (root), Kushner (affine surname).

## Analysis

The Google-scoped sweep has well-known limits: the Arolsen collections UI is JavaScript-heavy and its internal person search is not fully indexed by Google. Absence of site-scoped Google results is weak negative evidence only. Arolsen's own UI offers fuzzy and phonetic matching that this sweep could not invoke. The alphabetic range hit (San-Wol, Zal-Zuc, 643093601) is the single most promising next step for a direct drill-down. Off-Arolsen findings (Yad Vashem Rivkah Zalmanson of Latvia; EHRI Rivke Shlomovitz of Prienai) are plausible leads but cannot be wired into the vault without dates and places matching primary records.

Overall confidence: open. The sweep is not exhaustive; this hypothesis should remain open pending a direct Arolsen portal search and an Arolsen inquiry submission.
