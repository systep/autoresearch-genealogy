# Peter Jungblut (Peter Youngblood Sr.)

Immigrant progenitor of the Southern US Youngblood cluster (SC/GA Edgefield line). Palatine German, naturalized in Prince George's County, Maryland, on 6 March 1738/9 together with sons William and Peter and daughters Sarah and Mary. This is the corrected jurisdiction: the naturalization is a **Maryland** Provincial Council commission, not a Pennsylvania court record.

## Core Facts

- **Name variants**: Jungblut, Jungbluth, Jongbloet (German); Youngblood (anglicized; attested from 1739 naturalization).
- **Born**: Palatinate, Germany, circa 1700. Specific village unknown (see `Jungblut_Peter_1739_PA_Naturalization_Research_2026_04_19.md` for candidate regions).
- **Arrival**: Prior to 1728 (reported in "disputed territory" in that year). Port of entry not yet identified; Annapolis, Maryland, and Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, are both candidates. No Strassburger/Hinke passenger-list match yet.
- **First marriage**: To a woman whose name is unknown; she was the mother of William, Peter (Jr.), Sarah, and Mary. She was deceased before 6 March 1738/9 (not named in naturalization).
- **Second marriage**: Mary Wheals, 26 November 1750, Harford County, Maryland.
- **Naturalization**: 6 March 1738/9 (Old Style), Commission Book #82, Council of Maryland, Prince George's County. Declared birth in Germany.
- **Occupation**: Surveyor (secondary source, Maryland records).
- **Personal records**: Kept in German (secondary source).
- **Departure from Maryland**: Youngblood records thin in Maryland circa 1745; Peter Sr., Peter Jr., John, Thomas, Henry Youngblood appear in Orange and Johnston Counties, North Carolina, shortly after.
- **Death**: Circa 1770. Exact date and place unverified.
