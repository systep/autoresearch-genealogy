## Biography

Johann Kascher was born 28 October 1900 in Waldhütten, Transylvania, the fifth child of **Michael Kascher Sr.** and **Anna Binder**. He was the fourth surviving child (after Michael, Georg, and Friedrich, though Friedrich would die in 1903).

His father's 1935 obituary in the Youngstown Vindicator lists "John Kascher" among the surviving sons. The VATER church certificate was addressed to "Johann Kascher, wohnhaft 21 University Place, New York, U.S.A." (resident at 21 University Place, New York), indicating Johann was the family member who requested the genealogical certificates from Waldhütten in 1938.

The fact that Johann was the one requesting ancestral documentation from New York in 1938, during the Nazi era, suggests awareness of the need to document Aryan ancestry (required under the Nuremberg Laws for Germans abroad). The certificates explicitly note "Alle Obgenannten sind ev. A.B. und Deutscher Abstammung" (All named persons are Evangelical A.B. and of German descent).
