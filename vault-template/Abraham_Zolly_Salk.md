## Biography

Abraham (Charles) Zolly Salk was born about 1860 in Russia (likely the Russian Empire, which included present day Lithuania). His father was **Louis (Elizar)(Lazar) Salk** (b. ABT 1840, Lithuania; d. before 1910, Providence, Rhode Island). Louis also married Rose (surname unknown, born and died in Lithuania) and had a second wife, Deena (Hessel), with whom he had daughter Sarah Salk (b. June 1869, Lithuania). Abraham's exact relationship to the multiple families of Louis Salk requires further clarification.

Abraham immigrated to the United States; two arrival dates appear in the records: **1894** (age 33-34) and **1896** (age 35-36). The discrepancy may reflect separate immigration events (a preliminary trip followed by permanent settlement) or conflicting census declarations.

He married **Tauba**, who is recorded under a remarkable array of name variants: Toby, Tobe, Teibe, Tobrey, Theresa, Fannie, and Pheabe. Tauba was born about 1850 in Russia and immigrated separately, with arrival dates of either 1898 or 1901 listed in the records. On the **1910 and 1920 Census**, the family lived in Providence Ward 3, Rhode Island. Abraham was listed as head of household; both his and Tauba's birthplaces were recorded as Russia (which could encompass the Russian Empire, including Lithuania and parts of Poland).

Abraham and Tauba had at least three documented children:
- **Elizabeth Salk** (~1887), who married [[Barnett_Salmanson]] on September 12, 1909 in Providence, RI
- **Anna Salk** (b. July 22, 1893, Rhode Island; d. January 26, 1958, Fulton, Georgia; m. Samuel Zipperman)
- **Marry Salk** (m. Lyons)

### Surname Analysis

The surname "Salk" is an altered form of **Zalk**, a Jewish name from Lithuania, most likely a pet form of the Yiddish personal name **Zalkind** (Ancestry.com, Geneanet). Zalkind is the German Yiddish kinui (nickname) for the Hebrew name **Shlomo (Solomon)**. The middle name "Zolly" is itself a diminutive of Zalman/Solomon, reinforcing this etymology. The Zalk/Salk surname has documented Lithuanian Jewish origins, with a notable Zalk family from **Troskunai (Trashkun), Lithuania** documented on JewishGen KehilaLinks.

### Other Salk Families in Providence

Research (2026-04-13) identified at least two other distinct Salk families in Providence, Rhode Island during the same period:

1. **Hyman Salk** (immigrant, purchased tailor shop in Providence 1900, founder of Salk's Hardware). Sons: Sam Salk (expanded store to Oakland Beach, Warwick, 1917) and Lewis/Louis Salk (ran store in Conimicut). Sam's son Harold Salk (1932-2024) was 3rd generation; Harold's son Jeffrey Salk is 4th generation (current). Harold's parents were Samuel and Ruth (Fleisig) Salk. Louis's son Norton E. Salk (1927-2007, architect) married Belle (Feingold).

2. **Harvey M. Salk** (1921-1997), son of unknown parents, buried at Sons of David and Israel Cemetery, Providence. Siblings: Rose Grossman, Morton Salk, Albert Salk.

3. **Ida Salk** (1881-1982, lived to 101) and **Belle B. Salk** (1902-2003, lived to 101) of Providence: relationship to Abraham's line unknown.

Whether Abraham Salk was related to Hyman Salk (both Lithuanian Jewish immigrants to Providence in the 1890s 1900s) is a significant open question. Both families arrived in Providence from the Russian Empire around the same time. The Salk/Zalk surname's Lithuanian origin makes a common ancestral connection plausible, but this remains unverified.

### Jonas Salk Connection

Jonas Salk (1914-1995), developer of the polio vaccine, was the son of **Daniel Salk** (born in New Jersey to immigrant parents) and **Dora Press** (born in Minsk, Russia). Jonas's parents were Ashkenazi Jewish from the Russian Empire, like Abraham. However, Jonas's family was based in **New York City**, not Providence. No direct connection to Abraham Salk's line has been established. The shared Lithuanian Jewish surname suggests possible distant kinship, but this is speculative without genealogical documentation.

Abraham died February 22, 1925 in Rhode Island at the age of approximately 64-65. His burial location has not been confirmed, but **Lincoln Park Cemetery** in Warwick, RI is the most likely site, as multiple Salmanson family members (including his daughter Elizabeth, son in law Leonard Irving, and daughter in law Thelma) are buried there. No Find a Grave or BillionGraves record for Abraham has been located (searched 2026-04-13, 2026-04-14). The cemetery office (401-737-5333) may have records.

Through his daughter Elizabeth's marriage to Barnett Salmanson, Abraham is the maternal grandfather of the Providence Salmanson family, including [[Leonard_Irving_Salmanson]], [[Samuel_Salmanson]], [[Charles_Salmanson]], [[Martha_Salmanson_Corin]], and [[Donald_Salmanson]]. The Salmanson children founded the **Adams Drug Company** in 1932, which grew to 500+ stores across 14 states and became Brooks Pharmacy (now Rite Aid).

### Hebrew Name Question: Abraham vs. Moshe

Elizabeth (Salk) Salmanson's headstone at Lincoln Park Cemetery (BillionGraves, Tier 1) contains a Hebrew inscription that includes the text: **"הילדה בת משה"**. This appears to read **"Hilda bat Moshe"**, meaning "Hilda, daughter of Moshe (Moses)." If this parsing is correct, it would indicate that Elizabeth's Hebrew name was **Hilda** and her father's Hebrew name was **Moshe**, not Abraham/Avraham.

This creates a significant discrepancy with the GEDCOM, which lists her father as "Abraham (Charles) Zolly Salk." There are several possible explanations:

1. **Abraham's Hebrew name was actually Moshe**, and "Abraham" was his English/secular name (not derived from the Hebrew Avraham). This would be unusual but not unprecedented in Ashkenazi naming; however, Abraham and Moshe are completely different Hebrew names with no etymological connection.

2. **Abraham had a double Hebrew name** such as "Moshe Avraham" or "Avraham Moshe," and only "Moshe" was recorded on the headstone. Double Hebrew names were common in Eastern European Jewish communities.

3. **"Abraham" in the GEDCOM is incorrect**, and the father's actual name was Moshe/Moses, with "Abraham" being a transcription or memory error in the family tree.

4. **"Moshe" in the headstone refers to a different ancestor**, not Elizabeth's father. The full Hebrew inscription is complex and contains a chain of names with patronymics. Further professional parsing is needed.

The full Hebrew inscription from Elizabeth's headstone reads:
> הילדה בת משה שמואל בן דב בער זאוועל בן ר דב בערר ליבה בת ר אברהם ר דוב בער בר לוי יצחק סאלמאנסאן

**Preliminary parsing** (requires verification by a Hebrew epigraphy specialist):

The inscription appears to contain **two lineage chains**, one for Elizabeth and one for her husband Barnett Salmanson. A plausible reading:

- **Elizabeth's lineage**: "Hilda bat Moshe" (Hilda, daughter of Moshe) or possibly "Hilda bat Moshe Shmuel" if Moshe Shmuel is a double name for her father.
- **Barnett's lineage**: "Shmuel ben Dov Ber, Zavel ben R' Dov Ber" (this chain traces through Barnett's ancestors: Shmuel [possibly Srul Mendel] son of Dov Ber [Zawel], son of R' [Mr.] Dov Ber).
- **"Liba bat R' Avraham"**: This appears to be a **woman's name** (Liba/Libah, daughter of R' Avraham). Liba may be Barnett's mother or grandmother, not Elizabeth's father.
- **"R' Dov Ber bar Levi Yitzchak Salmanson"**: This traces back through the Salmanson/Zalmanson patriarchal line, consistent with the known Zalmanson genealogy (Levi Itzhak Zalmanson married in Rokiskis in 1877).

If this parsing is correct, then **"Avraham" in the inscription refers to a Zalmanson/Salmanson ancestor** (possibly Liba's father), not to Elizabeth's father at all. Elizabeth's father's Hebrew name would be **Moshe**. This would mean "Abraham Salk" in the GEDCOM may be based on a misattribution of the name Avraham from the headstone's Salmanson lineage to the Salk lineage.

**Resolution**: This is a critical discrepancy. The headstone is a Tier 1 primary source. If "Moshe" is confirmed as Elizabeth's father's Hebrew name, the father's entry should be updated to reflect "Moshe (Moses) Salk" as the primary Hebrew name, with "Abraham" noted as possibly incorrect or as a secondary name. The death record from Rhode Island (1925) would provide independent confirmation of the father's name.

### Distinction from Other Providence Salk Families

Research (2026-04-14) has further clarified the Salk family landscape in Providence:

- **Hyman Salk line** (hardware store founder, ~1900): Son **Sam Salk** (m. Ruth Fleisig), grandson **Harold Salk** (1932-2024, 3rd gen owner). Son **Louis/Lewis Salk** (m. Belle Feingold), grandson **Norton E. Salk** (1927-2007, architect). Norton's siblings: Donald Salk, Judith Benharris, Harriet Simon. Norton was born in Providence, son of the late Louis and Belle (Feingold) Salk. Harold was born and raised in Providence, son of the late Samuel and Ruth (Fleisig) Salk.

- **Harvey M. Salk** (1921-1997): Buried at Sons of David and Israel Cemetery (a different cemetery from Lincoln Park). Family includes Rose Grossman, Morton Salk, Albert Salk (siblings per existing data).

Abraham Salk's family is associated with **Lincoln Park Cemetery**, while Harvey M. Salk is buried at **Sons of David and Israel Cemetery**. This geographic separation within the Jewish cemetery system of Providence suggests distinct family/congregational affiliations but does not prove or disprove a kinship connection.
