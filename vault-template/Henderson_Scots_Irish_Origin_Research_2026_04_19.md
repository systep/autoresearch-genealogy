# Henderson Scots-Irish Origin Research (2026-04-19)

## Goal

Identify the pre-colonial Ulster and/or Scottish origin of the Henderson line leading to [[Ezekiel_Henderson]] and [[Argaleus_Hercules_Henderson]] of NC.

## Current Lineage (from vault)

```
William "the Sawyer" Henderson (b. bef. 1668, VA/MD Eastern Shore) + Sarah Bishop (m. 1685, Somerset MD)
  |
James "the Mariner" Henderson (c.1689-1752, Somerset Co MD)
  |
James Isaac Henderson Jr. (b. aft.1705, Somerset Co MD) + Mary Rhodes (~1700, possibly Scotland)
  |
Argaleus Hercules Henderson I (~1720-1806, Onslow then Chatham NC) + Molly Mary Clark
  |
Ezekiel Henderson (1763-1846, NC then Greenville SC)
```

The key upstream figure is **James Henderson Sr. (c.1632-bef.1692)** of Hungars Parish, Northampton Co VA and later Somerset Co MD, father of William "the Sawyer".

## Findings

### 1. Somerset County MD as an Ulster-Scot receiving colony (Tier 2, supported)

Multiple independent secondary sources confirm:

- Francis Makemie and William Trail, Presbyterian ministers from the Laggan (eastern Donegal, especially Lifford), arrived in Somerset County, Maryland in 1683 and founded the first Presbyterian churches in America.
- The 1689 Somerset County Grand Jury "Advice of Loyalty" to William and Mary carried 235 signers, the majority Presbyterian and heavily Ulster-Scot (Knox, Alexander, and others from Laggan/Lifford).
- **James Henderson was among the signers.** (WikiTree Henderson-1383 narrative; Clan Pollock "From Lifford to America" series; discoverulsterscots.com Ulster-Virginia migration page; confirmed by two independent web summaries.)

### 2. James Henderson Sr. of Somerset, primary source trail (Tier 1 CONFIRMED 2026-04-19)

- **May 5, 1661**: James and wife Mary baptize son John at Hungars Parish, Northampton Co VA. (Some sources give 12 May 1661; original Hungars Parish register entry.) This is the earliest documentary record. CONFIRMED.
- **November 17, 1665**: James patents 400 acres on the south side of the Pocomoke River, bounded east by land surveyed for **Thomas Davis**, in return for transporting 8 persons into the county (headrights). The land was in Accomack Co VA at patent; the 1668 VA/MD border resolution placed it in Somerset Co MD. Abstracted in Nugent, *Cavaliers and Pioneers* Vol. I. CONFIRMED as Tier 1.
- **August 16, 1667**: Second patent, 150 acres in Accomack Co VA, headrights Michael Towes, Joseph Miller, Davy Glascock. CONFIRMED as Tier 1.
- **November 28, 1689**: Signs Somerset County Address of Loyalty to William and Mary. Transcription at MDGenWeb, underlying primary at Archives of Maryland Vol. 106 (Somerset County Judicial Records 1689-1690). CONFIRMED as Tier 1. (Note: the document is properly titled "Address of Loyalty", not "Grand Jury Advice of Loyalty" as earlier session notes recorded; correction propagated.)
- **bef. August 9, 1692**: Dies in Somerset Co MD.

This timeline means James was physically in Virginia by 1661, so he did not arrive with the Makemie 1680s wave. He was either an earlier Scottish/Ulster emigrant who predated the organized Laggan migration, or an English-extraction settler who assimilated into the Presbyterian Ulster-Scot community when it arrived.

**Related headright**: Gilbert Henderson appears as a headright of Thomas Johnson Jr. in Northampton Co VA, May 1652 (Nugent, *Cavaliers and Pioneers*). Possible brother or close kin of James; supports a slightly earlier Henderson presence on the Eastern Shore than James's own 1661 record.

### 3. Scottish origin cluster (Tier 2, speculative)

- WikiTree Henderson-1383 notes: "A study of names and birth dates in 16th and 17th century Scottish commissariot records and parish registers indicate a cluster of Hendersons living around **Perth** who appear to be strong candidates as possible ancestors or relatives of James and Gilbert."
- Clan Henderson Society places the principal Lowland Henderson seat at **Fordell, County Fife** (patent 1511), with secondary concentrations in Caithness, Glencoe, and Shetland.
- The McCue "Henderson Chronicles" (Internet Archive full text available) documents **Alexander Henderson of Fordell, Fife**, three of whose sons emigrated to the American colonies before 1740 and settled near Alexandria, VA. This is a DIFFERENT Henderson line (not our Somerset line) but confirms direct Fife-to-Virginia migration patterns.
- Clan Henderson migration pattern: Shetland and Lowland Hendersons moved to Liddesdale, then to Ulster during the plantation (1610 onward), settling first in **Donegal**, then **Tyrone** and **Antrim**.

### 4. Ulster Historical Foundation / PRONI (Tier 1 databases, not free-text accessible)

- ancestryireland.com (UHF) indexes found: Donegal Town Merchants 1846, Cookstown Tyrone Merchants 1846, Irish Will Calendars 1878-1900. None of these are useful for a pre-1661 James Henderson.
- Relevant free UHF/PRONI databases for 17th-century Henderson tracing would be: 1630 Muster Rolls of Ulster, 1660s Hearth Money Rolls (Donegal, Tyrone, Antrim), Presbyterian session records from Laggan Presbytery (Convoy, Raphoe, Taughboyne). None were directly queryable via web search; most require in-person or subscription access.

### 5. Mary Rhodes "Scotland" claim (Tier 3, not verified)

Several user-submitted trees state Argaleus's mother Mary Rhodes (~1700) was born in Scotland. This is a tertiary claim with no cited primary source. Rhodes is an English surname more common in the Chesapeake. Treat as speculative until a church or land record surfaces.

## Best Hypothesis

**Primary hypothesis (Moderate Signal):** The Henderson line reaching Argaleus Hercules Henderson of NC descends from a **Lowland Scottish Henderson stock (probable Fife/Perth cluster) who passed through Ulster (probably eastern Donegal / Laggan area) during the early-to-mid 17th century, with James Henderson Sr. emigrating to the Virginia Eastern Shore by c.1660**, ahead of the larger Makemie-era Ulster-Scot wave but integrating with it when that community took root in Somerset Co MD in the 1680s.

**Migration path:** Scotland (Perth/Fife) -> Ulster (Donegal, possibly Lifford/Laggan) -> Virginia Eastern Shore (Northampton/Accomack) by 1661 -> Somerset Co MD by 1666 -> Onslow Co NC by c.1720 -> Chatham Co NC by 1778 -> Greenville Co SC post-1783.

**Confidence on the VA/MD colonial leg: Strong Signal** (upgraded 2026-04-19). The 1661 Hungars Parish baptism, the 1665 Pocomoke patent, the 1667 Accomack patent, and the 1689 Address of Loyalty signature are all Tier 1 primary records, independently corroborated, consistent in name, dates, and place.

**Confidence on the Scottish/Ulster origin leg: Moderate Signal.** Supported by the Makemie-Laggan context and the Perth Henderson cluster hypothesis, but no primary document names James Henderson Sr.'s specific Scottish or Ulster parish. Needs a matching Hearth Money Roll, muster roll, or session minute entry to reach Strong.

**Alternative hypothesis (Speculative):** James Henderson Sr. was an English (not Ulster-Scot) settler who arrived via Virginia tobacco migration and merely signed the 1689 Advice alongside Ulster-Scot neighbors. Arguments against: given name patterns (James, John, William) fit Scottish practice; Presbyterian association by 1689.

## Parallel Consideration: Richard Henderson

Richard Henderson (1735-1785), founder of the Transylvania Company and born in Hanover Co VA, is a prominent NC Henderson of the same era as Argaleus. He moved to Granville Co NC in 1762. No documented relationship to Argaleus has been found, but both families were in NC by the 1760s, and Hanover Co VA is a known secondary Ulster-Scot settlement point. If Y-DNA testing is pursued, compare to Richard Henderson's documented line (Transylvania Henderson DNA group).
