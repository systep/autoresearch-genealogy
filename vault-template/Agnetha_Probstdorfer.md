## Biography

Agnetha Probstdorfer was born on 12 February 1847. The Probstdorf village has been identified in the Sibiu district of Transylvania (now Romania), consistent with the family's Transylvanian Saxon heritage.

She was the daughter of Johann Probstdorfer (1821 to 1884) and Maria Ranft. Note: Maria Ranft's birth year of 1882 in the GEDCOM appears to be a data error, as she would need to predate her daughter's 1847 birth; this requires correction.

Agnetha married Mathias Falk (b. 3 August 1837, Petrești, Alba, Romania; d. 7 November 1892, Petrești). Together they had at least six children, including Mathias Falk (b. 1867), who later married [[Katharina_Pfaff]]; Marguarita (b. 1871), who married Stefan Pfaff; Simon Falk (b. 1873); Rosina Falk; Agneta Binder (born Falk); and a girl whose name is not recorded.

Agnetha died on 20 August 1888 in Petrești, Alba, Romania, at approximately age 41. Her husband Mathias Falk survived her by four years, dying in 1892.
