## Biography

James King (b. ~1787-1788, d. ~1867, South Carolina) was a son of [[Robert_King_II]] (c. 1750-1826), an Irish immigrant and Revolutionary War veteran who settled near Belton, Anderson County, SC. His mother was Tabitha Dolby (c. 1750-1815). He was one of 23 children (21 surviving).

James married **Mary Nancy Parker** (b. March 8, 1787; d. June 8, 1853), daughter of John Parker and Betty Parker of Anderson, SC (Geni, Tier 3).

He was a farmer in the Eastern Sub Division of Anderson County, South Carolina, with $3,000 in real estate (1850 Census, Family 398). In 1850, James (age 62) lived with his wife Mary (age 55) and daughter Elizabeth A. (age 19). His son [[George_Washington_King]] (age 35, farmer, Family 401) lived nearby. His brother Rev. Robert King III (age ~59, Baptist clergyman, Family 397) also lived on the same census page; Robert III's census age was recorded as 37, a significant enumeration error.

Known children: [[George_Washington_King]] (b. Dec 22, 1815), Sarah (Sallie) Ann King Fant, John King, Elizabeth Ann King Haynie (b. ~1831).

James King is [[Judson_King]]'s grandfather. The lineage is: Robert King II (Ireland) > James King Sr. > George Washington King > Judson King.
