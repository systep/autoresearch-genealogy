## Biography

James King (b. ~1788, South Carolina) was a farmer in the Eastern Sub Division of Anderson County, South Carolina, with $3,000 in real estate (1850 Census, Family 398). He appears on the same census page (page 54) as two other King households: Robert King (age 37, Baptist clergyman, Family 397) and [[George_Washington_King]] (age 35, farmer, Family 401), both likely his sons.

In 1850, James (age 62) lived with his wife Mary (age 55) and daughter Elizabeth A. (age 19). His older sons Robert and George W. King had established their own households nearby.

James King is the probable patriarch of the Greenville/Anderson County King family. His $3,000 real estate valuation indicates a substantial farm for the area.
