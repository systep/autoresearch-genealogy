# Hypothesis: Wert Family Pennsylvania German Origin

## Question

From where in the German-speaking lands did the vault's Wert family emigrate, and who is the immigrant ancestor?

## Summary of Vault Werts

Oldest vault Wert: Michael Wert (b. 10 May 1815, Harrisburg, Dauphin Co., Pennsylvania; d. 5 Jul 1893, Petersburg, Lincoln Co., Tennessee). All 10+ vault Wert person files descend from or relate to this Michael Wert line, based on geographic clustering in PA and TN. Source: MyHeritage GEDCOM (Tier 3, unverified).

Dauphin County, Pennsylvania is a well-documented Pennsylvania German settlement zone and was heavily populated by immigrants from the Palatinate, Wurttemberg, and Westerwald regions of southwest Germany.

## Best Hypothesis (Moderate Signal)

The vault's Wert family likely descends from **Johann Adam Wert (later Wirth) (1727-1806)** who:

- Was born in **Berod** in the **Westerwald region** of Germany (Nassau-Dillenburg area, near modern-day Bad Marienberg, Rhineland-Palatinate).
- Emigrated in 1753 aboard the **Ship Two Brothers**, Captain Thomas Arnot, from Rotterdam via Cowes, England.
- Arrived in Philadelphia on **28 September 1753**. Entered in Strassburger and Hinke, *Pennsylvania German Pioneers* (1727-1808), Vol. 1.
- Married **Eva Elizabeth Schnug** (1730-1800, also from Berod, arrived 1740) on 3 August 1755 at Bindnagel Evangelical Lutheran Church, Lebanon Co., PA, by Rev. John Caspar Stoever.
- Settled permanently in the **Lykens Valley, Upper Paxton Township, Dauphin County, Pennsylvania** in 1768, eventually owning 1,200 acres.
- Had 11 children, including a son Johannes (1758-1805) of Upper Paxton.
- Is buried at Wirth's Evangelical Lutheran Church Cemetery, Upper Paxton Township, Dauphin County.

Dauphin County's Upper Paxton Township matches the geographic center of the later Wert family presence in Harrisburg (Dauphin Co.), where Michael Wert was born in 1815.

## Alternative Hypothesis (Speculative)

Jonathan M. Wert (b. 23 Sep 1814, Upper Paxton, Dauphin Co.; published *The Wert family of Dauphin County, Pennsylvania* in 1996) cited his great-grandfather "John Wert (1)" as having been born and reared in **Wurttemberg, Germany**. This could indicate:

(a) An oral-tradition error or regional confusion (Wurttemberg vs Westerwald).
(b) A second, distinct Wert immigrant line separate from Johann Adam.
(c) A descendant of Johann Adam Wirth whose family later remembered a Wurttemberg origin due to intermarriage or generational memory drift.

Both Berod/Westerwald and Wurttemberg are in southwest German-speaking lands and are plausible Pennsylvania German source regions. The Westerwald lineage has stronger primary-source grounding (passenger list, church records, land records).

## Surname Variants Observed

- Wert
- Werth
- Wirt
- Wirth

Modern descendants in Dauphin Co. standardized on "Wert"; the original Palatine form was "Wirth." The church Johann Adam founded is Wirth's Evangelical Lutheran Church. Strassburger transcribes the 1753 signature as "Adam Wirth."

## Confidence: Moderate Signal

Reasoning: The Johann Adam Wert (Wirth) immigration is supported by (a) a Tier 1 passenger list (Strassburger/Hinke), (b) a Tier 2 published genealogy (Jonathan M. Wert, 1996), and (c) a Tier 2 cemetery/Find a Grave record. However, the chain from Johann Adam (1727-1806) down to Michael Wert (b. 1815) is not yet verified in the vault. Until that descent is documented with primary sources, the link to the vault is inferential based on geographic clustering (Dauphin Co. and Upper Paxton Township) and surname rarity.
