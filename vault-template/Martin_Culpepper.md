## Biography

Dr. Martin Culpepper (1540 to 1605) was an English physician, academic, and clergyman. He was educated at Winchester College (scholarship at age 13, 1554) and New College, Oxford, holding a fellowship from 1559 to 1567. He graduated B.A. (1562), M.A. (1566), B.Med. (1568), and D.Med. (1571) (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

He was elected Warden (head) of New College, Oxford, in 1573, serving until 1599. He also served as Vice-Chancellor of the University of Oxford in 1578 to 1579 (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Martin married Lettice Clarke on August 5, 1574, in Kingsnorth, Kent. They had at least two sons and two daughters (GEDCOM note, Tier 3).

He acquired the manor of Astwood in Feckenham, Worcestershire, in 1595. His will (dated October 1, 1605; proved December 12, 1605; P.C.C. Hayes 88) provides extensive detail about his properties and family, including: lands in Oxford, Worcester, Berks, and Sussex; references to "my son in law Sir Samuel Sandis of Ombersley" (i.e., [[Samuel_Sandys_MP]]); bequests to grandchildren including "Martin Sandys my godson" and "John Sandys my daur's youngest son"; provision for his late son Sir Martin Culpepper's daughters Lettice and Anne; and establishment of charitable giving to the parish of Feckenham (GEDCOM transcription of will, Tier 1).
