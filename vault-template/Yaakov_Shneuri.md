## Biography

Yaakov Shneuri, known in Chabad tradition as "Reb Yaakov," was the father-in-law of the Mitteler Rebbe, Rabbi Dovber Schneuri (1773-1827), the second Rebbe of the Chabad-Lubavitch Hasidic movement.

According to Chabad.org, at the age of 16 (c. 1789), Rabbi DovBer married Rebbetzin Shaina, "daughter of an impoverished teacher named Reb Yaakov." The Alter Rebbe (Shneur Zalman of Liadi, DovBer's father) reportedly encouraged the match, explaining that once Reb Yaakov's eldest would marry into their family, the other children would have no difficulty finding appropriate spouses (Chabad.org).

The surname "Shneuri" in the GEDCOM may be a later attribution from his son-in-law's family name. Dovber adopted the family name "Schneuri" after his father Shneur Zalman, and succeeding generations changed it to "Schneersohn" (Wikipedia: Dovber Schneuri). It is unclear whether Yaakov himself used this surname or whether it was applied retroactively in genealogical records.

The GEDCOM shows children including a daughter who married into the Shatz family and another into the Berlin family, but no further biographical detail is provided.
