## Biography

Rabbi Levi Yitzchak of Berditchev (1740-1809) was one of the most celebrated Hasidic masters and Jewish leaders of the 18th century. He was known as the "defense attorney" for the Jewish people, famous for interceding on behalf of Israel before God (Wikipedia; Chabad.org).

He was a primary disciple of the Maggid of Mezeritch (Dov Ber of Mezeritch), successor to the Baal Shem Tov, and also studied under Rabbi Shmelke of Nikolsburg (Wikipedia). He served as rabbi of Ryczywol, Zelechow, Pinsk, and finally Berdychiv (Berditchev), the city for which he is best known (Wikipedia).

His magnum opus, the Kedushat Levi, is a classic of Hasidic literature. It is a commentary on the Torah arranged by weekly portion and on Jewish holidays, expanding upon earlier Hasidic philosophy, Talmud, and Midrash. First published in 1798, it has been reprinted numerous times (Wikipedia).

The surname "Derbaremdiker" is derived from the Yiddish "der baremdiger" meaning "the merciful one," an epithet applied to him (Geni.com; GEDCOM). This epithet became a hereditary surname for his descendants.

He was the son of Rabbi Meir of Husakov and Sara Sosha (Chabad.org: "born to Rabbi Meir of Husakov and his wife Sara Sasha"). His father-in-law of Yosef Bonam is noted in the family tree through his daughter Luba Tirtsa (see [[Yosef_Bonam]]).
