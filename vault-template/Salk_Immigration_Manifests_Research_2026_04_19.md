# Hypothesis: Louis Elizar Salk and Abraham Zolly Salk originated in Kovno Gubernia, Lithuania

## Statement

Louis (Elizar) Salk (b. c. 1840) and his son Abraham (Zolly) Salk (b. c. 1860, imm. 1894 to 1896) originated in **Kovno Gubernia, Lithuania** (Russian Empire), most likely in the Ukmerge (Vilkomir) Uyezd or an adjacent district. The specific town has not been identified, but the family's settlement on Chalkstone Avenue in Providence, in the center of the Kovno-immigrant cluster, strongly suggests Kovno Gubernia origin rather than Vilna Gubernia, Poland proper, or Galicia.

## Evidence For

1. **Geographic concentration of Kovno-Gubernia Jews on Chalkstone Avenue, Providence** (Rhode Tour "Beth David-Anshei Kovno Congregation," Tier 2): Anshei Kovno Congregation was founded c. 1900 specifically by immigrants from Kovno, Lithuania. It was located on Chalkstone Boulevard, the same street where Abraham Salk resided (60 Chalkstone Avenue per his 1925 death certificate, Tier 1).

2. **Occupational match**: Abraham was a junk peddler (death cert, Tier 1). Chalkstone Avenue had 46 peddlers and 7 junk businesses clustered between numbers 38 and 313 (Rhode Island Jewish Historical Notes, Tier 2). This is the Kovno immigrant economic profile.

3. **Neighborhood-origin mapping** (Jews in Rhode Island, Geraldine Foster 1985, Tier 2): Lithuanian, Polish, and Byelorussian Jews settled in the **North End / Smith Hill**, while Galician, Romanian, and Ukrainian Jews settled in South Providence. Abraham's Chalkstone address is in the Lithuanian cluster.

4. **Surname etymology**: Salk derives from Zalk, a Lithuanian Jewish name (Ancestry, Geneanet, Tier 2). A Zalk family is documented in Troskunai, Kovno Gubernia, Ukmerge Uyezd (JewishGen KehilaLinks, Tier 2).

5. **Hebrew naming**: The middle name Zolly is a diminutive of Zalman/Solomon, consistent with Lithuanian Yiddish usage.

## Evidence Against

1. **Troskunai Zalk branch went to Duluth, MN** in 1905, not Providence (JewishGen KehilaLinks). This rules out the specific documented Troskunai line as Abraham's direct family, although a collateral Zalk branch from the same or nearby Kovno-Gubernia town remains plausible.

2. **1930 Census for Elizabeth Salk Salmanson** recorded her parents' birthplace as Poland, not Russia. This conflicts with the death certificate's Russia. Possible explanations: Kovno Gubernia was sometimes reported as Poland by Yiddish-speaking informants who conflated Russian-Poland with Poland proper; or the family may have originated west of the Kovno/Vilna area.

3. **No passenger manifest located** for Abraham via free web search. The Ellis Island Foundation database (statueofliberty.org) and Steve Morse One-Step at stevemorse.org require interactive use to confirm.

## Analysis

The circumstantial geographic evidence is strong: Abraham lived at the epicenter of the Kovno-Gubernia immigrant neighborhood in Providence (Chalkstone Avenue), worshipped in the orbit of the Anshei Kovno Congregation, and worked in the Kovno-immigrant trade (junk peddling). Surname and given name etymology independently point to Lithuania. Confidence level: **Moderate**.

The Tier 1 death certificate (Russia) plus Tier 2 neighborhood mapping plus Tier 2 surname etymology converge on **Kovno Gubernia, Lithuania**. The specific shtetl remains unknown, but the Ukmerge (Vilkomir) Uyezd is a plausible starting point given the documented Zalk family in Troskunai.

## Decisive Record

The following records would definitively confirm the origin town:

1. **Ellis Island / Castle Garden passenger manifest** for Abraham Salk (or variant: Zalk, Salkin, Salko, Sulk, Solk, Zalkin, Zaltsman) arriving 1892 to 1900 at NY port from Hamburg, Bremen, or Liverpool, stating last town of residence.
2. **US naturalization petition** filed by Abraham (post-1906) at the US District Court for Rhode Island (held at NARA Waltham) or Rhode Island Supreme Court Judicial Records Center in Pawtucket. Post-1906 petitions require the applicant's town of birth.
3. **Anshei Kovno Congregation membership roster** from c. 1900 to 1925 (Rhode Island Jewish Historical Association archives, 401 Elmgrove Avenue, Providence) listing Abraham Salk with shtetl of origin.
4. **Abraham's Rhode Island death certificate (1925)**, already obtained as No. 584/226. Re-examine for any town-of-birth field beyond Russia.
5. **Tobey/Tauba Salk's death certificate** (she was alive as of 1925; died later). Her later death cert may have a more specific birthplace.
