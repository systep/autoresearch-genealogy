The 1870 census also confirms that Johann/John and Catharine had no co-resident children at that address (household of 2 only), consistent with any children having married out by 1870 OR with the couple being childless. ## Notes

- **Geographic note**: Enzweihingen is a village in the Neckarkreis (Neckar district) of Württemberg, in present-day Baden-Württemberg, Germany. It is part of the town of Vaihingen an der Enz, Ludwigsburg Kreis. Confirms the GEDCOM's Enzweihingen attribution at Tier 1.
- **Century note**: 1870 Iowa was active German immigrant settlement territory. Württemberg Lutherans settled heavily in Ohio and Iowa in the 1850s-1870s.
- **"M" in 1870 census**: Most likely a census-taker casual rendering of the middle name Christoph (perhaps read as "Christ." then abbreviated, or a recorded self-identified variant). Alternative explanations: a stepfather or patronymic marker that didn't transmit into later paperwork.
- **Elisabeth Hahn (Schwarz) was 38 and Johann Christian Schwarz was 24 at the 1826 Krotoschin marriage**: a 14-year age gap where the bride is older. The "(Schwarz)" parenthetical on her name in the record suggests either she was a widow of a prior Schwarz (taking his surname to her next marriage, also to a Schwarz, creating surname continuity by coincidence), OR it is FamilySearch's convention of parenthesizing post-marriage surname. The former reading is more likely given her age of 38 at the marriage.
