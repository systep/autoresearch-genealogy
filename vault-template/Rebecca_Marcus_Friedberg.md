## Biography

Rebecca was born circa 1879 in **Libau, Russia** (present-day **Liepāja, Latvia**, then in the Kurland/Courland governorate of the Russian Empire). Her father was **Willy Friedberg** and her mother **Henriette Goldberg** (maiden name). Her age of 31 on the 24 November 1910 New York marriage certificate places her birth in 1879 or 1880.

She worked as a **piano teacher** in New York, living at **10 East 115th Street** in East Harlem by 1910. Her first marriage was to an unknown man surnamed Marcus; he died before November 1910.

On **24 November 1910** she married **[[Julius_Salk]]** (also a widower, a Kovno-born store keeper) in Manhattan. The officiant was **Rev. Jacob Edelborn**. The witnesses, **Bernhard Goldberg** and **Leo Friedberg**, were both from Rebecca's side of the family: Leo Friedberg is most likely her brother (shared surname), and Bernhard Goldberg was probably a maternal relative (same surname as Rebecca's mother's maiden name). See [[Julius_Salk_Rebecca_Marcus_NY_Marriage_1910]] for the full transcription.

### Libau / Liepāja origin

Libau (Yiddish: Liboy; Latvian: Liepāja) was a port city on the Baltic Sea. It had a substantial Jewish community in the late 19th century, with emigration peaks during the 1881-1914 Russian Empire Jewish exodus. Rebecca's origin in Libau distinguishes her from Julius and the Providence Salk cluster, which is rooted in Kovno Gubernia (Kaunas/Ukmerge) roughly 200 km to the southeast.
