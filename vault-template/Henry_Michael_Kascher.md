## Biography

Henry Michael Kascher was born December 10, 1914 in Youngstown, Mahoning County, Ohio. His mother's maiden name was **Falk** (from the Ohio birth certificate). The Kascher surname is rare, of German/Central European origin, with 3 Kascher families in Ohio in the 1920 census.

He was a **research chemist and inventor** at **Eastman Kodak** in Rochester, NY (Distillation Products, Inc. division, Research Laboratories). His published work and patents include:

- **Published paper**: "State of Vitamin A in Fish Liver Oils" by Henry M. Kascher and James G. Baxter, Research Laboratories, Distillation Products, Inc., Rochester, N.Y.
- **US Patent US-2686751-A** (1954): "Vitamin a stabilization," co-inventor with Norris D. Embree, assigned to Eastman Kodak Co.
- **US Patent US-2576104-A**: "Dehydration and isomerization of straight-chain-conjugated polyene esters and acids," co-inventor with Edgar M. Shantz and Charles D. Robeson, assigned to Eastman Kodak Co.
- **US Patent US-2063624-A** (1936): "Umbrella," co-inventor with Louis Raab (filed 1935, name listed as "Karcher Henry," a variant spelling).

A Kodak company newspaper clipping shows "Henry Kascher" competing in a duplicate bridge tournament alongside colleague Edgar Shantz (co-inventor), advancing to the championship round.

He married **Mary Elizabeth Bright** (born June 5, 1921, Madison). By 1950, the family was living at **49 Clay Avenue, Rochester, Monroe County, New York**. Henry was 35, Mary was 28, with daughter Kathryn (age 4).

**1936 Berlin Olympics:** Henry Michael Kascher himself (age 21) served as **Timekeeper/Scorer** for basketball at the **1936 Berlin Olympics**, representing Germany. He officiated multiple games: Uruguay-Belgium, Italy-Germany (58:16), Canada-Latvia, Czechoslovakia-Germany (20:9), Philippine Islands-Esthonia (39:22), Uruguay-Egypt, and others. A **K. Falk** (Germany) also served as scorer at the same tournament; Falk is Henry's **mother's maiden name**, suggesting a family member. FamilySearch passenger list index (1906-1942) shows **"Herr Henry Kascher"** among German passengers arriving in New York. He was born in Youngstown, Ohio (1914) but represented Germany at the Olympics, indicating he was living in Germany in 1936 or held German citizenship through his parents.

**Additional patent**: US-2908041-A "Method for producing beadlets" (1959), sole inventor Henry M Kascher, assigned to Eastman Kodak. Filed 1955, granted 1959. Also filed internationally (GB, FR).

Their daughter **Kathryn Ann Kascher** (b. January 1, 1946) married **Alan Carl Salmanson**, connecting the Ohio/Rochester Kascher family to the Providence Salmanson family.
