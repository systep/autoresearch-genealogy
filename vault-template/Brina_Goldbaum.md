## Biography

Brina Goldbaum was the wife of [[Joseph_Brandt]] (GEDCOM). The GEDCOM records her cause of death as the Holocaust. She was the mother of [[Helen_Brandt]], who married [[Morris_Markel]] and immigrated to the United States around 1928.

If Brina died in the Holocaust (1939-1945), she did not emigrate with her daughter Helen. This is consistent with the broader pattern in the Markel family: four of Morris Markel's siblings who remained in Europe were murdered in a Pogrom at the Rodzinka Forest on Rosh Hashanah 1939 (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). Brina may have suffered a similar fate in the same region.

### Surname Research

The surname "Goldbaum" is an Ashkenazi Jewish ornamental name composed of German "Gold" (gold) + "Baum" (tree), meaning "golden tree." Such compound ornamental names were adopted by Ashkenazi Jewish families in Central and Eastern Europe, typically during the 18th-19th century when governments began requiring Jews to register surnames.

The Goldbaum surname appears in:
- Tarnopol 1910 Jewish Census surname list (Gesher Galicia)
- Geneanet surname distribution database
- Jewish communities in Poland, Germany, Romania, Ukraine, and Argentina

The name "Brina" (also spelled Bryna, Brayna, Breina) is a Yiddish feminine name. The Hebrew Surnames database and Kveller note it as an Anglicized form of Yiddish "Brayne."
