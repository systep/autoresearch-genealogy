## Biography

Elizabeth Brasher was born about 1764 in the Greenville, South Carolina Colony (or possibly Chatham Co NC, where her family resided before migrating south), the daughter of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. (1731 Baltimore, MD - c. 1789 Greenville SC) and Sarah Lindsey. She married [[Ezekiel_Henderson]] (1763-1846) on 17 October 1782 in Chatham County, North Carolina, and bore him fifteen children over twenty-seven years (per WikiTree Henderson-1855), including [[Olive_Mary_Polly_Henderson_Cox]] (1795-1876) who married [[Thomas_B_Cox]].

Elizabeth's siblings constituted the patriarch generation of the Greenville-area Brasher/Brashier family. Her brother **Thomas Levi Brasher III (1765-1841)** is the strongest candidate father for [[Mary_Brashier]] (b. 1806), wife of [[Lewis_P_Huff]], per the existing Mary Brashier vault file's parentage hypothesis. If that hypothesis is confirmed, Mary Brashier would be Elizabeth's niece, making her a first cousin of Olive Mary Henderson Cox -- another instance of the Greenville cousin marriages already evident in this kin network (e.g., Rebecca Adeline Cox m. Henry Langford Henderson, Henderson grandson via Brasher Henderson).

The "Brasher" surname is traced to Baltimore MD via Thomas Levi Brasher Sr. (bap. 1701 St. George's Parish Baltimore, d. 1770 Orange Co NC). The earlier-hypothesized French-Huguenot Brasseur connection is **refuted** per the 2026-04-22 (part 7) MyHeritage sweep findings recorded in the Research_Log.
