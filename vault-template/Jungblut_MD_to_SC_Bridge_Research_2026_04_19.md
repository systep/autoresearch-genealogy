# Hypothesis: Edgefield SC Youngbloods Descend from Peter Jungblut's 1739 MD Children via NC Intermediate Generation

## Statement

The Edgefield County SC Youngblood cluster (James c.1740-1792, Peter Jr c.1725-1794 of Edgefield, Thomas c.1745-1822, and associated Peters, Jacobs, Johns) descends from the children of Peter Jungblut who was naturalized in Prince George's County, Maryland on 6 March 1738/9 with children William, Peter, Sarah, and Mary. Migration path: Prince George's/Baltimore/Harford Co MD (1739-1750) → Orange Co and Johnston Co NC (1750s-1760s) → Edgefield District SC (1766-1785). James Youngblood c.1740-1792 is most likely the son (or grandson) of **Peter Jungblut Jr.** (one of the four 1739-naturalized children), not a descendant of John Miles Youngblood of the earlier Baltimore line.

## Evidence For

1. **1739 MD naturalization (Tier 1 primary)**: Peter Jungblut + children William, Peter, Sarah, Mary naturalized Prince George's Co MD. Documented in [[Peter_Jungblut_1739_MD]].
2. **MD-to-NC disappearance/appearance pattern (Tier 2)**: Georgia Emanuel Youngblood migration compilation: "Youngbloods of Maryland began to disappear from that state about 1745" and Peter Sr, Peter Jr, John, Thomas, Henry Youngblood appeared in Orange and Johnston Cos NC. The timing aligns with Peter's 1750 remarriage to Mary Wheals and subsequent relocation.
3. **Orange Co NC land grant (Tier 1/2)**: Peter Youngblood Sr held grant east side of Deep River at Great Shoals by 1754 (WikiTree Youngblood-199, citing NC land records).
4. **Edgefield arrival (Tier 2)**: James Youngblood sold NC lands after the Revolution, bought 200 acres on Stevens Creek Edgefield SC, wrote will 3 Aug 1791, probated Mar 1792 (WikiTree Youngblood-746).
5. **Palatine settlement of Londonborough Township (historical context)**: German Palatines settled what became part of Edgefield Co SC during the 18th century, providing a plausible cultural/community corridor for a Palatinate-origin family.
6. **DNA project (Tier 2, ongoing)**: FamilyTreeDNA "Youngbloods of Edgefield SC" project segregates participants: GREEN = John Miles YB (1708 Baltimore, son of Thomas YB, a separate line); GRAY/BLUE/PURPLE = post-1710 Palatinate arrivals. YDNA explicitly rules out John Miles YB as ancestor of the Edgefield James c.1740-1792, leaving the 1739 Jungblut line as the leading candidate.
7. **Name coincidences matching 1739 children**: Peter Youngblood Jr. (WikiTree Youngblood-197, bef.1732-1788) m. Susannah Starke 26 Nov 1750 Baltimore City MD. A Peter Jr naturalized in 1739 as a minor would be roughly the right age to marry in 1750. Peter Youngblood (c.1725-c.1794) d. Edgefield SC had sons Jacob, John, George, Lewis, Abraham: a second-generation Peter matching the family pattern.

## Evidence Against

1. **No direct primary document bridging the names**: searches did not surface an NC deed naming "Peter Youngblood, son of Peter Jungblut of Prince George's Co MD" or equivalent. The bridge rests on indirect inference (timing, geography, DNA cluster membership).
2. **Craven Co NC attribution for James YB**: the colonial Craven precinct was reorganized multiple times; modern readers conflate the 18th-century administrative unit with the coastal county. This birthplace claim is Tier 3 and may be inaccurate.
3. **Persistent research confusion**: the two Maryland Youngblood lines (John Miles 1708 Baltimore vs the 1739 Jungblut Palatines) have been repeatedly conflated in user-contributed trees. Names like "Peter," "Thomas," "John," and "James" appear in both lines, inviting false merges.

## Analysis

**Confidence: Moderate Signal, leaning strong** for the overall MD → NC → SC migration thesis; **Speculative** for the specific son-to-son linkage between the 1739 children (William, Peter Jr, Sarah, Mary) and individual Edgefield Youngbloods.

The weight of Tier 2 evidence (migration compilations, WikiTree sourced profiles, FamilyTreeDNA project segregation) consistently points to the 1739 Jungblut family as the origin of the Edgefield SC Youngblood cluster. The DNA project findings are particularly persuasive because they actively rule out the competing John Miles YB line.

Within the Jungblut children, the best candidate for James Youngblood's father is **Peter Jungblut Jr.** (one of the four 1739-naturalized children): age fits (Peter Jr would be of fathering age in 1740), naming pattern fits (James's son Jeremiah, Eli, Thomas; the name "Peter" recurs in the Edgefield cluster), and NC arrival chronology fits (Peter Jr plausibly traveled with his father after the 1750 remarriage).

**William Jungblut** (eldest naturalized 1739 child) is the second candidate for the SC ancestor: as eldest son he would have been the most likely to receive land and have sons named Peter, James, and Thomas per Germanic naming conventions. Possible that the "Peter Sr" who appears in Orange Co NC by 1754 is in fact William's brother or son, with nomenclature slippage across the MD-NC move.

**Sarah and Mary Jungblut** are likely terminal to the Youngblood surname (daughters, married into other families); relevant for sibling cross-reference but not surname bridge.

## Decisive Record

One of the following would move this from Moderate to Strong:

1. **Orange Co NC or Johnston Co NC will** of "Peter Youngblood" (Peter Sr or Jr) c.1760-1775 naming sons including James and referencing MD origin or brother William.
2. **Prince George's Co MD or Harford Co MD estate record** of Peter Jungblut (after 1750) distributing property to named children William, Peter Jr, Sarah, Mary with residence notes placing them in NC or SC.
3. **YDNA Big-Y** on a documented William Jungblut (1739 MD) male-line descendant, confirming match to the Edgefield GRAY/BLUE/PURPLE cluster.
4. **Londonborough Township SC settler list** naming a Peter or William Youngblood as an original Palatine grantee.
