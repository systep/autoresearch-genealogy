## Biography

Eleanor of Aquitaine was born circa 1122 in the Duchy of Aquitaine, France, the eldest daughter of William X, Duke of Aquitaine, and Aenor de Chatellerault. She was one of the wealthiest and most powerful women in Western Europe during the High Middle Ages (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Eleanor first married Louis VII, King of France, in 1137, becoming Queen of France. She accompanied Louis on the Second Crusade (1147 to 1149). Their marriage was annulled on March 21, 1152, on grounds of consanguinity. They had two daughters (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Only eight weeks after the annulment, Eleanor married [[Henry_II]], Count of Anjou, who became King of England in 1154. Their marriage produced eight children, including four sons who survived to adulthood, among them [[King_John]]. Eleanor was imprisoned by Henry from 1173 to 1189 after supporting their sons' rebellion. After Henry's death in 1189, Eleanor was released and served as regent during the reign of her son Richard I (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Eleanor died on April 1, 1204, at Fontevraud Abbey, where she was buried alongside Henry II and Richard I. In the pedigree charts, she connects with [[Henry_II]] as the ancestral pair from which the royal descent to colonial American families is traced (Pedigree Charts).
