## Biography

Mary (Eliza) Brashier was born in 1806 in the United States (likely Greenville County, South Carolina). She married [[Lewis_P_Huff]] (b. 22 July 1803, Brunswick, Virginia; d. 24 February 1873, Simpsonville, Greenville, South Carolina).

**Brashier/Brasher family in Greenville County:** The Brashier (Brasher/Brazure) family migrated with the Cox family from Orange County, North Carolina to Greenville County, South Carolina in the 1780s. The family patriarch was Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. (c. 1731-c. 1789, WikiTree Brasher-122), who had children including Elizabeth Brasher (who married Ezekiel Henderson, creating the Henderson-Brasher intermarriage), Henry Collins Brasher Sr. (1771-1852), Aquilla B. Brasher (c. 1772-c. 1842), Samuel Robert Brasher, John P. Brasher (1775-1850), and Thomas Levi Brasher III (1765-1841). An earlier Mary Brasher (b. c. 1760, who married James George Seaborn) was a daughter of Thomas Levi Jr., but she is from a prior generation.

**Connection to Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. family (2026-04-16 research):** Mary Brashier (b. 1806) who married Lewis Huff was too young to be a daughter of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. (d. c. 1789). She is a granddaughter of Thomas Levi Jr., with three candidate fathers evaluated:

1. **Thomas Levi Brasher III (1765-1841): STRONGEST CANDIDATE.** He is the only son who remained in Greenville County after 1800. Crucially, his descendants are the ones who adopted the "Brashier" spelling (per "A Brazier/Brasher Saga" by Brashear and McCoy, 1998). His known children include Elizabeth Brashier, Thomas Brashier, John Brasher Sr., Aquilla "Cassa" Brashier, Sarah Pyle (nee Brashier), and Hannah Teague (nee Brashier). A Mary born 1806 could be an additional child not yet documented in online sources. The "Brashier" surname form used by Mary aligns specifically with the Thomas III line.

2. **Henry Collins Brasher Sr. (1771-1852): POSSIBLE BUT LESS LIKELY.** Married Eleanor Ashmore c. 1795; had 7 children. Five are named (Jane Cynthenia, John Andrew Jackson, Henry Jr. b. 1801, Samuel Henry Sr., Richard Harrison). Two unnamed children could include a Mary born c. 1806. However, Henry Collins moved to Shelby County, Alabama c. 1817, making it less likely that his daughter remained in Greenville to marry Lewis Huff. WikiTree Brasher-121; Find a Grave #66620694.

3. **Aquilla B. Brasher (c. 1772-c. 1842): UNLIKELY.** Moved to western Kentucky after 1800. His children (Lawrence, Thomas, Jane, Aquilla Jr.) are documented; no Mary appears. WikiTree Brasher-147.

Other sons (John P. Brasher, Samuel Robert Brasher) also left Greenville after 1800, making them unlikely candidates.

**Unsolved brick wall:** A Genealogy.com forum post seeks information about "Mary (Brauser) Huff (after 1815, about 1850) from Greenville, South Carolina" and asks about her parents, confirming this is an open question among Brasher/Huff researchers. WikiTree G2G also has an unresolved inquiry. The definitive reference, "A Brazier/Brasher Saga" by Brashear and McCoy (532 pages, 8,000+ family members), has not been directly consulted and may contain the answer.

WikiTree confirms the marriage of Lewis Huff and Mary Brashier under the profile Huff-142, providing a second Tier 3 source for this connection.

Mary's exact death date is unknown; the GEDCOM records it as between 1841 and 1906. Lewis P. Huff's father was [[James_Huff]] (b. 1768, Brunswick County, Colonial Virginia; d. 1842, Golden Grove, Greenville, SC) and mother was [[Rebecca_Moseley_Huff|Rebecca Moseley]].
