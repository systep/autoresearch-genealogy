## Biography

Sebastian Brecht was a son of [[Conrad_Kuntz_Brecht]] (c. 1563-1612) and Catharina Claus Camerer of Neudorf, and brother of the direct ancestor [[Christoph_Stoffel_Brecht]] (1591-1665). According to the Geni project "Conrad Kuntz Brecht (1563-1612) family" (record 45913, Tier 3), Sebastian was born in 1600 in Neudorf, Baden, married Elisabeth Maiden (c. 1605-1654), and **was settled in Schriesheim by 1637**, dying there 29 April 1670.

Sebastian's settlement in Schriesheim by 1637 is notable because his brother Christoph Stoffel and Christoph's wife Anna Barbara Wolpack also moved from Neudorf to Schriesheim "before 1636" (Geni; see [[Christoph_Stoffel_Brecht]]). Christoph's son Balthasar Hans was born in Schriesheim 24 August 1636. The two brothers therefore relocated to Schriesheim within roughly the same window (mid-to-late 1630s, during/after the Thirty Years' War), which establishes the family's beach-head in Schriesheim through a coordinated sibling migration rather than a single individual.

This is a **collateral line** (Sebastian is great-uncle of [[Balthasar_Hans_Brecht]] and three generations removed from the Pennsylvania emigrant [[Johann_Michael_Brecht]]); Sebastian's own descendants are not direct ancestors. The file is created here primarily to document the parallel migration and to anchor any future identification of present-day Schriesheim Brecht descendants whose line may pass through Sebastian rather than Christoph.

Confidence: **Moderate**. Five compiled-genealogy sources now corroborate the 1600-1670 dates, parents, and Schriesheim settlement: Geni project 45913, Geni profile 6000000009677445618, WikiTree Brecht-14, Familypedia, and the cmbower Brecht One-Name Study (audit upgrade 2026-05-02). WikiTree Brecht-14 adds a second marriage to Margaretha Kammerer on 24 April 1654 in Schriesheim (after Elisabeth Maiden's death same year). Independent verification from the **Schriesheim Familienbuch 1650-1900** (Schuhmann 2004, see [[Johann_Michael_Brecht]] §Local History Sources) is still pending direct page-level inspection and would upgrade confidence to high.
