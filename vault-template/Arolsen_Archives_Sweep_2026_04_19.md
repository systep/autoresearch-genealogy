# Hypothesis: Arolsen Archives hold documents on vault Jewish and Transylvanian-Saxon lines

## Statement

The Arolsen Archives (formerly ITS Bad Arolsen) hold at least one document (concentration camp, ghetto, DP, forced labor, or ITS tracing correspondence) pertaining to a direct or collateral relative of the vault's Ukrainian Jewish (Kuniansky, Fitterman, Freemer), Lithuanian Jewish (Salk, Zalmanson, Sheinberg, Deych, Wallis, Fain), Polish Jewish (Markel, Rodbell, Frei, Karp, Kleinberg), and Transylvanian Saxon (Kascher, Schuller, Konnerth, Binder, Brecht) lines.

## Search Protocol

15 Google searches executed 2026-04-19, mostly using `site:collections.arolsen-archives.org` + surname + geographic qualifier. Google-indexed snippets return mostly collection-level descriptions rather than individual-level index-card hits, because individual prisoner cards live inside paginated JavaScript views that Google does not always index. Negative Google results therefore do not equal archival absence.

### Important methodological caveat

Absence of Google snippets does NOT mean absence from Arolsen. The archive's internal search engine indexes every name on every card, whereas Google only indexes what it crawls. All "no hit" entries above must be re-run against the Arolsen internal search before treated as confirmed negative.

## Candidate Cross-Matches to Vault Person Files

Because only the KLEINBERG folder-range snippet returned a surname match, and because the folder spans many individuals, no confident cross-match to a specific vault person can be made from Google snippets alone.

Potential candidates to check against DP folder DP1917 (03010101-12-030) via the Arolsen internal interface:
- Any vault Kleinberg ancestor born Budapest or Galicia who was of DP-registration age (1915-1945) in 1945-1950
- Cross-reference against `Kleinberg` vault person files (if present) for name, approximate DOB, and place of birth

No transcription file is being created at this stage because confidence is Speculative (Tier 3 surname-only match, no name + date + place triangulation).

## Analysis

The sweep's Google-snippet mode has inherent limitations: Arolsen's document viewer uses client-side rendering for individual cards, so Google only indexes collection landing pages. The practical takeaway is:

1. Arolsen holds the right **collection types** for every one of the vault's Holocaust-era lines (Kauen, Riga, Auschwitz Hungarian-Jewish, Polish-Jewish survivor registries).
2. One collection-level surname hit surfaced (Kleinberg in DP1917).
3. To advance from Speculative to Moderate/Strong, a human researcher must run each surname + DOB-range + place-of-birth through the Arolsen internal search UI (which cannot be scripted by a logged-out AI).

## Decisive Record

Direct queries at https://collections.arolsen-archives.org/en/search for each surname with place-of-birth and date-of-birth filters, plus free inquiry-form requests at https://arolsen-archives.org/en/archive/inquiry/. Arolsen provides document copies at no cost for family descendants.
