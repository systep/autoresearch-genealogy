# Solomon Luria (Maharshal)

> **Confidence: Moderate.** Solomon Luria (the Maharshal) is a Wikipedia confirmed, well documented historical figure and one of the greatest halachic authorities. However, his role as ancestor in this tree is part of the claimed Davidic descent through the Maharal of Prague. This chain is contested by modern scholarship (Putik, Muneles). The connection from the Maharal back to King David relies on Meir Perels' Megillat Yuhasin (1745), which contains documented errors.

## Biography

Solomon Luria (1510-1573), universally known as the Maharshal, was one of the greatest halachic (Jewish legal) authorities of the 16th century. He served as head of the yeshiva in Lublin and was renowned for his independent approach to halachic decision-making, insisting on returning to the original Talmudic sources rather than relying on later codifications. His magnum opus, "Yam shel Shlomo" (Sea of Solomon), provides extensive analysis of Talmudic topics. His "Chokhmat Shlomo" contains glosses and corrections to the standard printed Talmud.

The Maharshal was a contemporary of Rabbi Moses Isserles (the Rema) and the two corresponded extensively, sometimes disagreeing sharply on points of law. He was also a relative of the Maharal of Prague through the Luria family network.

The GEDCOM does not contain a separate individual record for Solomon Luria. The Luria family chain in the GEDCOM (Nachman > Kalman > Leibish Kalman Loew) represents the branch leading to the Maharal rather than the Maharshal's specific line. However, the Maharshal's family claims the same Luria ancestry, and he is traditionally considered part of the same extended family that includes the ancestors listed in the GEDCOM's Luria chain.
