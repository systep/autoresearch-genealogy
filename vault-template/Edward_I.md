## Biography

Edward I, known as Edward Longshanks and the Hammer of the Scots, was born on June 17, 1239, at Westminster, the eldest son of King [[Henry_III]] and Eleanor of Provence (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Edward married Eleanor of Castile in 1254 in a political alliance with the Kingdom of Castile. They had at least fourteen children, including [[Joan_Plantagenet]] (born in Acre during the Ninth Crusade in 1272) and the future Edward II. Eleanor died in 1290, and Edward later married Margaret of France in 1299 (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Edward became king in 1272 upon his father's death. His reign was marked by legal reforms, the conquest of Wales, and prolonged wars with Scotland. He died on July 7, 1307, at Burgh by Sands while marching north for another Scottish campaign (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

In the pedigree charts, Edward I connects through his daughter [[Joan_Plantagenet]] to the De Clare line and subsequently to colonial American families.

## Gateway Ancestor Assessment (2026-04-14)

### Claimed Line of Descent

The pedigree charts (1-46.pdf, Tier 3) claim descent from Edward I through two routes:

**Route 1 (De Clare/Audley/Neville path)**: Edward I -> [[Joan_Plantagenet]] -> [[Gilbert_de_Clare]] + Joan -> [[Margaret_de_Clare]] -> [[Hugh_de_Audley]] + Margaret -> "Baroness Alice DeAudley" -> [[Ralph_Neville]] -> ... -> colonial families -> Cox line.

**Route 2 (Courtenay/Clifford/Cocke path)**: Edward I -> Joan Plantagenet -> ... -> Anne Courtenay (1550-1604) -> Anthony Clifford -> Charles Clifford (1570-1612) -> Elizabeth Clifford -> Robert Cocke (1561-1608) -> Thomas Cox (1626-1711) -> ... -> Coulson -> Embree -> William Richardson Cox Sr. (b. 1692) -> William Cox Sr. (c. 1730-1814) -> Isaac Cox -> ... -> Roxana Cox.

### Assessment: REFUTED (Confidence: Strong Signal)

**FOUR fatal breaks identified (Research_Log.md, 2026-04-10; verified 2026-04-14):**

**Break 1: Alice de Audley is Hugh's SISTER, not his daughter.** The pedigree charts claim "Baroness Alice DeAudley" was the daughter of [[Hugh_de_Audley]] (1st Earl of Gloucester) and [[Margaret_de_Clare]] (Plantagenet granddaughter). In fact, Alice de Audley (d. 1374) was the daughter of Hugh de Audley, 1st Baron Audley of Stratton Audley, and Isolde le Rous/Mortimer. She was the SISTER of Hugh de Audley, 1st Earl of Gloucester, not his daughter. The Earl's only child was Margaret de Audley, who married Ralph Stafford, 1st Earl of Stafford (Wikipedia: Hugh de Audley, 1st Earl of Gloucester; Geni: Alice de Audley, Baroness Neville; Tier 2). Alice therefore carried NO Plantagenet blood to Ralph Neville. This is the single most devastating break: the entire claimed Plantagenet descent through the Neville line collapses here.

**Break 2: "Sir Norman Cooke of Lavenham" is a KNOWN FABRICATION.** WikiTree has flagged the entire Cooke ancestry of Lavenham, Suffolk (pre-1442) as fabricated. The "Cooke-Dubious Pedigree" space page documents that profiles from approximately 1025-1425 in this line have "Disproven Existence" notices. No evidence has been proposed for any of the fabricated profiles (WikiTree Space:Cooke-Dubious Pedigree; Tier 2).

**Break 3: Margaret Neville's marriages do not match the pedigree charts.** The pedigree charts claim Margaret Neville (daughter of Ralph Neville) married "Jean Ross" or "Ena Ross." In reality, Margaret Neville (d. 1372) married William de Ros, 3rd Lord Ros of Helmsley, and then Henry Percy, KG. There is no "Jean Ross" or "Ena Ross" in the documented record. The names appear to be fictitious (Wikipedia: Ralph Neville, 2nd Baron Neville; WikiTree Neville-349; Tier 2).

**Break 4: William Cox Sr. (c. 1730-1814, SC) is NOT the son of William Richardson Cox (1692-1767, DE).** Y-DNA testing (Y-37, FamilyTreeDNA, haplogroup R1b-I "Carolina Coxes" subgroup R-M269) proves these are two different, unrelated families. William Cox Sr. was NOT a Quaker and NOT from Delaware. The Cox Family History Project (est. 2008, updated 2020) and WikiTree Cox-14022 confirm this is an erroneous merger found in Ancestry.com, FamilySearch, and Geni databases (Cox Family History Project DNA page; FamilyTreeDNA Cox DNA Study; WikiTree Cox-14022; Tier 1 DNA + Tier 2 compiled research).

### What IS Real in the Upper Portion

The medieval genealogy from Edward I down through Joan Plantagenet, Gilbert de Clare, and Margaret de Clare to Hugh de Audley is well documented in the Complete Peerage and secondary sources (Tier 2). The Courtenay family of Powderham, Devon DOES have a proven descent from Edward I through Elizabeth Plantagenet (d. 1316), daughter of Edward I, who married Humphrey de Bohun, 4th Earl of Hereford; their daughter Margaret de Bohun married Hugh Courtenay, 2nd Earl of Devon. Peter Courtenay of Ugbrooke/Chudleigh (d. 1552) was part of this Courtenay cadet branch, and his daughter Anne Courtenay (1550-1604) married Anthony Clifford. However, the chain from there through Elizabeth Clifford -> Robert Cocke -> Thomas Cox -> Coulson -> Embree -> William Richardson Cox -> William Cox Sr. contains unverified links and the terminal connection (Break 4) is DNA-disproven.

### Conclusion

This is NOT a credible Gateway Ancestor claim. The medieval royal persons (Edward I, Henry II, Eleanor of Aquitaine, Joan Plantagenet, Gilbert de Clare) are real historical figures with well-documented genealogies. However, the chains connecting them to the Cox family of South Carolina are broken at multiple points. The pedigree charts (Tier 3, user-contributed) contain demonstrable errors including confusing two different Audley individuals, incorporating known fabricated pedigree lines (Cooke of Lavenham), using fictitious names ("Jean Ross"), and connecting two DNA-disproven families. No scholarly genealogical source (Tier 2) supports this descent.
