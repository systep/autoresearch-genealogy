## Biography

Edward I, known as Edward Longshanks and the Hammer of the Scots, was born on June 17, 1239, at Westminster, the eldest son of King [[Henry_III]] and Eleanor of Provence (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Edward married Eleanor of Castile in 1254 in a political alliance with the Kingdom of Castile. They had at least fourteen children, including [[Joan_Plantagenet]] (born in Acre during the Ninth Crusade in 1272) and the future Edward II. Eleanor died in 1290, and Edward later married Margaret of France in 1299 (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Edward became king in 1272 upon his father's death. His reign was marked by legal reforms, the conquest of Wales, and prolonged wars with Scotland. He died on July 7, 1307, at Burgh by Sands while marching north for another Scottish campaign (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

In the pedigree charts, Edward I connects through his daughter [[Joan_Plantagenet]] to the De Clare line and subsequently to colonial American families.
