## Biography

Walter Culpepper (also Colepeper) was a professional soldier and Tudor-era military officer. He was the son of Sir John Colepeper and Agnes Gainsford (WikiTree Culpepper-600, Tier 3).

Walter married **Anne Aucher** (c. 1462 to after 1532), daughter of Henry Aucher, Esquire, and Elizabeth Guildford, at Bedgebury around 1480 (WikiTree Aucher-1, Tier 3). Note: [[Hester_Aucher]], who married [[Richard_Sandys]] in a later generation, is from the same Aucher family of Kent.

Walter served as the **Under-Marshal of Calais**, the English-held fortress town on the French coast. He is recorded in 1508 as Under-Marshal and was present at the treaty for the marriage of Mary, daughter of Henry VII, to the Duke of Burgundy (WikiTree Culpepper-600, Tier 3).

Walter and Anne had five children: three sons (Thomas, Anthony, and William) and two daughters (Anne and Elizabeth) (WikiTree, Tier 3). His youngest son [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]] (c. 1509 to 1559) became the ancestor of most living Culpeppers worldwide.

Walter died before June 24, 1515, and was buried in Calais. His will was probated on April 28, 1516. His widow Anne requested to be buried beside her father in England, not beside her husband in Calais (WikiTree Aucher-1, Tier 3).

## Connection to This Family Tree

Walter is the father of [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]]. The descent continues: William to [[Martin_Culpepper]] to Mary/Mercy Culpepper to [[Samuel_Sandys_MP]] to [[Richard_Sandys]] to [[Stephen_Sands]] to [[Eleanor_Sands]] and subsequent Hough/Huff descendants.

The Aucher family connection is notable: Walter married Anne Aucher, and in a later generation, [[Hester_Aucher]] married [[Richard_Sandys]] (Samuel Sandys's son). This means two Aucher women married into the direct ancestral line at different points.
