# Captain William Moseley of Old Rappahannock

## Summary

Captain William Moseley, born 5 December 1630 in Kinver (Kinfare), Staffordshire, England, and died before 3 January 1676/77 (alt. late 1681) in Sittingbourne Parish, Essex County (formerly Old Rappahannock), Virginia. Immigrant to Virginia 1649/50 via the Northern Neck, Burgess for Rappahannock County 1671 to 1674.

**He is a distinct individual** from [[William_Moseley_1649]] of Lower Norfolk (c. 1605 to bef. 1655), a common point of conflation in tertiary family trees.

## Key Facts

### Birth and English Origin

- Baptized St Peter's parish church, Kinver (then spelled Kinfare), Staffordshire, on 5 December 1630.
- Likely birthplace: Whittington, a hamlet in Kinver parish.
- Sixth child and third son of Edward Moseley (baptized Kinver 25 April 1599, died 1673) and his wife Margaret.
- The Kinver Moseleys are distinct from the Rolleston Hall Mosleys of eastern Staffordshire; no documented descent from Sir Edward Mosley of Rolleston (d. 1638 without issue).

### Arrival in Virginia

- Arrived Virginia 1649/50, transported as a headright of his brother-in-law Col. William Underwood.
- Patent of 22 May 1650 (Nugent's Cavaliers and Pioneers, vol. 1): Col. William Underwood granted 2,000 acres on the north side of the Rappahannock River in Old Rappahannock County, by headright for transporting 12 persons including "Wm. Moseley".
- Accompanied on the voyage by his sister Mary (Moseley) Underwood (Col. William Underwood's wife).

### Marriage

- Married Martha Brasseur before 21 January 1659/60 (Rappahannock Records, 1656-64, p. 99 deed).
- Martha: daughter of Robert Brasseur (b. bef. 1600 France, fled to Isle of Thanet, Kent, before c. 1628, arrived Virginia c. 1635) and Florence (Fowke) Brasseur. Huguenot descent.
- Martha b. c. 1636, likely Isle of Wight or Nansemond County, Virginia. Survived William, later married Captain George Taylor.

### Land Patents

- 22 May 1650: transported by Underwood, Old Rappahannock (headright only, not a patentee yet).
- 18 November 1663 (Nugent vol. 1): 1,157 acres in Rappahannock County by headright for transporting 27 persons. This is his own patent as sponsor.

### Office

- Burgess of the Colony of Virginia representing Rappahannock County, 1671 to 1674.
- Jamestowne Society Qualifying Ancestor.

### Death

- Died before 3 January 1676/77 per one line of sources; late 1681 per another. Needs Essex / Old Rappahannock probate record to resolve.
- Residence at death: Sittingbourne Parish, Essex County (formerly Old Rappahannock).

### Known Children

From Martha Brasseur:
- William Moseley II (c. 1660 to c. 1700)
- Edward Moseley
- Robert Moseley
- Elizabeth (Moseley) Hawkins (1666 to 1725)
- Benjamin Moseley
