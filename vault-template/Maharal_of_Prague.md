## Biography

Rabbi Judah Loew ben Bezalel, universally known as the Maharal of Prague (an acronym for "Moreinu HaRav Loew"), was one of the most prominent Jewish scholars of the 16th century. He served as Chief Rabbi of Prague and was renowned as a Talmudist, Kabbalist, philosopher, and mystic (GEDCOM). He also served as rabbi of Moravia (Mikulov) and Poznan before his appointment in Prague.

The Maharal is famous in Jewish folklore as the creator of the Golem of Prague, a being fashioned from clay to protect the Jewish community. While the historical basis for this legend is debated, his actual scholarly contributions to Jewish philosophy, ethics, and Talmudic commentary are well documented (Wikipedia).

His wife Perl (Perel Shmelkes Reich) was herself noted as a Torah scholar and merchant (GEDCOM). They married in 1544 in Prague (Geni.com). She died May 5, 1610, a year after the Maharal, and is buried in the Old Jewish Cemetery at Josefov, Prague.

The Maharal's lineage connects to the Chabad-Lubavitch dynasty through his son [[Bezalel_Charif_Loew]], whose descendants include [[Boruch_Poisner]] (father of the Alter Rebbe, [[Shneur_Zalman_of_Liadi]]).

**Note on birth year discrepancy:** The GEDCOM records a birth year of 1510, while the Family Tree and user instructions reference ~1525. Wikipedia also notes uncertainty; the 1520s date is more widely accepted by modern scholars. The 1510 date from the GEDCOM may reflect an older tradition. Both dates should be considered until resolved by primary scholarship.
