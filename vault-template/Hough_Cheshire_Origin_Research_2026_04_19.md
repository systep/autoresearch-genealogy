# Hypothesis: The colonial John Hough of Middletown, Bucks Co., PA was the son of Lawrance Hough of Chorley township, Wilmslow parish, Cheshire

## Statement

The John Hough who emigrated to Pennsylvania on the ship "Friendship" in September 1683, settled in Middletown Township, Bucks County, and died there January 1732, was born 17 July 1653 in Wilmslow parish, Cheshire, and christened that day at St. Bartholomew's Wilmslow. His father was Lawrance (Lawrence) Hough of Chorley township within Wilmslow parish (bur. 19 Nov 1668, Wilmslow). His siblings included Anne (chr. 1646) and Samuel (chr. 1659). His wife Hannah Worthington was a daughter of Isaacke Worthington "of The Hough" (chr. 14 Oct 1660, Wilmslow), and the couple married at Wilmslow on 19 Oct 1680.

## Evidence For

1. **Wilmslow parish register christening (Tier 1 via transcription)**: "John Hough, son of Lawrence Hough, residence Chorley; christened in Wilmslow on 17 Jul 1653." Cited by multiple secondary compilations referencing Cheshire Parish Registers 1538-2000 at Cheshire Record Office, Chester (FHL microfilm 2,106,692). This fixes the immigrant's christening date, parish, and father.

2. **Sibling christening records at Wilmslow**:
   - Anne Houghe, dau. of Lawrence Hough, res. Chorley, chr. 8 Mar 1646.
   - Samuel Hough, son of Lawrence Hough, chr. 27 Mar 1659.
   These corroborate Lawrance Hough as a resident of Chorley township within Wilmslow parish with multiple children baptized there across a 13-year span.

3. **Lawrance Hough's burial**: 19 Nov 1668, Wilmslow. Chronologically consistent with fathering children 1646-1659 and dying before the immigrant's 1680 marriage. (WikiTree Hough-2223; Tier 3, but re-citing the Wilmslow parish register.)

4. **Marriage of John Hough and Hannah Worthington**: 19 Oct 1680, St. Bartholomew's Wilmslow. The bride is identified in the parish register as "daughter of Isaacke Worthington of the Hough, Macclesfield Hundred, Cheshire." (Transcribed in multiple genealogical compilations; originally from Cheshire Parish Registers.)

5. **Emigration context**: On "Friendship" (60 tons, Liverpool, early Sept. 1683), arrived Philadelphia 21 Nov 1683, with wife Hannah and a ~2-year-old son John Jr. Falls Monthly Meeting records (Bucks County) confirm their membership; Middletown Twp. was their final settlement. (Welcome Society of Pennsylvania passenger lists.)

6. **Regional Hough distribution**: "Of the Cheshire and South Lancashire Houghs nearly all hail from Hough, now called 'the hough,' a portion of the parish of Wilmslow, Cheshire." (UKGA/GENUKI regional surname note.) Two Cheshire Hough hamlets exist (Wilmslow in Macclesfield Hundred; Wybunbury in Nantwich Hundred); the primary-source records place this family squarely in the Wilmslow hamlet.

7. **Cousin migration cluster (corroborating Quaker chain migration from Cheshire)**: Richard Hough (chr. 12 Nov 1654, St. Michael Macclesfield; d. 1705 Makefield Twp., Bucks Co.), arrived on ketch "Endeavor" 1683, brought certificate from Congleton Monthly Meeting 2 3M 1683. Seven or eight HOUGH individuals emigrated from Cheshire to PA 1681-1683 under William Penn's recruitment.

## Evidence Against

1. **GEDCOM "West Riding, Yorkshire" birthplace (Tier 3)**: GEDCOM @I501491@ records John Hough's birthplace as Yorkshire. This is contradicted by every independent source (WikiTree, Geni, Quaker records, parish registers, regional surname studies), all of which place the family in Cheshire. Most likely a data-entry error in the MyHeritage-sourced GEDCOM.

2. **GEDCOM death year 1659 for John Hough b. 1642 (Tier 3)**: Mathematically impossible given son born ~1660. Likely a conflation with a different John Hough or a spurious data point.

3. **Lawrance Hough's own parents remain unproven**: WikiTree Hough-2223 does not cite the parents of Lawrance Hough (b. before 1605). The upward link to Henery Hough (c. 1540-1618, Hough-2234) or to Edmund Hough (son of Roger Hough, chr. 1577 Wilmslow) has not been primary-source-verified in this research session.

## Analysis

Evidence strongly supports the hypothesis that the PA immigrant John Hough was the son of Lawrance Hough of Chorley, Wilmslow parish, Cheshire. Four distinct Wilmslow parish register entries (three baptisms of Lawrance's children 1646-1659, plus Lawrance's burial 1668) form a coherent family unit. The 1680 marriage record and the 1683 emigration record close the chain to the Bucks County immigrant. The existing vault file for Roger Hough (1540-1587) is consistent with this family's Chorley, Wilmslow location but has NOT been demonstrated in this session as a direct ancestor of Lawrance (two generational gaps remain unfilled: Roger's sons Henry (chr. 1564) and Edmund (chr. 1577) are documented, but whether either of them fathered Lawrance Hough c. 1605 is undetermined here).

Confidence tier: **Moderate-to-Strong** (multiple independent transcriptions of the Wilmslow register entries; primary microfilm exists at Chester Record Office; Quaker migration records align). To advance to Strong, the images of the Wilmslow parish register pages (1653 baptism, 1680 marriage) must be viewed directly.

The vault file `John_Hough.md` already correctly identifies John Hough (b. 1682) as the son of John Hough (b. c. 1660) via Cheshire. The file `John_Hough_Colonial.md` (the 1642-born individual) is the one that needs reconciliation: that individual as recorded in the GEDCOM (1642 Yorkshire, d. 1659) appears to be a GEDCOM artifact conflating the actual immigrant John Hough (chr. 1653 Wilmslow, d. 1732 Bucks Co.) with imaginary or mistaken data. The John b. c. 1653 Wilmslow (the "Friendship" immigrant) IS the father of John b. 1682, and his own father was Lawrance Hough of Chorley.

## Best Hypothesis for the Colonial John Hough's English Parish/Village

**Parish**: Wilmslow (St. Bartholomew), Cheshire (Macclesfield Hundred).
**Township within parish**: Chorley (Lawrance Hough's recorded residence for the 1646 and 1653 baptisms).
**Hamlet of family origin**: "The Hough" (within Wilmslow parish; this is the namesake settlement of the surname and was Hannah Worthington's birthplace).
**Father**: Lawrance (Lawrence) Hough of Chorley (bur. Wilmslow 19 Nov 1668).
**Mother**: Not yet identified from primary sources in this session.
