## Biography

Sore Leja Kunjanska appears in the JRI-Poland Białystok Jewish vital register death index as the subject of **record #103F, 1866** (microfilm LDS 1191934), naming her father simply as "Gersz." See [[Kuniansky_JRI_Poland_Bialystok_2026_04_19]] for the full derivation and context.

Her father is the plausible [[Gersz_Konjanski_Bialystok]] (d. 1874 Białystok, son of Srol), on the inference that the two records refer to the same Gersz (shared surname, shared town, chronologically consistent with Sore Leja predeceasing her father by 8 years). This is not explicitly proven without the full register images, which would likely also give her age at death, her mother's given name, and the informant / witnesses.

**Relationship to the vault's Atlanta Kuniansky cluster**: originally suggested as an early-generation collateral relative in a speculative Białystok candidate ancestry for [[Wolf_Kuniansky]]; that candidate ancestry was **downgraded to unlikely on 2026-04-19** after independent sources placed the Atlanta cluster's origin in Podolia Gubernia / Vinnytsia region (Ukraine). Sore Leja also likely died as a child or young woman without descendants, so she would not be an ancestor even if the Białystok link had held. See [[Kuniansky_JRI_Poland_Bialystok_2026_04_19]] for the reassessment.

Retained as a stub person file to preserve the evidence of a distinct Białystok Konjanski family.
