# Moseley of Kinver, Staffordshire: Parents of Edward Moseley (bp. 1599)

## Goal

Identify the parents of Edward Moseley (baptized St Peter's, Kinver, 25 April 1599; died 1673/74), father of Capt William Moseley (1630, Virginia immigrant 1650), extending the Moseley line one generation back.

## Best Hypothesis (Moderate-to-Strong Signal)

**Edward Moseley (bp. 25 Apr 1599 Kinver, d. 1673/74) was the son of:**

- **Father:** Edward Moseley Sr., baptized St Peter's, Kinver, 6 February 1564/65.
- **Mother:** Elizabeth Brettell, married Edward Moseley Sr. at St Peter's, Kinver, on 14 November 1597.

**One generation further back:**

- **Paternal grandfather:** Thomas Moseley (Mosley) of Kinver, active 1560s.
- **Paternal grandmother:** Isabell Lyn.
- Thomas also had a son Humphrey Moseley baptized 7 May 1564 at St Peter's, Kinver (Edward's elder brother).

## Children of Edward Moseley Sr. and Elizabeth Brettell (per WikiTree transcription of Kinver register)

1. Edward (bp. 25 Apr 1599) — our ancestor, father of Capt William Moseley.
2. Elizabeth (bp. 1604).
3. William (c. 1606).
4. Anne (bp. 1609).
5. Jane (bp. 1612).
6. John (bp. 1627).

## Why This Is the Kinver Line and Not Rolleston Hall

The Kinver/Kinfare Moseleys are a distinct Staffordshire gentry branch. Sir Edward Mosley of Rolleston Hall (d. 1638) died without issue and is in eastern Staffordshire; Kinver is in the southwestern corner of the county, near Worcestershire. The 1539 Muster Roll and continuous Kinver parish register entries from the 1560s onward establish the Moseleys as rooted in Kinver independently of the Rolleston line.
