# Hypothesis: Pre-Colonial Origin of the Southern Clark Line (James Hiriam Clark c.1770 to Eli Berry Clark)

## Scope

This hypothesis covers the southern branch only: James Hiriam Clark (c.1770 VA) -> Jeddiah W. Clark (1801 SC) -> Eli Berry Clark / Sarah Elizabeth Clark (Greenville SC). It does NOT cover John Clarke Colonial of Great Munden, Hertfordshire (1558 to 1579), which is an unrelated line tied into the vault through the Stackhouse family.

## Statement

The southern Clark line descends from Scots-Irish (and possibly mixed English) backcountry stock that moved down the Great Wagon Road from Virginia (most likely the Valley of Virginia, Augusta County cluster, or the Southside VA / NC Piedmont fringe) into Chatham/Orange County, North Carolina in the mid 18th century, then on to Greenville District, South Carolina in the 1780s to 1790s as part of a chain migration alongside the Henderson, Cox, Baldwin, and Long families. James Hiriam Clark (b. c.1770, VA) most likely belongs to a Chatham/Orange NC Clark household that was itself a cadet branch of a Virginia Clark family.

## Evidence For

1. **Virginia birth for James Hiriam**: Tier 3 tree data (MyHeritage GEDCOM, Ancestry tree james-hiriam-clark-24-7cqh41) consistently places his birth in Virginia around 1770. Not primary, but internally consistent across multiple Tier 3 compilers.
2. **Chatham/Orange NC Clark cluster in the source community**:
   - Molly Mary Clark (1722 to 1804) married Argaleus Hercules Henderson I (c.1720 to 1806) and lived in Chatham County, NC (Geni, Find a Grave memorial 11725276).
   - Silas Clark (1765/66 to 1857) married Frances Henderson (Molly and Argaleus's daughter) on 6 Nov 1794 in Chatham County (WikiTree Clark-5473). Two documented Clark-Henderson marriages in the same community strongly argues for a resident Clark kin group.
3. **Chain migration into Greenville**: The Henderson, Cox, Baldwin, and Long families in vault files all exit Chatham/Orange NC for Greenville SC in the 1780s to 1810s (Eli Baldwin bought 250 acres in Greenville in 1812). James Hiriam's appearance in SC by 1800 (his son born there in 1801) fits this pattern exactly.
4. **Great Wagon Road mechanics**: The Wagon Road funnels Ulster-Scots from PA through the Valley of VA, then into NC Piedmont (Salisbury, Charlotte), and into Greenville SC. This is the known transport backbone for every other family in this cluster.
5. **Augusta VA Clark presence**: James B Clark (c.1701 to 1778) moved from Tidewater VA to Augusta County in 1740, served in colonial militia, bought Beverley Manor land in 1746, and left many descendants who spread south. This is a candidate upstream cluster.

## Evidence Against

1. **No primary source for James Hiriam**: No census, deed, tax list, or church record has been located. The "VA 1770 birth" rests entirely on derivative user trees.
2. **Silas Clark as an English-born immigrant**: One WikiTree source states he was born in England in 1765, which weakens the assumption that the Chatham NC Clarks are a single Scots-Irish kin group. If Silas is an unrelated English arrival, then the Chatham Clark-Henderson cluster may be two distinct Clark families who happened to both marry Hendersons.
3. **Mary Neale wife identity unsourced**: Neale is a distinctively English/Irish surname with heavy presence in both Virginia Tidewater and Ulster; it does not discriminate between hypotheses.
4. **Patronymic and common-name problem**: "James Clark" is among the five most common 18th-century American name combinations. Many candidate James Clarks appear in 1790 Chatham, Orange, Rowan, and Augusta records; most are not related.

Best single hypothesis at this stage: **Hypothesis 1 (Chatham/Orange NC, earlier Virginia)**, moderate confidence, Tier 3 only.

## Migration Path (Proposed)

Virginia (unknown county, possibly Valley of VA or Southside) -> Chatham or Orange County, NC (mid 1700s, as part of the Henderson-Cox-Baldwin neighborhood cluster) -> Greenville District, SC (by 1800, in time for Jeddiah's 1801 birth) -> Simpsonville/Standing Springs community (by 1812 alongside Eli Baldwin's land purchase).

## Relationship to John Clarke Colonial

The John Clarke (1558 to 1579, Great Munden, Hertfordshire) file in the vault is a pre-colonial English Clarke in a completely different branch that enters the vault through the Stackhouse -> Cowgill -> Sands -> Hough path. It is NOT an ancestor of James Hiriam Clark and should not be merged with the southern line. The two lines currently share only a surname.

## Specific Next Steps

1. **Greenville District SC 1800 and 1810 census**: Search for James Clark head of household aged 26 to 45 with wife and young sons. FamilySearch and Ancestry both have these indexed.
2. **Greenville County deed books, 1800 to 1835**: Search deed book indexes (Deed Book L and neighbors already online at genealogytrails.com) for James Clark grantor or grantee.
3. **Greenville County estate files, 1830 to 1840**: James Hiriam died after 1835; his estate, if probated, names heirs and often the place of origin.
4. **Chatham County NC marriage bonds**: Search pre-1800 bonds for any James Clark marrying a Mary Neale or Neal.
5. **Chatham County NC 1790 and 1800 census**: Compare Clark heads of household with the Henderson and Cox households; proximity is corroborating evidence.
6. **Augusta County VA tithables 1760 to 1780**: Check for a James Clark b. c.1770 in James B Clark's descendancy.
7. **DNA strategy**: Run Clark-line Y-DNA against the FamilyTreeDNA Clark(e) Project. If a match clusters with known Augusta VA or Chatham NC Clarks, that is the most decisive non-paper evidence available.
8. **Naming pattern check**: Jeddiah is an unusual name. Search Chatham NC, Orange NC, and Augusta VA pre-1820 vital records for other Jeddiah / Jedidiah Clarks (strong clue if found).
9. **Molly Mary Clark parents**: Identifying her parents may bracket the Chatham Clark kin group and clarify whether James Hiriam descends from it.
