# Brasher / Brashier Pre-Colonial Origin Research (2026-04-19)

## Scope

Investigate the pre-colonial origin of the vault's Brasher/Brashier family. Relevant vault files: [[Elizabeth_Brasher]], [[Mary_Brashier]], [[Brashier_Henderson]]. The working hypothesis recorded in [[Elizabeth_Brasher]] is a French Huguenot origin via Robert Brasseur (~1595-~1663, Nansemond VA then Calvert Co. MD), anglicized to Brashear. This file consolidates that hypothesis, weighs it against the competing English-origin hypothesis, and lays out next steps. No person file updates were made because no newly located Tier 2+ source names a specific pre-colonial ancestor beyond what the existing vault already documents.

## Hypothesis A: French Huguenot Origin (Brasseur -> Brashear -> Brasher)

Most genealogical sources, including the 532-page "A Brazier/Brasher Saga" by Brashear and McCoy, sonsofdewittcolony.org, WikiTree Brasseur-19/2/20, and Geni, place the immigrant ancestor as **Robert Brasseur** (~1595, Bouches-du-Rhone, France -> Isle of Thanet, Kent, England -> Nansemond Co., VA by 1635 -> Calvert Co., MD by ~1658).

Primary-source support located in this session (Tier 1):

- **VA Land Patents.** Robert Brasseur received a 600-acre patent Feb 24, 1638 on Warrisquicke Creek, Nansemond Co. (with Peter Rey); a 100-acre patent Oct 6, 1640, Upper Norfolk Co., west branch of Nansemond River; and a 1200-acre grant on April 12, 1653 in Nansemond Co.
- **Maryland Denization.** Charles Calvert's declaration of December 4, 1662, granting Benois Brafseuir (Robert's son) "free Dennizen" status in the Province of Maryland; original in Maryland Hall of Records, Council Proceedings.
- **Estate Record.** Inventory of goods and chattels of Benjamin Brassear, February 24, 1663/4, Maryland Archives Inventories Book 1E, p. 92.
- **Huguenot Recognition.** Benois Brasseur is listed in the registers of the American Huguenot Society; Robert and Benjamin Brashear appear in the Register of Huguenot Ancestors. Benjamin Brashear (1620-c.1663) is on the First Families of Maryland qualifying-ancestors list.

Etymology supporting French origin: **Brasseur** is a French occupational surname ("brewer," from Old French "brasser" = to brew), with family seats noted in Normandy. The Americanized forms Brashear/Brashears/Brashier/Brasher all appear in colonial records.

### Confidence of Hypothesis A

**Strong** for Robert Brasseur -> Benois Brasseur -> early Calvert Co. MD (primary sources exist and are cited by Huguenot Society).

**Moderate** for Benois/Robert III -> William B. Brasher Sr. (d. 1708, Baltimore Co.) -> Thomas Brasher Sr. (b. 1701).

**Speculative** for the full chain from Robert Brasseur all the way down to Elizabeth Brasher (wife of Ezekiel Henderson), because the Baltimore Co. bridge 1674-1708 has not been verified from primary records in this research.

## Hypothesis B: English Origin (Brasier/Brazier/Brasher)

The surname Brasher/Brasier/Brazier/Brashier has an **independent, well-attested English etymology**, separate from French Brasseur: from Old English pre-7th century "broesian"/"brasian" (to cast in brass), plus agent suffix "-er," giving an occupational name for a brass-worker. Earliest English records include:

- William Brasier, 1327 Subsidy Rolls of Essex.
- Thomas Brasyer, 1381 Assize Court Rolls of Cambridgeshire.
- Modern English concentration: Greater London 13%, West Midlands 8%, Kent 8%.

The Grokipedia entry on the Brashear surname explicitly warns: "Brashear is an Americanized form of French Brasseur, while Brasher can be a variant of Brazier or a variant of Brashear... No evidence supports claims of Anglo-Saxon primacy for the core Brashear stem; such assertions likely stem from conflation with unrelated English variants like Brasher." This is a two-way warning: the French-origin Brashear line should not be claimed as Anglo-Saxon, but equally, many "Brasher" families in the colonies may have English (not Huguenot) roots.

### Confidence of Hypothesis B

**Speculative as applied to the vault's Brasher line.** The English-origin path cannot be ruled out on surname evidence alone, because the phonetically identical Brasher/Brazier of 1327 Essex is a plausible source. However, the vault's specific Brasher cluster (Baltimore Co. MD 1700s -> Orange/Chatham Co. NC 1753+ -> Greenville Co. SC 1785+) shares clustering and kinship with families widely documented in the Brasseur/Brashear Huguenot tradition, so Hypothesis A currently has the stronger circumstantial fit.

## Best Current Hypothesis

**French Huguenot origin via Robert Brasseur of Calvert Co., MD.** Confidence: **Moderate** overall. The pre-1700 portion is Strong (primary sources and Huguenot Society registers); the 1700-1731 portion (Calvert -> Baltimore Co. bridge) is the weak link and should be labeled Speculative until verified.

## Migration Path (Consolidated)

1. **France (pre-1620):** Bouches-du-Rhone region; Huguenot persecution.
2. **Kent, England (~1620s-1635):** Isle of Thanet; several Brasseur children born there.
3. **Virginia (1635-1658):** Isle of Wight and then Nansemond County; land patents 1636, 1638, 1640, 1653.
4. **Calvert County, Maryland (1658-~1700):** Anglicized to Brashear; denization 1662; Huguenot Society recognition.
5. **Baltimore County, Maryland (~1674-1753):** William B. Brasher Sr. (d. 1708) and son Thomas Brasher Sr. (b. 1701); St. George's Church marriage 1726.
6. **Orange County, NC (1753-1770s):** Thomas Brasher Sr. moved ~1753; will May 17, 1770, probated 1784.
7. **Chatham County, NC (1771-1785):** Formed from Orange Co. 1771; 1782 NC land grants; Aquilla and Thomas sold land 1784.
8. **Greenville County, SC (1785+):** Thomas Levi Jr. and brothers took up land on Cripple Creek/Reedy River; 1790 census shows large family cluster.
9. **Further dispersal (1806+):** Christian Co., KY (Elijah, Thomas, Aquilla, John); Shelby/Talladega Co., AL (James, Henry Collins ~1817); DeKalb Co., GA (Samuel descendants); Fayette Co., AL (Lawrence Aquilla and Frances Henderson Brasher 1820+). Thomas Levi III remained in Greenville; his descendants adopted the "Brashier" spelling.

## Convergence on the Henderson Line

Elizabeth Brasher (~1764/1765-~1839) married Ezekiel Henderson on Oct 10, 1782, likely in Chatham Co., NC. This brings the Brasher line (Huguenot-MD-NC) into the Henderson line (Scottish/English, Chatham Co. NC). Their son Brashier Henderson (b. May 10, 1790) was given his mother's surname as a first name, preserving the Brasher identity. Brashier's son Rev. Henry Langford Henderson (1812-1886) married his first cousin Rebecca Adeline Cox (daughter of Olive Mary Henderson), further consolidating the Henderson/Cox/Brasher cluster in Simpsonville/Standing Springs, Greenville Co., SC.

Mary (Eliza) Brashier (b. 1806), who married Lewis P. Huff, is most plausibly a granddaughter of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. via Thomas Levi III (the only son who remained in Greenville and whose descendants adopted the "Brashier" spelling). See [[Mary_Brashier]] parentage hypothesis table.
