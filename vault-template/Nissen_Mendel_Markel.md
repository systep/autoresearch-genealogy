## Biography

Nissen Mendel Markel is the earliest known ancestor of the Markel family. He is identified in the Yichus narrative (Tier 2, primary family document by Ruth Gold, Oct 9, 2011) as the father of **[[Dov_Markel]]** and the grandfather of **[[Heschel_Herman_Markel]]** (Heshel Markel; b. 1862; d. 1916).

The patrilineal line established by the Yichus narrative is: Nissen Mendel Markel to Dov Markel to Heshel Markel (Tier 2).

No dates, occupation, or other biographical details are provided for Nissen Mendel. The family was located in Vanyavitch, Galicia (near Lemberg/Lviv, Austria/Poland, now Ukraine), but it is unknown whether Nissen Mendel himself lived there.

Confidence: Low. Single Tier 2 source (Yichus narrative) provides only the name and position in the patrilineal chain. No corroboration from independent sources.

### Waniowice/Vanyavitch Village

Waniowice is confirmed as a real village near Sambor in Galicia. A German genealogy forum (Ahnenforschung.net) discusses "Tarnawa nizn, Szandrowiec und Waniowice bei Sambor in Galizien," confirming the village's location within the Sambor administrative district. The 1910 Galician census (Galizien Zensus 1910) lists Waniowice among Galician localities. No specific Jewish community records for Waniowice were found; Jews in small villages typically belonged to the nearest town's kahal. Based on the Sambor kehilla structure (which had jurisdiction over Jews in surrounding villages), Jews of Waniowice most likely belonged to the **Sambor kahal**. The Sambor kehilla was led by a board of 12 parnassim elected annually, and had compulsory authority over all Jews in the town and surrounding villages.

### Sambor District Jewish Records

Austrian civil registration began in 1784, requiring clergy to record births, marriages, and deaths. Jewish registers in Galicia were maintained under Catholic supervision from 1789, though most surviving Jewish registers date from the 1830s or later. Sambor district Jewish vital records are held in two main archives:

1. **Central State Historical Archives of Ukraine in Lviv (TsDIAL)**: Fond 701, Series 1. Contains bound volumes of vital records, index books, and loose vital records. Gesher Galicia has completed and uploaded indexes for Sambor records to the All Galicia Database (free, searchable online).

2. **AGAD (Archiwum Glowne Akt Dawnych), Warsaw**: Holds approximately 1,750 Jewish metrical books from the former Lwow, Stanislawow, and Tarnopol regions, primarily covering 1877 to 1911.

FamilySearch has microfilmed Jewish records from TsDIAL. Gesher Galicia's All Galicia Database is the primary free search tool for indexed Sambor records. JRI-Poland also has a Sambor town page.

### Isserles and Landau Family Connections (from Yichus)

The Yichus narrative mentions "Issarles and Landau" as family connections. On Geni.com:
- **Rabbi Moses Isserles (Rema, c. 1530 to 1572)**: 440 Isserles profiles on Geni. The Rema was a major Ashkenazic rabbi in Krakow, author of the Mappah (glosses on the Shulchan Aruch). His grandfather was Jehiel Luria, first Rabbi of Brisk. He studied under Rabbi Shalom Shachna in Lublin, alongside his relative Solomon Luria (Maharshal) and Chayyim ben Bezalel (brother of the Maharal of Prague).
- **Landau rabbinical family**: 12,656 Landau profiles on Geni. The most famous member is Rav Yechezkel Landau (the "Noda B'Yehudah," 1713 to 1793), born in Apta, Poland. Rabbi Moshe Landau served alongside the Rema in the Krakow Beit Din. Isaac Landau served as Rabbi of Krakow 1754 to 1769.

The Isserles and Landau families intermarried extensively with other major rabbinical dynasties (Horowitz, Jaffe, Luria, Katzenellenbogen). A claimed connection to these families from a Galician Jewish family like the Markels is plausible but unverified. Many Galician Jewish families claimed (sometimes correctly, sometimes not) descent from the Rema or other major rabbinical figures. Without specific genealogical documentation of the link, this remains **speculative (Tier 3)**.
