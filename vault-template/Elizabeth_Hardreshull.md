## Biography

Elizabeth de Hardreshull was a daughter and co-heiress of Sir John de Hardreshull of Hardreshull in Warwickshire. Through her marriage to Sir John Colepeper (c. 1305 to 1376), she brought the Hardreshull estates in Warwickshire into the Culpeper family, augmenting their existing holdings in Kent (WikiTree Hardreshall-1, Tier 3; University of Michigan genealogy, Tier 3).

Elizabeth's father, Sir John de Hardreshull, was a prominent military figure who served as a **Knight Banneret** under Edward, Prince of Wales (the Black Prince) at the Battle of Crecy in 1346 (WikiTree Hardreshull-2, Tier 3). He held the barony of Hache through his mother Juliana de Hache, daughter and heiress of Eustace de Hache, 1st Baron Hache (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Elizabeth's mother is listed in different sources as either Margaret de Stafford (daughter of James de Stafford of Sandon, Staffordshire) or Maud Mussenden (WikiTree, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3). Sir John de Hardreshull married at least twice: first to Margaret de Stafford, and second to Maud Mussenden (some sources reverse the order). Elizabeth was a daughter from one of these marriages.

## The Hardreshull Family: Noble Connections

The Hardreshull family connects to several notable medieval lines:

1. **Neville family**: Sir John de Hardreshull's grandmother was Joan de Neville, daughter of Sir Philip de Neville of Scotton, Lincolnshire (WikiTree, Tier 3). The Nevilles were one of England's most powerful noble families.

2. **Barony of Hache**: Through Juliana de Hache (Sir John's mother), the Hardreshulls inherited the Barony of Hache. Eustace de Hache, 1st Baron Hache, fought at Falkirk (1298), was present at the Siege of Caerlaverock (1300), and was a signatory of the Barons' Letter to Pope Boniface VIII (1301) (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

3. **Stafford family**: Margaret de Stafford was the daughter of James de Stafford of Sandon, a cadet branch of the Stafford barony. James's father William de Stafford descended from Hervey Bagot of Bramshall and Melicent de Stafford, heiress of the Stafford Barony (Geni, Tier 3).

## Connection to This Family Tree

Elizabeth Hardreshull married [[John_Colepeper_Hardreshull]], whose son Thomas of Bayhall continued the Culpeper line. The descent runs: Thomas of Bayhall to John Colepeper to [[Walter_Colepeper_Calais]] to [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]] to [[Martin_Culpepper]] to the Sandys/Sands line.

Through Elizabeth, the Culpeper family gained connections to the Neville, Hache, and Stafford families.
