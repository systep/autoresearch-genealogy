## Biography

Hyman Salk was born March 15, 1873 in Vilkomer (Ukmerge), Lithuania (GEDCOM, unverified). He was a son of [[Louis_Elizar_Salk]] and [[Rose_Salk]], and a brother of [[Abraham_Zolly_Salk]] (father of [[Elizabeth_Salk]], who married [[Barnett_Salmanson]]).

**Important note**: This Hyman Salk (b. 1873) is likely the same person as the Hyman Salk who founded Salk's Hardware in Providence circa 1900. The Abraham Zolly Salk vault file notes that a "Hyman Salk (immigrant, purchased tailor shop in Providence 1900, founder of Salk's Hardware)" was documented separately. If this identification is correct, it would mean the Salk's Hardware family and the Abraham Salk (Elizabeth's father) family are brothers, both sons of Louis (Elizar) Salk. This is based on GEDCOM data (Tier 3) and requires independent verification.

The GEDCOM shows Hyman's children included [[Samuel_Henry_Salk]] (b. October 16, 1899, Newark, NJ; d. January 8, 1971, Providence, RI; Manager, General Store) and Louis Salk (b. April 14, 1898, Newark, NJ; d. November 1979, Rhode Island). The Newark birthplaces for his children suggest Hyman may have lived in New Jersey before settling in Providence.

Hyman died February 22, 1949 in Providence, Rhode Island.
