## Biography

Roxanne Cox was born February 21, 1865, the daughter of **Thomas Manning Cox** (1828-1897) and **Sarah Elizabeth "Lizzie" Clark** (1836-1879). Her given name appears in several variant spellings: Rooksaner, Roxana, and Roxanne. She was part of the Cox family rooted in the Simpsonville and Standing Springs community of Greenville County, South Carolina.

Her father Thomas Manning Cox was the son of [[Thomas_B_Cox]] (1792-1857) and Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson, placing Roxanne in the direct descent from the colonial Cox line: William Cox Sr. (c. 1730-1814) migrated from Orange County, North Carolina to Greenville County, South Carolina in the 1780s with intermarried families including the Longs.

Her mother Sarah Elizabeth Clark died July 5, 1879 at Standing Springs Church, Simpsonville, when Roxanne was 14 years old. Sarah's parents were Jedediah W Clark and Gilly Elizabeth Clark (born Baldwin) (MyHeritage).

Roxanne had several siblings including Perry Franklin Cox, Alexander J Cox, John Andrew Cox, Giddie Cox, William L Cox, Manning Cox, and Florence Cox (MyHeritage, Thomas Manning Cox profile).

She married **William Goldsmith Long** (1862-1937), son of William Thomas Long (1825-1887) and Mary Elizabeth Huff (1838-1922). This marriage united the Cox and Long families, which had migrated together from North Carolina to South Carolina in the 1780s. The original Cox-Long intermarriage was Thomas Long married to Rachel Cox, a daughter of William Cox Sr.

Their daughter **Rebecca Moseley Long** (Sept 14, 1898 - 1985) married Albert Graham Bright (1893-1961). Through this marriage, Roxanne is the great grandmother of Kathryn Ann Kascher, who married Alan Carl Salmanson.

Roxanne died December 5, 1912 at age 47, when her daughter Rebecca was 14.
