# Hypothesis: English Origin of the Virginia Moseleys before 1660

## Question

Who was the first Moseley / Mousley / Mosley to arrive in colonial Virginia before 1660, and is that person an ancestor (or otherwise relevant to) the vault's [[William_Moseley_1649]] (c. 1605 to before 1655, Lower Norfolk County, Virginia)?

## Candidates Identified

Three distinct pre-1660 Moseley arrivals are attested in Tier 2 printed sources.

### 1. Joseph Mosley / Moseley, servant, arrived 1623 on the Providence

- Listed in the Musters of Virginia, 1624/25 as a 21 year old servant of Daniell Gookins, resident at Bass's Choice.
- Arrived in Virginia 1623 aboard the Providence.
- Source: 1624/25 Virginia Muster (cited via WikiTree Space "The very earliest Moseleys in America", Tier 3 summary of a Tier 1 muster record; also searchable at Virtual Jamestown).
- Earliest documentable Moseley in Virginia.
- No known subsequent record; likely died young or returned to England. Not plausibly the direct ancestor of William Moseley b. 1605 (who is of the same generation).

### 2. William Moseley, gent., arrived 1649 from Rotterdam to Lower Norfolk County

- This is the vault's [[William_Moseley_1649]].
- Born c. 1605, possibly baptized St Margaret's, Westminster, London, 15 or 17 December 1606 (unverified, contested).
- Schafmeister / Steward and Treasurer of the "English House" of the Merchant Adventurers in Delft and Rotterdam, 1635 to 1649.
- Arrived Lower Norfolk, Virginia, 30 November 1649 with wife Susanna (Burnet), sons William and Arthur, a stepdaughter, and six other persons; 550 acre headright granted 26 March 1650.
- Built estate called "Rolleston Hall" in Lynnhaven Parish, Lower Norfolk. The naming evidently inspired the long standing but contested claim that he descended from the Mosleys of Rolleston on Dove, Staffordshire.

### 3. Captain William Moseley, arrived 1650, of Old Rappahannock County

- Distinct individual from #2 above.
- Listed in George Cabell Greer, Early Virginia Immigrants 1623-1666, as arriving Virginia 1650, sponsored by his brother in law William Underwood.
- Married Martha Brasseur / Brashiers (likely Huguenot) before 31 January 1659/60, Old Rappahannock County.
- On 18 November 1663, acquired 1,157 acres in Old Rappahannock County by headright for transporting 27 persons (this is a Nugent "Cavaliers and Pioneers" entry).
- Burgess of Virginia for Rappahannock County, 1671 to 1674.
- His sister Mary (Moseley) Underwood was mother of William Underwood.
- Progenitor, through his grandson William "the Opportunist" Moseley III, of the Moseley lines that spread into North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, Alabama, Tennessee, Missouri, and Texas in the 18th and early 19th centuries. This is the most promising candidate for a connection to the vault's Surry / Brunswick County line via Jonathan Jonas James Moseley b. 1683.

## Likely English County of Origin

### For William Moseley of Lower Norfolk (1649 arrival)

- Staffordshire / Rolleston on Dove: refuted. Sir Edward Mosley (1569 to 1638) was the first Mosley at Rolleston Hall and died without issue (WikiTree; see also Dugdale's Staffordshire Visitation pedigrees). The "Rolleston Hall" estate name in Virginia is a commemorative or aspirational naming, not evidence of descent.
- London / Westminster: moderate signal. Two candidate baptisms at St Margaret's, Westminster (15 December 1606 and 17 December 1606) are frequently cited but not conclusively linked.
- Continental merchant network: strong signal. William spent 1633 to 1649 in Delft and Rotterdam serving the Company of Merchant Adventurers. A London origin is consistent with that Company's membership catchment.

Most likely English origin: London / Home Counties, connected through the Company of Merchant Adventurers. Not Staffordshire.

### For Captain William Moseley of Old Rappahannock (1650 arrival)

- No firm English county identified from the sources consulted in this session.
- Connection via brother in law William Underwood and marriage to a Huguenot-descended Brasseur wife suggests possible London or Kent / Essex mercantile networks.

## Best Candidate 17th Century Immigrant Ancestor

For the vault's William Moseley 1649, he is the immigrant; the open question is his English father, not a prior immigrant.

For the vault's probable Surry / Brunswick line (Jonathan Jonas James Moseley b. 1683 Southwarke Parish, Surry County), the best candidate immigrant ancestor is Captain William Moseley of Old Rappahannock (arrived 1650), not William Moseley of Lower Norfolk (arrived 1649). The two have been conflated in tertiary trees. The Rappahannock line is explicitly documented as spreading into the southern Piedmont, matching the Jonathan Moseley 1683 Surry / Brunswick pattern.

## Confidence Level

Moderate (for the three-candidate identification above) to Speculative (for any specific English parentage claim).
