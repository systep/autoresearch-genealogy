# Hypothesis: Robert King II emigrated 1770 via Larne on the ship Jupiter

## Claim Under Test

Robert King II (b. c. 1756, Ireland; d. 1826, Anderson County, SC) emigrated to North America in 1770 on the ship Jupiter, which departed Larne, County Antrim, arriving at either Philadelphia or Charleston, after which the family settled in the Pendleton District / Anderson County, SC by 1772.

## Status After Round 3 Corroboration (2026-04-19)

Status: **Open, partially refuted on specifics**. Core migration (Ireland 1770, Anderson SC by 1772) is consistent across multiple Tier 2/3 sources, but three sub-claims now face contradicting evidence from historical record of the period:

1. Ship name "Jupiter" is almost certainly a conflation with the well-documented Derry to Philadelphia 1772 voyage.
2. Port of departure "Larne" is undocumented for 1770; all known Larne emigrant sailings to SC are 1772 (Rev. William Martin flotilla).
3. Port of arrival is inconsistent across Tier 3 sources: Charleston (implied by SC settlement), Philadelphia (ship Jupiter's actual destination in 1772), or Baltimore/Chesapeake (smokykin.com, mytrees.com).

## Corroboration Attempted (Round 3)

Eight WebSearches run 2026-04-19 targeting primary sources for (a) ship Jupiter 1770 manifest, (b) Larne 1770 passenger list, (c) Philadelphia/Charleston/Baltimore 1770 arrival, (d) Robert King 1790 Pendleton / Washington District / Ninety-Six District census entry, (e) NC militia and DAR records.

### Result 1: No Larne 1770 passenger list exists

No centralised emigration records were kept in Ireland or Britain for this period. Where passenger lists exist, they are arrival records held by the destination port. No list has been located for a Larne 1770 departure carrying the King family.

### Result 2: The ship Jupiter sailed from Derry, not Larne, and in 1772, not 1770

Brian Mitchell's research (Derry Journal, "Derry to Philadelphia: The Irish Gateway to America") documents the Jupiter as completing the fastest recorded Atlantic crossing of the era, Derry to Philadelphia in 27 days, in 1772. Of 128 vessels advertised to sail from Derry 1750 to 1775, 99 went to Philadelphia and 10 to Charleston. No Larne departure for any ship named Jupiter in 1770 is attested in any source searched.

### Result 3: All documented Larne emigrant ships to Charleston date to 1772, not 1770

Jean Stephenson's Scotch-Irish Migration to South Carolina, 1772: Reverend William Martin And His Five Shiploads of Settlers documents the only large organised Scots-Irish migration from the Larne area to Charleston in this period. Ships:

| Ship | Departure Port | Departed | Arrived Charleston |
|---|---|---|---|
| James and Mary | Larne | 25 Aug 1772 | 16 Oct 1772 |
| Lord Dunluce | Larne | 4 Oct 1772 | 20 Dec 1772 |
| Hopewell | Belfast | 1772 | Dec 1772 |
| Pennsylvania Farmer | Newry | 1772 | 1772 |
| Free Mason | Newry | 1772 | 1772 |

None sailed in 1770. If the King family arrived at Charleston with the Martin migration, the correct year is 1772, not 1770, and the ship is almost certainly James and Mary or Lord Dunluce.

### Result 4: Baltimore/Chesapeake arrival alternative

smokykin.com (Smoky Mountain Ancestral Quest, TNG ID I352185): "The ship landed in the Baltimore, MD area, probably around Annapolis or somewhere in the Chesapeake Bay area." mytrees.com echoes this. Both are Tier 3, but they offer a third competing arrival hypothesis that should be tested before accepting the Charleston/Philadelphia narrative.

### Result 5: 1790 Pendleton County SC census has King entries

The S-K Publications 1790 Census Index of Pendleton County lists the surname KING on pages 7 and 10 of the original index. Specific given names and household composition were not visible in free search snippets. The printed index or the USGenWeb transcription at files.usgwarchives.net/sc/pendleton/census/pend1790.txt must be consulted directly to confirm Robert King's household in Pendleton in 1790.

## Revised Working Hypotheses (Ranked)

### H-1 (now most likely): 1772 Rev. William Martin migration, Larne to Charleston

Robert King II sailed with the Rev. William Martin flotilla in 1772 (likely James and Mary departing Larne 25 August 1772, or Lord Dunluce departing Larne 4 October 1772), arriving Charleston October or December 1772, and settled in the Pendleton/Ninety-Six District backcountry within months. This reconciles the "1770" date (off by 2 years in oral tradition, a common drift), the "Larne" origin, the "Antrim Scots-Irish Presbyterian" profile, and the SC backcountry settlement. Confidence: **Moderate, pending primary source**.

### H-2: 1770 Chesapeake arrival, later overland migration to SC

Robert King II arrived in the Chesapeake (Baltimore or Annapolis) in 1770 on an unidentified ship (not Jupiter), then moved south along the Great Wagon Road to the Carolina backcountry, arriving Pendleton area c. 1772. This fits the smokykin.com and mytrees.com tradition but not the Larne/Antrim origin. Confidence: **Speculative**.

### H-3: 1772 Derry to Philadelphia on Jupiter, overland to SC

Robert King II sailed on the actual historical Jupiter (Derry to Philadelphia, 1772), then migrated overland down the Great Wagon Road to SC. This fits the ship name but contradicts the Larne origin. Confidence: **Speculative**.
