### Surname Analysis

The surname "Salk" is an Americanized form of **Zalk**, a Jewish name from Lithuania (Ancestry.com, Geneanet). The name Zalk is most likely a pet form of the Yiddish personal name **Zalkind**, which is itself the German Yiddish kinui (nickname) for the Hebrew name **Shlomo (Solomon)**. The name "Elizar" or "Lazar" (Louis's alternate name) is the Yiddish form of the Hebrew name Eleazar.

A **Zalk family from Troskunai (Trashkun), Lithuania** is documented on JewishGen KehilaLinks, with the earliest known member being Leyzer Zalk. His son Zalkind Zalk married Freyda, and their son Samuel Zalk (b. ~1830) later emigrated to America. Some members of this family settled in **Duluth, Minnesota**. Louis Salk's Lithuanian origin and approximate birth year (~1840) place him in the same generation and region as the Troskunai Zalks, but no direct connection has been established.
