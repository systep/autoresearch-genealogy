## Biography

John Stackhouse (Johannis Stackhouse) was born in 1559 and baptized on January 15, 1559, at Giggleswick, Yorkshire, England (WikiTree Stackhouse-57, citing Church of England parish records). He married Ellen Clark (also recorded as "Ellen Armitstead J Stackhouse, born Clarke") on October 28, 1588/1599, at Giggleswick (FamilySearch, Tier 3; dates vary by source).

John and Ellen had numerous children, including Thomas Stackhouse (b. 1601, the father of Ellen Stackhouse who married Ralph Cowgill), James Stackhouse (b. c. 1603), Isabella, Alice (m. Hayhurst), Richard, Anthony, Henry, and Robert (FamilySearch, Tier 3).

John Stackhouse is described in Genealogie Online as "Lord of Stackhouse" (Tier 3, unverified), referring to the Stackhouse hamlet near Giggleswick in the West Riding of Yorkshire. He died on November 5, 1663, in Giggleswick.

The will of John Stackhouse, son of James and Agnes Stackhouse, names sons Thomas, Henry, Robert, and James (noted in FamilySearch records attached to GEDCOM).

The Stackhouse family were Quakers (Society of Friends) and several of John's descendants emigrated to Pennsylvania in the 1680s, including his granddaughter Ellen Stackhouse (who married Ralph Cowgill) and his grandson Thomas Stackhouse Sr.
