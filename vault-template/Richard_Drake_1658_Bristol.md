## Biography (Working Narrative, Partly Speculative)

Richard Drake was born about 1610-1620, likely in South Petherton Parish, Somerset, England, though his baptism has not yet been located in the surviving parish registers (which cover 1598-1639 and resume 1650; his birth falls within the 1598-1639 range if he was indeed baptized at Ss Peter and Paul, South Petherton).

He was by trade a serge maker, an occupation of the Somerset wool textile industry. He married a woman named Thomazine (surname unknown; marriage presumed between 1640 and 1647, likely falling in the 1640-1649 gap in the South Petherton register). The couple is reported to have been resident in South Petherton Parish from 1647 to 1650. A Drake child was reportedly baptized at South Petherton on 7 June 1650 (child's identity pending direct confirmation from the baptism register).

By 1658 the family had relocated to Bristol in Gloucestershire. On 6 September 1658, Richard indentured himself (3 year term), his wife Thomazine (4 years), and children Mary (4 years), John (10 years), and Thomas (12 years) to Andrew Ball, mariner, for transport to the Virginia colony. The indenture is recorded in the Bristol servants register, now held at Bristol Archives (ref. 04220) and transcribed in Peter Wilson Coldham's The Bristol Registers of Servants Sent to Foreign Plantations 1654-1686 (1988).

After arrival in Virginia, Richard is reported (by secondary sources) to have settled in Isle of Wight County, continued his trade as a serge maker, and acquired several parcels of land. He is said to have died circa 1689. These Virginia details require independent verification from Virginia colonial records (Isle of Wight County deed books, will books, and land patents; Cavaliers and Pioneers by Nell Marion Nugent).
