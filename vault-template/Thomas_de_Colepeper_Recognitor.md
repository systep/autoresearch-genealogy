## Biography

Sir Thomas de Colepeper, known as "the Recognitor," is the earliest documented ancestor of the Culpeper/Colepeper family. He was born about 1170 and was one of the Recognitores Magnae Assisae (Recognitors of the Grand Assize), an important judicial office during the reign of King John (WikiTree Culpepper-144, Tier 3; FamilySearch GH65-195, Tier 3).

The Grand Assize was a judicial proceeding or inquiry established by Henry II as an alternative to trial by combat in disputes over land rights. The Recognitors served as jurors who investigated all cases involving questions of right. As Recognitors were probably neighbors of the disputing parties, they were bound to "recognize" and speak the truth concerning the matter at issue (WikiTree Culpepper-144, Tier 3).

Thomas de Colepeper is described as "the first of the family of whom there is any mention" (culpepperconnections.com, Tier 3). The use of "de" in the surname, combined with the Norman French form "Colepeper," strongly suggests Norman origins, consistent with the Norman-dominated gentry in post-Conquest England (culpepperconnections.com, Tier 3). According to Culpepper Connections, the name "de Colepeper" likely derives from the first de Colepeper living at "the bottom of Pepenbury," a location in Kent.

He lived in Brenchley, Kent, which became the seat of the Colepeper family for subsequent generations. His death is placed at approximately 1247.

Confidence: Moderate. Multiple user-contributed genealogical sources (WikiTree, Geni, FamilySearch, culpepperconnections.com) agree on the basic outline. No primary source documents from the 12th to 13th century have been directly cited in these profiles, though the Grand Assize records are historical documents.
