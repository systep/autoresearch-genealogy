# Long Family Pre-Colonial Origin Research

**Research date:** 2026-04-19
**Scope:** Identify the pre-colonial origin (English, Scots-Irish, or other) of the Long family associated with the Greenville County SC / Standing Springs Baptist Church / Simpsonville cluster, as represented in the vault by [[Larkin_Long]] (c. 1801-1884), [[William_Thomas_Long]] (1825-1887), [[William_Goldsmith_Long]] (1862-1937), [[James_Louis_Long]] (1883-1965), [[Thomas_Clemson_Long]] (1890-1891), and others.

## Summary of Findings

The vault Long family almost certainly entered Greenville County, SC as part of a documented 1787 group migration from Orange County, North Carolina, led by William Cox Sr. The Long family is explicitly named in secondary Cox family research as one of several intermarried families (Longs, Blacks, Paynes/Paines, Lees, Brashiers/Brazures/Breaziers) that accompanied the Coxes from the Haw River / Cain Creek area of Orange County NC to what is now Simpsonville SC. This is a Tier 3 secondary claim, but it is internally consistent with the vault's existing William_Goldsmith_Long.md note stating that Long and Cox families "had migrated together from Orange County, North Carolina to Greenville County, South Carolina in the 1780s."

A pre-1787 (pre-Orange-County-NC) ancestor has not been identified. The most likely pre-colonial origin is English or Scots-Irish, arriving via the standard Great Wagon Road path from Pennsylvania through the Shenandoah Valley of Virginia into the Carolina Piedmont, 1740-1780.

## Records Found (Sparse)

### Direct hits for the vault's Long family

None. No WebSearch hit returned a primary or secondary source naming Larkin Long (c. 1801), William Thomas Long (1825), or any immediate ancestor of Larkin. Searches on Larkin Long, William Goldsmith Long, Clementine Long, and Standing Springs Long all returned zero specific matches.

### Indirect hits (migration context)

1. **Cox Family History Project** (Google Sites, Tier 3 secondary compilation): documents that William Cox Sr. sold 110 acres on Cain Creek and Haw River in Orange County NC on 13 October 1787 (Orange Deed Book 4), and moved to Greenville County SC later that year. The compilation explicitly states: "along with a number of other families such as the Lees, Brashiers, Longs and Blacks, settling around what is now Simpsonville, South Carolina." Source: https://sites.google.com/site/coxfamilyhistoryproject/genealogy-data/william-cox

2. **Standing Springs Baptist Church** (hmdb.org, sentinelinternet.com): organized 18 September 1818 as an arm of Fork Shoals Baptist Church. Ten charter members are listed (Henderson, Mayhall, Moore, Richardson, Robbins); **no Long is among the ten charter members**. Early meetings held in the home of James Cox (reinforcing the Cox-centered neighborhood the Longs joined). First meeting house constructed c. 1836 on Cox land.

3. **Scots-Irish/English migration context** (NCpedia, carolana.com, Wikipedia): the dominant migration stream into the Carolina Piedmont 1740-1780 was Scots-Irish and English Baptist settlers traveling from Pennsylvania down the Great Valley of Virginia. English Baptists specifically from central Virginia populated the Piedmont after the French and Indian War (1754-1763).

4. **Goldsmith collateral** (WikiTree Goldsmith-699, Geni, MontyHistNotes): William Thomas Goldsmith Sr. (b. 24 June 1762, Richmond/Henrico, Virginia; d. 21 March 1835, Simpsonville, Greenville SC) fought at Kings Mountain and moved Union County SC → Greenville County SC. This gives a firm Virginia-to-SC vector for the Goldsmith side (Sarah Goldsmith = Larkin Long's wife), which strengthens the inference that the spousal family was of English Virginia Tidewater/Piedmont stock. By the principle of geographic endogamy (people married their neighbors), the Long family that produced Larkin was in the same 1780s-1800s Simpsonville social orbit.

## Best Hypothesis

**Primary hypothesis (Moderate / Speculative split):**

The vault's Long family is of **English or Scots-Irish origin**, most likely arriving in British North America in the first half of the 18th century, migrating south via the Great Wagon Road (Pennsylvania → Shenandoah Valley of Virginia → Carolina Piedmont), and settling in Orange County NC (Haw River / Cain Creek area) by the 1760s-1770s. In 1787 the family joined the William Cox Sr. group migration to Greenville County SC, settling in what is now Simpsonville. Larkin Long (b. c. 1801, SC) was therefore a second-generation SC native whose parents or grandparents were Orange County NC residents.

- Confidence: **Speculative** for the specific Orange County NC claim (rests on one Tier 3 secondary source, the Cox Family History Project). Would upgrade to **Moderate** upon locating a Long surname in Orange County NC deed/tax records 1770-1787 AND a corresponding Long household in 1790 Greenville SC census.
- Confidence: **Moderate** for the general "English or Scots-Irish Piedmont stock via Virginia" framing, on the basis of demographic prior probability for the region and era.

**Alternative hypotheses to keep open:**

1. **Direct Virginia-to-SC migration** (bypassing NC), parallel to the Goldsmith path (Henrico VA → Union SC → Greenville SC). Would look like Long households appearing in Union District SC records c. 1780s-1790s.
2. **Maryland progenitor via David Long of Queen Anne's County** (flagged by Long Surname DNA Project). Low prior; no evidence yet ties the Greenville cluster to this line. Would require Y-DNA testing of a male-line descendant.
3. **German "Lang" anglicized to "Long"**, arriving via Pennsylvania Dutch migration into the NC Piedmont. Possible but less likely given the Baptist (not Lutheran/Reformed) religious affiliation suggested by the Standing Springs connection.

## VA-to-SC Migration Window

| Window | Path | Likelihood |
|---|---|---|
| 1740-1760 | PA → Shenandoah VA → Orange NC | Plausible arrival of Long progenitor in NC |
| 1760-1780 | Orange NC settled; children born | Likely generation of Larkin's grandparents |
| **1787** | **Orange NC → Greenville SC with Cox group** | **Documented (Tier 3) for "the Longs" collectively** |
| 1787-1801 | Greenville SC settlement; Larkin born c. 1801 | Matches vault record |

Best single-year anchor: **1787**, the Cox-Long-Black-Lee-Payne-Brashier migration from Cain Creek / Haw River, Orange County NC to the Simpsonville area of Greenville County SC.
