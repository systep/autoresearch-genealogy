## Biography

James Youngblood is identified in the GEDCOM file as the father of William Henry Youngblood. The GEDCOM records him as born in South Carolina, USA, with his wife Mary Bailey born in Alabama. This data was added via a WikiTree Smart Match on February 28, 2026 (GEDCOM reference number 38450).

A WikiTree profile (Youngblood-746) for a James Youngblood (c. 1740 to 1792) documents a man of the same name who lived in Johnston County, North Carolina, and later Edgefield County, South Carolina. That James Youngblood bought 100 acres on Swift Creek in Johnston County, NC, on November 7, 1766, later sold that land and obtained a 250 acre grant in Chatham County on the South Fork of Rocky River. After the Revolution, he relocated to Edgefield County, South Carolina, purchasing 200 acres on Steven's Creek. His wife is recorded on WikiTree as Mary ("Polly") Bradley (WikiTree Youngblood-746, Tier 3).

Whether the GEDCOM James Youngblood is the same person as WikiTree Youngblood-746 is unclear. The WikiTree James died c. 1792, and William H. Youngblood was born in 1828, leaving a 36 year gap that makes a direct father/son relationship unlikely unless there is an intermediate generation. The GEDCOM James may be a son or grandson of the WikiTree James.
