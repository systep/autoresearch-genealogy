## Biography

Margaret de Clare was born in 1292, the eldest daughter of [[Gilbert_de_Clare]], 6th Earl of Gloucester, and [[Joan_Plantagenet]], daughter of King [[Edward_I]]. She was thus a granddaughter of Edward I (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Margaret first married Piers Gaveston, 1st Earl of Cornwall and the notorious favorite of Edward II, in 1307. Gaveston was executed in 1312 by a faction of barons. Margaret then married [[Hugh_de_Audley]] in 1317. Hugh eventually received a share of the De Clare inheritance and was created Earl of Gloucester in 1337 (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Margaret and Hugh de Audley had a daughter, Margaret de Audley, who married [[Ralph_Neville]] of Raby Castle. This connection is the key link in the pedigree charts between the De Clare/Plantagenet royal line and the Neville family, which subsequently connects to colonial American families (Pedigree Charts).

Margaret de Clare died on April 9, 1342 (Wikipedia, Tier 2).
