## Biography

Wolf Kuniansky was born 15 DEC 1884 in Russia (unverified). He died 22 SEP 1964 in Fulton County, Georgia (unverified).

### Białystok candidate origin DOWNGRADED to unlikely; Podolia / Vinnytsia (Ukraine) now the working hypothesis (2026-04-19)

An initial JRI-Poland search (see [[Kuniansky_JRI_Poland_Bialystok_2026_04_19]]) had surfaced two Białystok Jewish community vital-register records in the Konjanski / Kunjanska surname family (1866 death of Sore Leja Kunjanska daughter of Gersz; 1874 death of Gersz Konjanski son of Srol), establishing a three-generation Białystok Konjanski family (Srol → Gersz → Sore Leja and presumably other children). That was tentatively held as a speculative candidate ancestry for Wolf.

Follow-up web research on 2026-04-19 across independent family-authored and institutional sources (kuniansky.org family site authored by documented descendant Max Lewis Kuniansky; Breman Museum oral history of Max Lewis Kuniansky interviewed 1990-07-28; Find a Grave / MyHeritage for Harry and Isadore Kuniansky) consistently places the Atlanta Kuniansky cluster's origin in **Podolia Gubernia / Vinnytsia region (central Ukraine)**, not Grodno Gubernia / Białystok:

- Wolf and his brother Harry were the **first of ~7 siblings** (children of grandfather Max Kuniansky and Huddle / Adele Freemer, adopted stepdaughter of Baruch Freemer b. ~1831) to emigrate from Russia to the US; grandfather Max was killed in an accident in Europe before emigration.
- Wolf's sister-in-law Annie Fitterman (wife of Wolf's brother Louis) emigrated from **Voroshilovka (now Voroshylivka, Tyvriv raion, Vinnytsia oblast, Ukraine)**, historically in Podolia Gubernia, Vinnitsa Uyezd. Whether the Kuniansky side also originated in Voroshilovka or in a neighbouring Podolian shtetl is the central open question.
- The vault's pre-existing records for Wolf's brothers [[Harry_Kuniansky]] and [[Isadore_Kuniansky]] give birthplace **"Komarovka, Odessa, Russian Empire"** — likely a Podolian or southern-Ukrainian Komarivka, geographically consistent with Voroshilovka and geographically inconsistent with Białystok (~600 miles north-west).
- The Białystok Konjanski family (Srol / Gersz / Sore Leja) and the Atlanta Kuniansky cluster are now assessed as **most likely unrelated Kuniansky-variant families** — a common pattern for surnames widely distributed across the Pale of Settlement.

**Revised confidence on Wolf's ancestry**: Białystok candidacy is downgraded from Speculative to **Unlikely** (would be re-opened only if a later-found Atlanta Kuniansky primary document cites Białystok, Grodno, or Podlasie). The new working hypothesis is a **Podolia / Vinnytsia origin**, currently at **Speculative** confidence (one family-authored Tier 3 site + one Tier 2 oral history via summary; awaiting Tier 1 corroboration from a ship manifest, naturalization petition, or Ukrainian vital register entry).
