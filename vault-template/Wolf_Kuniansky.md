### Candidate Białystok origin (2026-04-19, speculative)

A JRI-Poland search (see [[Kuniansky_JRI_Poland_Bialystok_2026_04_19]]) surfaced two Białystok Jewish community vital-register records in the Konjanski / Kunjanska surname family (1866 death of Sore Leja Kunjanska daughter of Gersz; 1874 death of Gersz Konjanski son of Srol). These establish a **three-generation Białystok Konjanski family** (Srol → Gersz → Sore Leja and presumably other children), placing a Kuniansky-variant family in Grodno Gubernia in the generation immediately prior to Wolf's 1884 birth.

Wolf b. 1884 Russia could plausibly be a **grandson or great-grandson of Srol Konjanski** via an unknown son of Gersz (Sore Leja predeceased her father in 1866 without descendants). This is **speculative** and does not meet the Two Independent Sources Standard; Białystok was a large Jewish center (~50,000 Jews in 1900) with potentially multiple unrelated Kuniansky families. Listed as a candidate ancestry pending corroboration from: full LDS microfilm register images (films 1191934 and 1191936), additional JRI-Poland Białystok record types (births, marriages), and US ship manifests showing Wolf's arrival from Białystok.
