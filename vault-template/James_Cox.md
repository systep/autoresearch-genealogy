## Biography

James Cox was born c.1778 in North Carolina, the son of Isaac Cox and grandson of William Cox Sr. (c.1730-1814). He migrated with his family from Orange County, NC to Greenville County, SC in the late 1780s.

He married Dianah (Diana) Black (b. 1784, NC). They had at least 11 children.

James was central to the founding of **Standing Springs Baptist Church**. The church was organized in 1818 as an arm of Fork Shoals Baptist Church, with early meetings held **in James Cox's home**. Around 1836, he donated approximately 3 acres of land on which the first meeting house was constructed.

A handwritten family note addressed "To Rebecca" (IMG_0791) states: "The church your great great grandfather gave the land for the Church, the graveyard & the organ. [He was] a faithful German, Deacon in the church for [many years]." Cross-reference audit concluded that the "German" identity likely refers to the intermarried Stangle family, not James Cox himself (Y-DNA for this Cox line is R1b, consistent with British Isles origin).

He died May 22, 1853 and is buried at Standing Springs Cemetery, Simpsonville, SC.
