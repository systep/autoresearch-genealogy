## Biography

Frieda Karp Weidenfeld was the daughter of **Joseph Karp** and **[[Feiga_Frei_Karp]]** (nee Frei) (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). Through her mother, Frieda was a niece of **[[Leah_Frei]]** (b. 1864; d. 1940) and a cousin of the Markel children. Her maternal grandparents were **[[Yoseph_Frei]]** and **[[Toba_Rivka_Frei]]**.

Frieda had two brothers: **Nissan Karp** and **Abraham Karp**, both recorded as deceased in the Yichus narrative (Tier 2).

Frieda married **Fishel Weidenfeld** (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). Both Frieda and Fishel were murdered in the Holocaust (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). The exact date and circumstances of their deaths are not specified in the narrative.

Their daughter, **Renya Tzoar** (nee Weidenfeld), survived the Holocaust by being **hidden in a convent** (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). Renya is of particular significance to the family record because she was the source of information about what happened to the family members who remained in Poland. She provided the account of the **Rosh Hashanah 1939 Pogrom** in which the Markel family members and others were forced from their homes and marched to the **Rodzinka Forest**, where they were murdered (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). Renya's testimony is the basis for much of what the family knows about the fate of those who perished.

Confidence: Moderate. Named in the Yichus narrative (Tier 2, primary family document by Ruth Gold, Oct 9, 2011). Frieda is identified as a daughter of Feiga Frei Karp and Joseph Karp, with her marriage and fate documented. The survival of her daughter Renya Tzoar and Renya's role as witness to the family's wartime fate is well established in the narrative.

## Holocaust Record Search (2026-04-17)

### USHMM Oral History: Rebecca Tzoar (irn619016)

The USHMM holds an oral history interview with **Rebecca Tzoar** (nee Rena Weidenfeld), recorded June 27, 2018 in West Orange, NJ, by Ina Navazelskis (Tier 2). Key details from the catalog record:

- Born **August 15, 1933** in **Sambor, Poland** (now Sambir, Ukraine)
- Mother: **Frida Karp** (confirmed independently of the Yichus)
- Father: **Philip (Fishel) Weidenfeld** (confirmed independently of the Yichus)
- Was sent to a **sanatorium in the Carpathian Mountains** for fresh air before the war
- After the German invasion in summer 1941, was **evacuated with other children to Kiev**, then to **another sanatorium near Kuybyshev (Samara, Russia)**
- Moved to a **collective farm** in the Kuybyshev area, housed with disabled Polish adults
- The farm was funded by the **Polish government in exile**
- **Learned in 1943 from a Christian girl that her family had been killed in Sambor**
- Remained at the farm until **1946**
- After returning to Poland, was **housed in a convent**, where she asked that a Jewish agency be told about her

This testimony provides **independent Tier 2 corroboration** of the Yichus narrative's account of Frieda and Fishel's murder, and of Renya's survival. It corrects the Yichus claim that Renya was "hidden in a convent" during the war: she was actually evacuated to Soviet sanatoriums/collective farms; the convent came later, after the war.

### Sambor Yizkor Book

The Sambor Yizkor Book necrology lists **"Karp, Yosef and Family (4 People)"** (Tier 2). This matches the family of Frieda's parents, **Joseph Karp** and **[[Feiga_Frei_Karp]]**. The "4 people" count may include Joseph, Feiga, and two of their children (possibly Nissan Karp and Frieda herself), though Frieda married out to the Weidenfeld name. Alternatively, the count may include Joseph, Feiga, Nissan, and one other relative, with Frieda counted separately under Weidenfeld.

### Date of Death

Frieda and Fishel were most likely murdered between **1941 and 1943** during the destruction of the Sambor Jewish community. See [[Avraham_Chaim_Markel]] for the detailed Sambor Holocaust timeline.
