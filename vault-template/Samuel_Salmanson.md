## Biography

Samuel Salmanson was born June 24, 1912 in Brooklyn, New York, the second son of Barnett (Berko) Salmanson and Elizabeth (Salk) Salmanson. His older brother Leonard Irving was born in 1910. The family relocated from Brooklyn to Providence, Rhode Island during the 1910s.

Samuel served in the **United States Navy** during World War II as a member of the **Seabees** (Naval Construction Battalions), stationed on Johnson Island in the South Pacific.

After the war, Samuel was a co-founder of **Adams Drug Company**, established in 1932 in Woonsocket and Pawtucket, Rhode Island by Barnett Salmanson and his sons. The company grew to over 500 stores in 14 states. His 2004 obituary noted that "his nephews, Jerrold and Alan" worked in the family business, indicating Samuel's involvement lasted decades. Adams Drug was listed on the NYSE in 1972 and sold to Pantry Pride in 1984 for $100 million, later becoming Brooks Pharmacy (1986) and then Rite Aid (2006).

Samuel married **Hilda Mittleman** (1916-2016), daughter of Max and Fannie Mittleman of Providence, RI. Their son **James A. Salmanson** (b. March 10, 1945, Providence) attended Syracuse University ('67) and earned an MBA from Babson College ('70), rising to vice chairman of Adams Drug. Their daughter **Tobey** married Richard Oresman.

Grandchildren: Matthew Oresman, Heather Wong, Lauren and Marc Regardie, Brian Salmanson, Nikki DeMartinis; six great-grandchildren (per Hilda Salmanson's obituary).

Samuel died October 21, 2004 in West Palm Beach, Florida.
