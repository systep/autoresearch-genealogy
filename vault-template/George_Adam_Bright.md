# George Adam Bright (Brecht) Sr.

## CRITICAL IDENTITY NOTE (added 2026-04-22 audit)

This file documents **George Adam Bright (b. 2 Sep 1730, d. 14 Jun 1804, buried Dutch Hollow Hanger Cemetery, Middlebrook, Augusta Co VA)**, the Brecht -> Bright anglicization keystone figure who migrated PA -> Frederick Co VA c. 1753 -> Augusta Co VA 1767. He is **NOT** the same person as **George Bright (b. 9 Feb 1731 Lancaster/Heidelberg PA, d. ~1783)**, a different individual (possibly a younger cousin) who appeared in Lancaster County PA records, scouted with Daniel Boone in Kentucky 1776, and died ~1783 (Find a Grave #285400993). Both men were previously conflated in MyHeritage GEDCOM (which attached this file to the 1731 PA birth and 1769 Lancaster death of the other George). Prior vault dates "b. 9 Feb 1731, d. 1769" were incorrect and combined attributes of the two men. Per Research_Log.md 2026-04-19 item #9 and Bright_Brecht_PA_VA_Descendants_Research_2026_04_19.md. See also cross_reference_audit.md row 62.

## Biography

George Adam Bright was born 2 September 1730 in Heidelberg Township, Lebanon/Lancaster County, Pennsylvania. He migrated to Frederick County, Virginia c. 1753, settled permanently in summer 1759, received VA land grants 1761 and 1765, and in 1767 bought 170 acres in Beverly Manor, Augusta County. He attended St. John's Reformed Church in Middlebrook. He received further grants in southern Augusta County in 1781 and 1787 near South Mountain.

His will, dated 14 April 1804, was proved 25 June 1805 in Augusta County, naming sons John, George, Windle, and David plus five daughters including Rebecka. He is buried at Dutch Hollow Hanger Cemetery, Middlebrook (Virginia DHR #007-6089), which contains graves of the first German immigrant settlers in the area.

All data is supported by Tier 2/3 sources (Find a Grave, Geni, WikiTree) and VA DHR records. No Tier 1 parish or deed transcription has yet been verified.
