## Biography

Daniel Huff was one of the earliest settlers in Brunswick County, Virginia. On November 12, 1753, he received a King's grant of 360 acres on the east side of Pigeon Roost Creek in Brunswick County (WikiTree Huff-1770, citing county records). In 1768, he served as a county surveyor. The 1768 poll taken at an election of Burgesses in Brunswick County lists both Daniel and William "Hoof" as voters, reflecting the contemporary spelling variant of Huff (WikiTree Huff-1766).

Daniel married **Mary Holloway** (WikiTree Huff-1770, Huff-446). Mary's surname connects the Huff family to the broader Holloway network in Brunswick County. William Holloway (c. 1734-1784), whose will was witnessed by William Moseley Jr., and whose daughter Martha "Patty" Holloway married Samuel Moseley, represents the intermarriage between Huff, Holloway, and Moseley families in this community.

**1773 Will (Tier 1 source):** Daniel wrote his will in 1773, which was probated November 24, 1777 in Brunswick County. The will names:
- Wife Mary
- Sons: Daniel, Philemon, Reuben, Lewis, and James
- Granddaughter: Tabitha
- Executors: Daniel (son) and James

The granddaughter Tabitha has generated some genealogical discussion. In the 1773 will, none of the known children named Tabitha (Philemon, James, and Lewis each later had daughters named Tabitha) had yet been born. The likely explanation is that Tabitha's father was a deceased older child of Daniel, or perhaps the son Daniel himself (WikiTree Huff-1770).

**Children's later histories:**
- **Daniel Huff Jr.** (before 1750 VA, d. 23 Oct 1795 Brunswick Co VA) married Southall Lundy, daughter of Isham Lundy (WikiTree Huff-1767).
- **Philemon Huff** (c. 1763-1843) married Martha Parham c. 1784 in Virginia; moved to Greenville County SC, settling on the Saluda River at Piedmont by 1810 (WikiTree Huff-446).
- **[[James_Huff]]** (14 Feb 1768, d. 31 May 1842) married [[Rebecca_Moseley_Huff]] (11 Apr 1772-7 Jan 1837/38) on 9 Nov 1793 in Brunswick, VA. He lived in Brunswick County until c. 1817 when he joined brother Philemon in Greenville County, SC (WikiTree Huff-1775).
- **Lewis Huff** and **Reuben Huff** are documented in the will but further details are limited.

**Relationship to William Huff (c. 1710-bef. 1781):** A man named William Huff (WikiTree Huff-1766), who married Elizabeth Lundy, also lived in Brunswick County and had a son named Daniel Huff. In 1770, William Huff made a gift "for love and affection" to his son Daniel. William's 1778 will (probated 25 June 1781) listed wife Elizabeth, sons Daniel and James, and daughter Silvia. WikiTree notes that "a probable brother, or other relative, Daniel Huff, settled in the same area shortly after William did," and that "this Daniel Huff and his wife Mary also had sons Daniel and James, as well as others named Lewis, Philemon, and Reuben." The two Daniel Huffs (son of William, and this Daniel who married Mary Holloway) appear to be closely related but are distinct individuals with different wives and different children.

**Surname origin:** The Huff families of Brunswick County are believed to be connected to the Hough family of Bucks County, Pennsylvania (Quakers who emigrated from Cheshire, England). The Hough/Huff Name Study on WikiTree and the Huff/Hough Newsletter (published by Max K. Huff, 1992-1993, FamilySearch catalog #638426) document this connection, though the exact generational link between the Bucks County Houghs and the Brunswick County Huffs remains unproven. The pedigree chart for this family erroneously identifies Daniel Hough (b. 1693, Bucks County PA, WikiTree Hough-149) as the father of James Huff. See [[Daniel_Hough]] for that individual.

Through James Huff's son [[Lewis_P_Huff]] (1803-1873), granddaughter [[Mary_Elizabeth_Huff]] (1838-1922), and the Long/Cox intermarriages, this line connects to the Bright, Kascher, and Salmanson families.
