# Drake Family Southern US Origin Research

## Research Question

What is the pre-colonial English origin of the vault's Drake family (12 person files, Deep-South cluster: MS/AL/LA, oldest vault member Matthew Bolivar Drake born 1814 North Carolina, died 1871 Oktibbeha County Mississippi)?

## Best Working Hypothesis

The vault's Drake family descends from the Drakes of **Nash and Warren Counties, North Carolina**, and ultimately from **Richard Drake, serge maker of South Petherton Parish, Somerset, England** (born c. 1610-1620), who emigrated via Bristol on 6 September 1658 and settled in Isle of Wight County, Virginia.

### Proposed Chain of Descent

1. **Richard Drake** (c. 1610-c. 1689), serge maker, South Petherton Parish, Somerset, England. Married Thomazine (Tomzin/Tamsin, West Country name). Indentured 6 Sep 1658 to Andrew Ball, mariner, at Bristol, for transport to Virginia. Settled Isle of Wight County, VA. Children indentured with him: Thomazine, Mary, John, Thomas.
2. Descendants migrated from Isle of Wight VA southward into **Southampton County VA** and then **Nash/Warren Counties, NC** over the late 17th and 18th centuries.
3. A later **Richard Drake** with brothers John and Thomas is said in compiled genealogies (ncgenweb) to have emigrated "about 1713" and settled Isle of Wight (then "South Hampton Colony"). This is likely either a second-generation descendant or a conflation with the 1658 event.
4. **Matthew Drake** of Nash County NC (generation of the late 1700s) married Ann Arrington. Children: Matthew, Frank, James, Mourning, Patsy, Ann, Betsy, Polly, Tempy, Dolly. Matthew Jr. married Milbrey Griffin.
5. **Matthew Bolivar Drake** (1814 NC - 1871 Oktibbeha MS), the vault's oldest known Drake, fits temporally as a grandson of the above Matthew Drake of Nash Co. (a plausible son of Matthew Jr. and Milbrey Griffin, or a close cousin). Migrated from NC to MS in the 1830s-1840s as part of the Great Migration to the Mississippi Territory and subsequent cotton frontier settlement.
6. Matthew Bolivar Drake's descendants include Albert Leander Drake (b. 1897 Columbus, Lowndes MS), Frank Marion Drake, John Wallace Drake, and the other 9 Drake vault individuals.

### Confidence

**Speculative to Moderate.** The Somerset, England - Isle of Wight VA - Nash NC - Oktibbeha MS corridor is well documented in secondary sources for the Drake surname in the Deep South broadly. The specific link from the vault's Matthew Bolivar Drake (1814) back to the Nash County Drake line is not yet anchored in a primary record. The Somerset (South Petherton) origin hypothesis supersedes the commonly folk-assumed Devon/Tavistock origin (which belongs to the Sir Francis Drake line, a separate Drake family).

## Key Evidence

### English Parish of Origin

- **South Petherton Parish, Somerset** (not Tavistock, Devon). Richard Drake was recorded as a serge maker (wool trade, consistent with Somerset textile industry) resident in South Petherton 1647-1650 before relocating to Bristol for emigration.
- The Tavistock Drakes (Sir Francis Drake, Crowndale Farm, Devon) are a distinct family. Popular conflation of all colonial Drakes with Sir Francis Drake's line is a common genealogical error.

### Migration Path

1. South Petherton, Somerset, England (pre-1658)
2. Bristol, Gloucestershire, England (embarkation 6 Sep 1658)
3. Isle of Wight County, Virginia (1658 onward)
4. Southampton County, VA / northward fringe (late 1600s-early 1700s)
5. Warren and Nash Counties, North Carolina (18th century)
6. Mississippi (Oktibbeha, Lowndes counties) (1830s-1840s, part of Great Migration to Mississippi Territory and cotton frontier)

### Vault Alignment

- Matthew Bolivar Drake: born NC 1814, died Oktibbeha MS 1871. The NC birth is the critical anchor connecting the vault family to the NC Drake line.
- Albert Leander Drake: born Columbus, Lowndes MS 1897. Lowndes adjoins Oktibbeha. Consistent with multi-generation MS settlement from the 1830s.

## Contradicting Evidence

- Two distinct Drake emigration narratives appear in the secondary literature (1658 Richard; 1713 Richard with brothers John and Thomas). These need disambiguation. The 1713 figure may be a grandson of the 1658 immigrant, or the 1713 date is a transcription error for an earlier event.
- No primary record (census, will, deed, church record) has yet been located that names Matthew Bolivar Drake's father.
- MyHeritage GEDCOM is the only vault source and is Tier 3 (user-contributed), per CLAUDE.md source hierarchy.

## Ruling Out Tavistock / Devon

The Sir Francis Drake family of Tavistock, Devon is a **different** Drake lineage. Search evidence places the colonial Virginia Drake progenitor in **Somerset** (South Petherton), not Devon. Popular family lore often assumes descent from Sir Francis Drake, which is unwarranted without specific documentary evidence. The serge-maker occupation ties Richard Drake firmly to the Somerset wool trade, not to Devon seafaring.
