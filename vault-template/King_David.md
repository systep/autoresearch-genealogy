# David, King of Israel and Judah

> **Confidence: Moderate.** David is a well attested historical and biblical figure. Wikipedia confirmed. However, his role as ancestor in this tree is part of the claimed Davidic descent through the Maharal of Prague. This chain is contested by modern scholarship (Putik, Muneles). The connection from the Maharal back to King David relies on Meir Perels' Megillat Yuhasin (1745), which contains documented errors.

## Biography

David is described in the Hebrew Bible as the third king of the United Monarchy of Israel and Judah, succeeding Saul and Ish-bosheth. According to biblical narrative, he was anointed by the prophet Samuel, served as a warrior under Saul, and eventually established Jerusalem as his capital. He is credited with composing many of the Psalms. The Davidic dynasty is central to Jewish messianic tradition, and claimed Davidic descent has been asserted by many Jewish families over the centuries.

The GEDCOM records him as "David, king of ancient Israel and Judah and the third king of the United Monarchy" with no dates provided in the file itself. No birth or death dates appear in the GEDCOM entry.
