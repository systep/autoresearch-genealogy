## Biography

Rabbi Dovber Shneuri, known as the **Mitteler Rebbe** ("Middle Rebbe"), was born November 13, 1773, the son of **Rabbi Shneur Zalman of Liadi** (the Alter Rebbe, founder of Chabad-Lubavitch) and **Sterna Segal**.

He succeeded his father as the **2nd Lubavitcher Rebbe** after Shneur Zalman's death in 1812. He was known for his extensive and detailed Hasidic discourses, expanding upon his father's teachings. He established the Chabad settlement in Hebron, Palestine, in 1820.

Dovber married **Sheyna** (also spelled Sheine), born circa 1773 or 1774, died 1855. She is referred to as the "2nd Rebbetzin of Chabad."

Their children included:
- **Menachem Nachum** (1788-1868)
- **Chaya Mushka**, who married **Menachem Mendel Schneersohn** (the Tzemach Tzedek, 3rd Lubavitcher Rebbe)
- **[[Rivka_Beila_Schneerson]]** (1797), who married [[Yekutiel_Zalman_Wallis]] (1794-1872)
- **Sarah Freida**, who married Israel Noah Schneerson, the Rebbe of Nizhyn

Dovber died November 16, 1827 and was buried in Nizhyn, Ukraine.

### Connection to the Salmanson Family

Dovber is the direct ancestor of the Salmanson family through his daughter Rivka Beila. Rivka Beila married Yekutiel Zalman Wallis (~1807). Their son [[Srul_Mendel_Zalmanson]] (1820-1854) was the father of [[Levi_Itzhak_Zalmanson]] (1851), grandfather of [[Barnett_Salmanson]] (1883-1960). The Schneerson connection runs through Dovber's line: Rivka Beila was the Mitteler Rebbe's daughter, making the Zalmanson/Salmanson descendants part of the extended Schneerson dynasty.
