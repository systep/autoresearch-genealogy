## Biography

Sir John Colepeper was the son of [[Thomas_Colepeper_Executed]] and Margery of Bayhall. His father was executed at Winchelsea in 1321/22 for supporting the Earl of Lancaster against Edward II. John was a child when his father's estates were forfeited to the Crown.

After the accession of Edward III (1327), who reversed many of his father's actions against the Lancaster party, the Colepeper family estates were gradually restored. John rebuilt the family's standing and became a prominent figure in Kent.

John married **Elizabeth**, daughter and co-heir of Sir John Hardreshull of Hardreshull in Warwickshire (WikiTree Colepeper-5, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3). Through this marriage he acquired the Hardreshull estates, adding to the restored Bayhall and Brenchley properties.

He served as **Sheriff of Kent** in 1364 to 1365, 1365 to 1366, and 1368 to 1369 (the 39th, 40th, and 43rd years of Edward III) (WikiTree Colepeper-5, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3).

John was responsible for building most of the present church at Pembury in about 1337 and built the chantry chapel of St. Mary in the churchyard in 1355 (WikiTree Colepeper-5, Tier 3).

John died before Easter term 1376, at which time his widow Idoine was named in the Court of Common Pleas in an Essex plea regarding a debt of 100 pounds (WikiTree Colepeper-5, Tier 3; Genealogy Online, Tier 3). The reference to "Idoine" as his widow suggests he may have married a second time after Elizabeth Hardreshull's death.

His son Sir Thomas Colepeper of Bayhall (c. 1335 to 1429) succeeded him and continued the family line.
