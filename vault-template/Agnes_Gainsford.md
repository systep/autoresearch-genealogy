## Biography

Agnes Gainsford was the daughter of John Gainsford, Esquire, of Crowhurst, Surrey, and Anne Wakehurst of Ardingly, Sussex (Syngeneia.org, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3). Her father was the eldest son of John Gainsford senior (will dated 1448, died 1450), who had three sons: John, William, and Nicholas (the famous [[Nicholas_Gaynesford]] of Carshalton).

Agnes first married **Richard Wakehurst the younger**, son of her mother's brother Richard Wakehurst of Wakehurst in Ardingly, Sussex (culpepperconnections.com, Tier 3). Richard the younger predeceased his father, leaving Agnes a widow with two young daughters: **Margaret** and **Elizabeth** Wakehurst. These two girls were heiresses to the Wakehurst estate.

After Richard the younger's death, the elderly Richard Wakehurst (grandfather of the girls) and his wife Elizabeth Echyngham placed the two granddaughters in the care of Agnes's second husband, **Sir John Culpeper of Bedgebury**, whom Agnes had married c. 1455 (culpepperconnections.com, Tier 3). This arrangement was disrupted by the abduction of the girls by Sir John's brothers Richard and Nicholas Culpeper (see below).

By Sir John Culpeper, Agnes had at least four children: Alexander (who inherited Bedgebury), Walter (who became Under-Marshal of Calais and ancestor of the Wigsell Culpepers), Joyce, and Isabel (WikiTree Colepeper-3, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3).

The Proceedings in Chancery relating to the abduction of Agnes's Wakehurst daughters proved the Gainsford connection, confirming Agnes as a sister of John and William Gainsford (culpepperconnections.com, Tier 3).

## Connection to This Family Tree

Agnes Gainsford is the mother of [[Walter_Colepeper_Calais]] by Sir John Culpeper. The line continues: Walter to [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]] to [[Martin_Culpepper]] to Mary/Mercy Culpepper to [[Samuel_Sandys_MP]] and subsequent Sandys/Sands descendants.

Agnes is also the link between the Culpeper family and the Gainsford and Wakehurst families of Surrey and Sussex.
