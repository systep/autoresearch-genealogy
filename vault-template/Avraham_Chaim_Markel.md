## Biography

Avraham Chaim Markel was the fifth of eight surviving children (out of 14 born) of **[[Heschel_Herman_Markel]]** (Heshel Markel; b. 1862; d. 1916) and **[[Leah_Frei]]** (b. 1864; d. 1940) (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). He remained in Galicia/Poland while several of his siblings emigrated to the United States.

The Yichus narrative records that his uncle (or brother) **Mattis Markel** was also killed (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). The narrative mentions Mattis as Heshel's brother, making him Avraham Chaim's uncle. The Yichus also names **Vigdor Markel** and **Etti Markel** as among those murdered (Tier 2).

On Rosh Hashanah 1939, Avraham Chaim was among those "forced to leave the premises and marched to the Rodzinka Forest where they were murdered" (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). The perpetrators are described as "Polish Cossacks" in the narrative, likely referring to local collaborators or irregular forces during the German invasion of Poland in September 1939. The information about the Rodzinka Forest murders was provided by **Renya Tzoar** (nee Weidenfeld), a surviving relative who was hidden in a convent during the war (Yichus narrative, Tier 2).

Avraham Chaim was "murdered with family," indicating his spouse and children perished alongside him (Yichus narrative, Tier 2). Their names are not recorded in the Yichus.

His siblings, in birth order per the Yichus narrative (pages 6-8):
1. **[[Minnie_Markel_Sternbach]]** (Freitca Miritza; 1887-1975), settled Rhode Island
2. **[[Masha_Markel_Tachner]]** (Mashe; murdered 1939)
3. **[[Morris_Markel]]** (Moishe Myer), Rabbi, settled Rhode Island
4. **[[Sarah_Markel_Tachner]]** (Tsirl)

## Holocaust Record Search (2026-04-17)

### Sambor Yizkor Book (Sefer Sambor, ed. Aleksander Manor, Tel Aviv, 1980)

The Sambor Yizkor Book necrology lists **"Markel, Yosef and Family (5 People)"** among the martyrs (Tier 2). This entry likely refers to a different Yosef Markel (see [[Mojzesz_Majer_Markiel]], whose Hebrew name would be Moshe Meir, not Yosef), or to an otherwise unknown relative. Avraham Chaim himself does not appear under his own name in the necrology, nor do Masha, Hannah, or Pessel. This is consistent with the necrology being "incomplete due to limited response to questionnaires" (Sambor Yizkor Book, introduction to necrology).

The "Karp, Yosef and Family (4 People)" entry in the same necrology matches the family of **Joseph Karp** and **[[Feiga_Frei_Karp]]** (Avraham Chaim's aunt), confirming their murder (Tier 2, corroborates Yichus narrative).

### Date Discrepancy: "Rosh Hashanah 1939" vs. Historical Record

The Yichus narrative states the family was "marched to the Rodzinka Forest where they were murdered" on **Rosh Hashanah 1939** (September 14-15, 1939) by "Polish Cossacks." However, detailed research into the Sambor district timeline reveals a significant discrepancy:

1. **Wehrmacht occupied Sambor September 8-11, 1939** (1st Mountain Division, Battle of Lwow). During this brief occupation, "German soldiers tortured the Jews and snatched them for forced labor. Two Jews were murdered." (Sambor Ghetto, Wikipedia; Pinkas Hakehillot, Tier 2).
2. **The Soviets entered Sambor on September 20, 1939** per the German-Soviet Frontier Treaty. Under Soviet rule (Sept 1939 to June 1941), there were no massacres of Jews.
3. **The major forest massacre near Sambor** occurred at **Radlowicze (Radlovitze, now Ralivka)**, where in January 1943, 1,500 Jews were trucked to the woods and shot, and on June 8-9, 1943, the final ghetto liquidation sent remaining Jews to the same killing fields.
4. **The Sambor Ghetto** was established in March 1942. Deportations to Belzec: August 4, 1942 (4,000 Jews); September 4, 1942 (100 elderly executed + 2,000 to Belzec); October 17, 1942 (3,000); December 1, 1942 (ghetto closed). Final actions: March 14, 1943 (900 killed at Jewish cemetery); April 14, 1943 (Passover, mass grave execution); June 8-9, 1943 (final liquidation at Radlowicze).
5. **"Rodzinka Forest"** (the Yichus spelling) may be a garbled memory of **Radlowicze** forest, the documented execution site near Sambor.

**Assessment**: The Yichus narrative's "Rosh Hashanah 1939" date is almost certainly incorrect. During Rosh Hashanah 1939, the Sambor area was in the chaotic transition between brief Wehrmacht occupation and Soviet takeover; no mass killing of Jews in a forest near Sambor is documented for this period. The family was most likely murdered during the **1941-1943 Holocaust events in the Sambor district**, either:
- (a) In the July 1, 1941 pogrom (50-100 Jews killed by Ukrainian nationalists), or
- (b) During a 1942 deportation to Belzec, or
- (c) At the Radlowicze forest execution site in January or June 1943.

The confusion may stem from the similar pattern of the **Dynow massacre** (September 1939, Rosh Hashanah, 170-200 Jews marched to Zurawiec forest and shot), which occurred about 100 km northeast of Sambor. Alternatively, the Yichus informant (Renya Tzoar, born 1933, evacuated to a sanatorium near Kuybyshev in 1941) learned of her family's murder secondhand in 1943 and may have conflated events.

**Note**: The USHMM oral history of Rebecca Tzoar (irn619016, June 27, 2018) states she was **evacuated to a sanatorium in the Carpathian Mountains, then to Kiev, then to Kuybyshev (Samara)** and learned from a Christian girl in 1943 that her family had been killed. This differs from the Yichus narrative's claim that she was "hidden in a convent." Both details are partially correct: the USHMM testimony says she was housed in a convent after returning to Poland (post-war), while the Yichus conflated the wartime sanatorium with the post-war convent.
