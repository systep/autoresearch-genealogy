# Stackhouse English Quaker Origin Research (2026-04-19)

## Research Question

What was the English parish of origin of the vault's Stackhouse family ([[John_Stackhouse]] b. 1559, [[Thomas_Stackhouse]] b. 1601, [[Ellen_Stackhouse]] spouse of [[Ralph_Cowgill]])? What Quaker meeting did they belong to, and what are the details of the family's colonial Pennsylvania arrival?

## Working Hypothesis (Supported, Tier 3 Convergent)

The Stackhouse family originated in the hamlet of **Stackhouse in the parish of Giggleswick, West Riding of Yorkshire** (north Ribblesdale, 1.2 miles north of Giggleswick, on the west bank of the River Ribble). Giggleswick parish registers record Stackhouses from 1560 onward, and the hamlet itself takes its name from the family or vice versa (earliest documentary mention 1440, Fountains Abbey).

By the mid 17th century, members of the family had joined the Religious Society of Friends and belonged to **Settle Monthly Meeting**, which served the Ribblesdale corridor across Yorkshire and adjoining Lancashire (constituent preparative meetings at Settle, Giggleswick, Slaidburn, Bolland, and nearby hamlets). Settle MM produced one of the most significant collective emigrations to Penn's colony.

### Emigration to Pennsylvania (1682)

- **Ship**: *Lamb*, 130 tons, Master John Tench
- **Loaded**: Liverpool, 26 June to 17 July 1682
- **Arrived**: Delaware River, 22 October 1682
- **Fleet**: One of William Penn's 23 ships (Penn himself sailed on *Welcome*)

### Passengers from Settle MM aboard *Lamb*

Included the interrelated families of **Cowgill, Croasdale, Dilworth, Hayhurst, Heaton, Stackhouse, Waln, and Wigglesworth**. Known Stackhouse-connected passengers:

| Person | Relation | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Thomas Stackhouse Sr. (b. c. 1635, Giggleswick) | Uncle of younger emigrating Stackhouses | Married Margery Hayhurst May 1682 at Settle MM; settled Neshaminy Creek, Middletown, Bucks Co. |
| Margery (Hayhurst) Stackhouse | Wife of Thomas Sr. | Died c. 15 Eleventh Month 1682/3, reputedly one of the first burials at Middletown Meeting |
| Thomas Stackhouse (the Younger, b. c. 1661) | Nephew of Thomas Sr. | Built Middletown Meetinghouse 1690; married Grace Heaton (also *Lamb* passenger) |
| John Stackhouse | Brother of Thomas the Younger | Same voyage |
| Ellen/Ellin Cowgill (widow) and children (John, Edmund, Jane, Jennett) | Named on Settle Certificate of Removal, 07 Aug 1682 | Son Ralph arrived earlier, May 1682, on *Friends Adventure* |

### The Vault's Ellen Stackhouse / Ralph Cowgill Problem

The Cowgill Family Association disputes the long-held identification of Ellen's husband as Ralph Cowgill; they propose **Edmund Cowgill (b. 1634, bap. Thornton in Craven), son of Edmund and Jennett Cowgill**. The widow Ellen Cowgill on the 1682 Settle Certificate is identified by some researchers as the "sister Ellin" named in Thomas Stackhouse's 1706 will, supporting Stackhouse as her maiden name but not confirming which Cowgill she married. This is a live discrepancy requiring primary-record resolution.

## Fit With Vault Data

The vault's [[John_Stackhouse]] (b. 1559, Giggleswick) and his son [[Thomas_Stackhouse]] (b. 1601, Giggleswick) are plausibly the grandparents and great-grandparents of the 1682 emigrants. The generational gap (1559 to 1635) and shared Giggleswick parish register the fit. However, this chain is currently supported only by Tier 3 user-contributed sources (WikiTree, Geni, Genealogie Online, MyHeritage) and the link from vault's Thomas (d. 1686, Giggleswick) to emigrant Thomas Sr. (b. c. 1635) is asserted but not yet independently verified against Giggleswick parish registers or Settle MM records.

## Colonial PA Context

- **Destination**: Neshaminy Creek, Middletown Township, Bucks County, Pennsylvania
- **Meeting**: Middletown Monthly Meeting (originally called Neshamina). Meetinghouse built by Thomas Stackhouse the Younger, completed 1690 at cost of £26 19s 5d plus £10 for stable.
- **Burials**: Margery Hayhurst Stackhouse among the earliest (1682/3)
