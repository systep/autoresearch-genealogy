## Biography

Elizabeth Brasher married Ezekiel Henderson on October 10, 1782 in South Carolina (Family_Tree.md, WikiTree Henderson-1855). Ezekiel was a Revolutionary War veteran who served as a private from 1781 to 1783 in both cavalry and infantry units, and was discharged at Hillsboro, North Carolina. He later received a pension of $57.50/year (Pension S6994, granted 1833-1834).

Elizabeth and Ezekiel settled in Greenville County, South Carolina after the war, where they lived for 47 years (RevWarApps pension file). Together they had 15 children (Family_Tree.md). Their children formed key connections in the Standing Springs / Simpsonville community:

Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson married Thomas B. Cox (1792-1857), linking the Henderson and Cox families. Brashier Henderson married Elizabeth Taylor and fathered Rev. Henry Langford Henderson (1812-1886). Notably, Rebecca Adeline Cox (daughter of Thomas B. Cox and Olive Mary Henderson) married Rev. Henry L. Henderson (son of Brashier Henderson), making this a first cousin marriage, as both Olive Mary and Brashier were children of Ezekiel and Elizabeth Brasher Henderson (Family_Tree.md).

Elizabeth's surname "Brasher" (also spelled Brashier) was given as a first name to her son Brashier Henderson, preserving the family name across generations. The WikiTree profile Brasher-153 documents this connection.

Ezekiel's will was signed on February 24, 1839, naming wife "Elisabeth" and son "Brasher Henderson" as executrix and executor (WikiTree Henderson-1855). Elizabeth likely died in 1839 (WikiTree gives Dec 24, 1839; Geni gives Feb 24, 1839; conflicting dates, see Data Discrepancies).

## Brasher Family Origin and Deep Ancestry

### Proposed Parents: Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. and Sarah Lindsey

Multiple Tier 3 sources (WikiTree Brasher-122, Geni.com, FamilySearch) identify Elizabeth's father as **Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.** (b. Jun 18, 1731, Baltimore Co., MD; d. ~1789, Greenville Co., SC) and her mother as **Sarah Lindsey** (marriage ~1770).

Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. was a son of **Thomas Brasher Sr.** (b. Sep 1, 1701, Baltimore Co., MD; d. ~1770, Orange Co., NC). Thomas Sr. moved from Baltimore County, Maryland to Orange County, North Carolina around 1753. He wrote his will on May 17, 1770, which was not probated until May 1784 in Orange County, NC. The will names sons Thomas, John, Aquilla, James, and Samuel; daughters Elizabeth Jones, Sarah Pyle, and Hannah Teague; and wife Hannah Brazier (his second wife).

Thomas Sr.'s first wife was **Sarah Constant** (m. Oct 23, 1726, St. George's Church, Baltimore Co., MD), daughter of John Constant. Children by Sarah Constant include: William (b. Nov 18, 1727), Elizabeth (b. May 15, 1729; d. May 18, 1729), Thomas (b. Jun 18, 1731; this is "Thomas Levi"), Elizabeth (b. Nov 25, 1733; m. Mr. Jones), John (b. May 23, 1736), and Aquilla (b. Jul 28, 1740). Children by second wife Hannah likely include daughters Hannah Teague, Mary, Cassa, Jean, Rachel, and sons James (~1754) and Samuel (~1752) (Empty Branches on the Family Tree blog).

**Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.** married Sarah (identified as "Lindsey" by researchers, but the "Lindsey" attribution lacks a primary source citation). In his will (dated Sep 27, 1789, proved Apr 6, 1790 in Greenville Co., SC), his widow is named Sarah. They reportedly had approximately ten children. WikiTree and Geni list the following as children of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. and Sarah:

1. **Elizabeth Brasher** (~1764/1765), married Ezekiel Henderson
2. **Thomas Levi Brasher III** (b. 1765; d. ~1841)
3. **Henry Collins Brasher** (b. Mar 1, 1771; d. 1852; m. Eleanor Ashmore ~1794 in Greenville Co.)
4. **Aquilla B. Brasher** (~1772; d. ~1842)
5. **Samuel Robert Brasher** (~1774; d. Nov 17, 1822, Greenville Co., SC)
6. **John P. Brasher** (b. 1775; d. 1850)
7. **Sarah Brasher** (dates unknown)

### Confidence Assessment for Parentage Claim

**Speculative.** The identification of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. and Sarah Lindsey as Elizabeth's parents rests entirely on Tier 3 sources (WikiTree, Geni, FamilySearch user trees, compiled family histories). No primary source has been located that directly names Elizabeth Brasher as a daughter of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. The claim appears to originate from the compiled research in "A Brazier/Brasher Saga" by Charles Brashear and Shirley Brasher McCoy (532 pages, covering ~8,000 family members), which has been widely copied across genealogical platforms.

Supporting circumstantial evidence:
1. Elizabeth married in 1782 in SC; Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. was in the same migration stream from NC to Greenville Co., SC.
2. The Brasher family was a tight cluster in Greenville County: the 1790 census shows Thomas, Aquilla, Samuel, and William all enumerated there.
3. The naming of Brashier Henderson (b. 1790) after his mother's maiden name is consistent with Elizabeth being from this Brasher family.
4. Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.'s will was proved in Greenville Co., SC in 1790, placing the family in the same area.
5. Land records show multiple Brasher family members taking up land on the Reedy River / Reedy Fork in Greenville Co. in the 1780s.

Weaknesses:
1. "Sarah Lindsey" as wife's surname has no primary source citation found.
2. Multiple online trees conflate Thomas Brasher Sr. (1701) with Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. (1731), creating unreliable data chains.
3. The "Empty Branches on the Family Tree" blog specifically warns about "mixed up information online about Thomas."
4. No marriage record, baptismal record, or court document directly links Elizabeth to this family.

### Migration Pattern: Maryland to North Carolina to South Carolina

The Brasher family followed a well documented migration path:

1. **Maryland (1700s)**: William B. Brasher Sr. (d. 1708, Baltimore Co., MD) left four orphans. His son Thomas (b. 1701) was raised in Baltimore County.
2. **North Carolina (~1753)**: Thomas Brasher Sr. moved to Orange County, NC around 1753. He owned land and wrote his will in 1770.
3. **Chatham County, NC (1771+)**: When Chatham County was formed from Orange County in 1771, some Brasher family members fell in the new county. Aquilla Brasher received a land grant in Chatham County on Oct 23, 1782 (200 acres on Terrell's Creek, waters of Haw River).
4. **Greenville County, SC (1785+)**: After the Revolution, Thomas's sons (Thomas L., John, Aquilla, Samuel, and James) moved to Greenville Co., SC and took up land. Thomas L. bought a 500 acre parcel on Cripple Creek of Reedy River around 1785. John Brasher's 208 acre grant in 96 District is dated Feb 5, 1787 on Reedy Fork of Reedy Creek. William Brasure received 240 acres on Baker's Creek on Dec 4, 1786.
5. **Further dispersal (post-1800)**: John and Aquilla moved to western Kentucky. Samuel moved to Georgia (descendants to Illinois). James and nephews moved to Shelby and Talladega County, Alabama. Thomas L. Brasher remained in Greenville.

### Huguenot Deep Ancestry (Tier 3, unverified)

The Brasher/Brashier/Brazier surname derives from the French **Brasseur** ("brewer"), an occupational name. The immigrant ancestor claimed by multiple genealogical sources is:

**Robert Brasseur** (b. ~1595, Bouches-du-Rhone, France; d. ~1663, Calvert Co., MD). He was a French Huguenot who fled religious persecution. He went first to the Isle of Thanet, Kent, England (children born 1622-1632), then to Virginia (~1635, Nansemond County), then to Calvert County, Maryland (~1658), where the family anglicized their name to Brashear.

The surname has appeared in records as: Brasseur, Brasher, Brashear(s), Brashier, Brasier, Brazier, Brasure, Basher, Bashier, and Bradshaw.

This Huguenot lineage is documented in WikiTree (Brasseur-19), Geni.com, and the book "A Brazier/Brasher Saga." However, the specific chain from Robert Brasseur to Elizabeth Brasher has not been verified with primary sources in this research.

### Elizabeth's Siblings in Greenville County

If the parentage claim is correct, Elizabeth's brothers were prominent early settlers of Greenville County:
- **Henry Collins Brasher** (1771-1852) married Eleanor Ashmore ~1794 in Greenville Co. (WikiTree Brasher-121)
- **Samuel Brasher** (~1774; d. Nov 17, 1822, Greenville Co.) married Mary "Polly" Long (WikiTree)
- **John P. Brasher** (1775-1850), whose son Lawrence Brasher (b. 1788, Greenville District) received a 295-acre grant on the waters of Reedy River (WikiTree Brasher-51, Brasher-52)
- **Aquilla B. Brasher** (~1772; d. ~1842), the 3rd son per WikiTree, married and had children in the area (WikiTree Brasher-147)

### The Brashier Legacy in Greenville County

The Brashier name became deeply rooted in the Simpsonville / Greenville area. Standing Springs Church Cemetery contains burials of Henderson and Cox family members intermarried with Brashiers. In modern times, the Brashier name remains prominent: Brashier Middle College Charter High School in Simpsonville is named for the family, and Walt Brashier (1934-2021), a Fork Shoals native and major Greenville developer/philanthropist, funded initiatives at Greenville Technical College's Brashier Campus.
