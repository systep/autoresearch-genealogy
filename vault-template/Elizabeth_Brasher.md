## Biography

Elizabeth Brasher married Ezekiel Henderson on October 10, 1782 in South Carolina (Family_Tree.md, WikiTree Henderson-1855). Ezekiel was a Revolutionary War veteran who served as a private from 1781 to 1783 in both cavalry and infantry units, and was discharged at Hillsboro, North Carolina. He later received a pension of $57.50/year (Pension S6994, granted 1833-1834).

Elizabeth and Ezekiel settled in Greenville County, South Carolina after the war, where they lived for 47 years (RevWarApps pension file). Together they had 15 children (Family_Tree.md). Their children formed key connections in the Standing Springs / Simpsonville community:

Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson married Thomas B. Cox (1792-1857), linking the Henderson and Cox families. Brashier Henderson married Elizabeth Taylor and fathered Rev. Henry Langford Henderson (1812-1886). Notably, Rebecca Adeline Cox (daughter of Thomas B. Cox and Olive Mary Henderson) married Rev. Henry L. Henderson (son of Brashier Henderson), making this a first cousin marriage, as both Olive Mary and Brashier were children of Ezekiel and Elizabeth Brasher Henderson (Family_Tree.md).

Elizabeth's surname "Brasher" (also spelled Brashier) was given as a first name to her son Brashier Henderson, preserving the family name across generations. The WikiTree profile Brasher-153 documents this connection.

Ezekiel's will was signed on February 24, 1839, naming wife "Elisabeth" and son "Brasher Henderson" as executrix and executor (WikiTree Henderson-1855). Elizabeth likely died in 1839 (WikiTree gives Dec 24, 1839; Geni gives Feb 24, 1839; conflicting dates, see Data Discrepancies).

## Brasher Family Origin and Deep Ancestry

### Proposed Parents: Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. and Sarah Lindsey

Multiple Tier 3 sources (WikiTree Brasher-122, Geni.com, FamilySearch) identify Elizabeth's father as **Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.** (b. Jun 18, 1731, Baltimore Co., MD; d. ~1789, Greenville Co., SC) and her mother as **Sarah Lindsey** (marriage ~1770).

Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. was a son of **Thomas Brasher Sr.** (b. Sep 1, 1701, Baltimore Co., MD; d. ~1770, Orange Co., NC). Thomas Sr. moved from Baltimore County, Maryland to Orange County, North Carolina around 1753. He wrote his will on May 17, 1770, which was not probated until May 1784 in Orange County, NC. The will names sons Thomas, John, Aquilla, James, and Samuel; daughters Elizabeth Jones, Sarah Pyle, and Hannah Teague; and wife Hannah Brazier (his second wife).

Thomas Sr.'s first wife was **Sarah Constant** (m. Oct 23, 1726, St. George's Church, Baltimore Co., MD), daughter of John Constant. Children by Sarah Constant include: William (b. Nov 18, 1727), Elizabeth (b. May 15, 1729; d. May 18, 1729), Thomas (b. Jun 18, 1731; this is "Thomas Levi"), Elizabeth (b. Nov 25, 1733; m. Mr. Jones), John (b. May 23, 1736), and Aquilla (b. Jul 28, 1740). Children by second wife Hannah likely include daughters Hannah Teague, Mary, Cassa, Jean, Rachel, and sons James (~1754) and Samuel (~1752) (Empty Branches on the Family Tree blog).

**Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.** married Sarah (identified as "Lindsey" by researchers, but the "Lindsey" attribution lacks a primary source citation). In his will (dated Sep 27, 1789, proved Apr 6, 1790 in Greenville Co., SC), his widow is named Sarah. They reportedly had approximately ten children. WikiTree and Geni list the following as children of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. and Sarah:

1. **Elizabeth Brasher** (~1764/1765), married Ezekiel Henderson
2. **Thomas Levi Brasher III** (b. 1765; d. ~1841)
3. **Henry Collins Brasher** (b. Mar 1, 1771; d. 1852; m. Eleanor Ashmore ~1794 in Greenville Co.)
4. **Aquilla B. Brasher** (~1772; d. ~1842)
5. **Samuel Robert Brasher** (~1774; d. Nov 17, 1822, Greenville Co., SC)
6. **John P. Brasher** (b. 1775; d. 1850)
7. **Sarah Brasher** (dates unknown)

### Confidence Assessment for Parentage Claim

**Moderate Signal (upgraded 2026-04-14 from Speculative).** The identification of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. and Sarah Lindsey as Elizabeth's parents rests on Tier 3 sources (WikiTree, Geni, FamilySearch user trees, compiled family histories) plus multiple independent circumstantial indicators. No primary source has been located that directly names Elizabeth Brasher as a daughter of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. The claim appears to originate from the compiled research in "A Brazier/Brasher Saga" by Charles Brashear and Shirley Brasher McCoy (532 pages, covering ~8,000 family members), which has been widely copied across genealogical platforms. However, the cross-family marriage of Frances Henderson (Elizabeth's daughter) to Lawrence Aquilla Brasher (son of Aquilla B., named in Thomas Levi's will) and colonial inheritance practices regarding married daughters strengthen the circumstantial case considerably.

**Critical finding (2026-04-13):** Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.'s will (dated Sep 27, 1789, proved Apr 6, 1790, SC Will Book Vol A-B 1787-1820, p. 28) **named five sons and no daughters**: Thomas, Aquilla (spelled "Quellea"), Henry, John, and Samuel. Elizabeth is NOT named in the will. This is significant because:

(a) If Elizabeth were an unmarried daughter in 1789, she would typically have been named for an inheritance share.
(b) Elizabeth had been married to Ezekiel Henderson since 1782, seven years before the will. Married daughters were often excluded from wills in the colonial/early republic period because they had already received their portion (dowry/dower) at marriage.
(c) The will's silence on daughters is therefore **not conclusive evidence against** the parentage claim, but it does mean this primary document provides no positive confirmation of it either.

**Alternate mother name discrepancy (2026-04-13):** Jane Brasher Ashmore (1776-1859, buried Ashmore Family Cemetery #2, Greenville Co., SC; Find a Grave memorial 129180905) is listed with father "Thomas L. Brasher" and mother "Naomi Brasher." If Jane is indeed a daughter of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr., this contradicts the identification of his wife as "Sarah Lindsey." However, it is possible that "Naomi" is an error on the Find a Grave memorial, or that Thomas may have had two wives (Sarah and Naomi). The 1789 will and the 1790 census both name the widow as "Sarah." The "Naomi" attribution requires further investigation.

**Additional finding (2026-04-13):** One source (Geni/WikiTree Henderson-1855) refers to Elizabeth as "daughter of Thos L and Elizabeth Brasher of Guilford Co, NC." This introduces yet another variant for the mother's name ("Elizabeth" rather than "Sarah" or "Naomi"). "Guilford County" is also a new location variant (the family is otherwise documented in Orange County and Chatham County, NC, though Guilford County borders both). This may represent confusion with another Elizabeth Brasher (b. Nov 25, 1733), who was a daughter of Thomas Brasher Sr. and sister of Thomas Levi Jr., and who married Mr. Jones.

Supporting circumstantial evidence:
1. Elizabeth married in 1782 in NC (likely Chatham County); Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. was in the same migration stream from Chatham/Orange County, NC to Greenville Co., SC.
2. The Brasher family was a tight cluster in Greenville County: the 1790 census shows Sarah (widow of Thomas), Aquilla, Samuel, James, John, Thomas, and William all enumerated there.
3. The naming of Brashier Henderson (b. 1790) after his mother's maiden name is consistent with Elizabeth being from this Brasher family.
4. Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.'s will was proved in Greenville Co., SC in 1790, placing the family in the same area.
5. Land records show multiple Brasher family members taking up land on the Reedy River / Reedy Fork / Cripple Creek in Greenville Co. in the 1780s. Thomas L. bought a 500-acre parcel on Cripple Creek of Reedy River about 1785 for 110 pounds.
6. **(Strongest new evidence, 2026-04-14)** Frances "Franky" Henderson (b. Oct 10, 1799, d. 1878, Fayette AL), daughter of Ezekiel and Elizabeth, married **Lawrence Aquilla Brasher** (b. 1801, Greenville SC; d. 1862, Fayette AL) in 1820 in Greenville, SC. Lawrence Aquilla was the son of **Aquilla B. Brasher** (~1772-~1842), who was the second son named in Thomas Levi Jr.'s 1789 will (spelled "Quellea"). If Elizabeth were Thomas Levi's daughter, then Frances married her first cousin once removed (her mother's nephew). This cross-family marriage between the Henderson and Brasher families in the next generation strongly implies a pre-existing kinship connection. (WikiTree Brasher-467; rootsweb ~alfayett/Families/BRASHER_Aquilla.html)
7. **(2026-04-14)** Ezekiel Henderson's estate settlement (petition April 12, 1856, Greenville Co., H.L. Henderson administrator) confirms the Henderson/Brasher intermarriage by listing among legatees: "A. Brasher and Frances his Wife in Alabama." The "A. Brasher" is Lawrence Aquilla Brasher. (WikiTree Henderson-1855)
8. **(2026-04-14)** Colonial/early republic inheritance practice confirms that married daughters who had received their "portion" (dowry) at marriage were commonly NOT named in the father's subsequent will. Elizabeth married seven years before the 1789 will. Her absence from the will is consistent with standard practice, not evidence of non-relationship. South Carolina followed the English statute of 1670 until the 1791 reform mandating equal shares regardless of gender.
9. The "A Brazier/Brasher Saga" book (532 pages, Tier 2) treats Elizabeth as a child of Thomas Levi Jr., and this is the source most online trees appear to derive from.
10. **(2026-04-14)** Thomas Brasher Sr.'s 1770 will (Orange Co., NC) names daughters alongside sons, establishing that the Brasher family did sometimes include daughters in wills. However, Thomas Sr. had more property and both married and unmarried daughters to provide for. Thomas Levi Jr.'s smaller estate may have been distributed only among sons who needed provision.

Weaknesses:
1. Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.'s 1789 will names five sons and **no daughters**. Elizabeth is not mentioned. (Though she was already married by 1782.)
2. "Sarah Lindsey" as wife's surname has no primary source citation found. The "Brazier/Brasher Saga" itself notes: "we think from circumstances that she is Sarah Lindsey." Some family sheets at the FHL in Salt Lake City claim "Sarah Custace," which may be a copyist's confusion with Thomas's mother Sarah Constant/Constance.
3. A Find a Grave memorial for Jane Brasher Ashmore (claimed daughter) names the mother as "Naomi," not "Sarah."
4. Another source names Elizabeth's mother as "Elizabeth" of Guilford County, NC.
5. Multiple online trees conflate Thomas Brasher Sr. (1701) with Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. (1731), creating unreliable data chains.
6. The "Empty Branches on the Family Tree" blog specifically warns about "mixed up information online about Thomas."
7. No marriage record, baptismal record, or court document directly links Elizabeth to this family.
8. The claim that Thomas Levi Brasher Jr. and Sarah had "ten children in 15 years" is from compiled Tier 3 sources, but the will only names five sons. If there were daughters, they may have already married, but this is inference, not evidence.

### Migration Pattern: Maryland to North Carolina to South Carolina

The Brasher family followed a well documented migration path:

1. **Maryland (1700s)**: William B. Brasher Sr. (d. 1708, Baltimore Co., MD) left four orphans. His son Thomas (b. 1701) was raised in Baltimore County.
2. **North Carolina (~1753)**: Thomas Brasher Sr. moved to Orange County, NC around 1753. He owned land and wrote his will in 1770.
3. **Chatham County, NC (1771+)**: When Chatham County was formed from Orange County in 1771, some Brasher family members fell in the new county. On Oct 23, 1782, Aquilla Brasher, Thomas Brasher, and the orphans of William Brasher (deceased) all recorded land grants from the state of North Carolina. Aquilla's grant: 200 acres on Terrell's Creek, waters of Haw River (NC Land Grant #596 for 300 acres on N fork of Terrell's Creek, Survey Certificate dated Nov 18, 1779). Thomas received a grant on the same date, and his boundary adjoined Aquilla's. On Aug 5, 1784, Aquilla and wife Mary sold their 200 acres to Eli Newlin (blacksmith) for 150 pounds. Thomas and wife Sarah sold their land on Oct 27, 1784.
4. **Greenville County, SC (1785+)**: After the Revolution, Thomas's sons (Thomas L., John, Aquilla, Samuel, and James) moved to Greenville Co., SC and took up land. Thomas L. bought for 110 pounds a 500-acre parcel on both sides of Cripple Creek of Reedy River near the head of said Creek (part of a tract originally granted to James White), about 1785. John Brasher's 208-acre grant in 96 District is dated Feb 5, 1787 on Reedy Fork of Reedy Creek (Book D, p. 195). William Brasure received 240 acres on Baker's Creek on Dec 4, 1786. A 1789 state census of Greenville County lists John Brasher with 4 adult sons in his house.
5. **1790 Census, Greenville District**: The 1790 federal census enumerated the following Brasher heads of household in Greenville: Sarah (widow of Thomas L., with 3 males over 16), Aquilla (3 males 16+, 1 male under 16, 4 females), James, John, Samuel, Thomas, and William. This large cluster confirms the family's established presence.
6. **Further dispersal (post-1800)**: After the 1800 census, Elijah and Thomas Brasher migrated (1806), followed by Aquilla and John Brasher (1808) to Christian County, Kentucky. Samuel moved to Georgia (descendants to Illinois and DeKalb Co., GA). James and some nephews moved to Shelby and Talladega County, Alabama. Henry Collins Brasher moved to Shelby County, Alabama ~1817. Thomas L. Brasher III remained in Greenville, where many descendants spell the surname Brashier.

### Huguenot Deep Ancestry (Tier 3, unverified)

The Brasher/Brashier/Brazier surname derives from the French **Brasseur** ("brewer"), an occupational name from Old French "brasser" (to brew). The immigrant ancestor claimed by multiple genealogical sources is:

**Robert Brasseur** (b. ~1595, Bouches-du-Rhone, France; d. ~1663, Calvert Co., MD). He was a French Huguenot who fled religious persecution. He first went to the Isle of Thanet, Kent, England (children born there ~1622-1632), then to Virginia (~1635, where he received a 1200-acre grant in Nansemond County), then to Calvert County, Maryland (~1658), where the family anglicized their name to Brashear.

Robert's known children include: Benois "Bennet" Brasseur (b. before 1620, Rennes, France; d. ~May 25, 1663, Calvert Co., MD), Thomas Brasseur (b. ~1626, probably France), a daughter (b. ~1632, Isle of Thanet, m. Mark Clare 1666), Martha Brasseur (b. ~1636, Isle of Wight Co., VA), and Mary Brassieur (b. Jan 3, 1646, Isle of Wight Co., VA).

The claimed lineage from Robert Brasseur to Elizabeth Brasher (if the parentage claim is correct):
1. **Robert Brasseur** (~1595-~1663), French Huguenot immigrant
2. **Benois "Bennet" Brasseur** (bef. 1620-1663), moved to Calvert Co., MD
3. **Robert Brasseur III** (1646-bef. 1712), Calvert Co., MD (WikiTree Brasseur-20)
4. **William B. Brasher Sr.** (~1674-1708), Baltimore Co., MD; died leaving four orphans
5. **Thomas Brasher Sr.** (Sep 1, 1701-~1770), Baltimore Co., MD to Orange Co., NC
6. **Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.** (Jun 18, 1731-~1789), Baltimore Co., MD to Greenville Co., SC
7. **Elizabeth Brasher** (~1764/1765-~1839), married Ezekiel Henderson 1782

The surname has appeared in records as: Brasseur, Brasher, Brashear(s), Brashier, Brasier, Brazier, Brazure, Brasure, Basher, Bashier, and Bradshaw. One source claims more than 35 variant spellings exist among descendants.

This Huguenot lineage is documented in WikiTree (Brasseur-19, Brasseur-2, Brasseur-20), Geni.com, sonsofdewittcolony.org, "A Brazier/Brasher Saga," and "A Brashear(s) Family History" (FamilySearch catalog 721589/734009). However, the specific chain from Robert Brasseur to Elizabeth Brasher has not been verified with primary sources in this research. Steps 1 through 5 appear better documented than steps 5 through 7.

### Elizabeth's Siblings in Greenville County

If the parentage claim is correct, the five sons named in Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.'s 1789 will were Elizabeth's brothers:

1. **Thomas Levi Brasher III** (b. 1765; d. ~1841). First son named in will. (WikiTree, Geni)
2. **Aquilla B. Brasher** (~1772; d. ~1842). Second son ("Quellea") in will. Inherited 120 acres on Cripple Creek from father in 1790. Later purchased additional 86 acres on Cripple Creek in 1811 (Greenville Co. Deed Book L, p. 84). Eventually moved to Shelby County, Alabama. (WikiTree Brasher-147)
3. **Henry Collins Brasher** (Mar 1, 1771, Orange Co., NC; d. Sep 22, 1857, Columbiana, Shelby Co., AL). Third son in will. Married Eleanor Ashmore late 1794 or early 1796 in Greenville Co. Seven children. Held land records in Greenville Co. 1817-1824. Moved to Shelby County, Alabama ~1817. Find a Grave memorial 66620694, Old Chapel Cemetery, Wilsonville, Shelby Co., AL. (WikiTree Brasher-121)
4. **John P. Brasher** (1775-1850). Fourth son in will. Received 208-acre SC grant in 96 District on Reedy Fork of Reedy Creek, Feb 5, 1787 (Book D, p. 195). His son Lawrence Brasher (b. 1788, Greenville District) received a 295-acre grant on the waters of Reedy River (Book D, p. 204). John later moved to Christian County, Kentucky (~1808). (WikiTree Brasher-52)
5. **Samuel Robert Brasher** (~1774; d. Nov 17, 1822, Greenville Co., SC). Fifth son in will. Married Mary "Polly" Long (~1785, SC; d. after 1840, DeKalb Co., GA). 1800 census: household of 9 in Greenville, SC. (WikiTree, Geni)

Possible sisters (not named in the will, all Tier 3):
- **Jane Brasher** (Jul 8, 1776; d. Feb 11, 1859). Married John Ashmore before 1790. Buried in Ashmore Family Cemetery #2, Greenville Co., SC (Find a Grave 129180905). Find a Grave memorial lists father as "Thomas L. Brasher" and mother as "Naomi Brasher." (Note: "Naomi" conflicts with other sources naming wife as "Sarah.")
- **Sarah Brasher** (dates unknown). Listed as a daughter by WikiTree and Geni.
- **Lucinda Jane Brasher** (dates unknown). Listed as a daughter by some Geni profiles. (unverified)

### The Brashier Legacy in Greenville County

The Brashier name became deeply rooted in the Simpsonville / Greenville area. Standing Springs Church Cemetery contains burials of Henderson and Cox family members intermarried with Brashiers, including: Austin Brashier (1920-1990; Find a Grave 8300037), Mary Jane Cox (wife of Lewis J. Brashier), Rebecca Adeline Cox Henderson, and Rev. Henry Langford Henderson. The Ashmore Family Cemetery #2 in Greenville holds Jane Brasher Ashmore (1776-1859; Find a Grave 129180905), a possible sister of Elizabeth.

Other Brashier family members documented in Greenville County cemeteries via Find a Grave include: Lillie Brashier (1883-1969, Simpsonville), Onie T. Brashier (1901-1997, Simpsonville), William D. Brashier (1911-1970, Simpsonville), and Charles F. Brashier (1918-1991, Simpsonville).

A child named "Greenville Brasher" (1818-1885; Find a Grave 187224635) was apparently named after the county, indicating the family's deep identification with the place.

In modern times, the Brashier name remains prominent: Brashier Middle College Charter High School in Simpsonville is named for the family, and Walt Brashier (1934-2021), a Fork Shoals native and major Greenville developer/philanthropist, funded initiatives at Greenville Technical College's Brashier Campus (Find a Grave 225014646, Woodlawn Memorial Park, Greenville).
