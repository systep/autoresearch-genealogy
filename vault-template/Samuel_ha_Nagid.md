# Samuel ibn Naghrela (Samuel ha-Nagid)

> **Confidence: Moderate.** Samuel ha-Nagid is a Wikipedia confirmed, well documented historical figure. However, his role as ancestor in this tree is part of the claimed Davidic descent through the Maharal of Prague. This chain is contested by modern scholarship (Putik, Muneles). The connection from the Maharal back to King David relies on Meir Perels' Megillat Yuhasin (1745), which contains documented errors.

## Biography

Samuel ibn Naghrela (993-1056), known as Samuel ha-Nagid, was one of the most remarkable figures of the Golden Age of Jewish culture in Spain. He served as vizier of the Taifa of Granada, military commander, Talmudic scholar, poet, and communal leader. He authored the "Mevo HaTalmud" (Introduction to the Talmud), which remains a standard reference work. He led Muslim armies in battle as commander in chief, a virtually unique achievement for a Jew in medieval Islamic lands.

The GEDCOM records extensive name variants in Hebrew and Arabic. His son Yosef (Jehoseph) succeeded him as vizier but was assassinated in the 1066 Granada massacre. His daughter Qasmuna married into the ibn Ezra family. Through the ibn Nagrela line, the claimed descent passes to the Luria family.
