## Biography

Lawrance Hough lived in the township of Chorley within Wilmslow parish, Cheshire, during the mid-17th century. His residence at Chorley is recorded in the Wilmslow parish register in the christening entries of two of his children, Anne (1646) and John (1653).

Three of his children are documented at Wilmslow:
- Anne Houghe, chr. 8 Mar 1646, residence Chorley.
- John Hough, chr. 17 Jul 1653, residence Chorley. This is the John Hough who emigrated to Pennsylvania on the "Friendship" in 1683 and settled at Middletown, Bucks County (see [[John_Hough_Colonial]]).
- Samuel Hough, chr. 27 Mar 1659.

Lawrance Hough was buried at Wilmslow on 19 Nov 1668, consistent with a man born c. 1605 and fathering his last known child in 1659.

His own parentage is not established from primary sources in the current research. Candidate ancestors from nearby branches of the Hough family of Wilmslow parish include Henery Hough (abt. 1540-1618, "of The Hough", Wilmslow; husbandman whose 1618 will named sons John, Henry, Edward, Thomas) and the descendants of [[Roger_Hough]] (1540-1587, of Chorley, Wilmslow). The generational gap between those men (d. 1587-1618) and Lawrance (b. c. 1605) would require one intervening generation. This is a target for future research (see [[Hough_Cheshire_Origin_Research_2026_04_19]]).

The family's residence at Chorley township (within Wilmslow parish) aligns with [[Roger_Hough]]'s residence at Chorley, making a Roger-to-Lawrance descent geographically plausible though not yet proven.
