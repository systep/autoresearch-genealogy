## Biography

William Thomas Long was born November 25, 1825, described in handwritten family notes as the "1st son of Larkin and Sarah Long." The Long family was part of the cluster of intermarried families (Coxes, Longs, Blacks, Paynes, Lees, Brashiers) who migrated together from Orange County, North Carolina to Greenville County, South Carolina in the 1780s. The original Cox-Long intermarriage was Thomas Long married to Rachel Cox, a daughter of William Cox Sr. (c. 1730-1814).

He married **Mary Elizabeth Huff** (June 21, 1838 - Aug 6, 1922) on **January 19, 1857**, with the ceremony performed by **Rev. K. Callard** (per handwritten family chart).

**1860 Census:** William Long appears in Madison County, Kentucky, with wife Elizabeth (age 22). This places the couple in Kentucky early in their marriage, though the family's roots and burial are in the Standing Springs area of South Carolina (Family Tree compiled data).

Their headstone in the Standing Springs area reads: "William T. Long 1825-1887 / Elizabeth Huff__ 18?0-1922" (partially illegible final digits on Elizabeth's birth year).

Their son **William Goldsmith Long** (1862-1937) married Roxanne (Rooksaner/Roxana) Cox (1865-1912), daughter of Thomas Manning Cox and Sarah Elizabeth Clark. Another son, **James Louis Long**, is also documented as a sibling of William Goldsmith.

William Thomas Long died in 1887. Through his son William Goldsmith, he is an ancestor of Rebecca Moseley Long (1898-1985), who married Albert Graham Bright, whose daughter Mary Elizabeth Bright married Henry Michael Kascher, connecting this line to the Salmanson family.
