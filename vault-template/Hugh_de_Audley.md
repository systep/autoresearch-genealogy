## Biography

Hugh de Audley was born in April 1289, the son of Hugh de Audley, Baron Audley, and Isolda de Mortimer. He rose to prominence through his marriage to [[Margaret_de_Clare]], granddaughter of King [[Edward_I]], in 1317 (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Through his wife, Hugh gained a share of the vast De Clare inheritance after the death of Gilbert de Clare, 7th Earl of Gloucester (his wife's brother), at the Battle of Bannockburn in 1314. The De Clare estates were eventually divided among Gilbert's three sisters, with Margaret (and thus Hugh) receiving a significant portion (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Hugh was created Earl of Gloucester on March 16, 1337, by Edward III. His career was turbulent; he was involved in the political upheavals of Edward II's reign, including the Despenser War and the eventual deposition of Edward II. Hugh died on November 10, 1347, and was buried at Tonbridge Priory (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Hugh and Margaret's daughter, Margaret de Audley, married [[Ralph_Neville]] of Raby Castle. This is the link in the pedigree charts through which the De Clare/Plantagenet royal descent passes to the Neville family and subsequently to colonial American lines (Pedigree Charts).
