## Biography

Sarah Sosha was the mother of Rabbi Levi Yitzchak of Berditchev, one of the most celebrated Hasidic masters. She is known in Hasidic tradition as "Sara Sasha," and Levi Yitzchak is commonly identified as "Levi Yitzchak ben Sarah Sasha" (Chabad.org).

The surname "Derbaremdiker" is retroactively applied from her son's famous epithet ("Der Baremdiger," the Merciful One). It was not her birth surname.

She was married to Rabbi Meir, who served as Av Beit Din (head of the rabbinical court) in Husakov (Husakiv, in the L'viv region of Ukraine). According to the GEDCOM, Meir's father was Moshe, ABD of Zamosc (GEDCOM I501242 note).

Her role in Hasidic tradition is primarily as the mother of Levi Yitzchak. In Hasidic prayer and tradition, invoking "Levi Yitzchak ben Sarah Sasha" is considered efficacious, which is why her name is widely remembered.

Her connection to [[Luba_Tirtsa_Derbaremdiker]] is through her son Levi Yitzchak: Luba Tirtsa was a daughter (or granddaughter) of Levi Yitzchak, making Sarah Sosha the grandmother (or great-grandmother) of Luba Tirtsa.
