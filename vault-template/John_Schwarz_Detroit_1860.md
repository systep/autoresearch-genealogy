## Biography

John Schwarz appears in the 25 June 1860 US Federal Census at the Fourth Ward of Detroit, Wayne County, Michigan, age 37 (→ b. ~1823), occupation Laborer, with personal property $300. His wife **Catharine** was age 32 (→ b. ~1828), also Württemberg-born. They had three young sons, all born in Michigan: **John** (age 6, → b. ~1854), **Nicolaus** (age 3, → b. ~1857), and **Leopold** (under 1, → b. late 1859 or early 1860).

The three Michigan-born sons establish that this John Schwarz arrived in the US no later than ~1853 and settled in Detroit. The Fourth Ward of Detroit was heavily German-immigrant in the 1860s.

### Distinction from vault [[Johann_Schwartz]]

This John Schwarz is likely a **distinct person from the vault's [[Johann_Schwartz]]** (Johann Christoph Schwarz, b. 17 May 1822 Enzweihingen, Tier 1 baptism; identified in the 1870 Iowa census with a different Catharine b. ~1821):

- **Birth year**: Record F (Detroit) suggests ~1823; vault's Johann is 1822. Close but not identical.
- **Catharine age conflict**: Detroit Catharine b. ~1828; Iowa 1870 Catharine b. ~1821 (age 49 in 1870). A 7-year gap cannot be reconciled as a single woman.
- **Children**: Detroit family has John, Nicolaus, Leopold all born Michigan 1854-1860. Iowa 1870 household has NO co-resident children, and the vault GEDCOM records no children for [[Johann_Schwartz]].

Under a remarriage hypothesis, the Detroit John could be the vault's Johann with a first wife (Catharine #1) who died between 1860 and 1870, after which he remarried a second Catharine (also Württemberg, of his own age) and relocated to Iowa, leaving the sons with other family. This is speculative; without additional records (death of first Catharine, second marriage, census of sons 1870+), treat this as a **separate person candidate** per CLAUDE.md Guard 6 (no merge on same-name-same-region alone).
