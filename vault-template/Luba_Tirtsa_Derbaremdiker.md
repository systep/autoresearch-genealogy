## Biography

Luba Tirtsa Derbaremdiker (also known as Liba Tirtza, or Perl) was a daughter of Rabbi Levi Yitzchak of Berditchev (1740-1809), one of the most beloved figures in Hasidic Judaism, known as "the Berdichever Rebbe" and author of "Kedushat Levi" (GEDCOM, Geni.com). Her mother was Perl Lea Peretz (1743-1843) of Lubartow, Poland.

She married [[Yosef_Bonam]] (Yosef Bunim Walles), and their son [[Yekutiel_Zalman_Wallis]] (1794-1872) married [[Rivka_Beila_Schneerson]], daughter of the Mitteler Rebbe ([[Dovber_Shneuri]]). This marriage connected the Berditchev and Chabad-Lubavitch dynasties.

Levi Yitzchak of Berditchev had multiple children recorded in the GEDCOM, including: Meir Derbaremdiker of Berdichev, Yisroel Israel Derbaremdiker of Pikov, Gitel Frenkel (born Derbaremdiker), Luba Tirtsa (this person), Esther Bushka (born Derbaremdiker), Golda Derbarmdiker, and Dov Ber Derbaremdiger (GEDCOM, Geni.com).

A GEDCOM note states: "It is actually not clear how many children this Rabbi had with Perl. Per at least one source, they had 6: Rabbi Meir, Rabbi Dov Ber, Rabbi Israel, a daughter that married Rabbi Yoseph Bonem Wales, a daughter that married Rabbi Nathan of Koznice, and one called Gitel."

**Confidence: moderate.** Geni.com confirmed. The Berditchev connection is well documented in Chabad and Hasidic genealogical sources, but specific dates and children counts are from user-contributed trees (Tier 3).
