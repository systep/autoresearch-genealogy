# Hypothesis: Cowgill and Hayhurst English Quaker Origins (Stackhouse in-laws, Lamb 1682)

## Statement

1. Ellin (Stackhouse) Cowgill's husband (deceased before June 1682) was **Edmund Cowgill** of Thornton in Craven, Yorkshire (baptized 23 Nov 1634), NOT Ralph Cowgill as commonly asserted in online trees. The "Ralph" identification reflects a name-swap with their son Ralph Cowgill, who sailed separately on the Friends Adventure in May 1682.
2. Margery Hayhurst (who married Thomas Stackhouse Sr. at Settle MM, May 1682, and died 15/11m/1682-3 at Middletown, Bucks Co., PA) belonged to the Bolland/Easington Hayhurst cluster under Settle MM care, closely related to (likely sister or cousin of) Cuthbert Hayhurst (c.1633-1683) of Easington, Yorkshire. Her specific parents remain unidentified in web-accessible records.

## Evidence For

1. **Cowgill Family Association research** (cowgillcousins.org, Edmund Cowgill Family Tree page): explicitly identifies Edmund Cowgill (son of Edmund and Jenet Rideogh Cowgill, baptized Thornton in Craven 23 Nov 1634) as "almost certainly head of the American Cowgill line that begins there in 1682," i.e., husband of Ellin and father of the children on the Lamb.
2. **Settle Certificate 7 Aug 1682** (wikitree Space:The_Settle_Certificate): Ellin is listed as "Ellen Cowgill widow & her children" (John, Edmund, Jane, Jennett). Son Ralph had preceded her on Friends Adventure May 1682. This son Ralph is the origin of the mistaken "Ralph as husband" identification.
3. **Thomas Stackhouse/Margery Hayhurst marriage**: Settle Monthly Meeting, May 1682, with marriage intentions declared 2nd and 3rd months and Certificate of Removal issued 4th month (per WikiTree Stackhouse-95 and Moser Family History, citing Settle MM records).
4. **Hayhurst regional context**: Cuthbert Hayhurst (b. Easington, Yorkshire c.1633; imprisoned 11m/1660 with West Riding Friends for refusing the oath of allegiance; m. Mary Rudd 28/8m/1666 at Newton-in-Bolland under Settle MM; sailed Lamb 1682) establishes the Hayhurst family firmly in the Bolland/Easington corner of Yorkshire under Settle MM. Margery's presence at Settle MM in May 1682 and her co-emigration on the Lamb place her in this same kin-group.
5. **Settle MM jurisdiction**: Settle MM covered Newton-in-Bolland, Thornton in Craven, Easington, Settle proper, and adjacent Yorkshire/Lancashire border parishes, accounting for all three families (Cowgill, Hayhurst, Stackhouse) appearing in its minutes.

## Evidence Against

1. **Ralph Cowgill identification persists** in Geni (Ralph Cowgill 1635-1682), WikiTree Cowgill-16, and a FamilySearch photo artifact labeled "Ralph Cowgill, Ellen Stackhouse marriage." These represent a tertiary-tier, cross-copied identification.
2. **No direct web-accessible quote** of the Settle MM minute entry naming Ellin's husband by forename was located in this round.
3. **Margery Hayhurst's parents**: no web-accessible source names them. Candidates within the Easington/Bolland Hayhurst cluster include the parents of Cuthbert Hayhurst (per Linton Research Fund Hayhurst tree), but this requires Settle MM minute-book verification.

## Analysis

The Cowgill Family Association is a Tier 2 secondary source with explicit reasoning (baptismal register of Thornton in Craven plus the Settle Certificate's widow-and-children naming) and its identification is internally coherent. The competing "Ralph Cowgill as husband" identification is Tier 3 (user trees), is contradicted by the known fact that Ralph Cowgill was a son who sailed separately on Friends Adventure May 1682, and most likely originates from a generational collapse in 19th/20th-century compiled genealogies.

Weight of evidence supports **Edmund Cowgill of Thornton in Craven** as Ellin's husband. This is Moderate Signal (one well-reasoned Tier 2 source, consistent with primary Settle Certificate evidence, no contradicting primary source). A Tier 1 Settle MM minute entry would upgrade to Strong Signal.

For Margery Hayhurst, web evidence is insufficient to name parents. Moderate Signal only that she belongs to the Easington/Bolland Hayhurst kin-group of Cuthbert Hayhurst.

## Decisive Record

- **For Ellin's husband**: Settle Monthly Meeting marriage register / minute book entry c.1658-1665 recording Ellin Stackhouse's marriage to her Cowgill husband, held at Yorkshire Quaker Archives (Brotherton Library, University of Leeds) or Borthwick Institute for Archives, York.
- **For Margery's parents**: Settle MM marriage intentions entry from 2nd/3rd month 1682, which per Quaker practice names parents or guardians when the bride/groom is under 30; alternatively a Hayhurst birth entry in Settle MM registers 1650-1665.
