## Biography

Cicely Barrett was born c. 1512 at Belhus in Aveley, Essex, the daughter of John Barrett, Esquire, and Philippa Harpesfield (WikiTree Barrett-194, Tier 3). She married **William Culpepper, Esquire**, Justice of the Peace for Kent, on January 4, 1530, at Wigsell, Salehurst, Sussex (WikiTree, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3).

William and Cicely had **seven sons and two daughters** (WikiTree Culpeper-39, Tier 3). Their third son, [[Martin_Culpepper]] (Dr. Martin Culpepper, 1540 to 1605), became Warden of New College, Oxford, and Vice-Chancellor of Oxford. "Most living Caucasian Culpeppers worldwide descend from" William and Cicely (culpepperconnections.com, Tier 3).

Cicely died before November 16, 1559, at about age 47, at Salehurst, Sussex. Her husband William died on December 6, 1559, shortly after her (WikiTree, Tier 3).

Some sources (Geni, Tier 3) claim Cicely's ancestry can be traced to Charlemagne through the Barrett and connected families. This deep medieval ancestry claim is noted as unverified.

## The Barrett Family of Belhus, Aveley, Essex

The Barrett family had deep roots in Essex. The estate of Belhus came to the Barretts through the marriage of **John Barrett of Hawkhurst, Kent**, to **Alice Belhouse**, heiress of Aveley, Essex, c. 1397 (WikiTree Barrett-475, Tier 3; Barrett Family History blog, Tier 3). Alice inherited the estate from her father.

Their son **Thomas Barrett** (c. 1306 to after 1337) was born in Hawkhurst, Kent, and through his mother's inheritance established the family at Belhus (WikiTree Barrett-476, Tier 3).

Cicely's father **John Barrett** (c. 1460 to 1526) inherited and rebuilt the manor house at Belhus. At his death, he owned approximately 1,400 acres in Aveley and additional property elsewhere in Essex (WikiTree Barrett-524, Tier 3).

John Barrett married four times. His second wife (and Cicely's mother) was **Philippa Harpesfield**, widow of Thomas Dyneley and daughter of Nicholas Harpesfield and Agnes Norton (WikiTree Harpesfield-1, Tier 3).
