# Culpepper / Colepeper Kent Medieval Pedigree: Corroboration Hypothesis (2026 April 19)

## Scope

This hypothesis file consolidates external research from a WebSearch-only session on the medieval English Culpeper / Colepeper line of Kent, England, as represented in the vault entries:

- [[Thomas_de_Colepeper_Recognitor]] (c. 1170 to c. 1247), Brenchley, Kent
- [[Thomas_Colepeper_Executed]] (c. 1271 to 1321 or 1322), Bayhall, executed Winchelsea
- [[John_Colepeper_Hardreshull]] (c. 1305 to 1376), Sheriff of Kent
- [[Nicholas_Culpeper_Wakehurst]] (c. 1437 to 1510), Ardingly brass, Wakehurst line
- [[Walter_Colepeper_Calais]] (c. 1455 to 1515), Under-Marshal of Calais
- [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]] (c. 1509 to 1559), Hunton and Wigsell
- [[Martin_Culpepper]] (1540 to 1605), Warden of New College, Oxford

The question posed: do external Tier 2 and Tier 3 sources corroborate the vault's canonical descent, and what is the best-supported earliest ancestor?

## Canonical Medieval Culpeper Pedigree (Corroborated)

All external sources consulted (culpepperconnections.com; WikiTree; Geni; kingrichard3.com compilation; Ancestral Trails 2016; Ardingly History Society; British History Online Parishes: Pembury; Wikipedia Culpeper baronets) agree on the following line, with confidence at the level of moderate Tier 2 / Tier 3 consensus:

1. **Sir Thomas de Colepeper, the Recognitor** (c. 1170 to c. 1247), of Brenchley, Kent. Recognitor of the Grand Assize under King John or Henry III. Described by culpepperconnections.com as "the first of the family of whom there is any mention" and "the oldest known Culpepper and probably the progenitor of all modern-day Culpeppers."
2. **John de Colepeper** (c. 1200), son of Thomas the Recognitor (per culpepperconnections.com descendant chart dc8397). Note: a separate reference to "John de Colepeper born circa 1140 at Bayhall, Pembury" appears in some trees but is not consistently supported and likely conflates generations.
3. **Sir Thomas Colepeper of Brenchley** (c. 1230 to c. 1309), son of John. Accumulated property in Bayhall and Pepinbury between 1299 and 1317 with wife Margery Bayhall (per genealogy compilations).
4. **Sir Thomas Colepeper of Bayhall** (c. 1271 to 1321 or 1322) = vault [[Thomas_Colepeper_Executed]]. Forester of Rotherfield (1315), keeper of Ashdown and Maresfield (1318, via patron Bartholomew de Badlesmere). Supporter of Thomas, Earl of Lancaster; executed at Winchelsea after the Battle of Boroughbridge (16 March 1322). Estates forfeited; Buxted and Maresfield lands restored 1324 to widow Margery.
5. **Sir John Colepeper of Hardreshull and Bayhall** (c. 1305 to before Easter 1376) = vault [[John_Colepeper_Hardreshull]]. Sheriff of Kent 1364 to 1365, 1365 to 1366, 1368 to 1369 (39, 40, 43 Edward III). Founded chantry of St. Mary at Pembury under Edward III licence (28 Edward III). Married Elizabeth, daughter and coheiress of Sir John Hardreshull of Warwickshire. British History Online (Survey of Kent, vol. 5) corroborates the Pembury chantry and the Bayhall tenure (Tier 2).
6. **Sir Thomas Colepeper of Bayhall and Hardreshull** (c. 1335 to 1429). Continues the line.
7. **Sir John Culpeper of Bedgebury** (c. 1424 to 1480). Knighted by Edward IV; Sheriff of Kent. Married Agnes Gainsford (sister of John Gainsford). Note: some trees insert an earlier **Walter Culpeper** (c. 1398 or 1402 to 1462) who married Agnes Roper, widow of John Bedgebury, bringing the Bedgebury estate into the family. A monumental brass at Goudhurst Church identifies this Walter as son of Sir Thomas Culpeper. This Walter would be the father of Sir John of Bedgebury and of [[Nicholas_Culpeper_Wakehurst]].
8. **Generation split (c. 1460s)**:
   - **Nicholas Culpeper of Wakehurst** (c. 1437 to 1510) = vault [[Nicholas_Culpeper_Wakehurst]]. Married Elizabeth Wakehurst (co-heiress). 10 sons, 8 daughters. Ardingly Church brass (Tier 1 artifact, Tier 2 secondary description via Ardingly History Society and the Metropolitan Museum of Art rubbing) records the family group. Ancestor of the herbalist Nicholas Culpeper (1616 to 1654).
   - **Sir John Culpeper of Bedgebury** (also in this generation) is grandfather of [[Walter_Colepeper_Calais]] in the vault's stated descent.
9. **Walter Colepeper, Under-Marshal of Calais** (c. 1455 to 1515) = vault [[Walter_Colepeper_Calais]]. Son of Sir John Culpeper and Agnes Gainsford. Married Anne Aucher at Bedgebury c. 1480.
10. **William Culpepper of Hunton** (c. 1509 to 1559) = vault [[William_Culpepper_Hunton]]. Justice of the Peace for Kent. Married Cicely Barrett 1530. Described in compiled genealogies as the progenitor of most living Culpeppers.
11. **Dr. Martin Culpepper** (1540 to 1605) = vault [[Martin_Culpepper]]. Warden of New College, Oxford 1573 to 1599; Vice-Chancellor 1578 to 1579. Father of Mary / Mercy who married Sir Samuel Sandys, MP.

## Best Earliest Named Ancestor

The vault currently names **Sir Thomas de Colepeper the Recognitor** (c. 1170 to c. 1247) as the earliest documented ancestor. External sources broadly agree: culpepperconnections.com explicitly calls him "the first of the family of whom there is any mention" and "the oldest known Culpepper and probably the progenitor of all modern-day Culpeppers."

The research question posed by the session (whether an earlier "Walter de Colepeper c. 1160 Hollingbourne" is better supported) is **not supported by the sources consulted**. WebSearch results find no c. 1160 Walter de Colepeper. The Hollingbourne association appears much later (a Sir John Culpeper "of Hollingbourne" from 1631 marriage licence, per culpepperconnections) and is not the origin point of the family. The Brenchley and Bayhall (Pembury) area, not Hollingbourne, is the documented medieval seat. Some trees note a "John de Colepeper c. 1140 of Bayhall," but this is inconsistent and likely a tree-inflation artifact.

**Recommended canonical earliest ancestor for the vault: Sir Thomas de Colepeper the Recognitor, c. 1170 to c. 1247, Brenchley, Kent.** Keep the vault entry as-is.

## Geographic Seats (Confirmed by Multiple Tier 2 Sources)

- **Brenchley**, Kent: earliest seat, residence of Thomas the Recognitor and Thomas of Brenchley (c. 1230)
- **Bayhall**, Pembury, Kent: acquired via marriage to Margery Bayhall late 13th century; principal seat from c. 1300
- **Hardreshull**, Warwickshire: acquired by Sir John Colepeper (c. 1305 to 1376) via marriage to Elizabeth Hardreshull
- **Bedgebury**, Kent: acquired by Walter Culpeper (c. 1398 to 1462) via marriage to Agnes Roper (widow of John Bedgebury); held by the family for c. 250 years
- **Wakehurst**, Ardingly, Sussex: acquired by Nicholas Culpeper c. 1460s via marriage to Elizabeth Wakehurst co-heiress
- **Wigsell** and **Hunton**, Kent: seats of William Culpepper (c. 1509 to 1559)
- **Hollingbourne**, Kent: later branch, post-medieval
- **Leeds Castle**, Kent: Castellan line (Sir Thomas Colepeper d. 1321)
- **Preston Hall**, Kent: seat of the Colepeper baronets (created 1627)

## Name Origin (Speculative, per culpepperconnections.com)

The Norman French form "Colepeper" combined with the "de" prefix suggests Norman origin. culpepperconnections.com proposes the name derives from "the bottom of Pepenbury" (Pepenbury being a variant of Pembury). This is speculative; no primary documentary evidence has been cited.

## Next Steps (Specific)

1. **Visitations of Kent**: Consult the published editions of the 1530 to 1531 (Benolte), 1574 (Cooke), and 1619 to 1621 (Philipot) heraldic visitations for the Culpeper pedigree as formally registered. These are Tier 2 with Tier 1 armorial anchoring. Harleian Society editions are online at archive.org and Internet Archive (no login).
2. **British History Online, Survey of Kent (Hasted)**: already cited for Pembury; consult volumes for Goudhurst (Bedgebury), Pembury (Bayhall), Hunton, and Brenchley for pedigree details. Free access.
3. **Ardingly Church brass**: direct photo or rubbing transcription. Ardingly History Society and Metropolitan Museum of Art hold images. Confirms Nicholas Culpeper Wakehurst family group.
4. **Chantry licence, Pembury 1355**: Calendar of Patent Rolls, Edward III (publicly available at archive.org and British History Online) should contain the licence to John Colepeper of Bayhall.
5. **1321 to 1324 forfeiture and restoration records**: Calendar of Close Rolls and Calendar of Patent Rolls for Edward II and early Edward III should contain the Bayhall forfeiture and the 1324 restoration to Margery.
6. **Court of Common Pleas 1376 plea**: the Essex plea naming Idoine as widow of Sir John Colepeper (cited by WikiTree Colepeper-5). AALT (Anglo-American Legal Tradition) hosts Common Pleas rolls images.
7. **Will of Walter Colepeper, Under-Marshal of Calais, probated 28 April 1516**: Prerogative Court of Canterbury. TNA (The National Archives) Discovery catalogue; request digitisation.
8. **Will of Dr Martin Culpeper PCC Hayes 88 (1605)**: already transcribed in GEDCOM; acquire full scan from TNA Discovery to verify transcription.
9. **1530 marriage settlement, William Culpepper and Cicely Barrett**: locate in county record offices (Kent History and Library Centre) or the Essex Record Office (Barrett of Aveley).
10. **Goudhurst brass of Walter Culpeper d. 1462**: photograph or published transcription; anchors the Walter to Sir John Culpeper of Bedgebury link.
