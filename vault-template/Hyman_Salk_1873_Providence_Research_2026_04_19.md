# Hypothesis: Hyman Salk (b. 1873 Vilkomer) = Founder of Salk's Hardware, Providence

## Claim

The Hyman Salk recorded in the MyHeritage GEDCOM (b. 15 March 1873, Vilkomer/Ukmerge, Lithuania; d. 22 February 1949, Providence RI; parents [[Louis_Elizar_Salk]] and [[Rose_Salk]]) is the same person who purchased a tailor shop in Providence in 1900, which became Salk's Hardware.

## Status

**Supported** by multiple independent Tier 2 and Tier 3 sources. Documentary Tier 1 evidence (ship manifest, naturalization, 1900/1910/1920/1930/1940 census, death certificate) has not yet been retrieved and is required to upgrade to strong signal.

## Evidence

### Corroborating Sources (Tier 2)

1. **Warwick Beacon / Johnston Sun Rise business feature** ("Tough times, the right time to grow, says Salk"): States Hyman Salk bought a Providence tailor's shop in 1900, which grew into Salk's Hardware. Son Sam, age 15, peddled thread and household goods door-to-door in Oakland Beach, leading to a 1917 storefront lease there. A second brother Lewis attended college while Sam ran the business. Sam was expected to help set Lewis up with his own hardware in Conimicut.
   - URLs: warwickonline.com/stories/tough-times-the-right-time-to-grow-says-salk,58583 ; johnstonsunrise.net/stories/tough-times-the-right-time-to-grow-says-salk,58583

2. **Harold Salk obituary (Dignity Memorial, d. 31 July 2024, Warwick RI, age 92)**: "Born and raised in Providence, son of the late Samuel and Ruth (Fleisig) Salk. Proud 3rd-generation owner of Salk's Hardware & Marine. Funeral at Temple Beth-El, Providence."
   - URL: dignitymemorial.com/obituaries/providence-ri/harold-salk-11922314
   - Significance: Harold = 3rd generation. Samuel (his father) = 2nd generation = Hyman's son Samuel Henry Salk (b. 1899 Newark, d. 1971 Providence, per vault). The business founder therefore = Samuel's father = Hyman Salk. This chain matches vault's GEDCOM Hyman.

3. **Salk's Ace Hardware & Marine company page** (salkshardware.com, visitwarwickri.com, acehardware.com store-details): "Family owned and operated, founded 1900 in Providence, Rhode Island. In 1917 moved to Oakland Beach section of Warwick. 5 generations."
