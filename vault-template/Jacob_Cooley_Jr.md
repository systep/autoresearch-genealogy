## Biography

Jacob Cooley Jr. was born 1760, probably in Pittsylvania County, Virginia, son of Jacob Cooley Sr. (b. 1735, d. 1820). He married Nancy Gover (b. 1763, d. before 1844) on 5 April 1787 in Pittsylvania County, VA. Together they had nine children, of whom **[[Hiram_Cooley|Hiram Cooley]]** (b. 1796 Greenville SC, d. 2 Apr 1864) is the great-grandfather of [[Charstee_King|Charstee Cooley King]] via Hiram's son John Joseph Cooley (1819-1896).

The Pittsylvania County VA → Greenville SC migration is consistent with the broader settler migration of the late-18th century along the Piedmont corridor. The family settled in Greenville District, SC, where Hiram Cooley would later (1841) be granted a charter to operate Cooley's Bridge.

Jacob Sr. (the next generation back) is a future expansion target, partially documented at gapeach2.wordpress.com "Jacob COOLEY 1735-1820".
