## Biography

Helen Brandt was born as **Dwora Goldbaum** (also using the alias Brand) in **Lisko (Lesko)**, a town in the Bieszczady Mountains of Galicia, approximately 80 km southwest of Sambor. She was the daughter of **[[Joseph_Brandt|Józef Brand]]** and **[[Brina_Goldbaum|Brancia Goldbaum]]**, both of Lisko. Her parents were religiously married but not civilly registered under Habsburg law, which is why her legal surname was Goldbaum (her mother's maiden name) rather than Brand (her father's surname). This "false" (alias) notation is standard Galician Jewish record-keeping convention.

By 1919, Dwora had moved from Lisko to Sambor (the district seat, about 80 km to the northeast), where on 13 December 1919 she married **[[Morris_Markel|Mojzesz Majer Markiel]]**, a merchant from Waniowice (AGAD Fond 424, File 212, Record 65, Page 315; Tier 1). The marriage banns were published on 29 November, 6 December, and 13 December 1919.

Upon immigration to the United States (approximately 1928), Dwora took the English name **Helen** and used her father's surname **Brandt** (anglicized from Brand). The choice of "Helen" as an English name for "Dwora" is unusual (Dwora is typically Americanized as Dorothy or Dora), but Jewish immigrants frequently chose English names without strict correspondence to their Yiddish/Hebrew names.

The Yichus narrative (Tier 2) confirms Helen as "Helen Brandt Markel," wife of Morris. Morris served as a learned man (described as Rabbi) at Ahavath Sholom Synagogue in Arctic (West Warwick), Rhode Island (Yichus narrative, Tier 2).

### Immigration

Based on the obituary of daughter Kate (Markel) Kaplan (Providence Journal, Jan 13, 2006), Kate was "born in Poland" and "moved to W. Warwick, RI at the age of 3." Kate was born December 6, 1925 (Find a Grave #232002112), placing the family's immigration to West Warwick at approximately 1928. This means older children Thelma (b. 1920) and possibly Anne and Frieda were also born in Poland. The family settled directly in West Warwick (Arctic), where Morris's sister [[Minnie_Markel_Sternbach]] and her husband Abraham Sternbach had been established since 1912 as founding members of the local Jewish community.

### Dates Clarification

The birth and death dates for Helen in the GEDCOM use the "ABT" and "EST" qualifiers with the date "27 JUL" appearing in both birth (~1889) and death (~1975), which is a system artifact or placeholder, not an actual date. The year estimates may be approximately correct, but the month and day should be disregarded.

The Yichus narrative (Tier 2, primary family document by Ruth Gold, Oct 9, 2011) confirms the marriage of Helen Brandt to Morris Markel but provides no additional biographical detail about Helen herself (birth date, parents, etc.). **However, the 2026-04-18 identification of Helen as Dwora Goldbaum/Brand from the 1919 Sambor marriage banns (Tier 1) now provides her Yiddish name, origin town, parents' names, and marriage date from a primary civil record.** This elevates Helen's overall confidence from Speculative to Moderate Signal.
