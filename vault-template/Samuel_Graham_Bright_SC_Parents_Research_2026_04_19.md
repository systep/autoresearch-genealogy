# Hypothesis: Samuel Graham Bright's Parents Are Already Documented in the Vault; The "Greenville SC" Framing Is a Misattribution of Greenbrier VA

## Statement

The research task's framing of Samuel Graham Bright as an 1825-born Greenville County South Carolina farmer with unidentified parents is **contradicted by the vault's own person file [[Samuel_Graham_Bright]]**, which already records:

- **Father**: George Michael Bright (Sr.) — per FamilySearch Family Tree record 40001:237052816
- **Mother**: Mary "Polly" McNeil — per FamilySearch
- **Birthplace**: Greenbrier, Virginia (not Greenville, South Carolina)
- **Death place**: Rocky Mount, Franklin, Virginia
- **Burial**: High Street Cemetery, Rocky Mount, Franklin, Virginia

Cross-referencing [[David_Bright]] (1740-1808) confirms the same George (c. 1782-1873) as David's son by Mary Grant, married to Mary McNeil, with children including Samuel Graham Bright. This extends the chain to Johann Michael Brecht II (1706-1794), the immigrant ancestor.

## Best-Evidence Ancestor Chain (Samuel Graham Bright's Patrilineal Line)

Generation 1: Johann Michael Brecht II (1706-1794, Schriesheim, Germany -> Germantown, PA 1726)
              m. Margareta Simone (1708-1778)

Generation 2: David Brecht/Bright (Aug 9, 1740 Heidelberg, Lancaster Co PA -
              1808 Greenbrier Co VA; miller, Revolutionary supplier to

Zero unnamed generations remain in this patriline.

## Correction to Round 9 Framing

The Round 9 Bright agent's prompt assumes:

1. Samuel Graham Bright (1825-1909) is "of Greenville County SC"
2. His parents are unknown
3. The bridge runs via George Adam Bright's Augusta/Botetourt VA sons migrating south to SC

All three premises conflict with the vault's own documentation:

1. **Location**: Vault [[Samuel_Graham_Bright]] places Samuel in Greenbrier VA (birth) and Franklin Co VA (life, death, burial), NOT Greenville SC. "Greenville" and "Greenbrier" are easily confused; prior research rounds appear to have propagated a misreading.
2. **Parents**: FamilySearch Family Tree names George Michael Bright Sr. and Mary McNeil as parents. This is a Tier 2 compiled index, consistent with RootsWeb and Montgomery county history.
3. **Bridge**: The bridge does NOT run through George Adam Bright of Augusta Co VA. It runs through David Bright (Johann Michael II's son) who moved PA -> Greenbrier Co VA c. 1784. Vault [[George_Michael_Bright]] explicitly rejects the George Adam line per RootsWeb.

## Confidence Tiers

- Samuel Graham Bright's parents are George Bright and Mary McNeil: **Moderate Signal** (1 Tier 2 compiled index corroborated by Tier 2 RootsWeb published genealogy; not yet backed by Tier 1 vital record).
- Samuel Graham Bright descends from Johann Michael Brecht II via David Bright (1740-1808): **Strong Signal** (FamilySearch + RootsWeb + Montgomery county history + vault GEDCOM all agree).
- Samuel was born in Greenbrier VA, not Greenville SC: **Strong Signal** (vault GEDCOM + FamilySearch concur; no open-web source places him in SC).
- George Bright's middle name was "Michael": **Speculative** (single FamilySearch source; RootsWeb and Montgomery list him simply as "George").
