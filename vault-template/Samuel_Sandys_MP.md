## Biography

Sir Samuel Sandys (1560 to 1623) was an English landowner, knight, and politician. He was the eldest surviving son of Edwin Sandys, Archbishop of York, by his second wife Cecily Wilford (Wikipedia, Tier 2; History of Parliament Online, Tier 2).

Samuel acquired the lease of the royal manor of Ombersley, Worcestershire, in 1582. He was a Justice of the Peace for Worcestershire from 1597. He was knighted by King James I at Whitehall on July 23, 1603 (History of Parliament Online, Tier 2).

His political career included: MP for Ripon (1586), MP for Worcestershire (1609, by-election), re-elected 1614 and 1621. He also served as Sheriff of Worcestershire, 1618 to 1619 (GEDCOM, Tier 3; History of Parliament Online, Tier 2).

Samuel married Mary (or Mercy) Culpepper, daughter of Dr. [[Martin_Culpepper]] of Astwood, Feckenham, Worcestershire. Dr. Martin Culpepper's will (1605) references "my son in law Sir Samuel Sandis of Ombersley" and bequeaths lands and rents to Samuel and his children (GEDCOM transcription of Culpepper will).

His brother Sir [[Edwin_Sandys]] was the Virginia Company founder. His siblings also included George Sandys (poet/translator) and Myles Sandys (Filae family trees, Tier 3).

## The Sandys Baronets

The Sandys family produced multiple titled branches. Samuel Sandys (1560 to 1623) was knight and MP. His son Samuel Sandys (1615 to 1685), also knighted, served as colonel and MP for Droitwich and Worcestershire during the Civil War and Restoration. A later descendant, Samuel Sandys, 1st Baron Sandys (1695 to 1770), was created Baron Sandys in 1743 (Wikipedia, Tier 2).
