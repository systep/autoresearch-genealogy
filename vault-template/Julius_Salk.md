## Biography

Julius Salk was born circa 1871 in Kovno (Kaunas), Russian Empire (now Lithuania), son of [[Benjamin_Salk]] and [[Esther_Rappaport]]. His age of 39 on the 24 November 1910 New York marriage certificate places his birth between November 1870 and November 1871.

By 1910 Julius was a **store keeper** living at **1800 Cedar Avenue** in what is now the Bronx, New York. He had been previously married; his first wife, name not recorded on the 1910 certificate, was deceased before November 1910.

On **24 November 1910** he married **Rebecca Marcus (née Friedberg)**, also a widow, at a ceremony in Manhattan officiated by **Rev. Jacob Edelborn** (residing at 62 E. 114 St, Harlem). Witnesses were **Bernhard Goldberg** and **Leo Friedberg**, both from the bride's side. The marriage was Rebecca's second as well. See the full transcription at [[Julius_Salk_Rebecca_Marcus_NY_Marriage_1910]].

### Relationship to the Providence Salk families

Julius Salk was born in Kovno in the same Lithuanian-Jewish diaspora as [[Louis_Elizar_Salk]], [[Nussen_Elia_Salk]], [[Abraham_Zolly_Salk]], and [[Hyman_Salk_1873]], but his father **Benjamin Salk** (the Benjamin who married Esther Rappaport, likely b. ~1840s) is distinct from the **Benjamin Salk b. 1889 St. Petersburg** listed as a child of [[Nussen_Elia_Salk]] in the MyHeritage GEDCOM. Julius is therefore probably a cousin of Abraham Zolly Salk, not a direct nephew. Detailed placement within the broader Salk/Zalk family network of Kovno Gubernia is an open question.
