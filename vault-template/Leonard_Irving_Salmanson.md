## Biography

Leonard Irving Salmanson was the eldest son of Barnett and Elizabeth (Salk) Salmanson. He was born June 21, 1910. Together with his father and brothers Samuel, Charles, and Donald, he founded Adams Drug Company in 1932 in Woonsocket and Pawtucket, Rhode Island. He led the company's diversification into manufacturing (Gong Bell Mfg. Co., Clover Bead & Jewelry Co., Colt Plastics Co.) and real estate.

He married Thelma Markel in 1947. They had three children: Alan C., Barbara, and Carol S. Salmanson. Alan graduated from Moses Brown School, Providence, RI, Class of 1969.

Leonard died May 15, 1975 and is buried at Lincoln Park Cemetery, Warwick, Rhode Island. After his death, Thelma remarried to Leonard Rodbell in 1978 and moved to Atlanta. Donald Salmanson succeeded Leonard as president of Adams Drug Co. from 1975 to 1984.
