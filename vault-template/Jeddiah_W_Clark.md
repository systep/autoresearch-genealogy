## Biography

Jeddiah W. Clark was born on 5 March 1801 in South Carolina. He was the son of [[James_Hiriam_Clark]] (b. c.1770, Virginia; d. after 1835, Simpsonville, SC) and [[Mary_Neale]]. He married [[Gilley_Elizabeth_Baldwin]] (b. 19 Aug 1805, Greenville, SC; d. 25 May 1886, Simpsonville, SC), daughter of [[Eli_Baldwin]] (c.1775 to 1840) of the Baldwin family of Chatham County, North Carolina.

Jeddiah and Gilley had at least four known children:
1. [[Eli_Berry_Clark]] (b. 31 Jul 1825; d. 26 Nov 1895, Greenville County, SC); married Eliza Jane Cox; buried at Sonpoint/Bethuel Baptist Church Cemetery, Greenville, SC
2. Perry F. Clark (b. 1831)
3. [[Sarah_Elizabeth_Clark]] (b. 13 Aug 1836; d. 5 Jul 1879); married [[Thomas_Manning_Cox]]
4. Jane Clark (dates unknown)

The family resided in the Simpsonville/Standing Springs area of Greenville County, South Carolina. Jeddiah's father in law Eli Baldwin had purchased 250 acres in Greenville County in 1812. The Clark, Cox, Henderson, Long, and Baldwin families were closely intermarried in this community during the 19th century. Jeddiah's son Eli Berry Clark married Eliza Jane Cox (a member of the Cox family), and his daughter Sarah Elizabeth married Thomas Manning Cox.

Jeddiah died on 27 January 1860 in Simpsonville, Greenville, South Carolina. His widow Gilley survived him by 26 years.
