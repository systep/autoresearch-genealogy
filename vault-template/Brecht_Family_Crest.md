# Brecht Family Crest and Coat of Arms

## Origin

**Language**: German (Old High German)
**Meaning**: "Bright" or "shining," derived from the Old High German word "breht" or "brecht," signifying someone who is intelligent, illustrious, or radiant. Short form of the personal name "Albrecht" (berht = bright). This etymological connection explains the direct semantic translation to the English surname "Bright" during colonial Americanization.
**Type**: Descriptive (derived from a personal name)
**Region**: Rhineland Palatinate, Baden-Wuerttemberg, Germany

## Coat of Arms Description (Commercial Heraldry Sources)

**IMPORTANT CAVEAT**: The following coat of arms description comes from commercial heraldry websites (crestsandarms.com, heraldrysinstitute.com), which are Tier 3 sources that generate or assign arms to surname groupings. In German heraldic tradition, coats of arms belong to specific individuals and their male-line descendants, NOT to everyone sharing a surname. No primary heraldic grant specific to the Brecht family of Schriesheim has been located in this research.

## The Brecht to Bright Name Transition

The anglicization from Brecht to Bright was a direct semantic translation (German "brecht" = English "bright"). The transition occurred gradually during the second generation of the American family:

1. **1759**: George Adam Brecht began calling himself George Adam Bright when he moved to Frederick County, Virginia (Geni, RootsWeb)
2. **1768**: The first documented written use of "Bright" appears in the signature of Michael (Johann Michael's third son, b. 1732) on the administration account for the estate of Conrad Bower, whose widow he married (Geni project)
3. **By 1783**: David Brecht's name was still written "Brecht" until 1783, after which "Bright" became standard (RootsWeb)
4. **By the Revolution**: The family was known as "the Bright family" (RootsWeb)
