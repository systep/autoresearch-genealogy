## Biography

Brashier Henderson was born May 10, 1790, the fourth of at least twelve children of **Ezekiel Henderson** (1763-1846) and **Elizabeth Brasher** (married October 10, 1782 in South Carolina). Ezekiel was a **Revolutionary War veteran** who served as a private (1781-1783) and received pension S6994. The family was rooted in Greenville County, South Carolina, where Ezekiel and Elizabeth lived for 47 years after the war.

Brashier's siblings included: Argaleus Hercules Henderson II (b. June 22, 1784), Sarah "Sary" Henderson (b. March 12, 1786), James Henderson (b. March 6, 1788), Nancy Henderson (b. June 14, 1792), John Henderson (b. 1795), **Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson** (b. March 29, 1795; d. Aug 21, 1876), Frances "Franky" Henderson (b. Oct 10, 1799), William Henderson (b. Oct 15, 1802), Rebecca Adeline Henderson (b. March 3, 1804), Thomas Lawson Henderson (b. May 5, 1806), and Delila "Lilla" Henderson (b. Sept 29, 1809).

Brashier married **Elizabeth Taylor**. Their children included **Rev. Henry Langford Henderson** (b. June 7, 1812; d. November 18, 1886), Elizabeth Rebecca Henderson, Samuel P. Henderson, and Frances E. Henderson.

**Key family connection:** Brashier's sister Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson married [[Thomas_B_Cox]] (1792-1857). Their daughter [[Rebecca_Adeline_Cox_Henderson]] (1813-1894) married Brashier's son Rev. Henry Langford Henderson, creating a **first cousin marriage**. This is a significant genealogical note for the family tree.

Brashier died before July 19, 1847. He predeceased his father Ezekiel by approximately one year (Ezekiel died November 2, 1846, though if this date is accurate, Brashier may have died after his father).

The Henderson surname was part of the intermarried Greenville County, South Carolina cluster of families including the Coxes, Longs, Brashiers/Brazures, and others who had migrated from North Carolina.
