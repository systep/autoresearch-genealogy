## Biography

George Michael Bright was born in 1852, the son of **Samuel Graham Bright** (1825-1909) and **Mary Jane Bernard** (1829-1900). Samuel Graham Bright is confirmed as a sibling of John M. Bright (WikiTree Bright-409), both from the O'Neal district, Greenville County, South Carolina.

The Bright family descends from the German Palatinate Brecht/Bright family. **Johann Michael Brecht** (1706-1794) emigrated in 1726 from the Palatinate of Germany to Germantown, Pennsylvania. His son **George Adam Bright (Brecht)** moved to Frederick County, Virginia in 1759 and began using the anglicized name "Bright." George Adam is buried in **Dutch Hollow Cemetery** west of Greenville, SC, establishing the family's arrival in the Greenville area (RootsWeb, "Descendants of Johann Brecht").

George Michael married **Willis Henrietta Youngblood** (1865-1952). Willis was the daughter of William H. Youngblood (1828-1916) and Ester Lucy (1827-deceased) (MyHeritage). The handwritten family chart notes Willis as the "1st Woman Graduate of U.S. [?]," though this claim requires verification.

Their son **Albert Graham Bright** (b. Aug 19, 1893, Abingdon, Virginia; d. Sept 5, 1961, Delray Beach, FL) married Rebecca Moseley Long (1898-1985), daughter of William Goldsmith Long and Roxanne Cox. Through this marriage, George Michael is the great grandfather of Kathryn Ann Kascher, who married Alan Carl Salmanson.

**Commodorie S. Bright** (1880-1959, Find a Grave #78562893) may be another son of George Michael, though this connection requires verification.
