# Solomon (Jedidiah), King of Israel

> **Confidence: Moderate.** Solomon is a well attested biblical figure. Wikipedia confirmed. However, his role as ancestor in this tree is part of the claimed Davidic descent through the Maharal of Prague. This chain is contested by modern scholarship (Putik, Muneles). The connection from the Maharal back to King David relies on Meir Perels' Megillat Yuhasin (1745), which contains documented errors.

## Biography

Solomon (also called Jedidiah, meaning "beloved of the Lord") is described in the Hebrew Bible as the son of David and Bathsheba and the last king of the United Monarchy before its division. He is credited with building the First Temple in Jerusalem and is traditionally associated with great wisdom, wealth, and literary works including Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and the Song of Solomon.

The GEDCOM records him as "Solomon; Jedidiah / 2nd King House Of David" with FAMS @F500706@ (family as spouse) and FAMC @F500707@ (child of David). No dates are provided in the GEDCOM entry.
