# Ralph Neville

**Caveat**: The claimed Plantagenet descent through Ralph Neville has been REFUTED. His wife Alice de Audley was the daughter of Hugh de Audley, 1st Baron of Stratton Audley (no Plantagenet blood), NOT the daughter of Hugh de Audley, 1st Earl of Gloucester and Margaret de Clare (who carried Plantagenet blood). ## Biography

Ralph Neville was born circa 1291, the son of Ranulf de Neville, 1st Baron Neville de Raby, and Euphemia de Clavering. The Nevilles were one of the most powerful families in northern England, centered on Raby Castle in County Durham (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

The pedigree charts (Tier 3) claim Ralph married "Margaret de Audley, daughter of Hugh de Audley, Earl of Gloucester, and Margaret de Clare." This is INCORRECT. Ralph actually married **Alice de Audley** (d. 1374), who was the daughter of Hugh de Audley, 1st Baron Audley of Stratton Audley, and Isolde le Rous/Mortimer (Wikipedia: Ralph Neville, 2nd Baron Neville; Geni: Alice de Audley, Baroness Neville; Tier 2). Alice was the SISTER of Hugh de Audley, 1st Earl of Gloucester, not his daughter. The Earl's only child, Margaret de Audley, married Ralph Stafford, 1st Earl of Stafford (Wikipedia: Margaret Audley, 2nd Baroness Audley; Tier 2). Alice de Audley therefore carried NO Plantagenet blood, and any descent through Ralph Neville does not connect to the Plantagenet royal house through this claimed route.

Ralph succeeded his father as 2nd Baron Neville de Raby in 1331. He served in the Scottish wars and was a prominent figure in the north of England. He died on August 5, 1367, and was buried at Durham Cathedral (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

The Neville family is the key bridging line in the pedigree charts between the medieval Plantagenet royal house and the later colonial American families. The specific intermediate links from the Neville line forward to colonial settlers require verification.
