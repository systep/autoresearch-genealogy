## Biography

Ralph Neville was born circa 1291, the son of Ranulf de Neville, 1st Baron Neville de Raby, and Euphemia de Clavering. The Nevilles were one of the most powerful families in northern England, centered on Raby Castle in County Durham (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Ralph married Margaret de Audley, daughter of [[Hugh_de_Audley]], Earl of Gloucester, and [[Margaret_de_Clare]]. Through his wife, Ralph's descendants could claim descent from the Plantagenet royal house via the De Clare and De Audley families, tracing back through [[Joan_Plantagenet]], [[Edward_I]], [[Henry_III]], [[King_John]], [[Henry_II]], and [[Eleanor_of_Aquitaine]] (Pedigree Charts).

Ralph succeeded his father as 2nd Baron Neville de Raby in 1331. He served in the Scottish wars and was a prominent figure in the north of England. He died on August 5, 1367, and was buried at Durham Cathedral (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

The Neville family is the key bridging line in the pedigree charts between the medieval Plantagenet royal house and the later colonial American families. The specific intermediate links from the Neville line forward to colonial settlers require verification.
