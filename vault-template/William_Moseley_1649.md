## Biography

William Moseley was an English Merchant Adventurer who emigrated from Rotterdam, the Netherlands, to Lower Norfolk County, Virginia, in 1649. He is one of the earliest documented Moseley settlers in America (WikiTree Moseley-506, Tier 3; Colonial Virginia Portraits, Tier 2).

By August 14, 1643, William was serving as Schafmeister (Steward) of the "English House" of the Merchant Adventurers in Rotterdam. He was a broker trading goods of significant value as a member of the Company of Merchant Adventurers (WikiTree, Tier 3).

William Moseley, his wife Susanna (Burnet/Burnett), their sons William and Arthur, and seven other individuals arrived in Lower Norfolk, Virginia, on November 30, 1649. At a court hearing on March 26, 1650, William applied for headright on 550 acres based on transporting 11 persons to the Colony (WikiTree, Tier 3).

He built an estate in Lynnhaven Parish that came to be known as "Rolleston Hall," named after his ancestral family seat in Staffordshire, England. Dutch portraits, believed to depict William, his wife Susannah, and their two sons, were brought to America and later engraved (published by Bolton in 1919) (WikiTree, Tier 3; Colonial Virginia Portraits, Tier 2).

William Moseley died before 1655 in Lower Norfolk County. His son William Moseley II (c. 1634 to 1671) continued the Virginia line.

## Connection to This Family Tree

The connection between William Moseley of Lower Norfolk (1649) and the Surry/Brunswick County Moseley line (Jonathan Jonas James Moseley, b. 1683) has not been confirmed by primary sources. Both families are in colonial Virginia, and the Staffordshire origins of William Moseley align with the Staffordshire roots of the later Mosley/Moseley line (Richard Mosley of Kingsley, Staffordshire). WikiTree's "The very earliest Moseleys in America" page discusses the relationship between various Moseley branches in Virginia.
