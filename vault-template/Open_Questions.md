### 13. Elizabeth Brasher's parentage: Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.'s will names no daughters

Searched genealogy.com Brasher forums, Empty Branches blog, WikiTree Brasher-122 and Brasher-153. Found Ezekiel Henderson's 1856 estate settlement listing all legatees by name. Found cross-family marriage: Frances "Franky" Henderson (daughter of Ezekiel and Elizabeth) married Lawrence Aquilla Brasher (son of Aquilla B. Found "Empty Branches" blog warns: "There is a lot of mixed up information online about Thomas. - **New circumstantial evidence supporting parentage claim (2026-04-14)**:
  - (a) Frances Henderson (b. 1799, daughter of Ezekiel and Elizabeth) married Lawrence Aquilla Brasher (son of Aquilla B. Brasher) in 1820. Aquilla B. was named in Thomas Levi Jr.'s 1789 will. If Elizabeth were NOT Thomas Levi's daughter, this cross-family marriage between the Henderson and Brasher families would be a coincidence requiring explanation. The naming pattern (Frances married back into the Brasher family) suggests an ongoing familial connection.
  - (b) Ezekiel Henderson's estate settlement (1856, Greenville Co., H.L. Henderson administrator) lists "A. Brasher and Frances his Wife in Alabama" among legatees, confirming the Henderson/Brasher intermarriage.
  - (c) The name "Brashier Henderson" (b. 1790) given to a son of Ezekiel and Elizabeth preserves the maternal family name, a common colonial naming practice.
  - (d) Colonial/early republic practice: married daughters who had received their "portion" (dowry) at marriage were commonly excluded from fathers' wills. Elizabeth married seven years before the 1789 will. Exclusion of married daughters was customary, not evidence of non-relationship.
  - (e) Thomas Brasher Sr.'s 1770 will (Orange Co., NC) names daughters (Elizabeth Jones, Sarah Pyle, Hannah Teague) alongside sons, but Thomas Sr. was wealthier with more property; Thomas Levi Jr.'s smaller estate may have been distributed only among unmarried sons who needed it.

- **Pension file S6994 status (2026-04-14)**: The PDF at revwarapps.org/s6994.pdf exists but could not be fetched (403). Web search summaries of the pension declaration describe military service details only (drafted 3 months, volunteered 12 months, cavalry/infantry, discharged at Hillsboro NC, moved to SC, lived in Greenville 47 years). The pension file is an Act of 1832 application (service-based, not widow's pension), which typically does NOT include marriage certificates or family details beyond the applicant's own service. A widow's pension (W-series) would be more likely to contain marriage and family information, but Elizabeth predeceased Ezekiel so no widow's application exists.
- **Assessment update (2026-04-14)**: The circumstantial case for Elizabeth being Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.'s daughter has strengthened but remains without direct primary source confirmation. The cross-family marriage of Frances Henderson to Lawrence Aquilla Brasher is the strongest new evidence. Confidence upgraded from SPECULATIVE to MODERATE SIGNAL (multiple circumstantial indicators from independent facts align, but still no single primary document names Elizabeth as Thomas Levi's daughter).
- **Best leads remaining**: (1) Chatham County NC marriage bond for Henderson/Brasher, Oct 1782 (FamilySearch catalog 191194; bondsman identity could prove relationship). (2) Full text of Thomas Levi Brasher Jr.'s 1789 will from SC Will Book Vol A-B 1787-1820, p. 28 (Greenville Co.; FamilySearch film 0024025; images 2172-2178).
