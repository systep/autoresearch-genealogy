## Biography

Samuel Mathews Jr. (1630, 1660) served as the Commonwealth Governor of Virginia from 1656 to 1660, during the Interregnum period when Virginia was governed under the authority of the English Commonwealth. He was the son of Samuel Mathews Sr., a prominent early Virginia planter who arrived at Jamestown in 1619, and Frances Greville.

Mathews Sr. was one of the wealthiest planters in early Virginia, establishing a large estate. His son Samuel Jr. rose to political prominence and was elected governor by the colonial assembly. Governor Mathews died in office in 1660, at which point the colony's government transitioned as the English monarchy was restored.

Governor Mathews had a son, [[John_Mathews]] (b. 1659), who inherited substantial landholdings. Through John Mathews's daughter Elizabeth (who married David Holloway), this line connects to the Holloway family in the vault.
