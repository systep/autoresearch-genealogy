## Biography

Sarah S. Goldsmith was born c. 1800 in South Carolina. She married **[[Larkin_Long]]** (c. 1801-Mar 1884). Their son **[[William_Thomas_Long]]** (b. 25 Nov 1825, Simpsonville, Greenville, SC) was described in handwritten family notes as "1st son of Larkin and Sarah Long." The Geni record for William Thomas Long identifies his mother as "Sarah 'Sally' Long (born Goldsmith)," confirming the Goldsmith maiden name.

**Goldsmith family connection (speculative but plausible):** William Thomas Goldsmith Sr. (b. 24 Jun 1762, Richmond, VA; d. 21 Mar 1835, Simpsonville, Greenville, SC) was a Revolutionary War veteran who settled in Simpsonville. He married Anne "Isabella" Elizabeth Roundtree and had at least 12 children (WikiTree Goldsmith-699, Geni, Find a Grave #201972480). His documented children include: Mary Ann (1785), John Alexander (1787), Thomas (1787), William Jr. (1796), Amelia "Milley" (1800), Elizabeth "Bettie" (1802), Turner (1802), **Sarah "Sally" Goldsmith** (1805), Nancy (1807), Mary "Polly" (1809), Emily "Milly" (1811), and Lucille (1813).

The Sarah "Sally" Goldsmith (b. 1805) listed as William Thomas Goldsmith's daughter is a strong candidate for [[Larkin_Long]]'s wife Sarah S. Goldsmith (b. c. 1800). Both lived in Simpsonville, Greenville County SC. The birth date discrepancy (1800 per pedigree chart vs. 1805 per Geni/WikiTree) is minor and within the range of estimation error. One WikiTree source identifies Sarah as "Sarah (Goldsmith) Willingham," which could indicate either a second marriage or a different Sarah. This connection remains (unverified) pending primary source confirmation.

**William Goldsmith Long naming convention:** Sarah's grandson was named **[[William_Goldsmith_Long]]** (1862-1937). The "Goldsmith" middle name preserves his grandmother's maiden surname, a Southern naming convention consistent with "[[Rebecca_Moseley_Long]]" (named after Rebecca Moseley, 1772-1837/38).
