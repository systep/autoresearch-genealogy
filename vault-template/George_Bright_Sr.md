## Biography

George Bright Sr. was born c. 1782 in Pine Grove Township, Berks County, Pennsylvania (GEDCOM, Tier 3; Geni, Tier 3). He was the son of [[David_Bright]] (born David Brecht, 1740 to 1808) and Mary Gail Grant. David Bright's will, filed in Berks County, names "George" as receiving the residue of his estate (referenced in family records, Tier 1). The will refers to him simply as "George," with no middle name recorded.

George is listed in the David Bright file as "George Michael Bright Sr." However, the "Michael" middle name is unverified. David Bright's will uses only the name "George." The "Michael" name may be a later attribution based on the naming of his grandson [[George_Michael_Bright]] (1852 to 1939), or it may reflect a genuine family middle name that was not recorded in the will. Until a primary source confirms the "Michael" middle name, it should be treated as unverified.

George married Mary "Polly" McNeil (also recorded as M. Neil, McNeil) on **April 24, 1808 in Botetourt County, Virginia** (MyHeritage pedigree chart, page 4). The couple had many children, with [[Samuel_Graham_Bright]] (1825 to 1909) being among the best documented. Samuel was born in Greenbrier, Virginia (now West Virginia), indicating the family was living there by 1825. Other known children include Thomas Jefferson Bright, Sarah B. Bright, Dr. David B. Bright, John Moore Bright, and several others (FamilySearch, Tier 2).

George and his family lived in Greenbrier County, Virginia (now part of West Virginia), before relocating to Floyd County, Virginia, where he died in 1873. The move from Berks County, Pennsylvania, to Greenbrier, Virginia, is consistent with his father David Bright's death place, which is also recorded as Greenbrier, Virginia (GEDCOM, Tier 3). This suggests the family may have migrated together from Pennsylvania to Virginia in the early 19th century.

George lived to approximately 91 years of age, dying in 1873 in Floyd County, Virginia (WikiTree Bright-298, Tier 3).

## The Brecht/Bright Surname Transition

George was part of the third generation of the Brecht to Bright name change. His grandfather Johann Michael Brecht II (1706 to 1794) emigrated from Schriesheim in the Palatinate of Germany in 1726 as "Brecht." His father David was born as David Brecht in 1740. By George's generation, the anglicized form "Bright" was the established family surname (RootsWeb "Descendants of Johann Brecht," Tier 3).

## Connection to This Family Tree

George Bright Sr. is the father of [[Samuel_Graham_Bright]] (1825 to 1909). Samuel married [[Mary_Jane_Bernard]] and fathered [[George_Michael_Bright]] (1852 to 1939). George Michael married [[Willis_Henrietta_Youngblood]] (1865 to 1952). Their son [[Albert_Graham_Bright]] (1893 to 1961) married Rebecca Moseley Long (1898 to 1985), whose daughter [[Mary_Elizabeth_Bright]] (1921) married [[Henry_Michael_Kascher]]. Their daughter Kathryn Ann Kascher married Alan Carl Salmanson.

George Bright Sr. is therefore the 4th great grandfather of Leonard Daniel Salmanson through the Bright maternal line.
