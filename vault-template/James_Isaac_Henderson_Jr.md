## Biography

James Isaac Henderson Jr. was born after 1705 in Somerset County, Maryland (Family_Tree.md). He was the son of James "the Mariner" Henderson (c.1689-1752), who was himself the son of William "the Sawyer" Henderson (b. before 1668) and Sarah Bishop (m. 1685, Somerset County, MD). The Henderson family had been rooted in the Maryland/Virginia Eastern Shore since at least the 1660s (Family_Tree.md, WikiTree).

James married Mary Rhodes, who was born circa 1700. Some user-contributed trees suggest Mary was from Scotland, but this has not been independently confirmed (Family_Tree.md).

James and Mary settled in Onslow County, North Carolina. Their son Argaleus Hercules Henderson I (b. ~1720-1727) became a notable figure as a Quaker at Cane Creek Meeting who was disowned for participation in the NC Regulators movement (Family_Tree.md).

**Note on the "Friendship" ship claim:** Family_Tree.md documents that the claim James arrived as a convict on the ship "Friendship" in 1716 is NOT SUPPORTED. The Friendship of Belfast did carry 80 Jacobite prisoners to Maryland on August 20, 1716; however, no James Henderson appears on any of the 10 Jacobite transport ships' passenger lists. The Henderson family was already established in Somerset County, MD by the 1680s. This claim likely originated from user-contributed trees (Strong Signal for negative result).
