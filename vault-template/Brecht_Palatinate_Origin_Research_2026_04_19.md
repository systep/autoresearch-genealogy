# Hypothesis: Johann Michael Brecht (1706-1794) as 1710 NY Palatine Refugee

## Hypothesis Statement

Johann Michael Brecht (b. May 28, 1706 Schriesheim; d. Sep 13, 1794 Reading, PA; vault file [[Johann_Michael_Brecht]]) arrived in colonial America as part of the 1710 mass Palatine refugee wave to New York, indexed in Henry Z. Jones Jr., *The Palatine Families of New York 1710* (1985, 2 vols), or in Jones' companion *The Palatine Families of Ireland*.

## Status: REFUTED (Strong Signal)

## Evidence Against

### 1. Documented 1726 direct Pennsylvania arrival
Multiple tertiary and secondary sources converge on a well-documented 1726 direct-to-Philadelphia emigration that is inconsistent with the 1710 NY wave:

- **Schriesheim emigration petition, May 6, 1726**: Anna Catherina Brecht (widow of Johannes Michael Brecht I, d. 1719) petitioned with her two sons Stefan and Johann Michael for permission to emigrate to America. This is a Tier 2 record referenced in the Geni "Conrad Kuntz Brecht" project and RootsWeb "Descendants of Johann Brecht".
- **Arrival October 1726**: Landed Philadelphia, settled first in the Germantown section, then Heidelberg Township, Lancaster County, PA before April 1728 (Geni, RootsWeb, WikiTree Brecht-58).
- **Temporal impossibility of 1710 wave**: Johann Michael was 4 years old in 1710. His 1726 emigration, at age 20 with his widowed mother, is 16 years AFTER the Jones-indexed 1710 wave.

### 2. WikiTree Brecht-58 source citations do NOT include Jones
The profile's documented sources are:
- A.G. Green, "Historical Sketch of the Bright Family of Berks County, PA" (1900)
- H.W. Mills (1941, 142 pages)
- Neither "Palatine Families of New York 1710" nor "Palatine Families of Ireland" appears in citations for any Brecht family member connected to this lineage.

### 3. Surname search results
- WebSearch for "Johann Michael Brecht" + "Henry Z. Jones" returned zero matches linking the two.
- WebSearch for "Michael Brecht" / "Michael Precht" / "Michael Pracht" + 1710 Palatine New York returned no Jones-book matches. The only period-appropriate Precht/Pracht surnames on FEEFHS Palatine index pages do not identify a Michael b. c. 1706.
- WebSearch for "Hunter Subsistence List" + Brecht/Precht/Pracht returned no positive results.

### 4. Geographic and temporal mismatch
The 1710 wave settled in Hudson River camps (West Camp, East Camp) and then Schoharie, NY, with later Pennsylvania dispersal from the 1720s. Johann Michael's older brother Johannes Stephan Brecht (1692-1747, WikiTree Brecht-10) is noted as having "first settled in Schoharie, New York", which may be confused folklore, but his own emigration is also documented to 1726-1727. No primary source places either brother on the 1710 Rotterdam Sailing List, London Census of Palatines 1709, or the Hunter Subsistence List 1710-1712, the three principal identifying sources of the Jones book.

## Conclusion

Johann Michael Brecht is NOT present in the 1710 NY Palatine wave as documented by Henry Z. Jones. He is an independent 1726 Pennsylvania direct-arrival immigrant, predating the Strassburger and Hinke "Pennsylvania German Pioneers" ship-list series (which begins 1727).

## Alternate Hypotheses to Investigate

### H2: Pre-1727 direct PA arrival (HIGH PRIORITY, supported)
Johann Michael arrived Philadelphia in October 1726 with his mother and brother, aboard an unrecorded vessel, in the 1709-1727 pre-registry period. This matches all documented evidence. Research next steps:
- Albert G. Green, "Historical Sketch of the Bright Family of Berks County, PA" (1900), FamilySearch catalog #1036369, not yet obtained.
- "The Bright-Brecht Family of Pennsylvania" (227 pages), Historical Society of Pennsylvania, discover.hsp.org/Record/marc-67954.
- Schriesheim Reformed Church registers (christening May 30, 1706 already cited in GEDCOM).
- Kurpfalz emigration permit records in the Generallandesarchiv Karlsruhe (GLA) may hold the May 1726 petition original.

### H3: Germantown 1683 founder descent (REFUTED)
Johann Michael was not born in Germantown; he was born 1706 Schriesheim, so this is refuted by birthplace alone.

### H4: Non-German / non-Palatine origin (REFUTED)
Schriesheim is unambiguously in the Electoral Palatinate (Kurpfalz). Surname Brecht is attested in the region from at least 1563 (Conrad "Kunst" Brecht, WikiTree Brecht-13). Refuted.

### H5: Irish Palatine detour (NOT SUPPORTED)
Jones' "Palatine Families of Ireland" covers the ~1709-1710 Palatines who settled temporarily in Ireland before onward migration. Johann Michael's documented 1726 direct Schriesheim-to-Philadelphia route makes this unlikely. Not actively pursued.

## Recommendation

Close this hypothesis. Prioritize H2 research actions (obtain Green 1900 and Bright-Brecht HSP manuscript) to solidify the independently-strong 1726 direct Pennsylvania arrival narrative. No further Jones-book lookups needed unless a sibling or collateral relative is suspected of 1710 NY arrival.
