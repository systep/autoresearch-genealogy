## Biography

Eli Baldwin was born circa 1775 in Chatham County, North Carolina. He was the son of John Baldwin Sr. (c.1730, Chester, Pennsylvania, to c.1811, Chatham, NC) and Alice Francis Lindley (1736 to 1781). Alice Lindley was a Quaker, the daughter of James Lindley of Chester County, Pennsylvania, whose family had been members of the Society of Friends since the seventeenth century in Ireland.

Eli's siblings included Rachel (married Thompson, moved to Tennessee), Samuel (owned Baldwin's Mill on Terrell's Creek in Chatham County), John James, Nancy (married John Reeves, moved to South Carolina), John Jr., and William. In August 1801, John Baldwin Sr. deeded land to Eli in Chatham County (Deed Book L, pp. 239-240). In November 1802, Eli Baldwin deeded land to William Baldwin, suggesting he was preparing to leave Chatham County.

Eli migrated to Greenville County, South Carolina between 1802 and 1810. The 1810 census lists Eli Baldwin in Greenville, South Carolina. In 1812, he purchased 250 acres from John Croft in Greenville County (Deed Book I, p. 218), establishing the Baldwin family's presence in the Standing Springs area.

Eli married a woman named Elizabeth (surname unknown, per WikiTree) or Edaline L. Transon (per the MyHeritage GEDCOM). They had at least two children: Gilley Elizabeth Baldwin (who married Jeddiah W. Clark) and Sylvanus Julius Baldwin.

Eli died on 18 January 1840 in Greenville, South Carolina, at approximately age 65.
