# Hypothesis: The Vault's 30+ Bright Person Files Descend from Johann Michael Brecht (b. 1706 Schriesheim, emigrated 1726) via a Documented PA -> VA Anglicization Chain

## Statement

The vault's Bright cluster centered on [[Albert_Graham_Bright]] (b. 1893 Abingdon, Washington County, Virginia) descends from [[Johann_Michael_Brecht]] (b. 30 May 1706 Schriesheim, Electoral Palatinate; emigrated 6 May 1726 with mother Anna Catherina and brother Johann Stefan; arrived Philadelphia October 1726; m. Margareta Simone 1728). The Brecht -> Bright anglicization is documented in three secondary works (Green 1900, HSP manuscript, Gearhart manuscript) and was catalyzed by the 1750s-1760s migration of Johann Michael's son [[George_Adam_Bright]] (b. 1730, d. 1804) to Frederick, then Augusta, County, Virginia.

## Bridge from Vault Brecht Files to Vault Bright Files

### Vault Brecht ancestors (confirmed)
- [[Conrad_Kuntz_Brecht]] (~1563 to ~1612, Neudorf / Lindenfels)
- ... German generations ...
- [[Balthasar_Hans_Brecht]] and [[Christoph_Stoffel_Brecht]] (Schriesheim ancestors, per Brecht_Schriesheim_Parents_Research_2026_04_19)
- [[Johann_Michael_Brecht]] (b. 1706 Schriesheim) + mother [[Anna_Catherina_Bright_Brecht]] + brother [[Johann_Stephan_Bright]] (Stefan), emigrated May 6 1726

### Second generation in America (PA, Heidelberg Twp, Lancaster Co, then Berks Co)
Per Green 1900 and WikiTree/Geni, Johann Michael and Margareta Simone had 10 children. The fifth child was the first to systematically use the Anglicized "Bright."

Candidate vault matches for second-generation children (need verification against Green 1900 child list):
- [[George_Adam_Bright]] (b. 1730, d. 1804): documented as son of Johann Michael, migrated to Frederick County VA c. 1753; this is the keystone figure for the Brecht -> Bright -> Virginia transition
- [[George_Bright_Sr]], [[George_Bright]], [[George_Michael_Bright]]: possibly duplicates or children of George Adam; the vault Geni result says "George Adam Bright (Brecht), Sr. (1730-1804)" which matches the profile URL found
- [[Michael_Bright]], [[Peter_Bright]], [[Jacob_Bright]], [[John_Bright]], [[Henry_Bright]], [[Samuel_Bright]], [[David_Bright]], [[Mary_Bright]]: candidate children or grandchildren

### Third-to-fourth generation (Virginia)
Georg Adam Bright's will names sons: John, George, Windle, David; daughter Rebecka plus four other daughters.
Vault matches for this VA generation would be among:
- [[John_Bright]], [[George_Bright]], [[David_Bright]]
- A "Windle Bright" does not currently appear in vault; flag for creation if corroborated

Henry Bright Sr. (1760-1856) moved PA -> Lincoln County VA (later KY) in 1785; this may or may not correspond to vault [[Henry_Bright]].

### Fifth-to-seventh generation (Washington County VA / Abingdon)
This is the UNBRIDGED segment. No web-accessible source located in this session directly connects Georg Adam Bright's Augusta County VA descendants, or Henry Bright's Lincoln County line, to Washington County VA / Abingdon before the vault's 19th-century Brights (Zadock_Jesse, Thomas_Jefferson, Jesse_W, Samuel, Samuel_David, Albert_Graham, etc.).

The geographic gap: Augusta County VA (Georg Adam's seat) is approximately 200 miles north-east of Abingdon. A southward migration along the Great Wagon Road through Botetourt, Montgomery, and into Washington County VA in the late 18th or early 19th century is the expected bridge; this needs deed/will/census corroboration.
