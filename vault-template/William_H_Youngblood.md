## Biography

William Henry Youngblood was born in May 1828 in Bibb County, Alabama, the son of James Youngblood (from South Carolina) and Mary Bailey (from Alabama). He married Esther Lucy Kyle, who was born around July 1827 in Alabama (GEDCOM, Tier 3).

By 1850, William was living in Lowndes County, Mississippi, where he appears in the census (GEDCOM residence data). The family remained in Lowndes County through the 1870 and 1880 censuses. In the 1880 Census, William (age 50) is listed as head of household in Beat 5, Lowndes, Mississippi, with wife Lucy (age 52) and daughters Viola (22), Eleanor (19), and Willie (17) (1880 US Census, Tier 1).

By 1900, the family was still in Columbus, Lowndes, Mississippi (Beat 2, Columbus city Ward 3 to 4). Between 1900 and 1910, William relocated to Chattanooga, Tennessee, appearing in the 1910 Census in Chattanooga Ward 7, Hamilton, Tennessee (GEDCOM). Some records also show a 1910 residence at Beat 1, Lowndes, Mississippi, suggesting he may have maintained ties to both locations.

His wife Esther Lucy died May 20, 1912, and was buried at Forest Hills Cemetery, Chattanooga. William Henry Youngblood died on March 24, 1916, in Chattanooga and was buried the following day at Forest Hills Cemetery alongside his wife (GEDCOM, Tier 3).

The death certificate of Robert Kyle Youngblood identifies his parents as "William H. Kyle Youngblood" and "Lucy Kyle," which is consistent but adds the middle name "Kyle" (unverified).
