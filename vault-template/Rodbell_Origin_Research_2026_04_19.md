# Rodbell Origin Research, 2026-04-19

## Goal

Identify the immigrant-generation Rodbell to the United States and the most probable pre-emigration region (Russia, Poland, or elsewhere in the Pale of Settlement).

## Summary of Findings

The surname Rodbell appears to be a rare Ashkenazi Jewish surname. Public, non-login records identify a concrete Rodbell immigrant cluster in Baltimore, Maryland, not directly referenced in the current vault. No Ellis Island or Castle Garden result indexed by open web search surfaced for the surname in the queries run, but the Baltimore lineage is well documented through the Jewish Museum of Maryland and Nobel Prize biographical materials.

## Candidate Immigrant-Generation Rodbells (Strong Signal)

### Abram Rodbell (immigrant, undated, Poland to Baltimore)

Baker. Emigrated from Poland to Baltimore "several years before" 1894. Was Solomon's teacher in the old country. Operated his own bakery in Baltimore.

Source: Jewish Museum of Maryland, "Family Fare" article (Tier 2, secondary, well documented).

### Solomon Rodbell (immigrant, 1894, Poland to Baltimore)

Baker. Emigrated 1894 from Poland with wife Fannie and two-year-old daughter Dora (b. ~1892 Poland). Worked for brother Abram for one year, then opened bakeries on Pratt Street and later Lombard Street in Baltimore. Daughter Dora married Isaac Silber, who eventually took over Solomon's bakery, becoming Silber's Bakery.

Source: Jewish Museum of Maryland, "Family Fare" article (Tier 2).

## Secondary Finding: The Martin Rodbell Line (Baltimore, probable descent from Solomon or Abram)

- Martin Rodbell, Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 1994, born 1 December 1925 in Baltimore, died 7 December 1998.
- Parents: Milton W. Rodbell (grocer) and Shirley Abrams (1904-1982, Find a Grave memorial 208750452, buried United Hebrew Cemetery, Baltimore).
- Wife: Barbara Charlotte Rose Ledermann (1925-2024), Holocaust survivor from Berlin via Amsterdam, married 1950.
- Children of Martin and Barbara: Paul, Susanne, Andrew, Phillip.

Note: Milton Rodbell being Baltimore-based and of the right generation (ca. 1900 birth) strongly suggests he is a son or nephew of Solomon or Abram Rodbell. This is a Moderate Signal hypothesis pending documentary confirmation.

The vault data is entirely MyHeritage GEDCOM (Tier 3) and lists the origin as "Russia" for the ~1865 generation but "Poland" for the 1886 birth of Joseph Henry. This is consistent with a family from the Russian Partition of Poland, which after the 1867 Congress Poland arrangement was administered by the Russian Empire but was ethnically and geographically Polish. A single informant on different US documents would naturally record this region as either "Russia" or "Poland," explaining the GEDCOM discrepancy.

The vault's Atlanta Rodbells (Leonard Bland, Clyde A.) do NOT obviously map to the Baltimore bakery Rodbells (Solomon, Abram, Milton, Martin) found above. They may represent:

1. A separate immigrant branch (same surname, different origin village).
2. A migrated branch of the Baltimore Rodbells that moved south to Georgia.
3. A coincidence of surname (the name is rare enough that this is unlikely but not impossible).

## Best Hypothesis for Pre-Emigration Origin

**Moderate Signal**: The Rodbell family is Ashkenazi Jewish and originated in the Russian Partition of Poland (Congress Poland / Vistula Land, ca. 1867-1915), which at the time was administered by the Russian Empire. This is consistent with:

- The vault's GEDCOM "Russia" entries for ~1865 births.
- The vault's GEDCOM "Poland" entry for the 1886 birth of Joseph Henry.
- The Jewish Museum of Maryland article stating Solomon and Abram came "from Poland" without specifying Russian administration.
- Baker and grocer occupations, both common in Jewish shtetl economies.

**Specific town unknown.** No source consulted here names the shtetl of origin. Alexander Beider's "Dictionary of Jewish Surnames from the Kingdom of Poland" and "Dictionary of Jewish Surnames from the Russian Empire" (Avotaynu) should be consulted for a surname entry. The surname Rodbell may be a toponymic (from a place name such as Radbel, Radbyl, or similar) or derived from Yiddish "rot" (red) plus a suffix, but this is speculation without dictionary verification.

## Confidence Summary

- That the Rodbell surname is rare Ashkenazi Jewish and originates in the Russian Partition of Poland: **Moderate Signal**.
- That Solomon and Abram Rodbell are immigrant-generation Rodbells in Baltimore ca. 1880s-1894: **Strong Signal** (Jewish Museum of Maryland article, Tier 2).
- That these Baltimore Rodbells are connected to the vault's Atlanta Rodbells: **Speculative**, requires documentary bridge.
- That the vault's Phillip Rodbell (~1865, Russia) is the immigrant-generation head for the vault's line: **Moderate Signal**, but origin as "Russia vs Poland" is ambiguous and almost certainly means Russian-administered Poland.

## Session 2 (2026-04-19, afternoon): Atlanta bridge research

### Tier 2 findings (Legacy.com / Dignity Memorial obituary of Clyde A. Rodbell, 2015)

- Clyde Armand Rodbell (1927-2015, Atlanta) was the son of the late Joseph Henry Rodbell and the late Fannie Turetsky Rodbell, both of Atlanta.
- This establishes Fannie Turetsky (not Fannie, Solomon Rodbell's wife of Baltimore) as the mother of the Atlanta Rodbell siblings.
- Joseph Henry Rodbell (b. 1886 Poland per GEDCOM, d. 1964) is therefore confirmed as the father of Clyde Armand Rodbell and (via Apex Supply corporate history) of Leonard Bland Rodbell.

### Tier 2 findings (Apex Supply corporate history / Supply House Times)

- Joseph Rodbell founded Capitol Hide and Metal Company in Atlanta in 1911 (one source says 1912). This was a scrap metal business.
- Leonard Rodbell, son of Joseph, rebranded/pivoted the firm to Apex Plumbing Specialties in 1947.
- Clyde Rodbell, Leonard's brother, joined the firm as equal partner in 1949.
- Leonard Bland Rodbell was the father of Sidney Rodbell (consistent with vault's [[Sidney_Rodbell]]).

### Tier 2 finding (William Breman Jewish Heritage Museum, Atlanta)

- Oral history interview with Sidney Rodbell conducted 11 January 2022 at the Breman Museum. Available on the Aviary platform. Likely contains direct Rodbell family history from Sidney's perspective; should be accessed in person or via digital request to confirm grandfather Joseph Henry's origins and parents.

### Bridge hypothesis (updated, Moderate Signal)

Joseph Henry Rodbell (Atlanta, b. 1886 Poland) and Solomon Rodbell (Baltimore, b. ~1860s Poland, arr. 1894) share:

1. Rare Ashkenazi Jewish surname Rodbell.
2. Stated origin Poland.
3. Overlapping US timeframe (Solomon arrives 1894 Baltimore; Joseph establishes business 1911 Atlanta at age ~25, consistent with earlier US arrival as a young immigrant who then relocated from Baltimore to Atlanta).
4. A wife named Fannie for both men (coincidental; Solomon's wife Fannie is a different person from Joseph's wife Fannie Turetsky).

Most probable relationship: Joseph Henry is a nephew of Solomon and Abram (i.e., son of a third Rodbell brother still in Poland, or son of Abram or Solomon himself) who emigrated later, stopped in Baltimore with his uncles / father, then moved to Atlanta by 1911. The rarity of the surname and shared origin makes this far more plausible than coincidence.

Less likely but possible: Joseph Henry and Solomon/Abram are first cousins rather than nephew/uncle.

Remaining documentary steps to confirm:

1. 1900 US Census Baltimore: look for a young Joseph Rodbell (aged ~14) in Solomon's or Abram's household, or in a boarding house near them.
2. 1910 US Census Atlanta: Joseph Henry Rodbell aged 24, should be in Atlanta already, possibly newly married to Fannie Turetsky.
3. Joseph Henry Rodbell's Social Security application (SS-5 form) via NARA: would name parents directly.
4. Naturalization papers for Joseph Henry Rodbell in Fulton County, Georgia or Baltimore City.
5. Joseph Henry Rodbell's obituary (Atlanta Constitution, March 1964): should name siblings and parents.
6. Breman Museum Sidney Rodbell oral history: first-person family narrative.
