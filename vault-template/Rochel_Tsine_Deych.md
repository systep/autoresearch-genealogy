## Biography

Rochel Tsine Deych was the mother of [[Barnett_Salmanson]] (Berko Zalmanson, 1883-1960), the immigrant patriarch of the Salmanson family in Providence, Rhode Island. She was the daughter of [[Zawel_Deykh]].

She married [[Levi_Itzhak_Zalmanson]] (b. 1851) on December 2, 1877 in Rokiskis (Yiddish: Rakishok), Rokiskis District, Panevezys County, Lithuania (GEDCOM, Family Tree). She was approximately 16 years old at the time of marriage, which was typical for Jewish women in the Russian Empire during this period. This marriage connected the Deych family to the Zalmanson line, which descends from [[Yekutiel_Zalman_Wallis]] and [[Rivka_Beila_Schneerson]] (daughter of the Mitteler Rebbe).

**Rokiskis context:** Rokiskis was a predominantly Jewish town (75% Jewish in 1900, population 2,067 Jews) where virtually all Jews were Chabad (Lubavitch) Hasidim. The marriage of a Deych daughter to a Zalmanson, a family with direct Schneerson rabbinical lineage, suggests the Deych family held respected standing within the Rokiskis Chabad community.

Rochel Tsine was Levi Itzhak's first wife; he later married Bella Measroch (GEDCOM). Together, Rochel Tsine and Levi Itzhak had at least seven children. Their eldest, Gita (b. 1878), was named for Levi Itzhak's mother Gita Zalmanson, following Ashkenazi naming tradition. Their son Barnett (Berko) immigrated to the United States in 1904 on the SS Baltic.

The GEDCOM records her death date as estimated July 26, 1935, sourced from the US Obituary Index match for Barnett Salmanson, suggesting this date may come from Barnett's obituary mentioning his mother's death. If this date is correct, she died in Lithuania (she is not known to have emigrated).

**Geographic note:** The marriage took place in Rokiskis (northeastern Lithuania, near the Latvian border). Barnett was born in Vilnius in 1883, suggesting the family moved between 1877 and 1883 (Family Tree). This migration pattern (from smaller town to Vilna) was common among Lithuanian Jews in this period.

**Name note:** "Tsine" (also Tsina, Tsinya) is a Yiddish feminine given name, sometimes used as a standalone name and sometimes paired with a Hebrew name. "Rochel Tsine" would be the double name used in religious and legal contexts.

The December 2, 1877 marriage in Rokiskis falls within the period covered by the Rokiskis vital records abstracts (1876-1894), a book of abstracts created by the town administration and now held in the Kaunas Regional Archives. An index to these records is owned by the Rokiskis SIG. The original marriage record (if extant at LVIA) would contain:
