## Biography

Thomas B. Cox was born May 15, 1792, likely in Orange County, North Carolina or Greenville County, South Carolina. He was the son of William Cox Jr. (d. 1798) and Connie Baker (b. 1760). His father died when Thomas was approximately six years old. His mother Connie subsequently remarried to William Richardson.

Thomas's grandfather, [[William_Cox_Sr]], had migrated from Orange County, North Carolina to Greenville County, South Carolina around 1787, along with intermarried families including the Longs, Blacks, Paynes, Lees, and Brashiers.

Thomas married **Olive Mary "Polly" Henderson**, daughter of Ezekiel Henderson (1763-1846, Revolutionary War veteran) and Elizabeth Brasher. Polly was born March 29, 1795 in Simpsonville, South Carolina, and died August 21, 1876.

The couple had **twelve children**:

1. Rebecca Adeline Cox (1813-1894), married Rev. Henry Langford Henderson (first-cousin marriage: Polly Henderson and Brashier Henderson were siblings)
2. William J. "Billy Slick" Cox
3. Harvey Cox
4. Sarah Cox
5. Elizabeth Cox (possibly the "T. Elizabeth Cox" from the large handwritten chart)
6. Margaret A. Cox
7. **Thomas Manley Cox** (1828-1897), married Sarah Elizabeth "Lizzie" Clark (1836-1879); their daughter Roxanne Cox married William Goldsmith Long, continuing the line to the present family
8. Isaac Cox (1828-1905)
9. Henry Cox
10. James Perry Cox
11. Mary Caroline Cox
12. Jefferson Cox

Thomas B. Cox died June 12, 1857 in South Carolina. His son Thomas Manning (Manley) Cox is the direct ancestor connecting this line to the present family through the Cox-Long intermarriage.
