## Implied three-generation chain

Assuming the two records refer to the same Gersz (which is highly plausible given both share surname, town, and Gersz-as-named):

```
Srol Konjanski (b. ~1810-1830; alive when Gersz was born)
    |
    Gersz Konjanski (b. ~1830-1850, died 1874 Białystok)
        |
        Sore Leja Kunjanska (d. 1866 Białystok; predeceased her father Gersz by 8 years)
        ... additional children of Gersz, undocumented in these 2 index entries
```

This is a **candidate ancestral chain** for the vault's Atlanta Kuniansky cluster. Timeline is consistent with the vault's earliest documented Atlanta Kuniansky immigrant [[Wolf_Kuniansky]] (b. 15 Dec 1884 Russia, d. 22 Sep 1964 Fulton County GA) being a **grandchild or great-grandchild of Srol Konjanski** via an unknown son of Gersz (Sore Leja died as a child in 1866 and had no descendants).

**Important caveat**: Białystok was a very large Jewish community (~50,000 Jews in 1900) with multiple unrelated Konjanski/Kuniansky families possible. Two shared-surname index hits 8 years apart do not prove connection to the Atlanta cluster — corroboration required.

## Notes

- **Białystok was heavily bombed in 1941 by the Luftwaffe and its Jewish community (~50,000) nearly eliminated in 1941-1943 (Białystok Ghetto Uprising Aug 1943).** Very few Kuniansky descendants remained in Białystok post-Holocaust. This is a strong emotional context for the research.
- Surname variants noted in the JRI-Poland index: **Kunjanska** (feminine), **Konjanski** (masculine standard Polish-Yiddish), **Kuniansky** (Americanized). Yiddish source-form is likely something like קוניאַנסקי (Kunianski), with variable transliteration depending on era and indexer.
- **Gersz** in Polish-Yiddish = Hirsh/Hersh/Tzvi in Hebrew = "deer" (a common male name). **Srol** = Israel. **Sore Leja** = Sarah Leah.
- The Grodno Gubernia context places the family in what is now northeastern Poland and western Belarus. Białystok itself is in present-day Podlaskie Voivodeship, Poland.
