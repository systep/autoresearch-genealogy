## Biography

Yehuda Leib the Elder is a claimed ancestor of the Maharal of Prague. His tombstone in the Old Jewish Cemetery in Prague bears an inscription reading "descended from Yishai" (Jesse, father of King David), which has been the basis for the Davidic descent claim of the Maharal's family.

According to Meir Perels' Megillat Yuhasin (1745), Chaim (the Maharal's grandfather) was a grandson of this Yehuda Leib the Elder, thus establishing the Maharal's Davidic lineage.

**CRITICAL SCHOLARLY DISPUTE:** Otto Muneles discovered in the early 1950s that Perels misread the Hebrew date on the tombstone. Perels recorded the death date as corresponding to 1439/1440 (Hebrew year). Muneles showed the actual date reads "shin" (Hebrew year 5300), corresponding to 1539/1540, a full century later (Wikipedia: Judah Leib the Elder; Chaim Freedman blog).

This date correction creates a fatal problem for the genealogy: since Ashkenazi Jewish naming practice forbids naming a child after a living ancestor, and the Maharal (born c. 1525) bore the same name "Yehuda," the Maharal could not have been descended from someone who was still alive at his birth (Chaim Freedman).

However, Freedman and others note that this does not necessarily eliminate all Davidic descent claims for the Maharal. Other traditions trace the family's royal descent through Rabbi Yehudah Hanassi, of the House of Hillel, who descended from King David's son Shefatiah.

While the tombstone physically exists in Prague, the genealogical connection to the Maharal's family is based on Perels' Megillat Yuhasin, which scholars have shown to contain deliberate errors. The critical date discrepancy (1439 vs. 1539) undermines the core claim. The person existed, but his relationship to the Maharal is contested.
