## Biography

Gilley Elizabeth Baldwin was born on 19 August 1805 in Greenville, South Carolina. She was the daughter of Eli Baldwin (c.1775 to 18 January 1840) and either Elizabeth (surname unknown, per WikiTree) or Edaline L. Transon (per GEDCOM); see Data Discrepancies below. The Baldwin family originated in Chatham County, North Carolina, where Eli's father John Baldwin Sr. (c.1730 to c.1811) had settled by the 1750s. Eli Baldwin migrated to Greenville County, South Carolina between 1802 and 1810, purchasing 250 acres from John Croft in 1812 (Deed Book I, p. 218).

Gilley Elizabeth's paternal grandfather John Baldwin Sr. married Alice Francis Lindley (1736 to 1781), a Quaker descended from James Lindley of Chester County, Pennsylvania. John and Alice had seven known children: Rachel (married Thompson, moved to Tennessee), Samuel (owned Baldwin's Mill on Terrell's Creek in Chatham Co.), John James, Nancy (married John Reeves, moved to SC), Eli, John Jr., and William. Several of these siblings migrated from Chatham County to Greenville County, South Carolina, including Nancy and Eli.

Gilley Elizabeth married Jeddiah W. Clark (b. 5 March 1801, South Carolina; d. 27 January 1860, Simpsonville, Greenville, SC). Together they had several children, including:
1. Eli Berry Clark (b. 31 July 1825; d. 26 November 1895; married Eliza Jane Cox; buried at Sonpoint/Bethuel Baptist Church Cemetery, Greenville, SC)
2. Perry F. Clark (b. 1831)
3. Sarah Elizabeth "Lizzie" Clark (b. 13 August 1836; d. 5 July 1879; married Thomas Manning Cox)
4. Jane Clark (dates unknown)
5. Possibly others not yet identified

Gilley Elizabeth's brother Sylvanus Julius Baldwin also resided in the area (WikiTree Baldwin-2330).

Gilley Elizabeth survived her husband by 26 years, dying on 25 May 1886 in Simpsonville, Greenville, South Carolina.
