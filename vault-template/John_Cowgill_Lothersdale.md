## Biography

John Cowgill, known as "of Lothersdale," was baptized September 27, 1572 at Thornton in Craven, Yorkshire, England. He was the son of [[Bryan_Cowgill]] (d. August 7, 1599) and Ellen. The designation "of Lothersdale" indicates his primary residence in the hamlet of Lothersdale within the parish.

John married his first wife, Margaret Gardiner, on May 3, 1601. Their son Edmund Cowgill Sr. was baptized in 1601 or 1602. Edmund would continue the Cowgill line that eventually emigrated to the American colonies.

Late in life, John married a second time to Mary Crosley on June 4, 1634. He was buried just weeks later on July 23, 1634 at Carleton in Craven, Yorkshire, suggesting he may have been ill at the time of his second marriage or that the marriage was intended to settle estate matters.

Through his son Edmund Cowgill Sr. and the subsequent generations, this line connects to [[Ralph_Cowgill]], [[Jane_Cowgill]], and the Sands/Hough families in the vault.
