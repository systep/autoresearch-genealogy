## Surname Analysis

The surname Deykh/Deych/Deich is a variant of "Deutsch" (German for "German"), a common Ashkenazic Jewish surname. In Yiddish, "Daytsh" means "German," and the surname was typically given to Jews who had migrated from German speaking regions to Eastern Europe, or adopted during the Russian Empire's mandatory surname adoption period (early 19th century). Variant spellings include: Deych, Deich, Daych, Daitsh, Daitch, Deiches, Deutsch, Teutsch.

The Daiches/Deiches family is a documented Lithuanian Jewish rabbinic family originating from Eishishok (near Vilna/Lida), with members serving as rabbis in Kovno, Vilna, and elsewhere. Whether Zawel's Deykh family is a branch of this rabbinic Deiches family or an independent family sharing the same etymological surname origin is unknown.

## Given Name Analysis

"Zawel" is likely the Yiddish form "Zavel," a diminutive of the Hebrew name Zevulun (Zebulun). The famous cantor Zevulun Kwartin (1874-1952) was universally known as "Zavel." In Russian records, the name may appear as "Savely" or "Sawel."
