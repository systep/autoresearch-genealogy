# Zavel Mogil (a.k.a. Zawel Deykh)

## 2026-04-22 Major Revision: Identity Resolved via LitvakSIG Tier 1

**The Rokiskis 1877 marriage record of daughter Rokhel Tsine names the father-of-the-bride as "Zavel MOGIL", not "Zavel Deych".** Two LitvakSIG-indexed sources found via MyHeritage 2026-04-22:
- *Lithuanian-Jewish Marriages and Divorces from LitvakSIG, 1808-1940*: Rokhel Tsine Deych (Zelmenzon), Residence Vidz, b. ~1861, Marriage **Dec 2, 1877, Rokiskis, Panevezys, Kaunas, Lithuania**, Groom Leyvi Itsik Zelmenzon, **Father: Zavel Mogil**.
- *Lithuanian-Jewish Records from LitvakSIG, 1795-1940*: same event, father listed as **Zavel Mogil**, daughter's birth ~1862 in Vidz.

This resolves OQ #15 at **Strong Signal**. The family patriarch's surname was **Mogil**, not Deych. Possible explanations for Rokhel Tsine's "Deych" surname:
- Her mother's maiden name was Deych, and she went by her mother's family name (common in Jewish communities when mother's family had higher standing)
- Ashkenazi Jewish surnames were not fully hereditary across generations until late 19th century; individual identity often tied to patronymic + locational/trade identifier independent of a stable family surname
- Deych ("Deutsch" = "German") may have been an identifier for Rokhel's family branch's German-speaking origins, distinct from Zavel's own Mogil surname

"Mogil" is a Jewish surname of locational origin, from **Mogilev** (Mahilyow), a city in Belarus. Zavel Mogil's family may originally have come from Mogilev, linking to the broader Belarus-Lithuania Jewish migration.

## Surname Analysis

The surname Deykh/Deych/Deich is a variant of "Deutsch" (German for "German"), a common Ashkenazic Jewish surname. In Yiddish, "Daytsh" means "German," and the surname was typically given to Jews who had migrated from German speaking regions to Eastern Europe, or adopted during the Russian Empire's mandatory surname adoption period (early 19th century). Variant spellings include: Deych, Deich, Daych, Daitsh, Daitch, Deiches, Deutsch, Teutsch.

The Daiches/Deiches family is a documented Lithuanian Jewish rabbinic family originating from Eishishok (near Vilna/Lida), with members serving as rabbis in Kovno, Vilna, and elsewhere. Whether Zawel's Deykh family is a branch of this rabbinic Deiches family or an independent family sharing the same etymological surname origin is unknown.

## Given Name Analysis

"Zawel" is likely the Yiddish form "Zavel," a diminutive of the Hebrew name Zevulun (Zebulun). The famous cantor Zevulun Kwartin (1874-1952) was universally known as "Zavel." In Russian records, the name may appear as "Savely" or "Sawel."

## Biography

Zawel Deykh was the father of [[Rochel_Tsine_Deych]] (b. 1861) and the maternal grandfather of [[Barnett_Salmanson]] (Berko Zalmanson, 1883-1960). He is identified in the GEDCOM (individual I500679) as a male with the surname Deykh. The Geni.com record for Rochel Tsine confirms her father as Zawel Deykh.

No birth or death dates, locations, or occupation are recorded in the GEDCOM for Zawel Deykh. His daughter married [[Levi_Itzhak_Zalmanson]] on December 2, 1877 in Rokiskis, Lithuania, strongly suggesting Zawel lived in or near Rokiskis. Rokiskis (Yiddish: Rakishok) was a predominantly Jewish town in the Zarasai (Novoalexandrovsk) District of Kovno Gubernia, and virtually all its Jewish residents were Chabad (Lubavitch) Hasidim. If Zawel lived there, he was almost certainly a Chabad Hasid.

The Family Tree file records him at the same generation as [[Srul_Mendel_Zalmanson]] (Generation 3) and lists his confidence as "strong" based on MyHeritage records.

The marriage of his daughter to Levi Itzhak Zalmanson, a descendant of the Schneerson rabbinic dynasty, suggests the Deykh family held sufficient standing within the Chabad community for such an alliance.

## Research Leads (2026-04-13)

The following databases and archives may contain records for Zawel Deykh:

1. **LitvakSIG All Lithuania Database (ALD)**: Free, 2.8+ million records. Search for surname "Deych" / "Deich" / "Daych" in Zarasai District. URL: litvaksig.org/search-ald/
2. **Rokiskis vital records abstracts (1876-1894)**: The Rokiskis town administration created a book of abstracts from Jewish vital records covering 1876 to 1894. Rochel Tsine's 1877 marriage should appear in these abstracts; the marriage record would name the bride's father.
3. **Rokiskis 1897 All-Russian Census**: Owned by the Rokiskis SIG (kehilalinks.jewishgen.org/rokiskis/). If Zawel was alive in 1897, he would appear with his household.
4. **Rokiskis 1908 Family List**: Also owned by the Rokiskis SIG. Would list family members.
5. **LVIA (Lithuanian State Historical Archives, Vilnius)**: Holds Rokiskis Jewish vital records. Marriage records from 1877 would name the bride's father, his occupation, and possibly his place of residence. Email research requests accepted in English.
6. **JewishGen Yizkor Book Master Name Index**: Search for Deych/Deich in the Rokiskis memorial book (Yisker-bukh fun Rakishok un umgegnt, 1952).
7. **Avotaynu Consolidated Jewish Surname Index**: Searches 31 databases using Daitch-Mokotoff Soundex for surname variants.
8. **Ancestry Collection 1416**: Lithuania, Jewish Birth Records from Various Towns, 1837-1940. Subscription required.
9. **FamilySearch microfilms**: Lithuanian Jewish vital records microfilmed by LDS; viewable at FamilySearch Centers.
