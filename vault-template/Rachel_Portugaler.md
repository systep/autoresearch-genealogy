## Biography

Rachel Portugaler was born in 1693 in Poznan, Poland (GEDCOM). The surname "Portugaler" indicates Portuguese/Sephardic origin. Her nickname was Rochel (GEDCOM).

She married [[Schneur_Zalman_Loew]] (born circa 1692, Poznan), who was the paternal grandfather of the Alter Rebbe ([[Shneur_Zalman_of_Liadi]]) (GEDCOM). Per the Hebrew biographical notes in the GEDCOM, Rachel was the daughter of Rabbi Baruch Batlan of Poznan, and her mother's lineage traced back to the Maharshal (Rabbi Solomon Luria), renowned as a kabbalist.

The GEDCOM describes Rachel as a uniquely learned and righteous woman who was meticulous in observing commandments, including a particular care about morning hand-washing (netilat yadayim) even for nursing infants. She married Schneur Zalman at age nineteen.

Rachel and her husband moved from Poznan to Vitebsk, where she died in 1735 while her children Devorah Leah and Yisroel Baruch (the future father of the Alter Rebbe) were still young. Her husband Schneur Zalman died shortly after her (GEDCOM Hebrew notes).
