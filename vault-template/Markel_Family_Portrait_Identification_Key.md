## Mapping to the Family Portrait (Image B)

The portrait shows ~15 people arranged 7 / 6 / 4 (back / middle / front), and the identification key has 15 circles arranged 5 / 6 / 4 (top / middle / bottom). **The key has 5 in the top row vs. the photo's 7 in the back row** — so the key may not literally map row-for-row to the photo. Two possibilities:

1. The key author labeled only those people whose identity was known to her, leaving 2 back-row figures unidentified. The 5+6+4 arrangement is therefore selective, not positional.
2. The key was for a *different* photograph than the large sepia portrait, and the photographs are being mistakenly conflated.

**Without confirmation from the key author (presumably Ruth Gold or sibling), positional mapping cannot be established.** This vault note records the key contents; positional pairing to the photograph requires human review by someone who knew the family.

## Photograph Setting and Dating

Inferences from Image B alone:

- **Dress**: Women in early-1920s flapper-era dresses (drop waists, long pearl necklaces, bobbed hair). Men in formal dark suits and bowler / fedora hats. Children in light short dresses (girls) or matching jumpers (boys). Strongly suggests **mid-1920s**, possibly 1924-1928.
- **Setting**: Studio, classical column visible upper-left. Common for Providence / New York studios of the period.
- **Composition**: Large extended-family combined portrait. The presence of **both** Markel siblings (Pessel, etc.) and Karp relatives (Feiga, Moishe Myer) suggests this was a **gathering of Galician immigrants in the United States**, likely **Rhode Island** (where Morris Markel, Minnie Markel Sternbach, and Moishe Myer Karp settled — see [[Morris_Markel]], [[Minnie_Markel_Sternbach]], [[Feiga_Frei_Karp]]).
- **Hard constraint from the key labels**: The photo includes **Pessel Markel** (murdered c. 1939/1940 in Galicia) and **Helen Brandt Markel** (Morris's wife, married 1919 in Sambor). Pessel was in Galicia; Helen was in Galicia at marriage and emigrated with Morris later. If the photo was taken in the US, Pessel was visiting; if taken in Galicia, Morris was visiting from the US.

**Most likely date and place: c. 1925-1928, Galicia (Sambor or Lwów region), during a visit by emigrated relatives back to the home country.** Alternative: c. 1925-1928, Rhode Island, during a visit by Galicia relatives (less likely given Pessel's documented absence from US records). **Both remain plausible until corroborated.**
