# Lockspeiser Children 1936 Photograph

## Source

`Assets/Lockspeisers-Mom's Maternal Aunt's Family.pdf` page 1. The PDF is supplied by the family with the title "Lockspeisers — Mom's Maternal Aunt's Family"; "Mom" = [[Florence_Markel_Mermelstein]].

## Photograph Description

Studio photograph, sepia / black-and-white, of **6 children** in matching dark sailor suits with white collars and dark caps, posed in a single row against a plain studio backdrop. The children appear to be approximately ages 4 to 10. The pose is symmetrical: arranged shoulder-to-shoulder, all facing camera, varying expressions. The two outermost children wear long pants; the four interior children appear to wear shorter pants or knickers. Cap style is consistent across all children — dark with a small visor. The sailor-suit uniformity strongly suggests the photo was a sibling-group sitting (not multiple families combined) and that the family prepared them for the studio.

A studio signature or stamp is faintly visible at the lower right of the photograph (illegible at current rendering resolution; possibly a Polish, Yiddish, or German studio name typical of Lemberg / Lwów / Sambor / Drohobych studios in the 1930s).

## Inscriptions on the Back

Two distinct handwritings:

1. **Period inscription (pencil, German cursive, top-left):**
   `Zum Andenken / Jahre 1936`
   Translation: "As a memento / Year 1936"
   This is the inscription added when the photograph was originally inscribed, likely by Chawa or someone in the Lockspeiser family, to be sent abroad as a keepsake.

2. **Later inscription (blue ink, English, top-center):**
   `Tante Chava's / Children`
   This is the identifier added by an American descendant — almost certainly Florence Markel Mermelstein or her daughter Ruth Gold (author of the Yichus narrative) — to label the photo within the family archive.

Two horizontal lines and a printed `1` in the lower-right corner are typical of a postcard-format photo back (the lines are address-line guides, the `1` is a manufacturer's batch indicator).

## Identification of the Children

**Names: Unknown.** No individual names are written on the photo back. The only collective identifier is "Tante Chava's Children." The Yichus narrative does not enumerate Chawa's children. The identification key (chat image, see [[Markel_Family_Portrait_Identification_Key]]) does not name them either, as the key labels people in a different photograph (the large sepia Markel/Karp family portrait).

## Dating Cross-Check

- Inscribed year: **1936**.
- Children's apparent ages: **4 to 10**.
- Therefore: oldest child b. c. 1926, youngest b. c. 1932. This places Chawa's childbearing years in the early-to-mid 1920s and early 1930s.
- If Chawa is the same generation as her sister [[Leah_Frei]] (b. 1864), Chawa would be ~62-72 in 1936, which is too old to have children of these ages. **Conclusion**: either (a) the 6 children are Chawa's grandchildren, not her children (and the caption is colloquial — "kinder" in Yiddish can mean either children or grandchildren); or (b) Chawa was substantially younger than Leah Frei (perhaps 25-30 years younger, b. ~1890-1895), making her a much-younger sister; or (c) the caption is in error and these are nephew/niece children. **UNRESOLVED — see Open Questions on [[Chawa_Frei_Lockspeiser]].**
