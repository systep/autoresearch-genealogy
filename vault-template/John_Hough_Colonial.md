## Biography

John Hough was christened on 17 July 1653 at St. Bartholomew's Church, Wilmslow, Cheshire, England, as the son of Lawrance (Lawrence) Hough of Chorley township, Wilmslow parish. His known siblings include Anne (chr. 1646) and Samuel (chr. 1659). The family name derives from "The Hough", a hamlet within Wilmslow parish in Macclesfield Hundred, Cheshire.

On 19 October 1680, John married Hannah Worthington at St. Bartholomew's Wilmslow. Hannah was the daughter of Isaacke Worthington "of The Hough" and had been christened there 14 October 1660. (Earlier speculation that her maiden name was "Rossell" was superseded when her Wilmslow marriage record was identified; see [[John_Hough]] for context.)

In early September 1683, John, Hannah, and their roughly two-year-old son John Jr. sailed from Liverpool on the 60-ton "Friendship", part of the Quaker migration recruited by William Penn. They arrived at Philadelphia on 21 November 1683. On the day of arrival, John Hough was granted a Warrant for 125 acres to be laid out in Chester County, but he and Hannah ultimately settled in Middletown Township, Bucks County. They became members of the Falls Monthly Meeting of Friends (organized 1683) in Bucks County.

John Hough and Hannah Worthington had numerous children, with those born in Pennsylvania recorded in Falls MM registers: Daniel (b. 14 Apr 1693), Isaac (b. 28 Dec 1694/5), Sarah (b. 31 Apr 1701), Joseph (b. 14 Jan 1703), along with their elder children John Jr. (b. 1682 in Cheshire), Mary, Stephen, Hannah, and others.

John of Middletown died in January 1732. His estate was granted to his son John Hough Jr. on 20 January 1732.

See [[Hough_Cheshire_Origin_Research_2026_04_19]] for the detailed research establishing the Lawrance-to-John filiation and the Wilmslow parish register citations.
