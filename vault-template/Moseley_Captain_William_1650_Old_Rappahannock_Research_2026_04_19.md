# Hypothesis: Captain William Moseley of Old Rappahannock (1650) is the Virginia Progenitor, not William Moseley gent. of Lower Norfolk (1649)

## Claim

The Virginia Moseley progenitor most plausibly ancestral to the vault's Surry / Brunswick County line (via Jonathan Moseley b. c. 1683 Southwarke Parish, Surry County) is **Captain William Moseley of Old Rappahannock (c. 1630 to bef. 1677/1681)**, NOT the vault-attributed William Moseley gent. of Lower Norfolk (c. 1605 to bef. 1655).

The two have been conflated in tertiary trees. They are distinct men:

| | William Moseley gent. of Lower Norfolk | Capt William Moseley of Old Rappahannock |
|---|---|---|
| Birth | c. 1605 (possibly St Margaret's Westminster 1606, unverified) | 5 Dec 1630, baptized St Peter's, Kinver (Kinfare), Staffordshire |
| Parents | Unknown | Edward Moseley (c. 1595-1673) and Margaret |
| Arrival | 30 Nov 1649, from Rotterdam, with wife Susanna Burnet | 1649/50, as headright of brother-in-law Col. William Underwood |
| Virginia county | Lower Norfolk (Lynnhaven Parish) | Old Rappahannock (later Essex) |
| Spouse | Susanna Burnet | Martha Brasseur, daughter of Huguenot Robert Brasseur |
| Occupation | Schafmeister / Steward Merchant Adventurers, Delft/Rotterdam | Burgess for Rappahannock County 1671-1674 |
| Death | before 1655 | before 3 Jan 1676/77 (per one source) or late 1681 (per another) |
| Estate | Rolleston Hall, Lynnhaven | Old Rappahannock / Sittingbourne Parish, Essex |

## Virginia Primary-Source Records Located

### Nugent, Cavaliers and Pioneers, vol. 1 (1623-1666) [Tier 2, published primary-source abstract]

- **22 May 1650 patent**: Col. William Underwood, 2,000 acres on the north side of the Rappahannock River (Old Rappahannock County), by headright for transporting 12 persons including "Wm. Moseley, William Underwood (4 times), Mary his wife, and others". This establishes:
  - William Moseley's arrival on or before 22 May 1650, sponsored by brother-in-law William Underwood.
  - William Moseley's sister Mary Moseley was the wife of Col. William Underwood, making Underwood his brother-in-law.

- **18 November 1663 patent**: William Moseley, 1,157 acres in Rappahannock County, by headright for transporting 27 persons. This establishes William Moseley's own role as a sponsoring patentee by the 1660s.

### Rappahannock Records, 1656-64, p. 99 [Tier 1 abstract]

- **21 January 1659/60 deed**: William Moseley and wife Martha, naming Martha's father as Robert Brasseur. This establishes the Brasseur marriage and the Huguenot connection.

### Office-Holding Record [Tier 2]

- Burgess of Virginia representing Rappahannock County, 1671 to 1674 (Jamestowne Society Qualifying Ancestor).

## English Origin: Kinver (Kinfare), Staffordshire

**Strong Signal, pending original-record confirmation.**

- William Moseley baptized at St Peter's parish church, Kinver (Kinfare), Staffordshire, on **5 December 1630**.
- Sixth child and third son of Edward Moseley (c. 1595 to 1673) and his wife Margaret.
- Likely birthplace: Whittington, in Kinver parish.
- Edward Moseley was himself baptized at St Peter's Kinver, 25 April 1599 (WikiTree Moseley-4599), son of an earlier Moseley generation in the Kinfare cluster.
- The Kinfare Moseleys are distinct from the Rolleston Hall (Staffordshire) Mosleys. Sir Edward Mosley of Rolleston Hall (1569 to 1638) died without issue, and the "Rolleston Hall" naming on the Lower Norfolk William Moseley's Virginia estate is commemorative, not genealogical evidence.

**To confirm at Tier 1**: order or access the Kinver St Peter's parish register at Staffordshire Record Office (Stafford) or via FindMyPast (Staffordshire Parish Registers) for the 5 Dec 1630 baptism of William Moseley, son of Edward and Margaret. This is a single-document confirmation task.

## Implication for the Vault's Surry / Brunswick Line

The vault attributes Jonathan Jonas James Moseley b. c. 1683 Southwarke Parish, Surry County, back to William Moseley of Lower Norfolk (1649 arrival). Based on this research:

1. That attribution is likely **incorrect by conflation**. The Surry / Brunswick Moseleys descend from the Rappahannock cluster (via Capt William Moseley's grandson William "the Opportunist" Moseley III), which is the line documented as spreading into the southern Piedmont.
2. The Lower Norfolk line (William Moseley gent. 1649) stayed predominantly in Princess Anne / Norfolk County and produced the well-documented "Rolleston" line, not the Surry / Brunswick line.
3. **The direct paternity of Jonathan Moseley b. 1683 is still not proven**. Candidates in Southwarke Parish, Surry County, tithables 1668 to 1703 need to be searched. The Rappahannock descent is the best working hypothesis but is not yet documentarily closed.

## Status

**Supported** (Tier 2 convergence + Tier 1 deed abstract) for the following claims:
- Capt William Moseley of Old Rappahannock is a distinct individual from William Moseley gent. of Lower Norfolk.
- He arrived 1649/50 as headright of Col. William Underwood (brother-in-law via sister Mary Moseley).
- He married Martha Brasseur, daughter of Huguenot Robert Brasseur, by 21 Jan 1659/60.
- He patented 1,157 acres Rappahannock 18 Nov 1663 and served as Burgess 1671 to 1674.
- English origin: Kinver (Kinfare), Staffordshire, baptized 5 Dec 1630, son of Edward and Margaret Moseley.

**Open** (Speculative):
- Direct paternity of Jonathan Moseley b. c. 1683 Southwarke Parish, Surry County.
- Original-register confirmation of the Kinver 1630 baptism.
