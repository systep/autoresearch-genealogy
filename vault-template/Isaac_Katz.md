## Biography

Rabbi Yitzchak haKohen Katz was the son-in-law of the Maharal of Prague, having married two of the Maharal's daughters successively. He first married Leah Loew, who died childless; he then married her sister Vogele (GEDCOM notes; Jewish Encyclopedia via web search; Geni.com).

Isaac served as Rabbi and Av Beis Din (head of rabbinical court) in Nikolsburg, Moravia, and later in Prague (GEDCOM; Jewish Encyclopedia). He was known as a prominent Talmudist and philanthropist.

According to the GEDCOM notes (citing the Jewish Encyclopedia), "Isaac was in the habit of writing acrostic introductions to his own and other works." His published works include a supplement to "Hatan Damim" (Prague, 1605), glosses to "Pa'neah Raza" (1602), a commentary on the Pentateuch in German (1608), notes on Midrash Tehillim (1613), and "Kizzur Mizrahi" on Rashi.

In 1592, Isaac accompanied his father-in-law the Maharal to an audience with Emperor Rudolf II and recorded an account of the meeting (GEDCOM note). He is also considered the author (or at least the commentator) of the first Yiddish translation of the Pentateuch (Basel, 1583; Prague, 1610).

He is buried in the Old Jewish Cemetery in Prague (GEDCOM note).
