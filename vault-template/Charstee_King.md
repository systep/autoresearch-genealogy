## Biography

Charstee King (maiden name unknown, b. ~1843, South Carolina) was the wife of [[Judson_King]], a farmer in Oaklawn Township, Greenville County, South Carolina. "Charstee" is an unusual given name, possibly a phonetic variant of "Charity" or "Chastity."

By 1880, Charstee and Judson had seven children living in their household: Dora S., Andrew L., Mary E., James E., William C., Tabitha J., and John W. King (1880 Census, Oaklawn Township, Greenville County, SC). Her occupation was listed as "Keeping House."

In 1920, Charstee (age 77) and Judson (age 78) were enumerated as the sole occupants of their household in Oaklawn Township (1920 Census, Series T625, Sheet 3-B, Family 51, Line 67). All children had left by this time. Both Charstee and Judson were recorded as literate.

A user-provided photograph shows Charstee and Judson King together in formal late 19th or early 20th century attire.

Judson died May 12, 1921 of chronic nephritis. Charstee's date of death is unknown; she was alive as of the January 8, 1920 census enumeration. She does not appear as informant on Judson's death certificate (H.H. King of Pelzer, SC served as informant), which may suggest she was unable to serve in that capacity due to age or health, or had predeceased him.
