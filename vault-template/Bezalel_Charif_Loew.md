## Biography

Bezalel "Charif" Loew was a son of the [[Maharal_of_Prague]] and served as a rabbi and head of the rabbinical court (ABD) in Kolin, Bohemia (GEDCOM). The epithet "Charif" (meaning "sharp" or "keen") reflects his reputation as a sharp Talmudic mind. He is listed as "Maharal son #3" in the GEDCOM, indicating he was the third child.

He predeceased his famous father by about a decade, dying in 1599 in Kolin.

His son [[Shmuel_Loew]] (Shmuel ben Bezalel) continued the family's rabbinical lineage, serving as a rabbi in Hessen and Prague. Through this line, the Maharal's descendants eventually connect to the Chabad-Lubavitch dynasty.
