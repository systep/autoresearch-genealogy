## Biography

Joan Plantagenet, known as Joan of Acre, was born in April 1272 in Acre, Palestine, during her father [[Edward_I]]'s participation in the Ninth Crusade. She was the daughter of King Edward I of England and Eleanor of Castile (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Joan first married [[Gilbert_de_Clare]], 7th Earl of Gloucester and Hertford, in 1290. Gilbert was one of the most powerful magnates in England. They had four children, including [[Margaret_de_Clare]] (b. 1292). Gilbert died in 1295 (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

After Gilbert's death, Joan secretly married Ralph de Monthermer, a knight in her household, in 1297. Edward I was furious at the mismatch and briefly imprisoned Monthermer, but eventually relented and allowed the marriage. Joan died in 1307 at Clare, Suffolk (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Joan is a key link in the pedigree charts connecting colonial American families through the De Clare, De Audley, and Neville lines back to the Plantagenet royal house.
