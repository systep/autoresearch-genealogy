## Biography

Rabbi Chaim ben Bezalel Loew-Beer was the grandfather of the Maharal of Prague. He served as Rabbi in Worms, Germany (GEDCOM; loebtree.com). According to the GEDCOM notes, he was "Rabbi in Worms, descendant of King David," though this Davidic descent claim is contested by modern scholars (see [[Yehuda_Leib_the_Elder]]).

Chaim married Vogelin Feigele, and they had four sons: Nathan Loeb, Zvi Helman, Yaakov (who became Reichsrabbiner, Chief Rabbi of the German Jews), and Bezalel ben Chaim (father of the Maharal). The GEDCOM also references sources from "Bobov Tree Anaf Samech (Gimmel)" and "Rosenstein Rabbinical Chart & Chaim Fredman article on MaHaRal."

The Hebrew note in the GEDCOM cites "Alef Margaliyot [537]" which states he was the son or grandson of Yehuda Leib HaZakein of Prague, and that his origins were from Alsace, dying in approximately 1530 (Hebrew year 290).
