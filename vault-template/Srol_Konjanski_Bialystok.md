## Biography

Srol Konjanski appears only indirectly in the record, as the **father** named in the 1874 Białystok Jewish vital register death record of his son Gersz Konjanski (JRI-Poland Białystok death index, record #138M, 1874, microfilm LDS 1191936). See [[Kuniansky_JRI_Poland_Bialystok_2026_04_19]] for the full derivation and surrounding context.

He is the presumed **grandfather** of [[Sore_Leja_Kunjanska_Bialystok]] (d. 1866 Białystok), daughter of Gersz, on the plausible but unproven assumption that the Gersz named in both records (1866 Sore Leja daughter-of-Gersz; 1874 Gersz son-of-Srol) is the same individual.

**Relationship to the vault's Atlanta Kuniansky cluster ([[Wolf_Kuniansky]], [[Harry_Kuniansky]], [[Isadore_Kuniansky]], [[Milton_Kuniansky]], etc.)**: originally held as a speculative candidate ancestor, **downgraded to unlikely on 2026-04-19** after independent family-authored and institutional sources (kuniansky.org; Breman Museum oral history of Max Lewis Kuniansky 1990-07-28; Find a Grave for Harry Kuniansky) consistently placed the Atlanta cluster's origin in Podolia Gubernia / Vinnytsia region (Ukraine), not Grodno Gubernia / Białystok. See [[Kuniansky_JRI_Poland_Bialystok_2026_04_19]] for the full reassessment.

Retained as a stub person file to preserve the evidence of a distinct Białystok Konjanski family for surname-distribution and control-case purposes.
