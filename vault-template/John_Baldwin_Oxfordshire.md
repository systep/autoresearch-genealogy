## Biography

John Baldwin Sr. was born circa 1657 in Oxfordshire, England, likely at Hook Norton (see [[Baldwin_Chesham_Quaker_Research_2026_04_19]]). In 1682, he sailed to Pennsylvania as one of three Baldwin brothers who were servants of Joshua Hastings, a Quaker. The other brothers were [[Thomas_Baldwin_Oxfordshire|Thomas Baldwin]] and Francis Baldwin (b. Hook Norton 20 Apr 1667, d. 1702 New Castle Co. Delaware, miller). John's 1731 will names legacies to "Thomas, John, and Ann, children of his brother Francis Baldwin," confirming the three-brother relationship.

John settled in Chester County, Pennsylvania, where he worked as a carpenter. He became a wealthy merchant over time. On record at the Chester Monthly Meeting, he married Katherine (Carter) Turner in 1689 (Chester Monthly Meeting Records, Tier 1). Katherine was previously married with the surname Turner before her marriage to John.

John's 1732 will (Chester County probate records, Tier 1) mentions his grandsons John and Joshua, the sons of his son [[John_Baldwin_Chester_PA|John Baldwin Jr.]]. This will provides key evidence linking the generations.
