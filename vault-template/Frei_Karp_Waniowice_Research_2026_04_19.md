## Findings

### 1. Gesher Galicia Frey Family Page (HIGH PRIORITY LEAD)

Gesher Galicia publishes a dedicated page for the Frey family of Galicia at `https://www.geshergalicia.org/families/?id=frey`. This directly confirms "Frey" is a recognized, indexed Galician Jewish surname in the All Galicia Database (AGD, 900,000+ records). The page is surfaced as the #1 result for "Frei Frey family Waniowice Sambor Galicia Jewish 19th century rabbinic." Membership may gate full content, but the FamilyFinder entries are typically visible to free users. (Tier 2 lead; pointer, not evidence.)

### 2. Gesher Galicia Karp Family Page (HIGH PRIORITY LEAD)

Parallel page for the Karp family of Galicia at `https://www.geshergalicia.org/families/?id=karp`, with 2 FamilyFinder entries noted in result snippet. This confirms Karp as a recognized Galician Jewish surname, though count is small. (Tier 2 lead.)

### 3. JRI-Poland Sambor Vital Records Project (ACTIVE)

`https://www.jri-poland.org/project/sambor/Vital-Records-Project/` exists and is described as "currently in migration" to the new search system. Waniowice falls under the Sambor civil registration district; Sambor records are therefore the correct JRI-Poland project for Jozef/Taube Frei and any surviving siblings. Human action: email the project coordinator to request a Frei/Frey/Fry Waniowice-limited lookup.

### 4. Pnei Yehoshua Descent Claim (CAUTION)

The Yichus narrative claim that the Frei family descends from R. Jacob Joshua Falk ("Pnei Yehoshua," 1680-1756) is a common oral tradition. Wikipedia (en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacob_Joshua_Falk) and Geni confirm the Pnei Yehoshua had a second wife Toba and at least four sons and two daughters; however, familymemory.org/falk-family explicitly warns that "research has not found any reliable, published information or contemporary records to support the connection of Jacob Jehuda Loebel Falk to the Pnei Jehoshua." Same caution applies here: the Frei-to-Pnei-Yehoshua claim is **Tier 3 (oral tradition) unless a documentary bridge is produced**. Coincidence of the name "Toba" (our [[Toba_Rivka_Frei]]) with the Pnei Yehoshua's second wife is suggestive but onomastically common and cannot stand as evidence.

### 5. Karp Surname Etymology (RESOLVED, NON-RABBINIC)

Per Ancestry, Geneanet, 23andMe, Wiktionary, and hebrewsurnames.com:

- Primary meaning: from Middle High German *karp(f)e* / Slavic *karp* / Yiddish *karp* = 'carp' (the fish). A metonymic occupational name for a carp fisherman or seller, or a nickname for someone resembling the fish. Falls within the Ashkenazi animal-surname class (Hirsch, Adler, Hecht, Wolf, etc.).
- Secondary: shortened vernacular form of Latin *Polycarpus* / Greek *Polykarpos* ('fruitful'). Less likely in Yiddish-speaking Galicia.
- Tertiary (speculative): acronym. The pattern exists (e.g., HARPAK = *Hatan Rabbi Pinchas Kalman*), but no source found for a specific Karp acronym. This remains a hypothesis only and should not be asserted without primary evidence.

**Conclusion**: Karp in Galicia is most likely an occupational/animal surname, not intrinsically rabbinic. Any rabbinic status in our Karp branch must be argued from biography (yeshiva attendance, ordination records), not from the surname itself.

### 6. Sambor Jewish Context

- Sambor had 1,377 Jews in 1880 (Wikipedia / sztetl.org.pl).
- Sambor and Drohobycz districts were jointly administered by *Kreisältester* (district parnassim), per jewishgen.org/yizkor/drohobycz/dro011.html.
- Staryi Sambir (Stary Sambor) is an adjacent but distinct locality; cadastral map 1853 held at Gesher Galicia Map Room. Waniowice is in the Sambor (not Staryi Sambir) commune cluster.
