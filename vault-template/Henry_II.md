## Biography

Henry II was born on March 5, 1133, at Le Mans, France, the eldest son of Geoffrey V, Count of Anjou, and Empress Matilda (daughter of Henry I of England). He was the first Plantagenet (Angevin) king of England (Pedigree Charts; Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Henry married [[Eleanor_of_Aquitaine]] on May 18, 1152, shortly after her marriage to Louis VII of France was annulled. Through this marriage, Henry gained control of the vast Duchy of Aquitaine, making the Angevin Empire the largest domain in Western Europe. They had eight children, including four sons who survived to adulthood: Henry the Young King, Richard (later Richard I), Geoffrey, and [[King_John]] (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

Henry became king in 1154. His reign was marked by legal reforms (including the development of common law and the establishment of jury trials), conflict with Thomas Becket (Archbishop of Canterbury, murdered in 1170), and eventual rebellion by his sons. Henry died on July 6, 1189, at Chinon Castle in France and was buried at Fontevraud Abbey (Wikipedia, Tier 2).

In the pedigree charts, Henry II is the earliest English king in the direct patrilineal royal descent, connecting through [[King_John]], [[Henry_III]], [[Edward_I]], and [[Joan_Plantagenet]] to the colonial American lines. The pedigree charts note the descent continues further back through the Counts of Anjou, Brittany, and Gatinais to approximately 800 to 900 CE (Research_Log.md).
