## Biography

Rabbi Shneur Zalman of Liadi, known as the **Alter Rebbe** ("Old Rebbe"), was born September 4, 1745 in Liozna, Belarus, to Boruch Poisner (1721-1790) and Rivkah Loew. He was a descendant of Rabbi Judah Loew ben Bezalel (the Maharal of Prague), though this claim of Maharal descent is contested in modern scholarship.

He is the **founder of Chabad-Lubavitch Hasidism**, one of the most influential movements in Jewish history. He authored the **Tanya** (Likutei Amarim), the foundational text of Chabad philosophy, and the **Shulchan Aruch HaRav**, a comprehensive code of Jewish law.

Shneur Zalman married **Sterna Segal** (b. 1745). Their son **Dovber Shneuri** (1773-1827) succeeded him as the second Lubavitcher Rebbe (the Mitteler Rebbe). Another descendant, **Chaya Mushka**, married **Menachem Mendel Schneersohn** (the Tzemach Tzedek, 3rd Lubavitcher Rebbe).

He died December 15, 1812 while fleeing Napoleon's invasion of Russia.

### Connection to the Salmanson Family

Shneur Zalman is the direct patrilineal ancestor of the Salmanson family through the following line: Shneur Zalman of Liadi (1745-1812) founded Chabad. His son Dovber Shneuri (1773-1827) was the Mitteler Rebbe. Dovber's daughter [[Rivka_Beila_Schneerson]] (1797) married [[Yekutiel_Zalman_Wallis]] (1794-1872). Their son [[Srul_Mendel_Zalmanson]] (1820-1854) was the father of [[Levi_Itzhak_Zalmanson]] (1851), who was the father of [[Barnett_Salmanson]] (1883-1960).

The historical Zalmanson-Schneerson connection is confirmed at Strong Signal by authoritative sources (Wikipedia, Chabad.org official timeline, the 7th Rebbe's letter).
