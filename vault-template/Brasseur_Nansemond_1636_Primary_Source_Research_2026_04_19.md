# Brasseur Nansemond 1636 Primary Source Research (2026-04-19)

## Scope

Push the Brasher line from Tier 2 to Tier 1 by pinning Robert Brasseur's VA Colonial Land Office patents (1636, 1638, 1640, 1653 Nansemond Co.) to primary-source abstracts in Nugent's Cavaliers and Pioneers Vol. 1, and by attempting to identify his specific French Huguenot origin parish. Ultra-narrow session, WebSearch only.

### Tier 1: Rappahannock probate / deed

- 21 Jan 1659/60 Rappahannock deed (Rappahannock Records 1656-64, p. 99): **"...whereas Robert Brusner [sic] gent new deced did by his last will and testament give unto his daughter Martha now wife me William Mosely..."** specifying land in Nansemond. This ties Robert's Nansemond patent estate to his daughter Martha, wife of [[Captain_William_Moseley_1650_Rappahannock]], and places Robert's death immediately before 21 Jan 1659/60 (not 1663 as some tertiary trees claim; the 1663 Calvert Co. MD date belongs to the separate Robert Brasseur of Calvert Co.).

### Waypoint: Isle of Thanet, Kent (1622-1632)

Five Brasseur children's baptisms are registered on the Isle of Thanet, Kent between 1622 and 1632. Named children documented to the Isle of Thanet parish registers:

- Katherine Brasseur, b. 1622-1632
- John Brasseur, b. 1624
- Thomas Brasseur, b. 1626

The Isle of Thanet was a well-known receiving point for French Huguenot refugees fleeing the 1620s Catholic persecution (Siege of La Rochelle, 1627-1628).

### French Origin: NOT YET CONFIRMED

Secondary sources (next1000.com, lmperry.net, WikiTree Brasseur-19) propose three candidate regions, but none is tied to a parish register:

1. **Bouches-du-Rhone** (about 20 miles from the Mediterranean), often cited in compiled genealogies.
2. **Loire valley, just north of La Rochelle** (i.e., Poitou/Aunis/Saintonge, the classic Huguenot core).
3. **Rhone valley downstream from Geneva, Switzerland** (Dauphine).

Geographical-distribution sources that list Huguenot French home towns in the 1620s emigrant wave name **La Rochelle, Dieppe, Le Havre, Marennes, Saint-Jean-d'Angely, Vitre** (provinces of Aunis, Saintonge, Poitou, Normandy, Dauphine, Languedoc). Robert's first stop in Kent (Isle of Thanet) is most consistent with a Channel-coast departure from **Dieppe or Le Havre (Normandy)** or an Atlantic-coast departure from **La Rochelle (Aunis)**; the Rhone-valley candidate is geographically awkward for an Isle of Thanet landfall.

**Best current guess (speculative, not primary-sourced):** Normandy (Dieppe or Le Havre) or La Rochelle / Aunis. Downgrade the Bouches-du-Rhone claim to speculative.

## Clarification: Two Robert Brasseurs

A recurring conflation across Geni, Ancestry, and some WikiTree profiles merges:

- **Robert Brasseur of Nansemond Co., VA** (this file): b. before 1600 in France, Isle of Thanet 1622-1632, VA patents 1636-1653, died Nansemond/Rappahannock **before 21 Jan 1659/60**. Father of Martha (Brasseur) Moseley.
- **Robert Brasseur of Calvert Co., MD**: different person, died c. 1663 Calvert Co. MD (the ancestor relevant to the Brashear/Brasher line through Benois "Bennet" Brasseur and Maryland denization 1662).

They may be related (father/son, uncle/nephew) but they are not the same man. The 1653 patent's transport of "Bennet Brasseur" along with Robert and Florence suggests Benois was a son of the Nansemond Robert (and later the Calvert Co. Robert is likely Benois himself or his son).

## Specific Next Steps

1. **Direct archive.org consultation of Nugent, Cavaliers and Pioneers, Vol. 1 (1623-1666)** at https://archive.org/details/cavalierspioneer00nuge to pin exact page numbers for the 1638, 1640, and 1653 patents. Confirm p. 41 for the 1636 note. (Next session; WebFetch or manual read of djvu.txt.)
2. **Isle of Thanet parish registers** (Kent Archives, Maidstone; also on FindMyPast). Search 1620-1635 for Brasseur baptisms. Five are expected; the father's name, witnesses, and the specific Thanet parish (St John the Baptist at Margate, St Peter at Broadstairs, or Minster-in-Thanet) will sharply constrain the French parish of origin.
3. **French Huguenot Church London registers** (Threadneedle Street, also the Walloon Church of Canterbury). Families that transited Kent often registered births, marriages, or deaths at these churches, and the registers routinely record the parish of French origin.
4. **Walloon/Huguenot Church of Canterbury** (Crypt of Canterbury Cathedral) registers, 1575-1700. A "Brasseur" entry naming a French parish of origin would be the decisive Tier 1 French-origin record.
5. **Nansemond Co. VA burned-record reconstruction.** Nansemond County records were largely destroyed in 1734 and 1779; cross-check via neighboring Isle of Wight Co. and Lower Norfolk/Norfolk Co. deed books, and the VA Colonial Land Office microfilm at Library of Virginia for the original patent texts (not just Nugent's abstracts).
6. **Rappahannock Records, 1656-64, p. 99** (original, Library of Virginia): confirm the 21 Jan 1659/60 deed transcription, and extract any other named parties that may identify Robert Brasseur's surviving wife or other heirs.
7. **Rule out Rhone valley / Bouches-du-Rhone**: the Isle of Thanet landfall is geographically inconsistent with a Mediterranean-basin origin. Downgrade that claim in future versions of [[Brasher_Brashier_Origin_Research_2026_04_19]].
