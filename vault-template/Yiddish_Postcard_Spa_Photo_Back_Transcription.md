## Physical Description

- **Postcard format**. The back is divided by a faint vertical center line — standard postcard layout (post-1907 international Postal Union format) separating message (left half) from address (right half).
- **"Carte Postale"** watermark printed across the top center in Latin script — characteristic of French-printed postcard stock that was used internationally, including across Austro-Hungarian / Polish Galicia.
- **Text fills both halves of the back**, suggesting the message overflowed into the address area; the addressing was likely done elsewhere (perhaps the photograph was sent inside an envelope alongside other correspondence rather than mailed loose).
- **Ink**: dark blue / black, fountain or steel-nib pen.
- **Light pinkish stain at upper right** (age-related discoloration or contamination).
- **Approximately 9 lines** of text visible, written in Hebrew/Yiddish cursive.

## Script Analysis

- The script is **Yiddish handwritten cursive** (handgeshribene yiddishe kursiv), not Hebrew block letters. This is the everyday handwriting style of Eastern European Jews ~1880-1939.
- Letter forms are consistent with the **Galician / Polish Yiddish** script tradition; many letters have the rounded loops typical of Lemberg / Lwów / Sambor / Drohobych writers.
- **No date stamp, postmark, or stamp visible** on the back side as scanned. Either the postcard was hand-carried (never mailed), or the stamp/postmark side is the photograph side and the watermark/postmark area is hidden by the photograph itself, or postmark was on a separate envelope.
- **No salutation line in Latin script; no signature in Latin script.**

## Transcription Status

**A speculative full transcription would be more harmful than helpful** per CLAUDE.md Guard Rail #1 (do not fabricate). The Yiddish cursive at the resolution of the pushed image is dense and overlapping; reliable transcription requires a specialist.

### Recommended specialist transcription routes

1. **JewishGen ViewMate** — community translation board (jewishgen.org/viewmate). Free; turnaround days to weeks. Submit both faces of the postcard for context.
2. **Yivo Institute for Jewish Research** — paid professional transcription (yivo.org). Faster turnaround, higher accuracy.
3. **Sambor / Galician Jewish heritage networks** — Sambor District Research Group on JewishGen has volunteers familiar with the regional script tradition.

### What I can observe (low confidence)

- A possible name or place reference midway through the text — capital-style cluster ending in what *could* be "...בארג" *...berg* (a common Galician town-name suffix, e.g., Lemberg).
- Line lengths and density consistent with a personal letter rather than a formal communication.
