## Notes

- **Primary source, Tier 1.** New York State official marriage record. Supersedes the MyHeritage/FamilySearch index entry which surfaced the same record but only in summary form.
- Cedar Ave in 1910 NYC is in **the Bronx** (not Manhattan). 1800 Cedar Ave was a residential address near the University Heights/Morris Heights border. Julius's store likely also in the Bronx.
- 10 E. 115 St is in **East Harlem, Manhattan** — a heavily Jewish neighborhood in 1910.
- **Rev. Jacob Edelborn** officiated at a Harlem synagogue; the address (E. 114 St) points to that neighborhood. A research hook for Julius + Rebecca's congregation.
- **Witnesses**: Leo Friedberg is likely Rebecca's brother (shared surname); Bernhard Goldberg is a relative through Rebecca's mother Henriette Goldberg (likely maternal uncle or cousin). Both are new Friedberg-family data points.
- Two previously unknown people implied: Julius's deceased first wife (name not stated) and Rebecca's deceased first husband (surname Marcus, given name not stated).
- **Libau, Russia** is present-day **Liepāja, Latvia** (formerly in the Kurland/Courland governorate of the Russian Empire, not Kovno). Rebecca is therefore not from the Kovno/Ukmerge Salk cluster; she is from a different Baltic Jewish community.
- Cross-reference: this couple also appears in the MyHeritage index as "Julius Salk b. circa 1871 Kovno, m. Rebecca Marcus (born Friedberg) Nov 24 1910 Manhattan, parents Benjamin Salk + Esther Rappaport." Tier 2 index → Tier 1 certificate upgrade confirmed.
